# Roy

> **One-line reading.** Roy is the heart — the vulnerable, authentic self the whole project is trying to return to, and the self [[Sir Lucius]] was built to protect.

Roy is the center of gravity in the Jagwar Twin world. He's the human being underneath the masks, the voice most songs are sung through, and the figure every other character is defined in relation to. He's also a real person — and the work draws openly on his life, which is part of why it lands the way it does.

## Who he is

Brandon Roy Wronski, born May 22, 1988, in Los Angeles. He came up through bands (Eye Alaska among them), went solo as **Roy English** in 2015, and became **Jagwar Twin** in 2018. He signs himself **El Roy**. By his own account he's been dropped by a major label, been through a hard breakup, and lost a close friend — the raw material the songs metabolize.

## The wound: the ballerina boy

The earliest injury the work keeps returning to happened at age seven. Roy did ballet at a Christian school. A teacher told his father they thought he was gay; Roy overheard. Surrounded by girls who told him he *wasn't a real boy*, he prayed to be made *good*, and he buried the part of himself that came alive on stage. He never danced again.

> "I never danced again / I killed that ballerina boy / I buried him inside"

That buried child — the [[Ballerina Boy]] — is the thing the whole arc is trying to recover. The adult dance videos Roy later made are, in his telling, shadow-work: going back for what he left on the floor.

## The collapse and the mirror

At thirty-five Roy hit bottom — broke, back in his childhood bedroom — and wrote the song [[Lucius Lullaby]] into a laptop mic in desperation. He doesn't hide the darkness; he's described "really deep depression… really deep crippling anxiety, where I couldn't go to the grocery store." But he frames it as the cost of the work: *"I'm glad I've seen darkness, because I can understand it, and there's something on the other side."*

Out of that comes the thing he names as his purpose — to be a **mirror**:

> "My mission with music is to be a mirror — the clearest mirror I can be, to remind people who they truly are."

To do that, he says, he has to dive into his own psyche first and come back as a clearer reflection for everyone else. It's the discipline the entire mythology runs on, and it has a one-line resolution he keeps returning to: **"My mind must serve my heart."** Whether he gets there is the open question of the project.

## Relationships

- **[[Sir Lucius]]** — the mind divorced from the heart, formed from Roy's own interior life. *"I made you."* Lucius pulled Roy out of the bottom; the deal may have been worth it. Their tension — mind serving the heart versus mind replacing it — *is* the project.
- **[[Mary]]** — the divine-feminine voice that blesses where the world shamed. She's the *"you were born to shine"* that answers the childhood *"you're not a real boy."*
- **[[The Jaguar]]** — the project's emblem of seeing: the cat that looks into the souls of others and back into itself. The frame around everything, not a separate character.

## Two ways to say the same thing

A small, clean illustration of the Roy/Lucius split: both have said *"everyone's a guru."* From Roy it's rueful humility — *we're all trapped performing authority, me included.* From Lucius (on the same account) it's contempt — *everyone's worthless except me.* One observation, two hearts. That gap is the whole difference between them.
