# Mary

> **One-line reading.** Mary is the divine-feminine voice in the Jagwar Twin world — a faceless blessing-presence that appears where shame tried to erase the self and says, *you were born to shine.*

If [[Sir Lucius]] is the voice that tells you you're a failure, Mary is the voice that tells you who you actually are. She's the counter-frequency to the shame-voice — and unlike Lucius, she doesn't need an audience and doesn't change shape depending on who's watching. She's the same in every room.

## Who she is

Mary is best understood as a recurring **figure rather than a fixed character** — a feminine presence whose marker is consistent across the catalogue: a **face hidden or out of sight**, and a blessing meant to call someone back to themselves. She is not the credited backing vocalist on any track (that's a real collaborator, not a symbol); Mary is the *presence* the songs keep reaching for.

## Where she appears

She's named outright once, and felt several other times:

- **"#1 Champion (la Conquistadora)"** — the named appearance: *"Mary, Mary, so contrary… saw a halo but I didn't see a head… La Conquistadora with a gun in her hand… fawning over no man."* Faceless (a halo, no head), armed, autonomous — and she's the one who unseats the boastful "champion." Her titles, *La Conquistadora* and *Queen of California*, are repurposed names for the Virgin (Our Lady of the Conquest, Queen of Heaven).
- **"Weirdness"** — *"she looked like an angel baby, but her face was out of sight… stop pretending, baby, you were born to shine."*
- **"Ballerina Boy"** — the spoken outro is an older, motherly female voice: a blessing — *"my son, let your light shine"* — laid directly over the childhood wound. The clearest instance of the voice in the whole catalogue.

There's a direction to these appearances worth noticing. On *Subject to Flooding* (2018), a song is dedicated *to* the faceless — *"this one's for the faceless"* — who **receive** the care. By 2025–26 the faceless one is the one **giving** the blessing. The object of care becomes the source of care.

## What she does

Where [[Sir Lucius]] traps a person in the image — the mirror that reflects and holds — Mary **transmits**. She passes the source-light through rather than catching it. Her blessing, *"born to shine,"* is the structural answer to the shame-voice that started it all (*"you're not a real boy,"* the ballerina-boy wound on [[Roy]]'s page). She appears precisely when things destabilize and change becomes possible.

## How to read her

One strong interpretive frame — drawn from the Kabbalah that Roy openly studies — reads Mary as the **Shekhinah**, the divine feminine *in exile*: the moon that reflects a light not its own, longing to be restored. On that reading, *"saw a halo but I didn't see a head"* is the crown glimpsed but not yet possessed — the presence that has almost arrived but whose source stays hidden. It's a framework correspondence, not something the work states outright, but it fits the recurring shape: a diminished, face-hidden presence promising a light that's coming.

## Relationships

- **[[Roy]]** — the one she blesses; the self she's calling back to life.
- **[[Sir Lucius]]** — not simply her opposite. Where he reflects, she transmits; where he needs watchers, she's constant. They can be read as *two ways of relating to the same underlying drive* — which is why she's the one thing his system can't absorb.
