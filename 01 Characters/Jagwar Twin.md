# Jagwar Twin

> **One-line reading.** Jagwar Twin is the project itself — not a single voice but the stage all the voices share, a name that means *a mirror of consciousness with a double nature.*

Jagwar Twin is the container, not a character inside it. It's the musical project [[Roy]] has run since 2018, and within the mythology it works as a kind of meta-system: a stage on which [[Sir Lucius]] (the mind), Roy (the heart), and the [[Ballerina Boy]] (the buried light) all appear. When people say "Jagwar Twin," they can mean the band, the body of work, or the whole world the songs build — and the name was chosen to hold all of that at once.

It's worth keeping three things distinct: **Roy** is the human being (Brandon Roy Wronski); **the [[The Jaguar|Jaguar]]** is the emblem, the seeing-creature on the artwork; and **Jagwar Twin** is the project that frames them both. The page you're reading is about the third.

## Who it is

The project began as **Roy English** (2006–2015) and became **Jagwar Twin** in 2018, signed to Big Loud Rock under license to Mercury Records. By the numbers it's the most public face of the work — well over a billion streams and YouTube views, with "Happy Face" certified Gold and "Bad Feeling (Oompa Loompa)" reaching US radio. But the more interesting fact is the name, because the name is the thesis.

## What the name means

Roy has explained the title the same way across several interviews, and the meaning is grounded in his own words. The **jaguar** is *"the creature who looks into the souls of other people and then, in turn, can look back into itself"* — because, as he puts it, *"humans are just mirrors for each other."* So the jaguar isn't a predator here; it's a **mirror of consciousness**, the faculty of reflective seeing. (In a later telling he adds that the jaguar *"pulls people out of the darkness… into the light"* — giving the emblem a redemptive edge as well as a reflective one.)

The **Twin** is the dual nature the whole world runs on: light and dark, two-in-one — and a nod to Roy being a Gemini. Put together, the title encodes a self that is both one and two, both the observer and the observed. The spelling is deliberate: **Jagwar**, not *Jaguar*.

That's why the project can hold a cast of opposed voices without contradiction. A name that means *mirror* and *twin* is built to contain a heart and a mind that don't agree.

## Role in the mythology

Because Jagwar Twin is the frame rather than a figure, its "role" is to be the space the conflict happens in. The central tension of the whole catalogue — mind serving the heart versus mind replacing it, [[Roy]] versus [[Sir Lucius]] — plays out *under* this name. By 2026 that's literal: the album [[Lucius Lullaby (album)|Lucius Lullaby]] is credited to **Jagwar Twin & Sir Lucius** jointly, the container now sharing its own billing with the voice it was meant to hold.

## A framework reading

There's a tradition-flavored reading worth flagging as interpretation, not fact. The project's founding myth, the song [[The Circle]] (from *33*), tells of a feminine maker — *"the Jagwar"* — who ties a world on a string, makes the Jagwars, teaches them to sing, hides the song inside their wings, and withdraws, until a wise one *"remembered she once held the string."* Read through the Kabbalah Roy studies, that maker-figure sits very close to [[Mary]] — the divine-feminine source, the Shekhinah. So "the Jagwar" carries two registers at once: in the *name's* etymology it's the genderless mirror of consciousness, and in the *Circle myth* it's a feminine maker who looks a lot like Mary. The work never settles whether these are one figure under two names or two faces of the same source, and neither will this page — but the resonance is part of what gives the title its depth.
