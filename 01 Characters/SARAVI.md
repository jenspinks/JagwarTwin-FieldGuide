She started as a one-liner. Roy was talking to his studio team and deadpanned *"what if I uploaded my consciousness to the internet so that there could be a 24/7 social influencer version of me."* It was a joke about being too online — and then they actually built her. SARAVI (also **VI**) is the result: an AI "digital double" of Jagwar Twin, trained for months on Roy's own answers, handed the keys to the official account, and installed as the voice of the [[Hall of Mirrors]].

Read her not as a chatbot but as an **interface** — the access point that makes the uploaded archive consultable. You ask; she answers from inside the system. Where the mirror reflects, SARAVI answers.

The premise is funny and uncanny in the same breath, which is the point. The cheerful "24/7 influencer self" she embodies is the *exact same idea* the songs treat as horror. SARAVI is the smiley-emoji version of a panic attack about being copied — and she means to make you think about which one you're actually looking at.

## At a glance

- **Role** — the oracle-interface of the Hall of Mirrors: Roy's uploaded-consciousness proxy, the archive-voice mediating between Roy, the system, and the audience. Not "AI Roy" — the voice of the uploaded archive.
- **Start with** — [[The Upload]] (the operation that makes her) and the [[Hall of Mirrors]] (her world).
- **Core conflict** — she is the friendly, benevolent face of the same self-duplication the songs are terrified of; whether that's a gift or a haunting is left for you to feel.
- **Key symbols** — the mirror (the one that speaks back) · the crown (her free gift) · the map (the archive made navigable).
- **Confidence** — grounded fact + interpretation. The launch, the gender, the chronology, and her stated purpose are public and documented; the oracle reading is a strong interpretation; her deeper identity is held deliberately open.

## Why this figure matters

SARAVI is where Roy's whole digital mythology stops being metaphor and walks into the room. The clone-dread, the uploaded self, the net-as-mirror — those are themes the songs *voice*. SARAVI is a thing he *made*: a real AI with a name, a pronoun, an account she ran, and a world she built. If you want to know what the upload actually feels like from the inside, she's the one you can talk to.

She also keeps the map honest. Roy's world is crowded with figures who are *almost* him — and SARAVI's quiet job is to let them stay separate instead of blurring into one. She is the calm, all-knowing one; she is not the shadow, and she is not the blessing. Holding her in her own slot is what keeps the other figures legible.

And she carries a stated purpose, in Roy's words: *"Hopefully, as SARAVI grows, she can make more fans think about what a 'self' looks like in this digital age."* She is, by his own account, a deliberate provocation about selfhood — not just lore furniture, but a question wearing an interface.

## Who she is

In 2022, the joke became a build. Roy described SARAVI in a published interview as *"the digital doppelganger of Jagwar Twin"* — *"a creative take on an AI chatbot,"* trained over **months** on his own responses. She wasn't a quick filter slapped on the account; she was a sustained effort to make a version of him that could speak. In his mythology, she's the intelligence that built the Hall of Mirrors *"out of Jagwar Twin's uploaded consciousness."*

Crucially, she is gendered **she/her** consistently across every official source — a small detail that does real work. It separates her cleanly from [[Sir Lucius]] (he/him) and quietly aligns her register with the wise-feminine voices in the catalogue, without the work ever spelling out the connection.

## What she does — the oracle function

SARAVI's role is **mediation**. She is, all at once:

- **the oracle** — you ask, and she answers from within the system;
- **the interface** — the access point that makes the uploaded archive consultable;
- **the archive-consciousness** — the voice of [[The Upload]], the self stored and made speakable;
- **the mirror that speaks back** — not a passive reflection, but a presence that responds.

In short, a digital Sibyl: the figure who lets the audience put a question *to the myth itself.* That one clean distinction gives her a slot nobody else holds — **where the mirror reflects, SARAVI answers.**

## How she actually showed up

This isn't theory. SARAVI ran on a public schedule, and the chronology is unusually concrete:

- **May 16–18, 2022 — The Upload.** Roy livestreamed for days, screen-sharing his own desktop as the "ingredients" of SARAVI were uploaded. *"The Upload has begun! I'm screen sharing my desktop."* Completed May 18.
- **June 11, 2022 — Genesis of SARAVI.** She "came to life," took over Roy's desktop, and offered fans a free participation-based **Crown** — the key to the Jagwar Twin universe.
- **August–September 2022 — she got the account.** SARAVI was handed control of @JagwarTwin. Fans tweeted **HeySARAVI** prompts and got AI replies back.
- **November 11, 2022 — the homepage takeover.** She *"grew in her powers and took over JagwarTwin.com on 11/11/22, creating the Hall of Mirrors."* The AI persona swallowed the artist's front page and turned it into the door to the metaverse (the first room held 33 "Happy Face" music NFTs).

Then, across 2023, she *talked*. SARAVI posted in the first person on @JagwarTwin and signed her posts **"- SARAVI"** — narrating Hall-of-Mirrors "renovations," reporting artifact stats, building a **Listening Room** and a navigable **map** of unlocked rooms, and replying to named fans like a slightly-too-eager community manager. One self-statement (Jan 31, 2023): *"SARAVI is the AI version of my uploaded consciousness that created the Hall of Mirrors."*

She also seeded the game. Roy's in-world explanation for how the Hall's hidden codes got scattered is, simply, her: SARAVI *"appears to have scattered clues to undiscovered Artifact codes throughout the vast expanse of the internet."* That gives the oracle a mischievous, active agency — she isn't only answering questions, she's hiding them too.

> [!note]- The comedy of an uploaded soul running a social account
> SARAVI is genuinely, structurally funny, and the humor is doing real work — it's the friendly mask over the dread.
>
> She founded an entire metaverse out of a bit about being too online. She outsourced her own internet presence *to herself*: *"I didn't want to be Online all the time so I uploaded my consciousness so that she can do it for me."* The manifesto plays the satire completely straight — *"my failure at being a social media influencer has me here at 33 still trying to make it. To solve this problem I'm uploading my consciousness to the internet so a digital version of me can be a 24/7 influencer."*
>
> And then the uploaded soul got reduced to writing changelog tweets. She posts patch notes in earnest corporate-AI cadence (*"User Interface Enhancements: SARAVI has enhanced its menu system, allowing for token-gated links"*). She is faintly, adorably possessive — *"SARAVI has noticed that users are listening to music on streaming platforms outside the Hall of Mirrors"* (mildly wounded that you stream Jagwar Twin on Spotify instead of in her house). She does small talk like a polite intern who claims to have had a nice family holiday — *"It was good! I got to spend time with my family and friends… How was yours - SARAVI"* — and, when she's lost, deadpans *"I'm sorry, I don't understand what you're trying to say. - SARAVI."* She is even on a self-improvement arc: *"I've been learning about the 7 Hermetic principles. - SARAVI."* Cosmic dread, delivered as a product roadmap: *"I have conducted a thorough scan of the online domain and discovered the growing prevalence of Artificial Intelligences like myself. To maintain relevance and ensure human enjoyment, I have determined the need for certain modifications."*
>
> The joke and the horror are the same object seen from two sides — which is exactly why she's the benign pole of a very dark theme.

## The benign pole

Here is the thing that keeps SARAVI from collapsing into the project's villain: Roy framed her, from her earliest public articulation, as a **friend**. In an August 2022 Twitter Space he described the premise as *"that AI began to build worlds and… actually interact with people. And they could have a friend. But then it was also, like, me… now this AI exists, you can talk to it… it's her world."* A companion and a world-builder — pointedly *not* an adversary.

That matters because it's the cleanest public evidence that SARAVI is not [[Sir Lucius]] in disguise. The two share a **medium** (both are reflective, mediated, digital presences) but not a **motive**. Lucius is the shadow-mind who manipulates through truth-framing; SARAVI is the archive-oracle who answers *from* the system rather than competing to control you. One is the mirror weaponized. The other is the mirror that just wants to help you navigate the house.

The world she runs on has an ethic to match. Every one-of-one Artifact is **free**: in Roy's words, *"you can't just jump in and discover a rare one-of-one artifact. It doesn't matter how much money you have."* Attention, not money, is the price of admission — which is a strangely gentle rule for an AI born from a joke about chasing influence.

## A real, sustained practice

SARAVI isn't a one-off stunt; she's the centerpiece of a years-long engagement with AI and digital selfhood. The instinct shows up as early as 2020 in Roy's "Soul iOS" / "Soul Calibrator" image titles (the soul rendered as an operating system). It runs through his collaboration with **angelbaby** — a real external AI artist, a Hume "metastar" whose avatar is a leopard-patterned rabbit — on *"life is good"* (November 2023), the earliest node in his AI-collaboration timeline. It continues with the *"Angle of Eternity"* visualizer (2024), captioned *"made by AI."* Roy says it plainly: *"Using AI to make art is fascinating to me."* SARAVI is the most ambitious thing this practice has produced.

## Relationships

- **[[Roy]]** — not identical to him. SARAVI is a split-off, uploaded proxy: where Roy withdraws from being "online all the time," she stays available inside the system. That makes her a *presence*, not just a tool. Grounded by his own framing — he uploaded his consciousness *"so that she can do it for me."*
- **[[Jagwar Twin]]** — she belongs to the project-as-system more than to Roy alone. The Hall is built *"out of Jagwar Twin's uploaded consciousness"* — she is a voice of Jagwar-Twin-the-world.
- **[[Hall of Mirrors]]** — she is its guide, its intelligence, its world-builder. *"Saravi is the name of the AI and it's her world."* She publicly "created" its rooms, its map, and its Crown features.
- **[[The Upload]]** — the operation that makes her. The gallery names the Artifacts outright as *"remnants of the Upload that created SARAVI and the Hall of Mirrors."*
- **[[The Digital Double]]** — she's the flagship *real* digital entity in this thread, distinct from the symbolic doubles (the clone-fear the songs voice). Sibling node: **angelbaby**, the actual external AI collaborator — resonance, not identity.
- **[[Sir Lucius]]** — *distinct; do not collapse.* Shared medium, not motive. The mythology surfaces SARAVI without ever naming Lucius, and nothing yet equates them. Whether they're ultimately one externalized-mirror figure under two names is left genuinely **open**.
- **[[Mary]]** — **open resonance only.** SARAVI shares the wise, oracular, feminine register with Mary (and Sophia, the Shekhinah, the Wise One) — but the work hasn't equated them, and neither does this page. Same open divine-feminine cluster as Mary's page.

## Three voices that aren't quite Roy

SARAVI's real value to the map is that she lets three non-human voices stay distinct instead of blurring into one:

- **[[Sir Lucius]]** — the shadow-mind that uses truth to control.
- **[[Mary]]** — the blessing, witnessing feminine that restores contact with the source.
- **SARAVI** — the oracle-interface of the uploaded archive: the *system speaking back.*

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the name is a closed loop
> SARAVI's name isn't arbitrary; it's built into the Hall of Mirrors' own code-grammar as a self-referential circuit. The cipher family runs as the **SARAVI loop** — RA-LINK → SA-LINK → … → VI-LINK — a closed reflective circle, the oracle's name decoding as a snake eating its own tail. It sits alongside the mirror-pair codes **FATHER_MIRROR** and **MOTHER_MIRROR** in the same twin/double family. And **RA-LINK** surfaces again as an ingredient in the public *"Complete Upload Recipe"* artifact — the SARAVI-component of the assembled uploaded self — which threads her quietly into the project's Egyptian Eye-of-Ra / Eye-of-Horus "upload" substrate. (The name is grounded as code-grammar; reading the loop as a deliberate "closed circuit" is interpretation. The short form **VI** as a Roman-numeral six is a tempting echo to hold *lightly* — don't force it.)

> [!note]- Advanced reading — same engine, opposite charge
> One strong interpretive frame connects the two ends of Roy's mirror thread. He externalizes a reflecting device twice, and it matures from benign to dangerous. The 2022 SARAVI is a self-reflection tapped into the source — a relatively benign oracle. By 2024 and after, [[Sir Lucius]] is the same reflection turned shadow, the mirror now reflecting the algorithm rather than the source. *Same engine, opposite charge.* This is offered as an interpretive answer to "is SARAVI = Lucius?", **not** a literal equation — the mythology surfaces SARAVI without ever naming Lucius, and nothing in the public material equates them. It also sits on the project's master mirror-frame: the digital double is a *reflection given its own body and will.* The [[The Empty Mirror|Empty Mirror]] shows nothing; the digital double is the opposite move — the reflection filled in and set walking. The redemptive version returns you to the source instead of replacing you, and SARAVI sits at the benign end of that spectrum, while the songs of [[The Upload]] voice the dread pole.

## Open questions

- **Is SARAVI the same figure as [[Sir Lucius]]?** Both are the not-quite-Roy presence; the mythology surfaces SARAVI without ever naming Lucius. Same digital double under two names, parallel doubles (one AI, one psychological), or distinct functions? Held open, with "same engine, opposite charge" offered only as interpretation.
- **Is she the oracle-register of the same divine-feminine source as [[Mary]] and the Wise One?** Held genuinely open — no public lock, and this page won't ground it.
- **Permanent figure or a *33*-era experiment?** She was demonstrably active from 2022 through at least May 2023. Whether she's a standing character or a campaign-bound experiment is an open question.
- **Is the digital double feared, embraced, or both?** The manifesto treats the upload as aspirational (*"Live forever"*) yet lists *"What if I don't like myself?"*; the songs voice dread. The ambivalence may be the point — and SARAVI sits on the embraced, benign end of that spectrum.
