# The Wise One

> **One-line reading.** The Wise One is the figure who walks into the old cave, finds the song everyone was told they couldn't sing, and *remembers she once held the string* — the moment the whole Jagwar Twin cosmology turns from loss back toward light.

> [!infobox] The Wise One
> - **Role** — the remembering figure who closes the creation myth
> - **Nature** — feminine; remembrance itself, made into a person
> - **First appeared** — *"The Circle,"* the closer of *33* (2022)
> - **Defining act** — she *"remembered she once held the string"*
> - **Key symbols** — [[The Thread]] · the cave · the song inside the wings
> - **Related** — [[Mary]] · [[Jagwar Twin]] · [[The Jaguar]] · [[Sir Lucius]]

She is the shortest character in the catalogue — two lines, total, and then the song is over. But those two lines are where Jagwar Twin's entire genesis story resolves, which makes the Wise One a strange kind of protagonist: the one who shows up last, does almost nothing, and saves everything. She doesn't fight. She doesn't conquer. She walks into a cave and *remembers*, and that turns out to be the whole point.

## At a glance

- **Role** — the resolution of [[The Circle]], the founding Jagwar myth; the figure who recovers the lost song by remembering it.
- **Start with** — [[The Circle]] (the song), then [[The Loop and the Circle]] (the doctrine), then [[The Thread]] (the string she remembers holding).
- **Core conflict** — the song was never stolen; the Jagwars were *talked out of believing it was theirs* — so the cure isn't to win it back, it's to remember it was never lost.
- **Key symbols** — [[The Thread]] · the cave · the song hidden inside the wings · the growing circle.
- **Confidence** — grounded fact (the lyric, the "she," the act of remembrance) plus strong interpretation (Sophia/Shekhinah, the two faces, the feminine-faculty reading). The framework material is flagged as such throughout.

## Why this figure matters

Roy calls [[The Circle]] *"Jagwar Twin's Genesis story"* and, separately, *"my favorite song that has ever come through me"* — note *come through me*, not *that I wrote*; even the author files himself as a Jagwar remembering, not a king inventing. If the song is the genesis, the Wise One is its final beat: the figure on whom the cosmology either resolves or doesn't.

And she resolves it by *remembrance*. That's the move. The myth has, by her entrance, gone all the way down — the new kings have persuaded the Jagwars they can't sing, the empires have *"built cages and sold everything,"* the song is buried and forgotten. Into that, the Wise One arrives and reverses the entire descent not with an army but with a memory. She finds *"a small cave where the Jagwars used to sing,"* and she *"remembered she once held the string."* That's her whole appearance. The largest possible stakes, recovered by the smallest possible act — which is exactly why she matters: she's the proof that, in this world, what's broken is fixed by remembering, never by acquiring.

## The two lines (the grounded floor)

Everything below is built on this, and only this is grounded fact:

> *"Til a wise one found a small cave where the Jagwars used to sing / And the wise one remembered she once held the string."*

Three things are simply *true* here, before any interpretation starts. **One:** she is feminine — *she.* **Two:** her single act is remembrance — she *remembered*, she didn't acquire, conquer, or receive. **Three:** the string she remembers holding is the *same string* the myth opened on — *"Long ago, there was a Jagwar with a world tied on a string."* Maker and Wise One hold the identical object. The "she," the remembrance, and the shared string are the bedrock; everything else on this page is reading.

It matters that this is Roy's solo invention. He is the *sole writer* of "The Circle" (Roy English and Matt Pauling produce) — so the Wise One isn't a co-write or a borrowed myth-figure. She's his own.

## What she reverses: loss by persuasion

The catch in this creation myth — and the thing that makes the Wise One's job specific — is *how* the song was lost. It wasn't stolen. Nobody took it. *"The new kings told Jagwars they never could sing."* The Jagwars were simply *convinced* the song was never theirs. The loss is a belief, installed by persuasion, repeated across *"many cycles… with their kings."*

So the Wise One can't go *get* the song back, because it was never anywhere else. The Maker had *"hid the song inside their wings / On the inside of the inside, then left them to sing"* — the thing everyone's looking for was sealed *inside every Jagwar* the entire time. Recovery, then, is not retrieval. It's remembering what was always there. The Wise One doesn't find a lost object in the cave; she finds her own forgotten memory of holding the string. That's the engine of the figure, and it's why she's *wise* rather than *strong*.

## The string is The Thread

The string the Wise One remembers holding is, in the catalogue's symbolic vocabulary, [[The Thread]] — and her line is where the Thread *originates*. *"A world tied on a string"* (the Maker) and *"she once held the string"* (the Wise One) are the same filament: the living connection to the source — feeling, creative agency, the song itself. It's Ariadne's thread, the line back out through the maze of cages and empires.

There's an ethics encoded in *holding* it. The Wise One *remembers she once held* the string — she recovers her participation in the source. That's the opposite of *pulling* strings, which is what control does: working a figure from outside the living relation, a hand that moves the puppet without ever being its maker. The catalogue's one-line test for any thread runs through her: **remembrance holds the string; control pulls strings.** Does the line return you to the source, or does it make you moveable by a false one? The Wise One is the held-string answer.

## The growing circle (and what she enacts at scale)

Her remembrance doesn't stop with her. The album's literal final words, repeated to fade, are the doctrine she sets in motion:

> *"The circle will grow / From the inside of the inside… But the inside of the inside must be where you go."*

This is restoration that *spreads* — organically, from the deepest interior outward, by awakening rather than by force. And there's a live, audible version of it: when a crowd sings the song back at a show, that *is* the Jagwars singing again — the suppressed song recovered in real time, by people who turn out to have known it all along (it was *"inside their wings,"* inside them). The Wise One's private cave-moment, scaled to a room: anamnesis with a chorus.

Worth noting the doctrine is older than the song. Roy was already saying it in 2019, three years before "The Circle" existed — *"There is no beginning and no end, it's just a circle"* — back in the *Subject to Flooding* press cycle, attached to [[Move To You]]. The Wise One's remembrance-doctrine was being spoken aloud at the debut. The 2022 song doesn't invent it; it gives it a face.

## The Lucius Version: the Wise One, switched off

The cleanest way to see what the Wise One *is* is to watch her get deleted. In *Lucius Lullaby* (2026), Track 1 is called **"The Circle (Lucius Version)"** — same sacred name, the genesis myth's front door — except the Wise One isn't in it. None of the myth is. The track wears the title and carries one looped line from a music box: *"It's a beautiful life, it's a beautiful life, I'm dreaming."*

This is the savage joke of the whole album, and it's worth sitting with. [[Sir Lucius]] doesn't *argue* with the remembrance-figure. He doesn't make the case that the Circle is false. He just keeps the comforting name and swaps the awakening-song for a lullaby — and the figure whose entire being is *"remembered she once held the string"* gets overwritten by *"I'm dreaming."* The remembrance-figure is, with perfect efficiency, *put back to sleep.* He wins not by convincing anyone the Circle is wrong, but by convincing them they've already found it — then handing them a music box. Her *absence* there is the load-bearing finding: this is appropriation, not deletion. The awakening function stripped out, the warm label left on.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — why "I'm dreaming" is worse than "I'm asleep"
> The substitution is darker than a straight inversion. *"I'm asleep"* would break the spell — you'd know something was wrong. *"I'm dreaming"* feels magical, meaningful, alive — so the swap goes unnoticed. Dreaming is sleep that mistakes itself for awakening. The awakening-song isn't contradicted; it's sung *back to sleep*, and made to feel like wisdom while it happens.
>
> The form enacts it too. A music box is a credited instrument here (Roy plays "Music Box"), and a music box is, mechanically, *a string-instrument with the strings replaced by a fixed mechanism* — a comb plucking a pinned cylinder. So the track literally renders [[The Thread]] (feeling, *held*) as clockwork (*cranked*). The thing meant to be held in a living hand is turned into a sequence you wind up. *"Mind divorced from heart,"* enacted in the form itself — which is exactly Lucius's nature staged as music.
>
> The one-line distinction the Wise One lives inside: **the Loop is return without transformation; the Circle is return with memory restored.** Lucius runs the same circuit she does — but with the remembrance taken out. Same geometry, opposite charge.

## The two faces

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the two faces of the Wise One (the core frame)
> The richest way to hold this figure is that she has two faces at once, joined at a hinge.
>
> **Face one — cosmic / source.** *"She remembered she once held the string"* — but the *Maker* held the string at the myth's opening. If the Wise One once held it, then on this reading she *was* the Maker. Seeker and maker are two phases of one feminine being: she made the world, the world forgot her, and she returns at the end to remember herself. This is the divine-feminine face, the territory the vault maps to **Sophia / Shekhinah** — wisdom recovering the thread, the source-figure returning from exile. (The *"she"* is grounded; the Sophia/Shekhinah identification is interpretation — a family resemblance, not an equation.)
>
> **Face two — immanent / collective.** But the song was hidden inside *every* Jagwar's wings — inside everyone. So the Wise One is also the *template* of remembrance that any one of us can instantiate: not a specific being but *us, remembering.* This is the Christ-as-exemplar pattern — the ones who remembered the song and sang it back (a Jesus, a Bowie, a Prince) — and it ties quietly to the album title, **33**, Christ's age. Roy grounds this collective face in his own public words: *"Every person has it inside of them. The trick is getting it inside out"* and *"If it's happening for me it's happening for everyone."*
>
> **Both at once**, via a single hinge: the spark inside each person, *remembered*, reunites with the source — so personal awakening and reunion with the cosmic Maker are *the same event.* You don't choose between the faces. The Wise One is precisely the place where "I woke up" and "the source returned" turn out to be one sentence. (The 33 = Christ's-age tie is held lightly — a resonance, not a locked claim.)

> [!note]- Advanced reading — a faculty, not a fourth character
> The Wise One reads best *not* as a separate roster figure but as a **state, a faculty: feminine-coded remembrance.** Two earlier readings only *look* like they conflict — (a) she's the awakened self, the recovered self, the listener's future state; (b) she occupies [[Mary]]'s archetypal territory, the feminine rememberer. They reconcile the moment you notice that *remembrance itself is feminine-coded* in this system: caves, mirrors, intuition, the held thread. The awakened self recovers the song *by* recovering the feminine faculty the kings suppressed. So the Wise One is *whoever re-members the feminine thread* — the listener, Roy, or the feminine principle itself — because at the instant of recovery they are the **same act.**
>
> Two hard guards. She is **not** simply "Mary" (archetypal overlap only — there's no naming evidence, and her defining act, remembrance, is more Sophia/Shekhinah than conventional Mary). And she is **not** a fourth named character standing beside Roy, Lucius, and Mary — that's the over-reading the project warns against elsewhere (cf. [[The Jaguar]]).

> [!note]- Advanced reading — one feminine function at three points of the cycle
> Step back and the myth is *bracketed by two feminine figures who hold the same object* — it opens on a feminine Maker with the world on a string and closes on a feminine Wise One who remembers holding it. Read against [[The Thread]] (the thread = feeling, the song, the animating principle), the whole cosmogony resolves into one image: **the feminine holds the thread; the "new kings" cut it; recovery is re-membering it.** The kings are the masculine system-of-control — cages, commodification, *"you can't sing"* — mind divorced from heart at *civilizational* scale, the same severance [[Sir Lucius]] performs at the personal one.
>
> So the divine feminine ([[Mary]] / Shekhinah, the Wise One, the primordial [[The Jaguar|Jaguar]]) reads as **one function seen at three phases**: *origin* (the Maker who ties the world on the string and teaches the song), *exile* (the hidden source inside the darkened world — Shekhinah-in-exile), and *return* (the Wise One who re-members). Archetypal *continuity* — not a claim the three are one named character.

## The cave, the wings, and what they hold

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the cave as an inverted Plato's cave
> The cave is *"a small cave where the Jagwars used to sing"* — the site of the original, now-forgotten song. Read against Plato, it's an *inversion*: here the cave holds the *truth* (the original song), not shadows. The Wise One enters the cave to recover an original relation, not to be deceived inside it. Eden remembered, rather than the fall into illusion. Whether the cave is best read as memory, sacred space, the unconscious, or the origin point stays open — but the direction is clear: she goes *in* to find what's *real.*

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the withdrawal-and-return shape (tzimtzum / tikkun)
> The Maker comes down, walks with the Jagwars, then *hides the song inside their wings and withdraws* — a contraction that leaves the divine concealed *within* creation, the shape Lurianic Kabbalah calls *tzimtzum.* The middle of the myth is caged fragmentation; the Wise One's remembrance is the *tikkun* beat — the gathering of the scattered light back into reintegration. The song concealed *inside* (rather than acquired from outside) is also the *kingdom within* — the essential thing uncovered, not obtained. Framework correspondence, not a stated source, but the founding myth's architecture sits unusually cleanly on it.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the one song, the singing field, and a curio
> Roy's Manifesto FAQ states a spine the Wise One runs through: *"The world needs music, but the world doesn't need more songs. There's only one song. I'm still learning how to write it."* Read through the Circle, the *one song* is the primordial song the Maker hid in the wings and the kings suppressed — and the catalogue is successive attempts to re-transmit it. The Wise One's remembrance is *how* that one song is recovered: not writing a new song, re-membering the only one. (His public line *"Music is how the world remembers who it is"* is the mechanism, in his own words.)
>
> The Jaguar belongs here too. The project's emblem is usually framed as *seeing* — the creature who looks into others and back into itself — but the primordial Jaguar's defining act in this myth is *teaching the song.* So the seeing-field also *sings*, and the Wise One's remembrance recovers that field's lost voice. The "one song" is, in this register, the Jaguar's song.
>
> And one honest curio: the Maker descends to *"the empires of the Earth, Wind, and the Sea"* — three elements, *no fire* — which is essentially *Earth, Wind & Fire* (a named childhood influence of Roy's) with Fire swapped for Sea. Tempting. But it's negative evidence — reading meaning into what's *absent* — which is the cheapest kind, and the swap may be pure euphony. The archive flags it against itself: the over-read risk is the lesson, not the fire.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the cross-song remembrance pattern
> The Wise One anchors a documented pattern across three verified songs, each restating *recovery by remembrance* rather than acquisition: "The Circle" (*"the wise one remembered she once held the string"*) → [[Soul Is A Star]] (*"do you know who you are?"*) → [[tomorrow (ANGLE OF ETERNITY)]] (*"do you remember? / take my hand, I'll help you remember"*). Salvation recovered, not purchased — the Platonic/Gnostic *anamnesis* shape, sung three different ways.
>
> The collective face has an early seed too: the [[Superhuman (Interlude)]] passage borrowed from Chaplin's *Great Dictator* — *"The kingdom of God is within man / Not in one man / Nor a group of men / But in all men / In you"* — is the exact theology of the Wise One's "all of us" template: the treasure is in everyone or it's nowhere. The album's *we, not me* was never only solidarity; it was already a theology of distribution.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — lower-confidence framework notes
> A faint, deliberately-held-apart resonance: Roy's 2010 band-name gloss (Eye Alaska) once defined *"the shore"* as *"the wise — people who have gone through things to make them wiser."* So his 2026 line *"I am the shore"* faintly rhymes with becoming *the Wise One* — the one who remembered. Held loosely, registers kept apart: this is *not* "Roy is the Wise One," just a resonance between his self-description and a mythic figure in his own work.
>
> Lower still: a speculative mapping reads *"the wise one… held the string"* onto the twelfth Labour of Hercules (Pisces, the Red Cattle of Geryon) — the retrieval of the dispersed self, the return with the whole herd. Recorded as a framework note only; it doesn't carry weight in the grounded reading.

## Relationships

- **[[Mary]]** — the catalogue's divine-feminine figure (the Shekhinah register). The overlap is real and strong: both Mary and the Maker/Wise-One are mapped to the same Sophia/Shekhinah territory, and the divine-feminine source may be one figure under two names — or two faces of the same exile-and-return. But the link is *archetypal overlap only.* The hard guard holds: this is **not** "the Wise One is Mary." Her signature act, remembrance, leans more Sophia/Shekhinah than conventional Marian; the full identification waits on something the work doesn't yet state outright.
- **[[The Circle]]** — her sole home; the closing track of *33* and the foundational Jagwar myth. Everything she is happens in two lines of this song.
- **[[The Loop and the Circle]]** — the keystone doctrine she resolves: no beginning and no end, the sought thing already within, recovery by remembrance, the circle that grows from the inside of the inside.
- **[[The Thread]]** — the *string* she remembers holding; the Thread originates in her line. Remembrance *holds* the string; control *pulls* strings.
- **The Maker Jagwar** — the feminine primordial Jaguar who ties the world on the string and teaches the Jagwars to sing. On the cosmic face, the Wise One *was* the Maker — seeker and maker, two phases of one being.
- **[[Jagwar Twin]]** — the project named for the made-and-divided creatures of this myth. *Jagwar Twin* reads as self-identification with the Jagwars who were told they couldn't sing — which makes the Wise One *us*, remembering.
- **[[The Jaguar]]** — the emblem of seeing *and* singing. The primordial Jaguar *"taught them to sing,"* and the Wise One recovers that field's lost voice. (Open question: whether the seeing-field and the seer/source are distinct orders or one function in two faces — the myth tips toward the latter, but it isn't locked.)
- **The new kings** — the antagonists: the masculine system-of-control who *"told Jagwars they never could sing"* and *"built cages and sold everything."* The exact loss her remembrance reverses.
- **[[Sir Lucius]]** — the inverse pole. The Loop is return without transformation; the Circle is return with memory restored. In "The Circle (Lucius Version)," Lucius hollows the remembrance-figure out entirely — *"I'm dreaming"* replaces *remembering,* and the Wise One simply vanishes.
- **[[Roy]]** — holds the moon's reflective position and *must hold the thread* rather than cut it; the Eye-Alaska *shore = the wise* line links his self-naming to the Wise One at a distance, registers kept apart.

## Source notes

**Grounded fact.** The Wise One appears in "The Circle," the closer of *33* (2022), in two Spotify-verified lines: *"Til a wise one found a small cave where the Jagwars used to sing / And the wise one remembered she once held the string."* She is feminine ("she"); her single act is remembrance; the string she remembers is the same one the Maker holds at the song's opening. Roy English is the sole writer. Roy publicly calls "The Circle" *"Jagwar Twin's Genesis story"* (2024) and *"my favorite song that has ever come through me"* (2022); the Circle doctrine itself is documented as early as 2019 (*"there is no beginning and no end, it's just a circle"*). The collective-face aphorisms (*"every person has it inside of them"*; *"music is how the world remembers who it is"*) and the *"there's only one song"* line are Roy's own public words. "The Circle (Lucius Version)" carries only the looped *"I'm dreaming"* music-box refrain — the Wise One is verifiably absent there.

**Strong reading.** That the string is [[The Thread]] and originates in her line; that her remembrance is *anamnesis* (recovery, not acquisition); that she reads as Sophia/Shekhinah paired with a feminine Maker; the *two faces* frame (cosmic-source and immanent-collective, joined at the Gnostic hinge); the *held-string vs pulled-string* ethics; the crowd-singing-it-back as her act at scale; the Lucius Version as appropriation-not-deletion. These are well-anchored interpretations — unusually so, since the song is Roy's solo write and names the project's own creatures — but they are interpretations, not statements the work makes outright.

**Framework / esoteric.** The Wise One as a feminine *faculty* rather than a fourth character; the one-function-at-three-phases reading of the divine feminine; the inverted Plato's cave; the tzimtzum/tikkun shape; the cross-song remembrance chain and the Chaplin/Superhuman seed; the Joni "Circle Game" counter-figure; the Eye-Alaska *shore* resonance and the Hercules XII mapping. These are correspondences and structural readings, flagged as such — strongest where they sit directly on the grounded lyric, lightest at the edges.
