# Shine

> **One-line reading.** The dawn after the deluge — the debut's closer answers a rigged, violent, drowning world not with the album's defensive momentum but with a sun-drenched insistence on faith, agency, and *chosen* light. Name the flood, refuse to run from it, choose the sun anyway — twice.

> [!info] Album & credits
> **Album:** *[[Subject to Flooding]]* (2018; Atlantic streaming metadata 2019) — **track 13 of 13, the album closer** · 2:56
> **Written by** Roy English *(and, per an early credit read, Matthew Pauling — unconfirmed; see [Source notes](#source-notes))* · **Producer / mix / master** not separately documented for this track
> **Recording owner** (℗-line): Atlantic Recording Corporation / WEA International Inc.
> Also released as **"Shine (Live In Studio)"** — standalone single, January 17, 2020, tied to a quarantine video project
> **Full lyrics:** [Genius](https://genius.com) · streaming

## At a glance

- **Era / release** — the *Subject to Flooding* debut (2018), the very last track; revived in 2020 as a "Live In Studio" quarantine single ("Jagwar Twin Lets Fans Shine During Quarantine").
- **In one line** — name the flood, refuse to run from it, choose the sun anyway — twice.
- **Who speaks** — [[Roy]], first-person *and* communal. This is 2018: no [[Sir Lucius]], no [[Mary]], no [[The Circus|Circus]] yet. Just the man, at dawn, after the album's flood-night. One of the last places in the catalogue you get him with no costume in the room.
- **Key symbols** — the rising sun · the flood / the sea ([[Subject to Flooding]]) · chosen light ([[The Light-Bearer]]) · the rigged house ([[The House and the Vessel]]) · the mirror ([[The Mirror]], via Roy's paratext, not the lyric).

The floor under every reading is Roy's own account of writing it, and it's almost comically plain. He wrote *Shine* watching *Troy* — the Brad Pitt war epic: *"A lot of war and killing people in that movie, so I wanted to write a chorus that felt happy, like the sun rising."* A sunrise chorus, born in reaction to a screen full of slaughter. Everything else on this page is built on top of that sincere, uncomplicated intent — and the page keeps it as the grounded baseline it is.

## The song, mapped

The architecture is one clean shape run **twice**: *diagnose the overwhelming dark → choose faith, agency, and light in spite of it → resolve into dawn.* The whole thing is bracketed by a sunrise refrain that opens and closes the track, so the sun literally gets the first and last word. Follow its own moves:

| # | The move | In the song | What it does |
|---|---|---|---|
| **0** | **Sunrise refrain** *(opens, x2)* | *"When I rise / to a brand new morning I / know the sun's gonna / shine, shine down on me"* | The spine. A paired couplet that frames the whole song — an **envelope of sunrise**. Note the verb: *rise*. Note also the doubled *shine, shine* — a word the hook will triple, and the later catalogue will turn into a blessing. |
| **1a** | **Diagnosis I** *(present tense)* | *"Wake up, look at my phone and read the news / it's bad, really bad / I mean really, really, really bad"* | Waking straight into the news cycle. The triple-*really* is the song's one wink — comic overstatement that undercuts the doom even while naming it. |
| **1b** | **The systemic rig** | *"They run the house and rig the game to get the upper hand"* | The trap isn't personal, it's structural — the game is fixed. Then the deadpan movie-longing (*"I wish that life was like a movie… I like those movies a lot"* — flat, almost a shrug) and the plain, almost childlike confusion at violence (*"we could all get along"*), played straight. |
| **1c** | **The turn — faith + agency** | *"So let's change the world, we can start today… move oceans and earth 'cause we got the faith"* | The pivot from diagnosis to decision. *We* can start *today*; faith is a lever big enough to move oceans. |
| **1d** | **Shine hook + sing-back** | *"Everywhere I go, I'm gonna shine, shine, shine… so **sing it** if you know you're gonna shine"* | The self *claims* the light — the *shine* now tripled — and then hands it to the crowd. An explicit invitation to sing it back. |
| **1e** | **The dawn image** | *"I was in the clouds, now the sun is coming out singing"* | The resolution lands as weather: cloud → break → sun. The turn made literal. |
| **0** | **Sunrise refrain returns** *(x2)* | *"When I rise / to a brand new morning…"* | The couplet comes back mid-song, re-centering the sunrise between the two passes. |
| **2a** | **Diagnosis II — the flood** *(second person)* | *"It's like an earthquake under your feet / but you're in the sea so you're gonna drown"* | The album's title metaphor, named at last — and named as *drowning*. The grammar has shifted from *I* to *you*: the dread is handed to the listener. |
| **2b** | **The totality of opposition** | *"And everyone's against you / from your family to your country"* · *"watching homeruns while you bunting"* | Opposition scaled all the way up (family → nation), plus a rueful, folksy image of rigged odds — a fixed game rendered as a hopeless at-bat. |
| **2c** | **The pivot to agency** | *"Nothing can stop you from doing what you believe"* · *"I had dreams and then I made 'em / something that no teacher teaching could ever teach"* | The locus of control moves *inside*. The power to manifest a reality no institution could hand you — victimhood refused without denying the rigged game. |
| **2d** | **Radical inclusivity** | *"We're all the same in every nation / gender of my generation"* | A one-humanity claim. (*"Gender"* is the correct word — providers misread it; see [Source notes](#source-notes).) |
| **3** | **Repeat + close** | chorus + shine hook, then the sunrise refrain *(x2)* to end | The *"let's change the world"* chorus and shine/sing-back return, resolving again on *"the sun is coming out."* The sunrise couplet gets the last word. |

**The pronoun arc is the engine.** The grammar travels *I → we → you → we*: it opens on *"I rise / down on **me**,"* widens to *"**we** got the faith,"* turns outward to *"sing it if **you** know you're gonna shine,"* diagnoses *"**you**,"* then lands on *"**we**'re all the same in every nation."* The song doesn't just declare the light — it **hands it to the listener.** That handoff is the seed the later catalogue picks up (more below).

## What it means

A few claims that cut across the whole song.

**1. Dawn after the flood — the closer's job.** The rest of the album is a set of *defenses for outrunning the coming deluge*: shame-preemption in [[Loser]], outlaw velocity in [[Hell Of A Night]] and [[Hell of a Night Pt. 2 (The Hallelujah Stickup)]]. *Shine* is the morning after the storm. It's the only track that **names the flood outright** — *you're in the sea so you're gonna drown* — and then **stands its ground instead of running.** The frantic momentum finally resolves into a steady solar dawn.

**2. The joy is earned, not blind.** This is the load-bearing distinction. The speaker names the violence, the rigged game, the drowning — and chooses to shine *anyway*. Roy says exactly this: *"even in dark times, there is always light if you want to find it. There's love in every moment if you know where to look."* Chosen light, not denial. It's the same decision-not-denial move the catalogue runs again years later in [[Life Is Good]] — and *Shine* is its 2018 antecedent. Worth remembering when he wrote it: *"a couple of years ago when the collective thought was this couldn't possibly get any worse!"* The news-cycle dread isn't incidental. It's the deliberate ground the sunrise has to rise over.

**3. Agency over circumstance.** *"When life was not a celebration, I had dreams and then I made 'em / something that no teacher teaching could ever teach"* moves the locus of control from *out there* (everyone's against you, the game is rigged) to *in here* — the power to manifest a reality no teacher could confer. It refuses pure victimhood **without** pretending the sea and the rigged game aren't real.

**4. Reflection → radiance.** Read across the album, *Shine* completes a turn. *Subject to Flooding* **opens in reflection** — the disguise "getting clearer" in [[Long Time Coming]], the self studying its own reflected light — and **closes in radiance**: the self no longer studying the mirror but trying to *become the source.* From *seeing the image* to *being the light.* That's the deep architecture the debut is running, and *Shine* is where it resolves (see [[Source vs Reflection]]).

**5. The earned dawn, not the false one.** The album has two brightnesses. [[Good Day]] is the *easy* dawn — light attached to circumstance, a good mood, a wave you're riding. *Shine* is the *expensive* dawn, the one that has passed through betrayal, dread, crime imagery, drowning, and mortality and chosen the light on the far side. *Subject to Flooding* isn't "everything is fine." It's **shine anyway.**

**6. The mirror doctrine attaches here.** In the same 2020 quarantine interview where he explained *Shine*, Roy gave the corpus's most direct statements of his mirror doctrine: *"I just want to be a mirror,"* and *"I hope that people see themselves. More and more I realize that a song is a mirror."* That's paratext — it's true of the *song's function*, not a line in the lyric — but it's what grounds reading *Shine* (and the whole debut) through [[Source vs Reflection]]. The sing-back invitation is the mirror made participatory: sing it back and you see yourself in it.

## Characters

- **[[Roy]]** — the speaker, first-person and communal, and *pre-persona*. In 2018 there is no mask to sort out, no shadow to argue with. *Shine* is Roy at dawn, straightforwardly. It's one of the last places in the catalogue you get him with no costume in the room.
- **[[Mary]]** — **does not appear here.** She's named only as the *later* voice who will adopt and invert Shine's vocabulary. Guard, stated plainly: *Shine* is the **seed**, not a Mary appearance — do not read the blessing-voice into it. She isn't here yet.
- **[[Sir Lucius]]** — absent; post-dates 2018 by years. Named only for the cross-era contrast the later catalogue builds: light *declared and transmitted outward* here; light *reflected outward as spectacle instead of transmitted as love* later.

## Symbols

- **The rising sun** — the central image and the chosen response to a dark world. *"Like the sun rising"* (Roy's stated design), *"the sun is coming out singing,"* *"the sun's gonna shine down on me."* The album's reflection-to-radiance turn, completed.
- **The flood / the sea** ([[Subject to Flooding]]) — named and *survived* rather than outrun: *"you're in the sea so you're gonna drown,"* *"an earthquake under your feet."* The title metaphor, met head-on in the closer. Read across the album it's **double-valent**: drowning (the threat) *and* the fall of the walls between us (see the Advanced reading below).
- **Chosen light** — *"there is always light if you want to find it."* Not blind optimism; light that has passed through the flood. This is where the catalogue's light-bearing vocabulary gets planted (see [[The Light-Bearer]]).
- **The rigged house** ([[The House and the Vessel]]) — *"they run the house and rig the game to get the upper hand,"* *"watching homeruns while you bunting."* The systemic-trap image; the House imagery recurs later (Bounce's *"tear the roof off my house,"* Pay Attention's *"big BIG house"*).
- **The mirror** ([[The Mirror]]) — attached via Roy's paratext (*"a song is a mirror"*), not the lyric itself; the ground of the reflection reading.
- **Dawn / morning** — *"a brand new morning,"* *"now the sun is coming out."* Emergence-from-storm; the album runs night-to-morning and, deeper, reflection-to-radiance.
- **The egg** *(resonance)* — via Roy's one-word 2021 caption *"Egg"* on a painting of a sun cracking open over the ocean: a still of *"the sun coming out,"* the same **drown → crack → dawn** move made into a found image (see the Advanced reading below).

## Connected songs

- **[[Subject to Flooding]]** — the album whose flood *Shine* names directly and meets head-on; *Shine* is its reflection→radiance closer, the earned dawn.
- **[[Precious Time]]** — the **immediate predecessor** (track 12). It widens the album from the personal (poverty, shame, fame, false friends) to the *existential* (mortality, mass death, time itself) — the album's moral crisis, not merely its low point. It's baptism inverted: submersion *reveals* but doesn't cleanse, and redemption is deferred exactly one track — to here. That widening is *why* the closer's hope reads as chosen: *Shine* answers a question [[Precious Time]] has made as large as death.
- **[[Long Time Coming]]** — the album's opening pole (the mirror / the disguise "getting clearer"). *Shine* is the closing pole: reflection → radiance.
- **[[Good Day]]** — the *false* dawn (easy brightness) against which *Shine* is the *earned* dawn.
- **[[Loser]]** — *"sunshine in my mind"* is **bunker-light** (a survival glow *inside* the bunker), the contrast case: brightness-as-survival vs Shine's brightness-as-emergence. Also shares the decision-not-denial / shame-preemption family.
- **[[Hell Of A Night]] + [[Hell of a Night Pt. 2 (The Hallelujah Stickup)]]** — the outlaw-velocity night this dawn *follows*; *Shine* answers the flood by rising, not outrunning.
- **[[Life Is Good]]** — chosen light in spite of the fire; *Shine* is logged as its 2018 antecedent (same move).
- **[[Down to You]]** *(33, 2022)* — a third "shine"-verb song, but sited differently: *"shine your light… like the flame at the top of your head."* Here the light lives at the **crown** — a flame the listener already carries — a distinct register from Shine's outward radiance.
- **[[Weirdness]] · [[Ballerina Boy]] · [[MAGIK]]** *(Lucius Lullaby, 2026)* — where the "shine" vocabulary *returns*, redirected into blessing. See the Advanced reading below.
- **[[Moths to the Flame]]** *(Lucius Lullaby, 2026)* — a dark-feminine inversion of the "born to shine" blessing (a closing, descending female *"MERCY"*); the structural inverse pole.
- **[[SOL]]** *(Lucius Lullaby, 2026)* — the possible **solar bookend**: the debut ends on a rising sun *declared outward*; the 2026 album ends on SOL (*sol* = sun; the Song of Love), the sun *withheld to wordless breath.* Held loosely (see below).
- **[[Dream - Dream]]** *(track 2)* — shares the participatory *"dream out loud"* / sing-it-back gesture (*"dream that dream with me"*).

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the "shine" lineage: claim → blessing (direction matters)
> *Shine*'s verb is **first-person and communal** — the self *claims* the light and invites the crowd to claim it too (*"sing it if you know you're gonna shine"*). Years later the same word comes back **inverted into a second-person blessing**, conferred by a divine-feminine, [[Mary]]-class voice: *"you were born to shine"* ([[Weirdness]]), *"let your light shine"* ([[Ballerina Boy]]), the witnessing benediction of [[MAGIK]]. **The direction is the whole point:** claim (*I / we*) → blessing (*you*); self → other. And it's audible at the word level: *Shine* doubles the verb in the refrain (*shine, shine*) and triples it in the hook (*shine, shine, shine*) — that repetitive shine-register is exactly what the later *"let your light shine"* language echoes. *Shine* is the debut anchor for that lineage — the place the blessing-vocabulary is first spoken, before anyone thinks to bless with it.
>
> **The guard that keeps this honest:** *Shine* is the **seed**, not a Mary appearance. Do not add it to the blessing-voice tracker, and don't read the divine-feminine voice back into 2018 — she isn't here yet. The song *plants* the word; the later voice *adopts and inverts* it. Keeping those two facts distinct is what stops the lineage from becoming a fantasy.
>
> **And the brake on the brake:** "shine" is among pop's most ubiquitous inspirational verbs, so a lineage built on *"let your light shine"* / *"born to shine"* risks treating gospel/inspirational boilerplate as a deliberate motif. Held at a thread-strength reading, not a proven design. The seed-not-appearance guard is exactly the right restraint.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the flood's double meaning (the album's title contains its cure)
> *"We are all subject to flooding"* reads two ways at once, and *Shine* touches both. One valence is **drowning** — *"you're in the sea so you're gonna drown,"* the threat the whole album has been outrunning. The other is **the walls coming down**: flood as the collapse of the ego-boundaries of perceived separation, the Jericho register where the barriers between us fall. *Shine*'s *"we're all the same in every nation"* and its *"sing it if you know you're gonna shine"* sit squarely on the **walls-down / communion** side of that pair. The same water that drowns the self also dissolves what separates it from everyone else — the album's title, in other words, contains its own cure.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the solar bookend, and why it's held loosely
> *Subject to Flooding* ends on a **rising sun** (*Shine*, declared and sung outward, 2018); *Lucius Lullaby* ends on **[[SOL]]** (*sol* = sun, the Song of Love, withheld to wordless breath, 2026). Declared radiance → interior/withheld radiance, eight years apart. It's an elegant rhyme — but *elegant* is the right word, not *architectural.* Lots of albums close bright; "architectural" implies design where shared aesthetic is at least as likely. Held as a resonance, not a blueprint.
>
> A found-image resonance in the same key: Roy captioned Vladimir Kush's painting *Sunrise by the Ocean* (October 2021) with a single word — **"Egg."** The painting is an egg cracking open over water, and what's born between the two halves is the sun rising over the ocean: **drown → crack → dawn**, the identical directional move the debut runs to *Shine*, compressed into one found image (the vessel that must crack to hatch). *Shine* sits in that image's resonance set. Suggestive, not load-bearing.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the sing-back device and the communion it prefigures
> *"So sing it if you know you're gonna shine"* is an **explicit sing-back invitation** — the crowd handed the line to sing back. It puts *Shine* in the catalogue's crowd-sings-it-back family, alongside [[Long Time Coming]], the *I→we* bridge of [[Loser]], and the *"dream out loud"* of [[Dream - Dream]]. That participatory gesture is the raw material the later work formalizes as **communion** (the many singing *as one*) — the thing [[Communion vs Spectacle|the Circle]] eventually codifies against mere spectacle (the many *watched*). In 2018 it's still instinct: sing it back, and the light stops being a thing the singer *has* and becomes a thing the room *shares.*

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the framework spine (albedo, radiance-pole, present-tense return)
> Read through the esoteric traditions Roy demonstrably works in, *Shine* sits at a specific station. In the alchemical sequence it reads as **albedo** — the *whitening*, the dawn/purification stage that follows the *nigredo* dissolution the album stages earlier (especially in [[Long Time Coming]]). Its dominant pole is **solar**; its recursion state is **return** (the refrain circles back); its narrative voice is Roy; its temporal register is **present tense** — the dawn is happening *now*, not remembered or promised. In [[Source vs Reflection]] terms, *Shine* is the **radiance pole's first full articulation** in the catalogue: the earlier tracks study reflected light, and here the self tries to become the source. A further framework overlay reads the whole album as a *descent of light down the Tree of Life* — opening in a source register ([[Long Time Coming]]) and landing, on *Shine*, in embodied radiance on the earth-plane (Malkuth). **All of this is framework correspondence — a lens the material rewards — not a claim about what Roy set out to encode.** His stated frame (a happy chorus *"like the sun rising"*) stays the grounded floor; the albedo / radiance / Tree-of-Life apparatus is layered *on* a sincere chosen-light closer, never underneath his intent. The explicit falsifier: if *Shine* is exactly the chosen-light sunrise anthem Roy describes, this apparatus is overlay on a sincere uplift closer — and that's fine. Honor the stated frame as the floor; tier the cosmology.

## Source notes

- **Grounded (Roy's own words, released music, public posts).** The song is track 13 of *Subject to Flooding*, the closer (2:56, Spotify ID `7ECdgylUCaSTNwxzY1Size`); first released September 21, 2018, with Atlantic/streaming redistribution metadata reading 2019 (recording owner ℗-line: **Atlantic Recording Corporation / WEA International Inc.**), and re-issued as **"Shine (Live In Studio)"** on January 17, 2020. Roy's writing account — sparked watching *Troy*, a chorus meant to feel *"happy, like the sun rising"* — and his thesis, *"even in dark times, there is always light if you want to find it,"* both from the April 2020 *American Songwriter* piece tied to that quarantine single (**the primary Shine paratext**). The mirror-doctrine statements (*"I just want to be a mirror"* / *"a song is a mirror"*) come from that same interview. A second 2020 quote (Honey Pop quarantine Q&A) makes the same case in the clear: *"Be a light in your own world. Everyone can be a giant sun and shine."* And a 2026 echo — Roy's public post *"The waters are rising but so am I"* (April 13, 2026) restates Shine's chorus verb (*"when I rise"*), evidence the Lucius-era Roy still answers the flood the *Shine* way: rise, don't outrun. (Sibling posts the same season: *"I am the shore,"* a Jericho repost.)
- **The lyric, and a provider error worth flagging.** The line is **"gender of my generation"** — the correct word, confirmed by Spotify's lyrics view and by ear. Apple Music and AZLyrics **mis-transcribe** the single word *"gender"* as a celebrity surname; an outside analysis once mistook that error-string for the lyric and read a "buried pun" that does not exist. Flag the wrong *word*; never quote the misread. On meaning: *"we're all the same in every nation / gender of my generation"* reads *gender* as **one more box the song sees past** — the anti-flattening move — not a statement *about* gender. (Other lines corrected against Spotify in the vault's ingest: *"we can start today"* not "could," *"it's like an earthquake"* not "feels like," *"upper hand,"* *"the sun is coming out singing."*)
- **Interpretation (strong).** Dawn-after-the-flood as the closer's function; joy-as-chosen-not-blind; agency-over-circumstance; reflection→radiance; the earned dawn vs Good Day's false one; the flood's drown/walls-down double valence; the shine → blessing lineage (held at thread strength, with the seed-not-appearance guard). Grounded in the material, but interpretive — labeled as such.
- **Framework (esoteric, syncretic — never authorial intent).** The albedo / radiance-pole placement; the [[Source vs Reflection]] apparatus; the Tree-of-Life "descent of light" reading (Malkuth on *Shine*); the solar-bookend rhyme with [[SOL]]; the Kush *Sunrise by the Ocean* / "Egg" resonance. The system *permits* these; it does not assert Roy intends them.
- **Open questions.** **Writer/producer credits are the biggest gap.** An early ingest lists writers as Matthew Pauling and Roy English, but this was never re-verified against Spotify's credits, and — alone among its album neighbors — *Shine* has **no confirmed credit entry** in the vault; production/mix/master for this track specifically are undocumented and should not be assumed from neighboring tracks. Confirm against the Spotify master. Also open: whether the 2020 "Live In Studio" single differs in arrangement from the album cut (unconfirmed).
- **Not a claim.** Joni Mitchell has her own *"Shine"* (2007 title track, *"let your little light shine,"* an outward benediction). Logged as a **title coincidence only** — no lineage built on it. (Likewise the album's [[Superhuman]] interlude, which paraphrases Chaplin's *The Great Dictator* on the "real self" shining through — adjacent solar/light vocabulary, a separate lineage, not a *Shine* connection.)
- **A known draft error, flagged so it doesn't spread.** A stray drafting note once stated that *"born to shine functions in Shine as the initial blessing-language"* and that the album was *"structured as a Tree of Life descent."* Both are wrong as stated: *"born to shine"* belongs to [[Weirdness]], **not** *Shine*; and the Tree-of-Life descent is an interpretive overlay (tiered above as framework), not something Roy structured. Corrected here on the record.
