# Imagine A World

> **One-line reading.** An invitation to imagine — framed as *authorship*, not escape — that runs through wonder, false gods, and media conditioning, and lands on Eden relocated inside: look, believe, conceive it. Not John Lennon's "Imagine." The extra word is doing work.

> [!info] Album & credits
> **Album:** *33* (2022, Big Loud Rock) — **track 9 of 10** · 4:44
> **Written & produced by** Roy English & Matt Pauling
> **Full lyrics:** [Genius](https://genius.com) · streaming

The album's capstone before the closer. *33* spends nine tracks diagnosing a world of attention-machines, false gods, and programmed thought — Online, Happy Face, and I Like to Party build that machine song by song — and here, one song from the end, it stops diagnosing and makes an offer: imagine your world, and while you're at it, notice you're the one writing it. It's the record's residual human appeal — the human pole answering the machine the rest of the album assembles. Everything the record has been circling — the designed God, the media conditioning, the self as author, the garden you can't find on a map — gets gathered up and handed forward to *The Circle*. It is the record at its most sincere and its most quietly booby-trapped, because one of the gods in its roll-call is a fraud, and the song knows it.

## At a glance

- **Era / release** — *33* (2022, Big Loud Rock), track 9 of 10, the penultimate track. Runtime 4:44.
- **In one line** — imagination as authorship of your own world, running wonder → false gods → media conditioning → the Eden within.
- **Who speaks** — Roy, as the inviting voice, addressing **the listener** the whole way through — *you* ×35, *I* ×0. The most other-directed song on the album. (Sir Lucius is *in* it, disguised — see OZ.)
- **Key symbols** — the Garden of Eden (relocated inward) · [[The Mirror|the Mirror]] (parents as reflections) · [[The Veil|OZ / the man behind the curtain]] · "Those Thoughts" (the conditioning force).

## The song, mapped

The lyric file is a continuous transcript — no *[Verse]* / *[Chorus]* headers, no chorus that repeats the same way twice. So the cleanest way to read it is as an **argument that keeps moving**, one persuasive step at a time. It plants an idea early ("a God you designed"), then cashes it out late (OZ). Following its own moves:

| # | The move | In the song | What it does |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | **The frame** | *"Imagine a world full of wonder"* → *"it's all inside of your mind, it's up to you to decide"* | States the thesis and *immediately relocates it inward.* Imagination isn't a window you look out of — it's the room you're standing in. |
| **2** | **Authorship declared** | *"you can't get it wrong / playing your note in a song / written by you for your life"* | Reframes imagining as a **low-stakes generative practice** — writing, not testing. You can't fail at it. |
| **3** | **The designed-divinity question** | *"Is there intelligent life? / Is there a God you designed?"* | Plants the seed the song detonates later: a god can be *authored.* |
| **4** | **Money as neutral, consequential** | *"money was magic / but it could make your life tragic / depending on how you acted / you were the cause and reaction"* | Worldview-as-lens applied to the most charged object going. Money isn't good or evil; your *frame* decides which. |
| **5** | **Passive vs. heroic** | *"do nothing… yell at people… get in fights… who do you think you are?"* set against *"be a hero… if you got out your own way / and went your own way"* | Lines up the deadbeat-and-hater menu, then pivots to agency. Imagination as the antidote to passivity. |
| **6** | **Choose your parents** | *"you pick your parents when you start / they're perfect for your lesson / the struggle and the blessing / reflections of who you are"* — then *"(It's not a phase mom!)"* | Converts circumstance into curriculum, and names parents as *reflections* (the Mirror, folded into the family). Then punctures its own solemnity with a teenage eyeroll. |
| **7** | **The conditioning sequence** | *"everything is brand new / then people start to label you / and you turn on the cable news / and Those Thoughts take ahold of you"* | The narrowing. Wonder is primary; labels and broadcast are the *secondary* squeeze. This is the album's attention-economy critique walking back onstage. |
| **8** | **The god-list, with a fraud in it** | *"where every religion was right / blue-headed gods / Brahma, Muhammad, and OZ / Horus, the Buddha, and Christ"* | Perennialism — *every* religion right — with the humbug slipped in deadpan beside real deities. The seed from move 3 blooms. |
| **9** | **The landing: Eden within** | *"The Garden Of Eden (In-A-Gadda-Da-Vida) / look inside you'll see it / you just gotta believe it / you just gotta Conceive it"* | Eden relocated from lost history to interior consciousness. **Believe → conceive:** imagination as creation, even birth. Hands off to *The Circle.* |

**The grammar is the argument.** Across all nine moves the song says *you* thirty-five times and *I* not once — a pure, other-directed second person. The album faces an audience and hands it the pen. (That "you-engine" is a *33* signature; by *Lucius Lullaby* the second person will quietly disappear.)

## What it means

A few claims that cut across the whole thing:

- **Imagination is authorship, not escape.** The whole invitation is welded to action, identity, and consequence — and, explicitly, to *writing*: *"playing your note in a song, written by you for your life."* "You can't get it wrong" makes it a practice, not an exam. This is the same self-as-world-author that [[Another Way II Heaven]] stages as cosmology — an "I" that creates by *speaking* — except here it's made participatory. That song is the myth; this one hands you the mic.
- **Worldview is a chosen lens.** The identical world shows up as wonder, tragedy, opportunity, or prison depending on how you frame it — *"depending on how you acted, you were the cause and reaction."* Imagination isn't a destination you travel to; it's the lens experience arrives through. Interpretation comes first, and reality follows it out.
- **Labels and media narrow the original wonder.** The conditioning sequence (move 7) is precise about the order of operations: wonder is what you're *born* into, and then categorization plus broadcast close in on it. The capitalized **"Those Thoughts"** taking hold is *33*'s programming critique personified — conditioning as the thief of possibility, the same force the earlier tracks build the attention-machine out of.
- **OZ is the tell.** This is the interpretive keystone. *"Every religion was right"* reads as generous perennialism — until you clock who's standing in the line-up. Beside Brahma, Muhammad, Horus, the Buddha, and Christ sits **OZ**: the humbug, the man behind the curtain, a projected "god" who's really a guy working levers. Put that next to *"is there a God you designed?"* and the wink lands — in a world you imagine, even the *fraud*-god is "right." Which is liberating and a warning in the same breath. It's the whole album's suspicion of the manufactured deity, smuggled into a line that sounds like pure inclusivity. *(Leap flagged: OZ could just be whimsy. But on an album this preoccupied with curtains and disguises, the fraud-god reading earns its keep.)*
- **Eden comes home.** The landing relocates paradise from a lost *place* to an interior *state*, reached by belief and then conception: *"look inside you'll see it, you just gotta believe it, you just gotta Conceive it."* It's the kingdom-within in its garden form — and the parenthetical does the joke for you. *"In-A-Gadda-Da-Vida"* is Iron Butterfly's famous slur of "In the Garden of Eden": the external Eden survives in the culture only as a mumbled 1968 rock echo. The garbled version is the point. The real one was never out there to mispronounce.

**And the honest counter-read:** none of this requires the machinery. *Imagine A World* works flat-out as a sincere indie-rock empowerment anthem — imagine, create, don't blame, be a hero — with the curtain-god and Eden-within architecture layered *on top* of an accessible, verified lyric, not smuggled underneath it. It reads as sincere-with-a-warning, not as an ironic inversion of the utopian "Imagine" register. OZ may be pure play. The deeper structure is real, but it's a floor you can decline to look at and still love the song.

## Characters

- [[Roy]] — the inviting voice; the self framed as the author of its own world.
- [[Sir Lucius]] — *present but in disguise.* He's the OZ in the roster and the "God you designed" — the curtain-god that the album's attention-force (*"televise is just my disguise"*) prototypes. The fraud-deity smuggled into the line of real ones is a very Lucius move: technically included, technically "right," entirely a projection.
- **The listener ("you")** — addressed throughout as co-creator. Not a bystander but the grammatical center of the song (*you* ×35), invited to complete the imagining. In the album's crowd-and-participation device, this is the *invitation* form: *just imagine* — and you're the one who finishes it.

## Symbols

- **The Garden of Eden** — relocated from lost history to interior consciousness; paradise as a state you conceive, not a place you return to.
- [[The Mirror|The Mirror]] — parents named as *"reflections of who you are,"* folding the catalogue's mirror motif into the family itself.
- [[The Veil|OZ / the man behind the curtain]] — the fabricated god; the disguise stretched over the machinery. The "designed God" made concrete, and the closest thing the song has to a villain.
- **"Those Thoughts"** *(capitalized in the lyric)* — the conditioning/programming force: cable news plus labeling, personified as an intruding agent that "takes ahold of you." The attention-economy critique given a proper noun.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — OZ, the "designed God," and the perennialist wink *(tap to open)*
> The move that makes OZ work is the perennialist frame it hides inside. *"Every religion was right"* is a real theological position (the perennialist / perennial-philosophy claim that the great traditions point at one truth through different symbols) — and it's stated warmly, without irony, in a line of genuine deities. That warmth is the camouflage. Drop **OZ** into the middle of Brahma, Muhammad, Horus, the Buddha, and Christ and you've inserted a *known fabrication* — L. Frank Baum's humbug, "the man behind the curtain," a projected god who is only a man working a machine — into a roll-call of the sincerely worshipped.
>
> The song has already loaded the gun: *"is there a God you designed?"* (move 3). OZ is that question's answer wearing a costume. And the wink points straight at the album's own antagonist — the attention-force of [[Pay Attention (feat. little luna)|Pay Attention]] whose *"televise is just my disguise,"* the curtain-god of the broadcast age. So the perennialist generosity carries a second meaning: **in a world you imagine, even the fraud-god is "right"** — which is exactly why you'd better notice you're the one designing him. The Deep-Dive discipline keeps this labeled as a strong reading, not stated intent: OZ could be pure whimsy, and the curtain-god tie is the reader's frame. But it's album-consistent to the point of being difficult to un-see.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the board's final rung: "10. The World," and why the *title* is a code
> The Eden-within reading isn't only textual; it's anchored on one of Roy's own public artifacts. His *"I'm 33 this year"* board ([[Public Posts]]) carries a handwritten 0–10 emanation list — glosses on the [[Kabbalah - The Tree of Life|Tree-of-Life]] spheres — running *"0. Nothing IS, Nothing Becomes, Nothing is Not / 1. I AM…"* and **terminating at "10. The World."** In the board's cosmology, the unlock code for that emanation-ladder resolves to a song title — *and the title it resolves to is this one.* So track 9 isn't merely the capstone-before-the-closer: **its title names stage 10 of Roy's own ascent, "The World."**
>
> That reframes the whole song as the *floor* of a descent-and-return. Roy glosses his cross / "+" tattoo as representing Christ *"and the 4 worlds"* — the four Kabbalistic worlds (Atziluth / Beriah / Yetzirah / Assiah) — and this song sits on the bottom one, Assiah, the material world, "The World." The further identification of that rung with *Malkhut* (the lowest, most material sphere, the "kingdom") stays a **correspondence**, not a claim — Roy's hand writes "The World," not the Hebrew — but the lyric makes the move legible without needing the vocabulary: the kingdom is real, and *"it's all inside of your mind."* The biblical rhyme underneath is the kingdom-within of Luke 17:21 in its garden form: not *lo here, lo there,* but *look inside.*

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the double HoM code, and the song as web3 artifact *(for the completists)*
> The song lives in the [[Hall of Mirrors]] web3 archive in **two** distinct ways, which is unusual:
> 1. **Its own mint.** *"IMAGINE A WORLD"* is a minted artifact (catalog #140, the "MINT BURIED" realm), and the unlock code for its room is simply the title. Minted by CTHDRL.ETH on 02-10-23 — one of four *33* song-mints dropped together (alongside *Down to You*, *Another Way II Heaven*, and *The Circle*).
> 2. **The emanation-ladder's answer.** A *separate* artifact — the "Tree-of-Life ladder" image (an APPEARANCE-realm piece) — has the unlock code *"Know how to spell the name of the song,"* which resolves to *Imagine A World.*
>
> So the title is **both its own room-code and the answer to the ascent-ladder's riddle** — the same three words close the album's argument, unlock the song's own vault, and complete the "10. The World" rung of Roy's board. It's rare to see a single phrase carry that much load across the lyric, the artwork, and the paratext at once.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the "own way" split (2018 → 2022) *(a cross-catalogue lead)*
> One line rewards a catalogue-wide ear. In *Dream / Dream* (Subject to Flooding, 2018), the "own way" is a single collective move: *"doing everything our own way"* — us, together, in rebellion. Here in 2022 it becomes **two distinct moves**, confirmed verbatim in the lyric: *"if you got out your own way / and went your own way?"*
>
> The 2022 version splits the ego-as-obstacle (*get out of* your own way — stop blocking yourself) from the path-as-autonomy (*go* your own way — choose your own road). That's the "get out of the way" doctrine — the one Roy repeats across the project as the whole move toward integration — folded quietly into a lyric. The 2018→2022 evolution is the reading; both lyrics are grounded. (Fleetwood Mac's *"Go Your Own Way"* hums underneath as cultural substrate, not a claimed source.) Vetted as one of the two strongest of the smaller *33* leads.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the resonance family (Neville Goddard, New Thought, the garbled sacred)
> The song's central idea — that imagination *authors* reality — sits in a lineage Roy has publicly pointed toward, which raises it from framework-attribution toward something closer to stated influence:
> - **Neville Goddard, named by Roy.** In his IG diary (~2021-01): *"listened to a lecture by Neville Goddard… What we tell ourselves really does become the reality we inhabit,"* paired with his recurring *"Imagination is the language of God."* Neville's whole system is imagination-as-creation — believe a state real and it materializes — which is *"you just gotta believe it, you just gotta Conceive it"* almost to the letter.
> - **Public shares corroborate.** In his community's public Discord he posted Neville content — a *"MIND to MATTER (Adam/Atom + Eve/Electron)"* lecture link (2021-12-20) and *"Neville Goddard – The Art of Dying"* (Halloween 2023). And a 2021 poem of his runs *"You're the architect / Imagination comes / In many forms… So keep on thinking up,"* landing on *"Then you'll know WHO designed it"* — a near-sibling of both this song's authorship frame and its *"is there a God you designed?"*
> - **The wider substrate:** imagination-as-creation in the Romantic / Hermetic / New Thought line; child-wonder narrowed by naming; Eden-as-inner-state over Eden-as-geography; and the *"In-A-Gadda-Da-Vida"* tag as the sacred surviving only as a degraded pop echo — the tell that the real garden was always inside. These last are framework correspondences, not stated intent.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — "choose your parents," and how literal any of it is *(open questions)*
> The song leaves several dials deliberately un-set, and it's more honest to name them than to resolve them:
> - **Is imagination here psychology, spirituality, or creativity?** Probably all three, held in one word without choosing.
> - **"You pick your parents"** — a perspective exercise for reframing your family as your curriculum, or a light brush against reincarnation / soul-contract ideas? The comic *"(It's not a phase mom!)"* is doing real work here: it *deflates the metaphysics on purpose,* keeping the move as a reframing device rather than a doctrine you're asked to sign. Read it as the former; note it could reach toward the latter.
> - **How literal is "Conceive it"?** Imagination as literal creation (the Neville reading), or as motivation dressed in cosmic language? The capital C leans toward the former; the anthem's accessibility keeps the latter available.
> - **Does Roy invert Lennon's "Imagine," or sustain it?** The canon answer: sustained, not inverted — sincere-with-a-warning rather than an ironic teardown of the utopian register. The distinctness from Lennon's *"Imagine"* is a deliberate authorial decision (the extra word, the different argument), not an accident of titling.
