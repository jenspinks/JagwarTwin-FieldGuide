# City of Angels

> **One-line reading.** The Circus's gospel of fame — a survival-chant becomes a promise of upload, and the reward for "making it" is not resurrection but reproducibility: everyone looks just like you because the particular self the dream promised has already been deleted to grant it.

> [!info] Album & credits
> **Album:** *Lucius Lullaby* (2026, Big Loud Rock) — billed *Jagwar Twin & Sir Lucius* · **track 10 of 15** · 2:55
> **Written by** Roy English & Matthew Pauling · **Produced by** Roy English & Matthew Pauling · **Mastered by** Dale Becker
> **Vocals:** *sir lucius* + *ROY* — the shadow is co-credited as a vocalist on the fame song
> **Lyrics:** [Genius](https://genius.com) · streaming

## At a glance

- **Era / release** — *Lucius Lullaby* (2026), the album that hands [[Sir Lucius]] the microphone. Track 10 of 15, deep in the long nigredo stretch — the descent, not the turn (the album's turn is track 13, [[Weirdness]]).
- **In one line** — a walk through Hollywood where a beautiful stranger sells you your own dreams, and the walker ends the song selling them to the next person.
- **Who speaks** — mostly the **City** (*"and she said…"*); the singer (**[[Roy]]**) originates almost nothing; **[[Sir Lucius]]** is co-credited as a voice on it, and it is *his* City — the City of *Light*.
- **Key symbols** — [[The Star-Lined Road|the star-lined road]] · the bright lights ([[Fire]]) · [[The Mirror|the mirror]] (*"everyone looks just like you"*) · the counterfeit crown.

This is the temple that [[Playing to the Gods]] is the seed of. Tracks 9 and 10 are one movement — they share the same *never gonna die* chant, and Jen first heard them live at the CD-release party as a single unbroken song; the album split them into two tracks after the fact. (It's the same seam-device the record uses for tracks 1→2 and 6→7.) So read them together: **Playing to the Gods** is the overture-mantra, **City of Angels** is the full song that swallows it whole.

## The song, mapped

City of Angels runs on one swapped pronoun, and the whole reading lives in watching it rotate. The stranger's pitch arrives in the **second person** (*your* dreams), gets **internalized** to the first (*my* dreams) after the bridge, then rotates **back to the second** in the outro — retagged "and she said." Follow that thread and the song tells you exactly what it's doing.

| # | The beat | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | *"In the City of Angels walking down the star-lined road / Another beautiful stranger passed me by, I said hello"* | The descent. He walks *down* the star-lined road, and offers the one agentive thing he'll say all song — a **hello**. |
| **2** | *(she said)* *"All **your** dreams are coming true / Everyone looks just like you"* | The **pitch**, in the second person. He gets a prophecy back, not a person. |
| **3** | *"In the City of Angels / Welcome to Hollywood"* | The chorus/tag. *"Welcome to Hollywood"* rings the *Hotel California* bell on purpose. |
| **4** | *"Some people wanna be saviours, gangsters / Live forever in the bright lights, godlike, live forever"* → *"Never gonna die… If I make it out alive, then I'm never gonna die"* | The bridge names **the two masks** (saviour / gangster) and then pulls in the survival-chant carried over from track 9. |
| **5** | *"All **my** dreams are coming true / Everyone looks just like you"* | **The pivot.** The pitch is now *his own speech.* He has swallowed the stranger. |
| **6** | *"In the City of Angels / Welcome to Hollywood / … / It's the City of **Light** / Welcome to Hollywood / And she said"* | *"City of Light"* (singular) folds LA into Lucius's domain — luc-, light, the light-bearer. *"And she said"* cues the re-transmission. |
| **7** | the *"never gonna die"* quatrain, repeated | The survival-loop foregrounded — the chant chewing on itself. |
| **8** | *(outro)* *"All **your** dreams are coming true / Everyone looks just like you / In the City of Angels"* | Rotated **back to the second person.** The song ends mid-rotation, parked in the recruiter's slot. No resolution. No waking. |

**The load-bearing move:** pitched (*your*) → internalized (*my*) → re-transmitted (*your* + "and she said"). The grammar performs the conversion. He doesn't escape the beautiful stranger; he *becomes* one, and the song leaves him there — recruiting.

And notice how little of this is actually him. Across both tracks the singer originates exactly **two** utterances — *"I said hello"* and the survival-chant. Everything else is the city speaking or the chant repeating. (The pronoun tally bears it out: *you* 10, *I* 9 — and in this album the residual *you* is the audience, not a beloved.) His single human word, a greeting, gets converted into advertisement. He says hi to a stranger and gets a sales pitch in return.

## What it means

**He becomes the thing that recruited him.** This is why City of Angels never wakes up the way its sister song does. [[Lucius Lullaby]] — the title track — can turn and confess *"it's all just a lie,"* because there's still a self standing outside the spell to say so. City of Angels ends with *no one left outside it.* The voice returns not to its source — the [[The Loop and the Circle|Circle]] — but to the recruiter's position, still pitching. The Loop, encoded in pronouns.

***Everyone looks just like you*** **is the song's deepest crime, and it is not vanity.** A circle needs distinct points held in communion — many separate voices singing one song back. Sameness deletes the points, and a circle of no distinct points collapses into a single point repeating: the Loop. That line *is* that operation. It's the forgery of communion — it says *we are all one* and means *you are all replaceable.* In a project literally named Jagwar *Twin* — built on the productive two, the mirror that *wakes* — this is twinning gone metastatic: infinite doubling with no other left to meet. Set it against the cosmology Roy maps elsewhere (*one becomes two… one day we'll get back together and see the great design*) and Hollywood is the **counterfeit return**: not unity-with-difference, just uniformity. See [[Source vs Reflection]].

**"Never gonna die" is a false immortality, and the catalogue tells us whose.** The deathless thing isn't the man — it's the *lullaby,* the replicated content. Lucius says it himself in the theatrical premiere series: *"I can't be stopped… my lullaby is everywhere as we speak."* ([[The Interview]], EP6) So *never gonna die* is the listener's side of the shadow's *I can't be stopped* — his ubiquity handed to the singer as if it were a gift. And the sentence didn't stay on screen: at the album's March-2026 release Roy ran a real **STOP SIR LUCIUS** campaign in public — flyers around Los Angeles and a **freeway overpass banner**, the account captioned *"We must stop him"* — and Lucius answered it, in a red coat, with *"It can't be stopped. 'lucius lullaby' is out everywhere."* ([[Public Posts]]) The physical banner is the fame-song's chorus made literal: the crowd tries to stop him, the shadow shrugs, *the lullaby is everywhere* — the accusatory-crowd/scapegoat layer this song shares with [[The Watchers]], staged on an actual overpass. And one of Roy's own artifacts names the mechanism flat-out: a board tab titled **"how to upload myself to the internet."** Upload = never-gonna-die = becoming reproducible (see [[The Upload]]). Which is precisely what the phoenix is *not.* The phoenix burns, dies, and is reborn *through* the death; *never gonna die* skips the burning and just preserves the same self, multiplied. **One is resurrection; the other is taxidermy.**

**The stranger is the true feminine, photographed and sold back.** [[Mary]] is present only by inversion. She is never seen directly — faceless, the seeing-without-reflection, the ground you look *from.* The "beautiful stranger" is her exact counterfeit: *faced,* visible, and generic ("*another* beautiful stranger"), an object you look *at.* The axis is visibility. The true feminine is invisible because she is source; the false one is hyper-visible because she is a reflection *for sale.* The true one remembers you to yourself; the false one promises your dreams and tells you everyone's the same. (Compare [[#1 Champion (la Conquistadora)|#1 Champion]]'s Mary, who "fawns over no man" — the stranger does nothing *but* fawn.)

The simplest reading survives all of this, and it's worth saying plainly: on its face this is a well-worn cautionary-Hollywood song — the city that promises stardom and hollows you out, where everyone's fake and interchangeable. That genre is old (the page flags *Hotel California* for a reason). The pronoun-Loop, the Circle→Loop collapse, the metastatic twin, the feminine-counterfeit — those are vault frameworks laid on top of a familiar LA-disillusionment track, and the plain reading is valid on its own. The apparatus is interpretation, layered.

## Characters

- **[[Sir Lucius]]** — the gospel's voice; the counterfeit-communion seller, and *co-credited as a vocalist on the track* (the shadow signs the fame song). The City of *Light* is his domain by etymology. In the visualizer he's in white joker face-paint under a spiked headpiece — one covered in **brand logos**, a crown made of ad copy.
- **[[Roy]]** — the nearly-mute self walking the road; a hello and a chant are his entire vocabulary here. (In the credits, the lead vocal is the character *"ROY"*; the author *"Roy English"* is credited only on background — a nice, quiet split.) This is the dark twin of the project's mirror doctrine, where Roy gets out of the way so others can see themselves. Here is a man so deep in the machine he has no voice left to get out of the way *with.* See [[The Voice]].
- **[[Mary]]** — present by inversion only (see above). Not named in the song; the true feminine the stranger negates.
- **The beautiful stranger** — the faced, visible, generic figure who delivers the pitch (*"and she said"*). The gatekeeper of the lower realm; the false Ariadne who hands you a reason to *stay* instead of the way *out.*
- **Saviours & gangsters** — not people but the two fame-roles the City offers: the light-mask (redeemer) and the shadow-mask (outlaw who dies young into legend). Both buy immortality with death-into-legend, and the machine doesn't care which one you pick. It's the City offering Roy his own Roy/Lucius split back to him *as merchandise* — be the light one or the dark one, godlike either way, just stay in the lights. This is the *bargain* the theatrical series stages plainly: in the early [[The Interview|"Interview"]] episodes Lucius trades Roy worldly success (the jet, the label's blessing, taking care of everyone he loves) for one algorithmic hit — *"Just give me one, Roy. One hit."* (EP2) City of Angels is what that trade sounds like once it's been accepted and set to a chorus.

## Symbols

- [[The Star-Lined Road|The star-lined road]] — the Walk of Fame; heaven pressed flat into pavement and trodden underfoot (an inverted Jacob's Ladder, run downward).
- [[Fire]] — the bright lights: the one Light (City of Light, *singular*) dispersed into many bright lights (plural), sparks multiplied but never gathered.
- [[The Mirror]] — *"everyone looks just like you,"* the reflection-for-sale; sameness as forged communion.
- **The City of Light** (singular) — folds LA into Lucius's domain; the counterfeit the plural "bright lights" scatters.
- **The counterfeit crown** — the visualizer's logo-covered headpiece: the crown you wear to *play the gods.*

> [!note]- Advanced reading — "playing to the gods": the gallery-gospel and its built-in bathos
> The companion title carries the catalogue's core inversion in a single idiom. In British theatre, **"the gods"** is old slang for the topmost, cheapest, furthest gallery — the nosebleeds. To *"play to the gods"* (or "play to the gallery") is to pitch broad for mass approval. So the joke is load-bearing: *the gods* are simultaneously deities you sacrifice to **and** the cheap seats — and to play to them is to grovel before the nosebleeds and call it worship. The highest place is the cheapest place. It's the performer-side of [[Pay Attention (feat. little luna)|Pay Attention]] (there, Attention is the hidden god you *pay*; here, you pay it by *playing* to it). The Circus has a gallery; Lucius runs it in a ringmaster's jacket.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the official visualizer (flatline, rabbit-bride, the wedding to the cameras)
> The official *"Playing to the Gods"* visualizer (Vevo) covers the seamed pair 9→10, so it's really the City of Angels visual too. Sir Lucius appears in white joker face-paint under a spiked headpiece **covered in brand-text and logos** — a crown of ad copy. Beside him stands a woman in a full **white rabbit mask** and black lace: faceless, still, passive throughout.
>
> The blocking is the pronoun rotation rendered as choreography. When the singing starts he **lets go of her hand** to step forward and bow/wave to the paparazzi cameras. He sings the **first verse toward her** and the **chorus to the camera** — private truth to her, public recruiter-pitch to the crowd. Her *only* movement in either song is a single step, and only when *he* reaches back and pulls her. At the end he turns and **leads her off** (screen right) — a seed of return, except he's still *leading,* and the pair never confesses the lie the way [[Lucius Lullaby]] does. The curtain, not the waking.
>
> The load-bearing beat: a **heart-monitor tone and flatline** enters under *"never gonna die"* and runs to the end of the song. The deathless chant is literally scored to a flatline. And the red-carpet rope-line is staged as a **wedding aisle** — he marries the cameras, and the bride is the accessory.
>
> Two cautions. The rabbit-woman's *identity* is genuinely open — the rabbit ties back to [[Good Time]]'s "rabbit vs rabbit = self vs self" and may be his benched anima, but do **not** equate an unidentified figure with Mary without an anchor. And the whole visualizer reading is built on grounded blocking but is itself interpretation.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the esoteric harmonics (inverted ladder, scattered sparks, the false Ariadne)
> Three framework resonances, held as resonance, not as claimed authorial intent:
>
> **Inverted Jacob's Ladder.** *"City of Angels / walking down the star-lined road"* runs the ladder-of-angels-among-stars backward three ways at once: he walks *down,* not up; the stars are the Walk of Fame — heaven pressed flat into pavement and trodden underfoot, *as above so below* reversed; and the angels have become "beautiful strangers," gatekeepers of the lower realm rather than messengers of the source.
>
> **Scattered sparks.** *"City of Light"* (singular — the one Light) dispersed into *"bright lights"* (plural) reads as the Lurianic shattering: sparks trapped in the husks of spectacle, multiplied but never gathered — the inverse of *tikkun* and the Great Design. This lyric is a public anchor for the [[Keilim - The Breaking of the Vessels|scattered-sparks]] idea, and it stands on its own words alone. See [[Keilim - The Breaking of the Vessels]].
>
> **The false Ariadne.** The stranger is the threshold-figure who *withholds the thread* — she hands you a reason to stay, not the way out. An inversion of [[The Thread|the thread]] through [[The Labyrinth|the labyrinth]].
>
> **Vocables / nonsense syllables.** The *la-la / na-na / ba-ba* textures read, in a tradition-correspondence sense, as vowel-spirit (angels) sung through consonant-structure (angles) — and "City of *Angels*" is one of the clearer public instances of the angels/angles pun. Roy has publicly used "angles" in his own voice about *33* (hoping the reimagined "angles" let everyone "find their own home"), which is a receipt for the *spaces* sense of the pun; the letter-play beyond that is tradition-correspondence, not a stated method.
>
> Two title-siblings sit in the same pattern, held lightly as resonance: [[tomorrow (ANGLE OF ETERNITY)]] fuses the pun into a title outright (*angle*/angel of eternity), and [[Another Way II Heaven]] is the co-clearest public instance — its *"To Heaven"* → *"II Heaven"* makes the angle-of-approach the whole argument (many angles on the one heaven). City of Angels is the shadow version: the angels flattened into "beautiful strangers," the angle bent downward.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the true/false immortality fork (the phoenix this counterfeits)
> The song sets up a fork the rest of Roy's cross-media work fills in. His cosmology drawing **"How to Live Forever"** supplies the *question* City of Angels answers in the negative: you live forever by *entering the pattern,* not by becoming endlessly copyable. And the media note on Vladimir Kush's **"Egg — Sunrise by the Ocean"** states the corrective — the shell *must* break; you live forever by *hatching,* not by *not-dying.* City of Angels is the false term both are set against. The phoenix dies **in order to** live forever; the City offers living forever **so you never have to** die. (Held lightly — whether Roy intends this fork is unconfirmed; the read is imported *from* City of Angels as much as it's read *onto* it.)

> [!note]- Advanced reading — what would make this wrong (the falsifiers, kept live)
> Anti-totalization, on the record:
> - The **pronoun-rotation keystone** may over-read ordinary address-shifting — *your → my → your* is how countless pop lyrics move. The Loop is *encodable* in the pronouns; it isn't *proven* by them.
> - **"Everyone looks just like you = counterfeit communion"** puts a lot of weight on one line, and the "Jagwar Twin" wordplay may be helping or may just be cute.
> - Co-written again (Roy + Pauling), which reopens the emergent-vs-encoded question: how much of the apparatus is *authored* vs *found.*
> - The clean **falsifier:** if Roy ever frames this as a straightforward fame-cautionary song, the whole apparatus demotes to overlay — real, but decorative. We keep that on the table.
> - Open, and worth holding: is a *true* immortality (the Great Design, the song that survives) hidden *under* the false one — or is the very *absence* of any waking-up the point, i.e. inside the City there is no vantage left to see the lie from? And *"make it out alive"* — out of *what*? (Industry, psyche, fame, the rock-bottom floor — all rooms in one building.)

On heaven, for the song's heaven-cluster: *"heaven is inside of you, inside the individual, but also external — and what's inside of you creates the external."*
- **Strong readings.** The pronoun rotation as the song's spine; "he becomes the beautiful stranger"; the Circle→Loop collapse; twinning-gone-metastatic; the true/false-feminine visibility axis; "never gonna die = reproducibility, not resurrection"; the near-mute self as dark twin of the mirror doctrine. Grounded in the material, but interpretive — labeled as such.
- **Framework (esoteric, syncretic — never claimed as authorial intent).** The inverted Jacob's Ladder; the Lurianic scattered-sparks reading; the false-Ariadne inversion; the true/false-immortality fork; the vocable angels/angles harmonics. The system *permits* these; it does not assert Roy intends them.
- **Intertext, not influence.** *Hotel California* (*"welcome to Hollywood"* / "you can never leave") and the general stairway-to-heaven ascent image are listener's intertexts, held at resonance. (Prince's *"I Would Die 4 U"* reads as a structural *inverse* of "never gonna die," but it is **not** a Jagwar Twin song and is carried only as a comparator.)
