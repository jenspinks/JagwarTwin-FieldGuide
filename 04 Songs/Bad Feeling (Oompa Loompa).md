# Bad Feeling (Oompa Loompa)

> **One-line reading.** The cheerful Wonka jingle, weaponized into a warning — a manufactured paradise insisting everything's perfect while the dread leaks through, and the body's alarm treated as more honest than the official story. Roy's own gloss: *"a chocolate covered reminder to trust your gut."*

> [!info] Album & credits
> **Album:** *Lucius Lullaby* (2026, Big Loud Rock / Mercury–UMG) — **track 5 of 15** · 2:13
> **Originally:** a single, December 15 2023 (Big Loud Rock) — timed to the *Wonka* film. An acoustic-live version, *"Live at the MOTH Gallery,"* closes the album as **track 15** (3:49) — the *BMRC bonus* cut (BMRC being the Circus's shadowy production-house-slash-weapons-brand, the industry-Babylon stamped on the era's releases and merch).
> **Written by** Roy English, Ryan Daly, Sean Van Vleet (a recurring era co-writer — also on [[Bounce]] and [[Great Time to Be Human]]) · **and** Leslie Bricusse & Anthony Newley (the 1971 *Willy Wonka* "Oompa-Loompa" songbook, interpolated)
> **Produced by** Roy English & Ryan Daly · Mix: Jeff Ellis · Master: Dale Becker
> **Drums:** Josh Dun (Twenty One Pilots)
> **Full lyrics:** [Genius](https://genius.com) · streaming
> **▶ Listen:** [Official video (YouTube)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aPxFKQfv-Co)

Here is a song that grew out of Roy digging through his own ancestry — German, Polish, Slavic roots, accordions and tubas, *"we need some oompa shit."* The order matters, and it runs the way you wouldn't guess: the **oom-pah came first.** The Wonka / Oompa-Loompa frame is *downstream* of that ancestral-music jam — the accordion-and-tuba idea arrived, and only then did it resolve into the chocolate factory. Wonka is where the oom-pah landed, not the seed it grew from. And somehow that arrived at a nursery sing-along about dread that pulled a million streams in nine days and landed on Top 40 radio. The candy is the bounce; the medicine is the warning. It's [[Great Time to Be Human]]'s smile-with-horror-underneath run the other way: not horror forced *through* the smile, but the smile that can't quite suppress the bad feeling.

## At a glance

- **Era / release** — Single, Dec 15 2023 (a *Wonka*-film tie-in); folded into [[Lucius Lullaby (album)|Lucius Lullaby]] (2026) as track 5, with an acoustic-live track 15. A whole 2024 rollout in between: gallery show, music video, live acoustic, remix, cemetery show.
- **In one line** — A candy-coated smoke alarm — and, in the same breath, a candy-factory product. The tell that warns you about the machine while running through it.
- **Who speaks** — [[Sir Lucius]] claims the track outright ("those are me"), so the warning issues from the shadow's own mouth. But the *gut* doing the warning is [[Roy]]'s. The song's one diagnostic line turns on the Crowd: *"bad feeling 'bout you."*
- **Key symbols** — [[Consumption]] · [[The Mask]] · [[Inversion]] · [[The Moon]] · candy/chocolate · [[The Circus]] · fire

## What it means

A handful of claims that cut across the whole thing.

**The cautionary chorus, moved earlier.** In *Willy Wonka & the Chocolate Factory* (1971), the Oompa-Loompas are the moralizing chorus that steps forward *after* each spoiled child is removed and sings the lesson — hindsight, delivered too late. Bad Feeling keeps the tune and the address and swaps a single phrase, and that swap does all the work: the cautionary chorus moves from *after* the collapse to *before* it. The question stops being "what did this child do wrong?" and becomes "why does this perfect place feel *wrong*?" The lesson-after-the-fact becomes a smoke alarm.

**"Everything's perfect here" is enforcement, not reassurance.** It's the audio of the [[Happy Face]] mask scaled up from a face to a whole environment — toxic positivity as *system speech.* The *(they say)* is the giveaway: perfection is the system's claim, quoted, doubted. And *"nobody's crying"* does quietly sinister work — in a manufactured paradise that isn't the observation *no one is hurt*; it's a **spec**, crying hidden or edited or forbidden. (The bare outro finally tells the truth — about the dread, not the paradise.)

**Intuition as a last honest instrument.** *"The truth is a lie that nobody can tell"* reads two ways at once: truth *inverted* into a lie, and truth become *unspeakable* — nobody can *tell* it, nobody can *tell the difference.* When ordinary truth-testing fails like that, the song proposes a fallback: the gut. The bad feeling becomes the one reading the candy hasn't coated — not paranoia but perception. This intuition-as-counter-epistemology is the heart of the song, and it's anchored by Roy's own gloss and his *"Trust your guts"* tattoo: the gut as the reliable instrument once the official story has gone false. Roy himself flagged the paradox line as the load-bearing center — he used it verbatim as a post headline, *"The truth is a lie that nobody can tell 🖤."*

**The crowd is appetite; the gaze turns on the listener.** Verse two renders the attention economy anatomically — *carnivores and cannibals… mandible, jaw split* — people consumed as content, scandal, outrage, on demand. It's [[Welcome to the Circus]]'s *"chew you up, spit you out"* turned into a buffet. And the one diagnostic line aims *outward*: bad feeling 'bout **you**, at the audience dancing along and ordering more. The crowd that sings is the crowd that gets eaten.

**The sugar is the joke, not just the trap.** For all the menace, the song is genuinely *funny*, and the comedy is load-bearing — deadpan is how the candy goes down. The chorus keeps swapping in throwaways (*"Oompa Loompa, I don't know, dude"*) — a register-collapse gag, the ominous cautionary tune shrugging mid-prophecy. The verse-two tongue-twister (*carnivores and cannibals… mandible, jaw split*) is clever-nonsense first, a manic mouthful that's fun to say — funny right up until you hear what it's actually saying (people eating people). Around the 2023–24 rollout Roy pitched the whole thing in an absurdist, half-delulu register — *"Just saw Wonka. iCried,"* wanting *"the Willy Wonka converse,"* dedicating the record to Timothée Chalamet (*"This one's for @RealChalamet"*) — the promo as bit. Even the real-world backdrop plays as farce: when a *"Willy Wonka Experience"* in Glasgow became a viral 2024 fiasco, Roy publicly waved it off — *"I swear I had nothing to do with this."* The sweetness is the delivery system for the dread and the punchline at once: you laugh, you sing along, and *that's* how it gets in.

**The alarm that is also the foreman — the deepest knot.** [[Sir Lucius]] claims the track in [[The Interview]] EP1: *"happy face, bad feeling, hundreds of millions of streams. Those are me. You need me."* So the warning comes from the shadow's own mouth — which doesn't *falsify* it so much as complete it. The release history proves the paradox: Bad Feeling dropped as a single **on the same day as the 2023 *Wonka* film** — a genuine "trust your gut" alarm that was itself a precisely-timed piece of nostalgia-bait content. The song exposes the candy factory *while being* a strategically-released candy-factory product. Threat-detector and factory-foreman in one — maybe the truest thing it knows about Lucius: the part of you warning you about the machine is also running it. (See the radio war, below, for the real-world enactment.)

## Characters

- **[[Sir Lucius]]** — the speaker and claimant; *"the mind divorced from the heart."* He names Bad Feeling in EP1 as one of his algorithm-friendly hits, so the alarm issues from the shadow himself. That complicates the warning rather than voiding it — he's the alarm *and* the foreman. (Theatrical claim, note — Lucius-the-character on-screen, not Roy stating real authorship.)
- **[[Roy]]** — the buried self whose gut *is* the alarm. The "trust your gut" gloss is his, and he signed the release-day and first-million-streams gratitude posts as *Roy,* not Lucius — genuine thanks in his own voice, *"after a hard year of life."* He's also the director who, back in 2023, described the video's world as a *"circus-like space."*
- **The Crowd / the audience** — where the song's gaze finally lands. The crowd dancing along and ordering more; the crowd that sings is the crowd that gets eaten. Fans were literally styled *"Jagwar Oompa Loompas."*
- **The Oompa-Loompas (borrowed cast)** — Wonka's moralizing worker-chorus, re-voiced as menace. In the vault's lighter resonance reading they're also *consumed labor* — imported workers paid in cacao, even used as test-subjects — so the chorus that sings the warning is itself the factory's product.

## Symbols

- [[Consumption]] — the crowd as appetite; people eaten as content, scandal, outrage, on demand. The mouth / mandible makes it bodily.
- [[The Mask]] — *"everything's perfect here"* is the [[Happy Face]] mask's audio, scaled to an entire environment.
- [[Inversion]] — *"the truth is a lie that nobody can tell"*: truth flipped into a lie and made unspeakable; the cautionary chorus flipped from after-the-fall to before-it. Bad Feeling is the middle stage of a three-song arc (see Advanced reading).
- Candy / chocolate — the coating: sweetness now, the medicine (dread) inside. Sugar as anesthetic; how a candy factory gets you to swallow things. *(The tooth-decay nightmare belongs to the adjacent [[Welcome to the Circus]], not here.)*
- [[The Moon]] — *"when the moon hits the sky, I'm a little unwell"* → luna / lunacy; the dread arriving under a reflected, borrowed light.
- [[The Circus]] — the 2024 video stages the fully-realized Circus (carousel, giant chess pieces, harlequin animal-masks, ringmaster Roy). The candy factory *is* the Circus.
- Fire — the 2024 video's closing tag: Lucius holds a candle while performers' heads burn. Light-Bearer, staged.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — interpolation as method, and the John 8:32 decay arc
> **Recoding a known song-cell is the album's signature.** Bad Feeling is the *credited* example — Bricusse & Newley carry the same evidentiary standing as Joni Mitchell on *Soul Is A Star* or Rockwell on *The Watchers* — alongside a shelf of public-domain nursery forms elsewhere (GT2BH's fable, [[Bananas]]' playground chant, #1 Champion's *Mary-Mary* and *A-B-C*, [[Not Your Homie]]'s *Ring-a-Rosies*). It's a debut-native practice you can trace all the way to [[Subject to Flooding]]'s *"rich man, poor man"* ([[Long Time Coming]], 2018). Fitting that the album where childhood forms turn fully sinister is the one named for *the hands that rock the cradle.* Frame it as *participation in an older song* — mythic emergence — not invented encoding. (One unverified thread worth a footnote and a caveat: the interpolation is said to have gone out under a *rare* clearance from the original composers' estate to reuse the Oompa-Loompa material — plausible given that Bricusse & Newley are formally credited, but treat it as an **open question** until a primary source surfaces; the vault hasn't traced it.)
>
> **The verse itself is a three-stage decay.** One Bible line — John 8:32, *"the truth will set you free"* — refracts across three eras, form kept, vector bent:
>
> | Era | Song | The line | Stage |
> |---|---|---|---|
> | 2022 | *It's Your Time* (*33*) | *"the truth will set you free / but the truth is hard to tell"* | **Held** — sincere |
> | 2023 | **Bad Feeling** | *"the truth is a lie that nobody can tell"* | **Paradox** — the verse turned against itself |
> | 2026 | *Lucius Lullaby* | *"the truth will set you free / but nothing's, nothing's what it seems"* | **Bent** — quoted by the serpent-voice |
>
> Bad Feeling is the middle hinge — the paradox stage. The final two stages both live on this one album, two tracks apart.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the "you" that won't resolve, and community-as-mirror mourned
> **The pronoun is deliberately un-fixable.** Who is the bad feeling *about* — listener, industry, Roy, Lucius, the appetite-machine? Who is the *"I"* — Lucius warning about the system, or the buried self warning about Lucius? The vault treats this as load-bearing, not sloppy: Roy's grounded doctrine is that *humans are mirrors for each other*, so the second person is always also the first. The pronoun that won't sit still **is** the mirror doctrine made structural.
>
> **The song mourns a lost mirroring.** Roy's own October 2025 *#badfeelingjt* post paired the *"everything's perfect here"* clip (Lucius in flames) with Rudolf Steiner: *"A healthy social life is found only when in the mirror of each soul the whole community finds its reflection."* Read that way, the *"perfect here"* surface is *precisely the absence of that mirroring* — the community-as-mirror ideal the song is grieving.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the grandest framework imports (kept deliberately light)
> The grounded "spectacle as a devouring system" is strong enough to stand alone; these are resonances, not claims:
> - **A corrupted *"That's Amore."*** *"When the moon hits the sky, I'm a little unwell"* inverts Dean Martin's moon-romance into sickness — luna → lunacy. (Held as resonance; Matchbox Twenty's *"Unwell"* is thematic only, not a claimed allusion.)
> - **The candy paradise as false Eden / Maya** — a closed system of tests and punishments, resonant with the writers Roy has posted: Kierkegaard (*"the crowd is the untruth"*), Alice Bailey (*"illusion is the first product of the mind divorced from the soul"*), Blavatsky and Steiner on Maya.
> - **The Oompa-Loompas as consumed labor** — imported workers paid in cacao beans, one turned to a blueberry: the chorus that sings the warning is itself the factory's product and victim.
> - **Jungian shadow-alarm / Gnostic archonic appetite** — the dread as the shadow's signal; the machine as a devouring intelligence.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the two videos: the shadow takes the mic (the visual-canon keystone)
> Bad Feeling exists as **two** videos, and the pair is the cleanest single proof that the *Lucius Lullaby* era is the *unveiling* of a mythology already present since 2020–21, not its birth.
>
> **2024 single video** — Roy performs; the Circus is staged in full (big-top, carousel = the Loop, giant chess pieces and Alice-mirror playing cards, harlequin dancers in animal masks, ringmaster Roy in a Pierrot ruff, fireworks, the moon). It **closes on Lucius silent**, holding a candle while three performers' heads burn — readable as the Light-Bearer (*lux-ferre*) setting the show alight; the mind-divorced-from-heart immolating; a counterfeit Pentecost (tongues of fire inverted); *Nigredo* staged. Note the dating: Roy names a *"circus-like space"* in his 2023 video commentary — **~2 years before** *Welcome to the Circus* names the Circus in canon. The visual-circus motif was in his directing vocabulary before the mythology named it.
>
> **2026 LL visualizer** — credited *"Jagwar Twin, sir lucius."* Lucius sings the **whole** song, at ease, on a crescent-moon-and-clouds lullaby stage. The same song before and after the unveiling: silent shadow lighting the fire → full-voiced, co-credited, center stage. The album is Lucius being handed the microphone.

## Source notes

**Grounded (Roy's own words, released music, public posts, official videos).** The release chain (single Dec 15 2023 → LL track 5; acoustic track 15, the *BMRC bonus* cut) and the Spotify credits (writers incl. The origin story — German/Polish/Slavic ancestry, accordion-and-tuba jamming, *"we need some oompa shit"* (Digital Journal, ~2023), where the load-bearing detail is the **direction**: the oom-pah jam came first and the Wonka/Oompa-Loompa frame grew out of it (a claim unique to that transcript, not on the current public song page) — and the *"circus-like space"* video commentary from the same interview. The absurdist 2023–24 promo register: the *Wonka*-film tie-in posts (Dec 2023: *"Just saw Wonka. iCried"; "This one's for @RealChalamet"*), wanting *"the Willy Wonka converse,"* and Roy's disavowal of the viral Glasgow *"Willy Wonka Experience"* fiasco — *"I swear I had nothing to do with this"* (X, Feb 28 2024). Lucius's EP1 claim ([[The Interview]], 2025-07-31). The chorus phrase as the title of Roy's first gallery show, *"Everything's Perfect Here"* (Lauren Powell Projects, LA, opened Jan 20 2024, a glowing orange sculpture). The 2024 rollout dates: gallery (Jan 20) → music video premiere (Mar 7) → live acoustic (Apr 3) → *PERFECT* remix (May 3) → the Masonic Lodge / Hollywood Forever cemetery show (Jun 25), fans as *"Jagwar Oompa Loompas."* The *#badfeelingjt* Steiner pairing (Oct 23 2025) and the *"truth is a lie"* headline post (Nov 10 2025). Roy's own stated figures: past 1M streams in 9 days (X, Dec 25 2023); *"700,000 streams a day"* and *"#39 on the Top 40 chart"* (Celeb Secrets, Apr 2024); Top 40 US radio (character page). Josh Dun as Roy's stated favorite band's drummer, playing on both this and *Happy Face* (Strife, 2024).

**The 2024 "Most Requested Live" radio war (public Discord).** In January 2024 Roy personally rallied the fan Circle to game iHeart's *Most Requested Live — "CALLING EVERYONE IN THE CIRCLE… it really is time to go to war, because we're at war with the forces of darkness in pop music"* (request line 855-495-7274) — then celebrated landing on Top 40 *"from chain reaction."* This is the literal, real-world proof of the song's central paradox: the alarm about the machine, run *through* the machine.

**Strong readings.** The cautionary-chorus → intuition-alarm shift; the Wonka factory as the Circus in nursery form; toxic-positivity as system speech; the comedy-of-menace read (the sweetness as both punchline and trap — the deadpan *"I don't know, dude,"* the tongue-twister as clever-nonsense-until-you-decode-it); the alarm-that-is-also-the-foreman; the gaze turning on the listener; *"(are you fucking serious?)"* as the rupture-point; intuition as counter-epistemology. Grounded in the material, interpretive on the join.

**Framework (esoteric / cross-song — never authorial intent).** The John 8:32 decay arc; the un-resolvable "you" as the mirror doctrine; the Steiner community-as-mirror mourning; the *"That's Amore"* / luna-lunacy echo; the false-Eden/Maya, consumed-labor, and shadow/archon imports; the two-video A/B as unveiling-not-birth. The system permits these; it doesn't assert Roy intends them.

**How we'd be wrong (the falsifier the page concedes).** If the candy/Wonka aesthetic was *primarily* a 2023 film tie-in marketing hook — a real possibility, given the same-day *Wonka*-film timing — the "spectacle-machine" depth reframes as commercially-occasioned dressing. Roy's own gloss is modest and personal; the systems-critique is the vault's reading layered on top. Open, too: whether the acoustic track 15 (*"Live at the MOTH Gallery,"* the *BMRC bonus* cut) reveals a more mournful core under the candy machinery — Roy favors stripped-down versions because *"it allows the audience to connect with the lyrics more,"* but the vault hasn't analyzed that audio yet.

There's also a **redemption-arc read** of the commercial story worth flagging with care. In a 2024 interview Roy framed a song's path as one of being *dropped by the label* and then *finding its way* to an audience anyway — a survival narrative that recasts the "candy-factory product" cynicism as vindication. **Attribution caveat:** the specific *dropped-by-the-label* anecdotes attach to *[[Happy Face]]* (*"I got dropped for this song… now it's my first Gold record… people slowly found it"*), while the blunter *"the song sucked"* framing is general to the gatekeeper years — so this arc colors Bad Feeling's commercial story by adjacency, not as a documented claim about this track. Held loosely, but it's the counter-narrative to the foreman read: not just the machine running through you, but the thing the machine tried to kill outliving it.
