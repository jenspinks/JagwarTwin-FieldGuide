# Life Is Good

> **One-line reading.** Joy as a decision, not a description — the speaker sees the flames, the lies, and his own sadness, and chooses *"life is good"* anyway, because it stays worth loving even when it isn't.

**Release:** standalone single, Jagwar Twin × angelbaby (a Hume AI artist), November 3, 2023 · **Full lyrics:** [Genius](https://genius.com) · streaming

## What happens in the song

The chorus is built on decision, not evidence: *"Life is good / I'm great… The world's in flames / but at least we're having fun."* Not good *because* things are well — good *despite* the fire. And the boast is immediately undercut by a flat confession: *"But I'm mad / and I'm sad / and I'm misunderstood."*

The speaker presents as a storyteller — *"Hey kids I'll sing you a song / and it won't be very long"* — over a bright, nursery-rhyme surface. There's a thread of overwhelm and dissociation: *"My head's an antenna / I'm wide awake," "Face down but I can feel my body moving / is this real or am I in an illusion," "get up get up I'm only human."* And a refusal at the center: *"Every one fighting, tell me which side are you / keep a smile on my face, too happy to choose."*

## Its place in the mythology

The chorus is joy as decision, and it's a step beyond [[Happy Face]]. Happy Face wears the smile as pure disguise; Life Is Good *admits* the pain — *"I'm mad / I'm sad / I'm misunderstood"* — and still chooses *"life is good."* That's polarity-holding (both true at once), not anesthesia — a progression in the project's forced-positivity thread rather than a repeat.

Its live tension is whether the smile is wisdom or evasion, and the song doesn't fully settle it. *"Too happy to choose / keep a smile"* reads two ways: as Roy-side defiance (refusing to let the outrage-machine — *"which side are you"* — consume your humanity; loving the world even as it burns so the fire doesn't win), or as Circus-complicity (fun-over-truth dissociation, the dumbing-down [[Welcome to the Circus]] runs on). The *"I'm mad and I'm sad"* admission tips toward defiance — you're not in pure denial if you can name the pain — but the dissociation imagery keeps complicity alive. The smile being either wisdom or evasion is the song; don't resolve it.

The self-aware bard delivery — announcing itself as a song sung to children while the world's in flames — is the catalogue's sweet-surface-over-darkness device made self-aware. And the antenna line marks the speaker as *too* awake, overwhelmed by signal (*"turn off the news / living in a lie and we can't see the truth"*); the smile is partly a way to survive the reception.

## Notes

There's an almost-too-on-the-nose meta-layer: a song asking *"is this real or am I in an illusion"* — about fake, plastic reality — is co-performed by a synthetic entity. How far that goes depends on whether angelbaby generated parts or only performed, which isn't public. A guard worth naming: the collaborator's name echoes the project's "angel baby" / divine-feminine imagery (as in [[Weirdness]]), but angelbaby is a real external collaborator and is not that symbolic voice — the resonance is a coincidence of names. Speculatively, *"too happy to choose"* can read as a refusal of the pole-pair rather than mere avoidance — holding the warring sides as two degrees of one thing — which sharpens rather than resolves the wisdom-or-evasion tension. The lyric is single-sourced and streaming-unverified; one line is filed as *"Nether circle round the sun,"* which, if instead *"Another,"* would shift the cosmology toward [[LOOP]]'s mechanical recurrence.
