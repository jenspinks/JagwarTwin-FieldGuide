# MAGIK

> **One-line reading.** A pure witnessing song — a voice telling someone at risk of disappearing that their ordinary presence is already miraculous, and that a plastic world's failure to recognize them doesn't make their light less real.

**Release:** *MAGIK* EP, 2025 (preceded the *Lucius Lullaby* album) · **Full lyrics:** [Genius](https://genius.com) · streaming

## What happens in the song

The song is built almost entirely as second-person reassurance: *"you don't gotta disappear," "everybody needs you here," "you don't have to try so hard," "you're more than who you think you are."* The speaker keeps seeing what the addressee cannot — *"you don't even see it / but you have it," "everywhere you are is so bright / but you don't even know that," "I see it in your eyes."* And it sets *"Magik"* against *"a world so plastic."*

Its emotional center, *"you don't gotta disappear / everybody needs you here,"* reads as spoken to someone at risk of erasing themselves. The song doesn't diagnose the disappearance — it interrupts it. Not an argument (*here's why you matter*) but intervention by blessing (*everybody needs you here* — your presence changes the world around you).

## Its place in the mythology

This is the catalogue's clearest case of witnessing rather than surveillance. Surveillance says *I see you, therefore I can predict or shape or consume you*; MAGIK says *I see you, therefore you don't have to disappear.* The addressee isn't exposed; they're returned to themselves — the El Roi axis (the seer who restores) at its plainest.

And the magic here is ordinary, not performative: *"everything you do is Magik"* — already present, the problem being perception, not capability. That's the exact inverse of [[Sir Lucius]]-style spectacle, which manufactures impact. MAGIK recognizes radiance instead, with no mask or stage-trick required. It blesses without recruiting: never *follow me / perform for me / become useful*, only *you already have it.* The *"world so plastic"* makes it an anti-Circus song — it locates value inside the person before the world can package it.

The directness is the move. The song risks sounding simple because it's so direct, and that's the point. Lucius songs work through irony, masks, threat, inversion, and spectacle; MAGIK does almost none of that. Its radical gesture isn't complexity — it's the refusal to participate in the plastic world's terms. Preceding the album, it reads as the light the album then watches Lucius try to suppress, the same role [[SOL]] plays as the love the shadow can't metabolize.

## Characters

- [[Roy|Jagwar Twin]] — emphatically the Roy-side voice: the witness, the blesser. The "you" is intentionally unstable (lover, fan, child, friend, listener, or Roy addressing a wounded part of himself), and the song's power comes from not choosing.

## Symbols

- [[The Moon]] — *"a lasso round the Moon,"* reaching for the unreachable as a love-gesture; the lunar register that runs through the project's Mary material.
- [[The Labyrinth]] — *"you're the middle of a maze."* Not lost in the maze but at its center; the way through isn't escape from the self but finding the center correctly.

## Notes

The K-spelling may gesture at the esoteric *"Magick"* (the spelling that distinguishes intentional will from stage illusion), but the lyric's content leans toward innate, unrecognized worth rather than occult will, so that reading is raised rather than asserted. The *"lasso round the Moon"* faintly echoes *It's a Wonderful Life*, where George Bailey offers to lasso the moon — notably for a woman named Mary, which quietly rhymes with the project's [[Mary]] without needing to carry weight.
