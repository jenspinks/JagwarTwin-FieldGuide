# tomorrow (ANGLE OF ETERNITY)

> **One-line reading.** A threshold-anthem of remembrance — standing at the doorway of mortality, the song dissolves certainty rather than resolving it, until what remains isn't *"I know"* but *"I AM."*

**Release:** standalone single, Jagwar Twin × lovelytheband · **Full lyrics:** [Genius](https://genius.com) · streaming

## What happens in the song

The title is stylized as a lowercase *"tomorrow"* against an uppercase *"(ANGLE OF ETERNITY)."* The lyric keeps surrendering certainty — *"maybe I don't know, I don't know,"* recurring, *"and that's fine with me"* — and reframes death as a threshold: *"if death is just a doorway… where you goin'?"*, *"do you remember?"* It calls reality holographic — *"we're all holograms / but even if that's all we are…"* — and offers a surrender hook, *"if we live like we ain't gonna die."* The outro resolves into a massive repeated chant of *"I AM."*

## Its place in the mythology

The lead is remembrance, not attainment. *"Do you remember?"* and *"take my hand, I'll help you remember"* make the song's salvation recovered rather than acquired — and that's a pattern across confirmed songs: [[The Circle]] (*"the wise one remembered she once held the string"*), [[Soul Is A Star]] (*"do you know who you are?"*), and here (*"do you remember?"*). The speaker takes the guide role, leading the other through the death-doorway by helping them remember.

It runs as a clean inversion of [[Playing to the Gods]]. Both hinge on an *if-then-deathlessness* structure with opposite engines. There, the ego clutches itself in fear — *"if I make it out alive, then I'm never gonna die"* — grasping a counterfeit deathlessness on condition. Here, the speaker releases it — *"if we live like we ain't gonna die"* — and finds they were never at risk. Survival-clutch versus surrender.

The *"I AM"* stack reads as the divine name of Exodus 3:14 — the name given at the burning bush, used across Western esotericism for the divine spark — and it contrasts Lucius's externalized boast (*"I'm the one they're talking 'bout,"* in [[Welcome to the Circus]]): identity rooted in source versus identity rooted in audience. Because this is a collaboration, the spiritual frame may be co-authored, so the pure-Roy attribution is held lightly.

The holograms aren't mere fakeness. A hologram is structure that encodes and projects deeper information, so the line is less nihilistic than it sounds: the visible form isn't the whole of reality, it's an expression of it. The speaker is fine with the projection because the underlying presence — *"I AM"* — is secure. There's no panic here, unlike the simulation-dread of [[LOOP]].

## Notes

The title's *"angle"* reads primarily as perspective or orientation: the song keeps asking *how are you looking at reality* — from fear and separation, or from eternity and remembrance? *"If death is just a doorway, where you goin'?"* changes entirely with the angle you stand at; the doorway is fixed, your angle isn't. The song never eliminates uncertainty — *"maybe I don't know… and that's fine with me"* holds both poles, which is why it reads mature rather than merely inspirational. Speculatively, the *"holograms… but even if that's all we are… I AM"* movement gestures at the distinction between a form and the breath that animates it, re-grounded on the public logos and word-creation tradition (John 1's *"the Word"*; Genesis's *"let there be"*; vibration-as-creation) rather than any private framework.
