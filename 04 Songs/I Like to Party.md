# I Like to Party

> **One-line reading.** An exuberant party anthem about people who never leave the house — they "stay in and pretend that we're in the club" — staged as an attention-force operating a crowd, and interrupted at the very end by a competing broadcast, *the kingdom is within you*, which the program cuts off mid-word.

> [!info] Album & credits
> **Album:** *33* (2022, Big Loud Rock) — **track 4 of 10** · 2:57 · the album's most-streamed song
> **First release:** standalone single, July 23 2021 (Strange Theory)
> **Written by** Roy English & Matt Pauling · **Produced by** Matt Pauling (sole — Roy is *not* a listed producer here, one of only two such *33* tracks, with [[Down to You]])
> **Music video:** dir. Kyle Vorbach, released August 6 2021
> **Full lyrics:** [Genius](https://genius.com) · streaming

## At a glance

- **Era / release** — *33* single era; out as a standalone single in **2021**, then track 4 on the **2022** album. (Some early pages mis-dated the video to 2022 — it's 2021.)
- **In one line** — a clap-along banger whose actual subject is that nobody leaves the house, and whose one moment of real awakening gets interrupted by a fake news bulletin.
- **Who speaks** — a herding narrator best read as [[Roy]] fronting the attention-force introduced one track earlier; the crowd answers in ad-libs; in the bridge a **Devil** and a **People** wake up at the same time.
- **Key symbols** — the [[Consumption|cake]] · the [[The Mask|mask]] · the [[The Circus|circus]] and "in circles" · [[Machine vs Human|Tell-A-Vision]] · the broadcast interrupt.

The joke lands before anything else does. This is a hype track — *"let's see you clap them hands!"* — and its confession is that the party is people staying home pretending: *"we don't go outside / we stay in and pretend that we're in the club."* The party **is** the not-partying. Play it straight and it's the great ironic lockdown banger of the *33* record; listen once more and the host turns out to own the room.

## The song, mapped

*I Like to Party* is a call-and-response dance-pop track built to loop. It opens on an MC hailing a crowd, runs a contradiction-hook four times, threads two clusters of media-and-isolation imagery, pivots into a doctrinal bridge — and then that bridge gets cut off mid-word by a broadcast interrupt that hard-returns to the chorus. The form doesn't decorate the argument; it *performs* it. Following the track's own shape:

| Section | In the song | What the beat does |
|---|---|---|
| **MC hail** | *"Let's see you clap them hands!"* | The officiant addresses a crowd before the song "starts." Live-MC framing on everything that follows. |
| **Hook** (×4) | *"I like to party with all of my friends / But we don't go outside / We stay in and pretend that we're in the club"* | The contradiction that anchors the whole thing: celebration staged as withdrawal. Connection *felt without connecting.* |
| **Verse A — the pitch** | *"A new direction / Affecting the state of your mind"* → *"Give me attention, attention / The people are mine"* | The host names his offer (alter your mind) and his claim (I own the crowd). The "in circles… like chickens with no head" image lands here, with the chickens/Dickens pun (see the note below). |
| **Count-off** (×2) | *"So one, two, three, four, follow me / To the beat of my drum / Sing along with me… Lock up your door / Get on the floor"* | The Pied-Piper module. The counting and call-and-response don't *describe* herding — they **enact** it. |
| **Verse B — isolation + manufacture** | *"All I wanna do is zoomy-zoomy, zoom-zoom / So I zoom everyday"* → *"Television on the news / Devil's Food is a cake… We don't want 'em awake / Bake a truth with a lie / Then we sell 'em the cake"* | Lockdown malaise rendered as baby-talk, then the spectacle named as a **manufactured product**: pure lies won't sell, so you bake truth in. |
| **Bridge** — the turn | *"Devil waking up to claim the kingdom / People waking up to claim what's in them / Heaven is within your heart / The kingdom, you're the kingdom… (Hallelujah)"* | The argument's pivot: **two opposite awakenings at once**, resolving toward inner sovereignty and escalating toward *"You're the King—"* |
| **The interrupt** — the key event | *"You're the King—" / "We interrupt our program to bring you this important message"* | The inner awakening is severed **mid-word** and the broadcast slams back to the chorus. The distraction-system reasserts and gets the last word. |
| **Outro** | *"(I like to party)" / "(we stay in)" / "(Let's see it! Let's see it!)"* → *"Let's see you clap them hands!"* | Dissolves back into the crowd-hail it opened on. The loop closes; the capture repeats. |

**The argument in five moves:** (1) an invitation to a party that is actually isolation → (2) the host revealed as an attention-owner running the crowd → (3) the distraction shown to be a manufactured product, truth folded into the lie so it sells → (4) the counter-truth briefly surfaces — *the kingdom is within you* → (5) the broadcast cuts that truth off mid-word and loops back to the hook. The form is a machine for capture: **the awakening is never allowed to finish.**

## What it means

A few claims that cut across the whole track:

- **The hook is a contradiction, and the contradiction is the thesis.** Enthusiasm for partying, welded to *"we don't go outside."* The gathering is simulated and indoors; the clap-along *form* is itself the deception, selling the togetherness the words say isn't real. Everything else in the song is that gap widened.
- **The narrator commands and owns.** *"Follow me," "sing along with me," "lock up your door," "get on the floor,"* counted *"one, two, three, four,"* and the tell: *"give me attention, attention / the people are mine."* He offers *"a new direction / affecting the state of your mind"* — the pitch of someone selling you an altered state, not a night out. Read against the previous track, this is the same acquisitive first-person **operating a crowd** (see [[#The attention-force, operating]] below).
- **The distraction is a product, and the target is the system.** *"Bake a truth with a lie / then we sell 'em the cake"* is a recipe: pure falsehood gets rejected, so truth is baked in to make it go down. The critique isn't aimed at gullible individuals — it's aimed at the information **machine** that manufactures the sweet lie. *"We don't want 'em awake"* says the quiet part.
- **The bridge stages two awakenings — and the wrong one wins the airtime.** *"Devil waking up to claim the kingdom"* vs *"People waking up to claim what's in them"*: one wakes to **seize** the kingdom as external possession, the other to **reclaim** it as interior inheritance. The song briefly hands the mic to inner sovereignty (*"Heaven is within your heart / you're the kingdom"*) — and then the broadcast interrupt severs it mid-word and returns to the chorus. It is **interrupted, not resolved.** That's the whole move.
- **It also just works as a stay-home banger.** None of the metaphysics is *declared* by the lyric. It reads perfectly well as a straight, ironic Zoom-era party song, and the bridge can be heard as a tonal gear-shift rather than a doctrine. The attention-force and kingdom-within layer is the deeper interpretation, not something the song asserts — and the band cheerfully tours it as a banger (*"Dubai likes to party"*). Both readings are true at once; that doubleness is the point, not a problem to solve.

### The attention-force, operating

*I Like to Party* sits directly after [[Pay Attention (feat. little luna)]], where a first-person power demands your awareness and Roy plays that force. Here, one track later, shared co-writer and all, that same acquisitive "I" is shown **running a crowd** — *"the people are mine"* is the casting note. It's a strong reading rather than a stated fact (Roy hasn't named them one character), but the anchors stack: adjacency, shared authorship, the lexical echo, and the matching first-person owner. This is the cleanest place in the catalogue to watch the attention-economy villain not just announced but *at work.*

The mechanics are the **Pied Piper's**: *"one, two, three, four, follow me / to the beat of my drum / sing along with me."* A following led away by an irresistible beat, synchronized into motion instead of invited to think. And it reads as **secular ritual** — clapping, chanting, counting, call-and-response, the whole texture of a ceremony — right up until the officiant names himself. Entertainment as liturgy, with a congregation that repeats and follows.

### Lucius-coded, not Lucius-voiced

The song dramatizes the exact mechanism [[Sir Lucius]] later personifies — spectacle as containment, false sweetness, media seduction, awakening-language converted to content — years **before** Lucius is named or credited. This is the catalogue's cleanest example of *Lucius-inspired-but-not-voiced*: Roy is the **object** here (the one being fed the cake in the video), not the speaker, which is precisely what separates it from the songs Lucius actually sings. The 2021 end-card even names him by function — *"Devil waking up to claim the kingdom"* — a full two years before the 2023 dream-origin and four before the 2026 co-credit. He was on the song before he had a name.

## Characters

- **[[Roy]]** — voices the herding narrator, the attention-force shown running the crowd. In the **video** he's also the one being force-fed cake and ringed by masks: both the operator of the spectacle and its victim/object. The party is happening *to* him as much as *through* him.
- **[[Sir Lucius]]** — not present by name or voice, but his operating signature is everywhere (see above). The clearest pre-name rendering of the figure in the whole catalogue.
- **[[The Crowd]]** — the faceless followers, *"the people are mine."* In the video they're a ring of blank grey masks, which Roy's own pinned comment names *"a beautiful group of mind/body/spirit complexes"* (a Law-of-One term). See also the [[The Audience|Audience]] as spectacle-participant.
- **The Devil** — named in the bridge as one of two waking parties (*"Devil waking up to claim the kingdom"*): the external usurper competing with the inner-sovereignty awakening.
- **The jeweled hand** *(video only, off-screen)* — the ringed hand that force-feeds the cake, read as the industry. Whose hand it is stays an open question — self-inflicted, industry-inflicted, or Lucius-inflicted — but its coming from *off-screen* makes the force external.

## Symbols

- **[[Consumption|The cake / Devil's Food]]** — the manufactured sweet-lie sold to a sleeping crowd (*"bake a truth with a lie / then we sell 'em the cake"*); rendered literal as force-feeding in the video and as the Saturn-cake cover art. The anchor of the "cake before the circus" consumption reading (and the real-world 2022 "cake party" NFT campaign — see Source notes).
- **[[The Mask]]** — the grey-masked congregation in the video; an early instance of the Mask/Circus iconography later billed as Lucius.
- **[[The Circus]] / "in circles"** — spectacle as a participatory control system; the circle run *outward* as dissipation rather than [[The Circle|inward return]]. *"Clap them hands"* is the audience cued on command.
- **[[Machine vs Human|Television / Tell-A-Vision]]** — the broadcast that *tells you a vision*. Grounded as intentional band language by the official **Tell-A-Vision Tee** (a vintage TV with a woman's face reading TELL-A-VISION). The sedation-engine turned into an icon.
- **The door** — *"lock up your door"*: isolation and withdrawal, the container/threshold sealed rather than opened.
- **The drum** — *"to the beat of my drum"*: the Piper's rhythm as allegiance (see the Advanced reading on whose drum you move to).
- **The crown / kingdom** — *"you're the kingdom / you're the King"*: inner sovereignty, the thing the interrupt cuts off. Echoes *33*'s "reclaim your crown" campaign language.
- **The broadcast interrupt** — *"we interrupt our program"* as the spectacle-machine's signature move: overriding the awakening and looping back to content.

## The comedy (and why it's load-bearing)

The band leans into the bit, and the bit *is* the insight. *"All I wanna do is zoomy-zoomy, zoom-zoom / so I zoom everyday"* is lockdown malaise as a nursery rhyme. *"Cake, cake, cake, cake!"* collapses the entire bread-and-circuses critique into a gleeful sugar-rush ad-lib. The real merch promo code was *"Beanie4menie"* (a beanie for me, knee). In 2022 Roy tweeted, deadpan, *"What is a party?"* and *"It's not a party without cake"* while literally throwing a cake party to auction off an AI upload of his own consciousness. Later captions ran the song as a straight tour anthem — *"Dubai likes to party," "Do you like to party?"* — cheerfully oblivious, on the surface, to its own satire.

That dual register — pop-friendly affect (clapping, *"yah, we're in the club"*) laid over genuinely dark content (loss of identity, force-fed cake, *"we don't want 'em awake"*) — is the same ironic mechanism that later produces *"great time to be human"* and *"welcome to the circus."* The sweetness is the delivery system for the horror. That's not a tonal accident; it's the thesis wearing a party hat.

> [!note]- The chickens/Dickens joke, and the tale that was told
> *"Running around like chickens with no head… / Looking like Charles Dickens / I mean dead, the tale was told."* It's a groaner pun first — **chickens/Dickens**, and "the dickens" as slang for the devil/for nothing — played for the wince and then abandoned. There's a faint after-image: *"the tale was told"* brushes *Macbeth*'s "a tale told by an idiot… signifying nothing" (empty spectacle), and Dickens's own most famous opening (*"it was the best of times, it was the worst of times"*) is the great duality sentence. But the honest verdict is that this is a **curio, not a keystone** — the rhyme came first, the resonance is decorative, and it shouldn't be built into anything load-bearing.

## Advanced reading

> [!note]- The broadcast war — three signals fighting for the listener
> The bridge and interrupt stage a three-way fight over **who authors reality**:
> 1. **Tell-A-Vision sedation** — the system, *"we don't want 'em awake," "bake a truth with a lie."*
> 2. **The kingdom-within awakening** — *"People waking up to claim what's in them,"* inner sovereignty.
> 3. **The "we interrupt our program" counter-broadcast** — carrying Chaplin's borrowed humanist words into the feed.
>
> The catch: the interrupt **cuts the awakening off mid-word** and returns to the chorus. The counter-broadcast breaks in, but the program still wins the airtime. The song is honest about which signal is loudest.

> [!note]- The Chaplin relay, re-voiced (not sampled)
> The bridge's *"kingdom within"* isn't only Luke 17:21 — it borrows the **words of Chaplin's *Great Dictator* speech** (*"the kingdom of God is within man"*), the transmission Jagwar Twin sampled verbatim back on [[Superhuman (Interlude)]] (2018). Here it's **re-voiced as Roy's own lyric, not a clip** — a humanist transmission inherited and carried forward into the *33* media-machine (the [[Machine vs Human]] arc, an [[Inherited Substrate|inherited-substrate]] relay at the level of the line itself). So *"we interrupt our program"* is, in spirit, the **Chaplin counter-broadcast** breaking into the Tell-A-Vision feed — even as the feed silences it.

> [!note]- The esoteric frame — Cave, Gnostic awakening, the kingdom within
> Several traditions rhyme with the song, held as correspondence rather than claimed method:
> - **Plato's Cave** — watching representations indoors while believing yourself fully present.
> - **[[Panem et Circenses|Bread and circuses]] / "let them eat cake"** — spectacle plus confection as population-pacifier, the classical critique made literal in dessert. Marie-Antoinette sits underneath the Devil's-Food.
> - **[[Gnosticism - The World as Trap|Gnostic awakening]]** — *"we don't want 'em awake"* vs *"claim what's in them"* = waking from illusion into remembrance, sharpened by the **Devil's** competing awakening. (Guard: the grounded floor is only Roy's public "society is fake, be yourself" posture; the specifically-Gnostic apparatus is a learned overlay on a plainer sentiment.)
> - **The kingdom within / [[Kabbalah - The Tree of Life|Malkhut]]** — *"Heaven is within your heart / you're the kingdom"* locates authority in the person, not the institution or broadcast (Luke 17:21; the Kabbalistic Malkhut). The song's axis is external programming vs internal sovereignty.

> [!note]- "In circles" — the Circle run outward
> The distracted crowd moves *"in circles"* like headless chickens. Against the catalogue's central [[The Circle|Circle]] — the inward *return* — this is the circle run as **spectacle and dissipation**, recursion pointed outward rather than back home: the [[The Circus|Circus]] as the Circle inverted. (Leap flagged: "in circles" is idiomatic for futility on its own; the Circle-architecture tie is the reader's frame, not the lyric's.)

> [!note]- Whose drum you move to = whose creature you are
> The Piper's *"to the beat of my drum… follow me"* (2021) sits between two other drums in the catalogue: a **true drum** — *"I can hear your heart, it beats"* (*Move To You*, 2018) — and an inherited **dread-drum** — *"coming like a drum from 1981"* (*All My Friends*, 2023). The point isn't that the word "drum" recurs (the cheap version); it's that **rhythm is allegiance**: the heart's beat, the Piper's beat, the dread-beat are three masters, and which one you move to is which one owns you. A framework reading of an allegiance motif, not a claimed authorial through-line.

> [!note]- The video as the "cake before the Circus"
> The 2021 video (dir. Kyle Vorbach) is the clearest **pre-naming** visual of the consumption mechanism: Roy is force-fed cake by an off-screen jeweled hand, escalating to cake smeared over his face and torso, while a ring of blank grey masks looks on. Four years before *Welcome to the Circus* names *"I'll chew you up, spit you out,"* the visual catalogue already shows Roy as the thing **being eaten**. The reading: cake = algorithm-friendly content; jeweled hand = the industry; Roy = what gets stuffed in and pulled out. And the **cover art** doubles it — a cake-slice plated as **Saturn/Cronus the devourer**, ringed by skulls and cherubs (Consumption plus memento mori: *"the party that eats you"*), under the tagline *"A new direction affecting the state of your mind."*

The reused TikTok captions over the song's sound (*"we stay in and pretend," "Follow me tho #influencer," "Do you like to party?," "Dubai likes to party"*).
- **Strong readings.** The narrator as the *Pay Attention* attention-force operating a crowd; the Pied-Piper structure; mass culture as secular ritual; the broadcast war; the Chaplin relay re-voiced; "Lucius-coded, not Lucius-voiced"; the video as the "cake before the Circus." The counterargument is kept live on purpose: this works, start to finish, as a straight ironic stay-home party banger with no metaphysics at all.
- **A note on dates.** The single is 2021, the album track is 2022 — worth stating cleanly, since a couple of pages once mis-dated the video to 2022.
