# Not Your Homie

> **One-line reading.** The song disguises itself as the shallowest thing on the album — aggressive, meme-shaped, deliberately dumb — and is one of the most important on it: the shadow, credited as a co-maker, takes the mic and proves his own thesis, that the algorithm rewards caricature over sincerity. The shallowness *is* the argument.

> [!info] Album & credits
> **Album:** *[[Lucius Lullaby (album)|Lucius Lullaby]]* (Jagwar Twin & [[Sir Lucius]], 2026, Big Loud Rock) — **track 11 of 15** · 2:47
> **Released:** as a pre-album single, **October 31, 2025** (Halloween)
> **Written by** Roy English, Mercedes Colletta Britton (MERCY) & Dave Burris · **Produced by** Jagwar Twin & Dave Burris · **Mixed & vocal-produced by** Roy English
> **Full lyrics:** [Genius](https://genius.com) · streaming
> **▶ Listen:** [Visualizer (YouTube)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dW_6VXrRkYE)

This is the other half speaking openly. Where the rest of the album debates what to *do* with [[Sir Lucius]], here he simply takes the mic and demonstrates: vulnerability loses, spectacle wins, meaning is optional. And here is the horror the song is built to deliver — on his own metrics, he's right. The track went viral. The song about the machine rewarding a cartoon of a person was itself rewarded by the machine. Form and content shook hands.

It helps to know how light Roy plays it in public. He called it *"the ultimate introverted extrovert anthem"* — a party banger written by someone who wants everyone to leave him alone (the merch capsule literally read *LEAVE ME ALONE*). That framing is the master key: Lucius is Roy's socially functional performance, the extrovert mask worn over the introvert underneath. The vault, characteristically, reads the same three minutes as a treatise on the shadow, the Beast, and the throat that wears *the God who sees me* while snarling *you don't know me.* Both are true. Hold them at once.

## At a glance

- **Era / release** — pre-album single, **Oct 31 2025**; track 11 on *[[Lucius Lullaby (album)|Lucius Lullaby]]* (Mar 27 2026). The middle link of the press-named rollout chain **[[LOOP]] → Not Your Homie → [[Welcome to the Circus]]**.
- **In one line** — the shadow, billed as a co-maker, refuses false kinship and proves the algorithm pays for caricature over soul.
- **Who speaks** — **[[Sir Lucius]]**, in full predatory-charm register. Not Roy singing *about* Lucius; Lucius with the microphone. ([[Roy]] appears only as the counter-pole — the one who *made the concession* off-camera. [[Mary]] and [[The Crowd]] are addressed, never heard.)
- **Key symbols** — [[The Mask]] · [[Inversion]] · [[The Green Apple]] · [[The Banana]] · [[The Moon]] · [[The Thread]]

## The song, mapped

Seven labeled sections, and none of them "develop" the way a normal song does. That's deliberate. The track behaves like a **hypnotic construct** — a memetic spell built to loop, not to arrive. The album's own metadata tags it *orbit*: the only "orbit"-tagged track on the record, where everything else *loops*. A loop can still close into a circle; an orbit just goes round. This one goes round.

| # | Section | Short of it | What the beat does |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Intro** | *"You're not my homie, you don't know me / You're no friend, let's not pretend"* → *"let's not pretend-tend-tend-tend-tend"* | The thesis-refusal, stated before any argument. Then the last word dissolves into pure recursion — the loop-structure made **audible** in the first ten seconds. |
| 2 | **Verse 1** | *"I'm ringing rosies, my pocket's full of posies / Coming in like a plague from the dark side"* | The **contagion** move. A nursery rhyme ("Ring a Ring o' Roses") fused to *plague* — identity as something that *spreads.* Bracketed by a jab at parasocial intimacy: *"stop acting like you know me."* |
| 3 | **Pre-Chorus** | *"Ashes, ashes, we all fall down / Take that smile, turn it upside-down"* · *(Leave me alone)* | The **inversion** beat. The plague rhyme resolves into the mask-flip — the [[Happy Face]] smile turned into a snarl — tagged with the boundary that became the hoodie. |
| 4 | **Chorus** | the refusal, repeated, ending again in *"pretend-tend"* | The song's true center of gravity is a **boundary**, not a love-hook. The pronoun count proves it: *you* 29, *I* 25 — this is the "you don't know me" **you**, not the beloved one. |
| 5 | **Verse 2** | self-inflation → *"might run for president, I'm so presidential"* → *"John Wick with my pencil / stick it in your eye"* → *"tame the beast… a champion ×4"* | The **escalation.** A crescendo from charm to demagogue-reach to office-supply violence to the seductive lure (*"now you're on the dark side"*) — landing on the album's load-bearing line. |
| 6 | **Bridge** | *"I love my life"* … *"I'll end your life"* (with *"I'm like Michael, I'm a psycho, so don't kill my vibe"* between) | The **signature**, distilled. Charm and threat in one breath via *one swapped word* — the mirror-flip that is Lucius's fingerprint, compressed to a couplet. |
| 7 | **Chorus (out)** | the refusal again, decaying into *"tend-tend-tend"* | It ends **inside the loop it opened with.** No return, no resolution — it orbits. |

So the whole argument-arc is: **refusal → contagion → inversion → the Beast's terms → charm-as-threat → unresolved loop.** The repetition and the dumbness aren't a lapse in craft. The *form enacts the thesis* — viral, hypnotic, meaning optional. A song that argues meaning is optional had better be catchy, and it is.

Two beats deserve their own paragraph.

**"Tame the beast… a champion."** Buried in the swagger of Verse 2 is arguably the strongest single line in the whole catalogue for the album's deepest reading: *"if you choose to tame the beast, you got to prove yourself to be a champion"* (that last word four times). It says **tame. Never kill.** The Beast is to be *integrated,* not destroyed — which is the entire moral architecture of the record, dropped almost offhandedly inside a taunt. (See *[[The Labors of Hercules]]* for why "tame, not slay" is load-bearing.)

**The bridge's one-word swap.** *"'Cause I'm classy, and I'm snazzy, and I love my life… but I'll end your life."* Charm and murder are the same sentence with a single word changed — *love* → *end.* That is Lucius's whole method rendered as a party trick: the [[Inversion|mirror-flip]], his fingerprint, small enough to miss and impossible to unhear. (*Michael* is left deliberately ambiguous — Jackson, the archangel, or Myers; the menace works whichever you reach for.)

> [!note] A lyric footnote, for completeness
> *"Now you're on the dark side (sucker, it's a vibe)"* — that parenthetical appears **only in Spotify's synced lyrics**, absent from both Apple Music and Genius. It's kept as an observation, not load-bearing; the rest of the lyric is stable across sources. Worth knowing if you're ever comparing panes.

## What it means

A handful of claims that cut across all seven sections.

- **The shallowness is a demonstration, not a slip.** This is the hinge of everything. Lucius takes the machinery of attention and *proves his thesis with it* — and he wants the hit to **validate his worldview**. The spiritually catastrophic beat isn't the aggression; it's what happens off-stage, where Roy *concedes* (see below). The song is Lucius's victory lap, and the lap is real.
- **Two meanings of "nonsense."** Roy's own nonsense is **generative** — meaning arriving through feeling before the conscious mind catches up. Lucius runs the *identical* machinery in reverse: hooks, loops, optimized fragments, engineered to produce **strategic emptiness** — form severed from soul. Mechanically the same, spiritually inverted. This is the cleanest statement of the difference between the two men anywhere on the album.
- **"You don't know me" is the whole song, and it means more than it says.** On the surface: an attack on **parasociality** — *you consume me, you project onto me, but you do not know me* — and *homie* is just one more mask stripped off. Underneath: the shadow refusing *shallow* recognition. Not friendship, not aesthetic appreciation — real **knowing.** And it accuses *both* the listener and Roy of incomplete self-knowledge. The devastating part: Lucius *longs to be truly known* while enforcing *you don't know me.* The denial and the longing share an address. This is the song where that contradiction is loudest.
- **Contagion is the delivery system.** *"Coming in like a plague from the dark side"* literalizes the ring-a-rosie plague rhyme: Lucius as **memetic contagion** — catchy, addictive, spreading like an idea. The shadow gone viral; the thing that spreads is unconsciousness itself.
- **It reaches for the throne.** *"Presidential,"* *"final boss,"* *"mastermind"* — Lucius keeps scaling up, from ringmaster to would-be sovereign. The violence targets the **eye** (*"stick it in your eye"*): perception itself, which is fitting, because his entire craft is managing what you're allowed to see.
- **The infantilization is the method — and it cuts back on him.** The playground rhymes, taunts, and cartoon violence are *of a piece* with the whole carnival-nursery-Oompa-Loompa register the album runs on. The wager underneath: modern culture is itself psychologically **regressed** — dopamine-driven, algorithmically conditioned — so a hyper-compressed viral identity-object is exactly the right bait for it. But the same register indicts its speaker. Across the surrounding lore Lucius behaves like a **child performing sovereignty** — which is textbook protector-self, formed around early pain. (See the [[The Protector|protector]] reading below.)

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the moon, the throat, and the Beast (the vault's heavy apparatus, kept whole)
> These are the deeper mappings. They're strong, coherent readings — but readings, not proof, and the page is honest about which is which.
>
> **Lucius as the moon — reflector, not source.** In the vault's [[Source vs Reflection|source/reflection]] frame, Lucius is lunar: he has no light of his own, only what falls on him. Illuminated by fear, algorithms, and audience hunger, he becomes viral and dominating. Illuminated by source-light — the heart, [[SOL]], the faceless mother — he becomes witness and bridge. He is *"the moon trying to become a sun."* That's his tragedy, and why he reads as morally complex rather than simply evil. *(One honest caveat, flagged in the vault's own ledger: a later re-mapping assigns the moon to [[Roy]] and reads Lucius instead as the morning star / Venus, with the lunar realm merely his operating theatre. The two framings coexist in the material; the moon-image here is the one the song itself keeps reaching for.)*
>
> **El Roi on the throat — the mirror wearing the seer's name.** Lucius wears Hebrew on his throat: *El Roi* (אל ראי), *"the God who sees me,"* from Genesis 16, where Hagar — seen in the wilderness — becomes the only person in scripture to name God. The tattoo is grounded; the resonance is a strong reading. In a Kabbalistic frame the throat is **Da'at**, the gate where knowing becomes utterance — so every word Lucius speaks passes through *"the God who sees me,"* and even the manipulation carries a trace of revelation. The irony is almost too tight: he wears *the God who sees me* on the very organ of the line *you don't know me.* Sharpest formulation the vault has: *the machine that watches everything cannot witness anyone.* (See [[Witnessing vs Surveillance]].)
>
> **The Beast — refinement, not a monster to slay.** *"Tame the beast"* reads through *[[The Labors of Hercules]]* as the Leo trial: the confrontation that *integrates* the beast rather than killing it. Lucius **is** the Beast, refined into strategy — Roy's anger, ambition, humiliation, fear, and charisma organized into a working system. So the peril the song stages isn't defeating Lucius; it's whether Roy will **serve** him instead of **integrating** him. Wholeness is heart *plus* shadow, never heart *instead of* shadow. And the offer is deliberately double-edged: *"prove yourself to be a champion"* is either a recruitment pitch (join me and rule) or an alchemical dare (face the Beast and be changed). The word won't resolve — which is the point.
>
> **The false crown.** That "championship" Lucius is hawking? In *[[#1 Champion (la Conquistadora)]]* it gets **revoked** — [[Mary]] as La Conquistadora unseats him. So in *Not Your Homie* he is **reselling a crown already taken from him,** still working the title after the title's been stripped. The swagger is a man who doesn't know, or won't admit, that he's already lost the belt.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the nursery-rhyme spine, and the plague-march as weaponized circle-game
> **"Ring a Ring o' Roses" is the structural spine** — part of the album-wide method Roy has described as *"twisted takes on adolescent musical themes."* The same move runs everywhere: *Row Row Row* under *[[Lucius Lullaby (album)|Lucius Lullaby]]*, dog↔god under *[[Great Time to Be Human]]*, the Oompa-Loompa interpolation in *[[Bad Feeling (Oompa Loompa)]]*, "Mary Mary" and the alphabet under *[[#1 Champion (la Conquistadora)]]*. It's debut-native — the trick traces all the way back to *[[Subject to Flooding]]* (2018).
>
> **The dark twist specific to this song:** ring-around-the-rosie is the most *communal* children's game there is — a circle of held hands. Lucius appropriates the Circle's own communal gesture and turns it into a **plague-march** (*"ashes, ashes, we all fall down"*). It slots into the album's [[Communion vs Spectacle|communion-vs-spectacle]] axis on the **spectacle** side: the commanded, harvested echo rather than the freely-given one. There's a Babel harmonic here too — forced unison collapsing into ruin — which is genuinely new and hasn't been formalized elsewhere. A children's circle, weaponized.

## Characters

- **[[Sir Lucius]]** — the speaking voice, in full predatory-charm register; the Beast refined into strategy. He reads across the lore as a [[The Dweller on the Threshold|Dweller on the Threshold]] / Jungian shadow / lunar reflector, wearing *El Roi* on the throat and signing everything with the dagger and the [[The Green Apple|green apple]].
- **[[Roy]]** — not the voice of the song, but its true counter-pole: the one who *made the concession.* The whole dialectic the track dramatizes — sincerity vs performance, integration vs manipulation — happens between Lucius's swagger here and Roy's *"fine, I'll give you this one"* off-stage.
- **[[Mary]]** — not in the song, but her shadow falls across it: the crown Lucius resells here is the one she **revokes** in *[[#1 Champion (la Conquistadora)]]*. The divine-feminine counter-frequency his system can't absorb. (Distinct from MERCY — see the source notes.)
- **Ballerina Boy** *(→ [[Ballerina Boy]])* — the buried, vulnerable child Lucius mocks in the surrounding lore (*"when you're vulnerable you LOSE!"* — EP3, [[The Interview]]) as proof of his thesis. The un-flattened self that *Not Your Homie*'s brand-mask is built to cover. (See [[Flattened Identity]].)
- **The enforcer & the Boss** — offstage but load-bearing to the world: Lucius's incompetent bodyguard **Leif** (*"Boss, I got this"*) is part of the "child performing sovereignty" evidence, and the unseen **Boss / BMRC** he reports to keeps one question open — is Lucius's power his own, or *assigned?* (Both live in *[[The Interview]]*.)

## Symbols

- [[The Mask]] — the *homie* identity as one more face; *"take that smile, turn it upside-down"* flips the [[Happy Face]] smile into menace.
- [[Inversion]] — the upside-down smile; the bridge's mirror-flip from *love my life* to *end your life,* one word swapped.
- [[The Green Apple]] — Lucius's mark: *"knowledge that hasn't been earned; intelligence divorced from spirit; power access before spiritual maturity"* (Roy's own decode, [[Public Posts]]). In the surrounding lore he eats it loudly — a child performing sovereignty.
- [[The Banana]] — his stable trigger-prop: he plays his hits off a browning banana lifted from the set's altar fruit-bowl (this song in EP2; *[[Welcome to the Circus]]* in EP3). Read as a sacred offering rerouted to broadcast *his* voice — real light in a sterile vessel that can't pass it on.
- [[The Moon]] — Lucius's lunar pole here: reflection, spectacle, *"the moon trying to become a sun."*
- [[The Thread]] — the feeling that must not be cut; what this bargain trades away.

## Its place in the mythology

The chorus came from **outside Roy** — which is the first thing that keeps the song honest. His collaborator **MERCY** sent him a joke voice note singing the hook after getting back from a party; he knew instantly it had to be a Sir Lucius song, and called it *"the ultimate introverted extrovert anthem."* So the title and the hook were *originated by someone else, as a party joke.* That's a hard brake on any reading that treats every syllable as buried single-author cipher — real, generative collaboration is not an authored code. (More on MERCY, and why she is emphatically *not* a symbol, in the source notes.)

The shallowness, though, is engineered — and the proof is in the visual lore. In *[[The Interview]]* EP2, *"The Rise of sir lucius,"* Roy brings *"my favorite song I've ever written… The Song of Love"* — that is, **[[SOL]]** — and sings its opening. Lucius cuts him off: *"STOP! No one wants this love stuff. You know your label doesn't want to put this out, right?"* Then he works **every lever at once** — the label's verdict (name-dropping a real Big Loud hitmaker, *"Morgan Wallen, Nickelback, Florida Georgia Line… enough to have his own jet"*), the money (*"take care of everyone you love"*), the people who depend on Roy (*"Michael's about to have his first kid"*), the humiliation (*"you're 37… living at your parents' house"*) — and then, for the physical punchline, presses a **browning banana like a play button** and *Not Your Homie* comes out of it. The exchange:

> Lucius: *"this is what the people want."*
> Roy: *"it doesn't mean anything."*
> Lucius: *"exactly. It's nonsense. It's all nonsense."*
> Roy: *"Fine, I'll give you this one."*

Two things resolve in that scene. The shallowness is **intentional** — *"it's nonsense"* is Lucius's own word for his own product, said with satisfaction. And the song traded away is **SOL**, the love-song buried so the caricature can run. The spiritually catastrophic beat is not the aggression of the track. It's the concession: *I'll give you this one.* (And SOL doesn't just get buried once — it's overridden *again* in EP3, this time for *[[Welcome to the Circus]]*. Buried twice.)

Then the world proved Lucius's math. The press-named rollout — **[[LOOP]] → Not Your Homie → [[Welcome to the Circus]]** — was framed as two opposing instincts toward one realization: *"one path seeks clarity beyond the system, the other chooses to master it from within."* And *Not Your Homie* took the second path all the way to the bank. In the theatrical layer, an EP3 DJ marvels that it's *"just blowing up — I hear it's getting a major movie placement,"* and Roy, narrating his own plot in character, wrote that *"sir lucius' plan in Episode 2 worked. not your homie is flying and success is undeniable."* The song about the algorithm rewarding a caricature *succeeded on the algorithm.* You cannot separate the argument from the outcome; that's the whole grim joke.

One timeline note that keeps the song honest: *Not Your Homie* does **not** originate Lucius, and does not introduce him. The figure predates and is independent of the song — his public emergence comes a week *after* release, already wearing *not your homie* merch. What the song stages is a **persona-hardening event**: the "I" refusing false kinship and setting a guarded border. The billing changed; the figure didn't begin here.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the loop, the Thread, and whether Lucius even wants out
> **Loop, not Circle.** *Not Your Homie* doesn't just *describe* loop-consciousness, it *enacts* it — *"pretend-tend-tend-tend,"* *champion* four times, nursery recursion, viral-hook structure. In the vault's [[The Loop and the Circle|loop-vs-circle]] frame, this is the loop that *[[LOOP]]* names from the outside, heard here from the *inside*: a hypnotic construct, a memetic spell. The cure the catalogue keeps pointing to is the **Circle** (return, wholeness), not the loop (recurrence without escape) — and this track is pure orbit, the record's only "orbit"-tagged song.
>
> **The Thread — the feeling that was sacrificed.** In the [[The Thread|Ariadne/labyrinth]] frame — the labyrinth navigated by a thread, the world *"tied on a string"* — *Not Your Homie* is the bargain where **feeling itself was traded away**: SOL suppressed, vulnerability sold, spectacle chosen. The arc later recognizes that the deal was real and the wound is real — but that feeling can't be traded a second time. The Thread is the one line the whole system says must never be cut, and this is the song where it's pulled tight.
>
> **Does Lucius *want* to be integrated?** Probably yes, no, and neither — all three. One part of him craves recognition, relief, reconnection. Another fears **dissolution**: if Roy becomes whole, Lucius no longer *rules the system.* A [[The Protector|protector]] formed around trauma craves healing and sabotages it in the same breath. This is exactly why he mocks *[[Ballerina Boy]]* in the surrounding lore — *"when you're vulnerable you LOSE!"* — sneering at the very tenderness that would end his reign. The song's cruelty and the song's loneliness are the same gesture, seen from two sides.

## The comedy (because it's genuinely funny, and the funniness is the tell)

It would be a mistake to solemnize this song past the fact that it is, on purpose, *hilarious* — and the jokes are load-bearing. Lucius plays his chart-topper off a **rotting banana**, pressing a piece of decaying fruit like a Walkman. He deadpans *"might run for president, I'm so presidential"* at cartoon volume (then Roy literalized it with a *"sir lucius for president"* post and an *"I might run for president"* clip, [[Public Posts]]). He is *"John Wick with my pencil"* — a legendary assassin deflated all the way down to **office-supply violence** (*stick it in your eye*). The whole bridge is a joke with a one-word punchline: *I love my life…* beat *…I'll end your life.* And when he brands his own product, he does it **proudly**: *"exactly. It's nonsense. It's all nonsense"* — a villain taking a bow for making something that means nothing.

The rollout ran the bit at scale. The visualizer dropped captioned *"one take. no edit. sir lucius says you're all welcome for this masterpiece"* ([[Public Posts]]) — the least-authentic character in the world taking a curtain call for a throwaback. Roy scattered absurdist adjacencies through the launch (*"I identify as musk,"* *"Are NPCs real?"*). And the merch capsule sold hoodies that said **LEAVE ME ALONE** — an anti-social slogan on a garment designed to be seen in public, which is the entire *introverted extrovert* paradox stitched onto cotton.

Here's the thing the comedy is doing: a figure who needs to broadcast his greatness *this* loudly, who cues chart-toppers off produce, who brands his own work "nonsense" with a grin — this is not a villain who's winning. It's a wounded thing performing enormousness so it never has to feel small. Never joke *through* the wound underneath. But the pomp on top of it is fair game, and the song knows it.

## Source notes

- **Grounded (released music, Roy's own posts, public video).** The release facts and credits (track 11; Oct 31 2025 single; writers Roy English / MERCY / Dave Burris; produced by Jagwar Twin & Dave Burris; MERCY credited *lead vocalist*, resolving the old "does MERCY sing lead?" question). The lyric itself, including *"tame the beast… a champion"* and the *love my life → end your life* bridge. Roy's *"ultimate introverted extrovert anthem"* framing and the MERCY-voicenote origin story (X, release day). The EP2 **bargain** scene, verbatim — SOL refused, the banana pressed, *"it's nonsense,"* *"I'll give you this one"* (*[[The Interview]]* EP2, publicly released). The EP3 "movie placement" beat and Roy's in-character plot narration. The press-named [[LOOP]] → NYH → [[Welcome to the Circus]] chain. The one-take visualizer. The *LEAVE ME ALONE* merch capsule. Roy naming *Happy Face* and *Not Your Homie* in the same breath (*"if you like happy face in 2025, you'll love not your homie"*). The *El Roi* throat tattoo.
- **MERCY, on the record.** MERCY is **Mercedes Colletta Britton** — a *real, recurring collaborator and co-writer* who sang background across much of the album, and who **originated this song's hook** on a party voice note. She is **not** a symbol — not a "Pillar of Mercy," not a [[Mary]] cipher — and the sound reading treats her as exactly what she is: a co-author. The general guard the vault calls *the MERCY rule* — check the industry/biographical explanation before mythologizing a name or a credit — was written partly for this song.
- **Strong readings.** The Lucius-triumph / shadow-demanding-recognition reading (tightly coherent with the whole visual-lore frame). The two-meanings-of-nonsense contrast. The parasociality-plus-Da'at reading of *"you don't know me."* The plague-as-memetic-contagion move. The inverted [[Happy Face]] mask. The false-crown reading against *[[#1 Champion (la Conquistadora)]]*. The "tame, not kill" integration claim.
- **Framework (esoteric / syncretic — resonance, not authorial intent).** The Tree-of-Life placements (throat as Da'at; lunar Yesod; the Leo trial of *[[The Labors of Hercules]]*). The consonants-as-angles / vowels-as-breath letter-mysticism behind the "two nonsenses" idea is *tradition-correspondence,* explicitly **not** a method Roy has stated. Roy did, notably, seed the song's intellectual weather in public — naming Blavatsky on release day (*"That cruelest of tyrants — public opinion"*), and clustering Gurdjieff, Steiner, Éliphas Lévi, Alan Watts, and Israel Regardie around the launch — so the *frame* is licensed even where a specific label is the reader's.
- **How we'd be wrong.** Roy's own *"strategic nonsense"* framing *is* the song explaining itself as deliberately shallow viral bait — and if that self-aware joke is the whole point, the deeper apparatus (Da'at, Yesod, the Beast, the protector) is overlay on a track that already told you what it is. We keep that falsifier on the table. And the hook being someone else's party joke is a standing caution ([[Inherited Substrate]]) against reading deep authored intent into a collaborative fragment.
