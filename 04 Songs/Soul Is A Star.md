# Soul Is A Star

> **One-line reading.** A transmission handed down corrupted but recoverable — wound and wisdom inherited together, then called back by name — sitting at the literal center of the album with Joni Mitchell co-credited on it.

**Release:** *33* (2022), center track · **Full lyrics:** [Genius](https://genius.com) · streaming

This is the heart of *33* — the album's midpoint, and the place where the record first poses its governing question, *"do you know who you are?"*

## What happens in the song

It is two third-person portraits framed by a second-person question. The first verse: *"I met a man who had a son,"* with a humble request, *"can I sing you just one verse?"* The son is the descending soul — he *"wrote songs you never heard… gave it up before Prophets ever turned."* The second verse meets a girl carrying a poisoned inheritance — *"a bullet in her genes," "papa was a sinner playin' God behind the scenes,"* a soul who *"wanted Love but got Fame and Power from The Thieves."* The image at the center is fall-as-planting: *"we fall down as seeds on the ground."*

The chorus turns outward to the listener: *"your soul is a star… do you know who you are?"* The song reaches toward recollection — a bridge briefly states *"Know who you are"* — and then returns to the question, ending unresolved.

## Its place in the mythology

*Soul Is A Star* is the album's pivot. It poses the question at the center (*"do you know who you are?"*) that the record then works through and answers at the close, where the song *The Circle* lands on *"the inside of the inside."* The attention-tracks pull awareness outward — the forgetting; this song asks the question; the closer gives the answer.

Its core is an inheritance architecture: a transmission that arrives corrupted but recoverable, with wound and wisdom handed down together and packed into one seed — the thing that fell and the thing that will grow. The falling *is* the planting. The catalogue's default is burial over destruction: what goes into the ground waits, latent rather than dead. Pain, though, transmits more reliably than wisdom — the bullet made it into the genes, but the songs didn't make it out of the room. The pronoun turn matters: the verses are witness ("I met a man," "I met a girl"), but the chorus breaks to "do you know who you are?" — so the one addressed includes the one singing. Roy is among the buried, not the boatman above.

## Notes

What is firmly grounded is dense. Joni Mitchell is a credited co-writer, and Roy has said so in his own words — that she "gave me her blessing and is a cowriter is still mind blowing." He attributes the title phrase to the Egyptian Book of the Dead — *"behold, thy soul is a living star."* And he treats the line as a working creed, not a one-off: to a fan who heard the song at her lowest, he wrote that her soul "felt the deep truth that YOUR SOUL IS A LIVING STAR." He names *Soul Is A Star* as one of his two favorites to play live. So the song functions as the project's pastoral, darkness-to-light instrument.

The richer reading is interpretation resting on that grounded floor. The song shares a remarkable number of structural beats with Joni Mitchell's *Woodstock* — a chance meeting with a stranger, a humble request, an identity question (*"I don't know who I am"* answered by *"do you know who you are?"*), stardust meeting star, and the garden meeting the seeds. Roy has framed the second-verse girl as woven through with Joni herself — the witness who saw the child of God in 1969, the genes-and-jeans echo, the "star maker machinery" lexicon of fame-as-thief — a trail meant to be walked rather than flatly named, and read as the industry that made-and-exploited her, not a portrait of her family. One vivid claim sometimes attached to this song — that Roy used Joni's *actual voice* as a non-lyrical part — is **not verified here** and should not be stated as fact; the co-write and the borrowing are grounded, an actual-voice sample is not. The deeper frame (Da'at and gnosis, the "angels and angles" of consonants surviving while breath is lost) is interpretive lineage-reading built on the verified parallels, and the song honestly ends without resolving whether the wound or the wisdom wins.
