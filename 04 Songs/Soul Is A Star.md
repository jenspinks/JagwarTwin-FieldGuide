# Soul Is A Star

> **One-line reading.** A transmission handed down corrupted but recoverable — wound and wisdom inherited together, then called back by name — sitting at the literal center of the album, with Joni Mitchell co-credited on it and, by Roy's account, personally *blessing* it.

> [!info] Album & credits
> **Album:** *33* (2022, Big Loud Rock) — **track 5 of 10** · 3:17
> **Written by** Roy English, **Joni Mitchell**, Matt Pauling · **Produced by** Roy English & Matt Pauling
> **Interpolates** Joni Mitchell's *"Woodstock"* (*Ladies of the Canyon*, 1970) — the borrowed element is most plausibly musical, not the famous lyric
> **Full lyrics:** [Genius](https://genius.com) · streaming
> **▶ Listen:** [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eY4_OAovs1E)

This is the heart of *33* — the album's exact midpoint, and the place where the record first stops and asks the one question the whole thing is about: *"do you know who you are?"* It's also the song with the most extraordinary paper trail in the catalogue. Joni Mitchell is a credited co-writer, and Roy has told the story of how that happened, which involves a chain of friends-of-friends, a flatly-refused clearance, and a comparison to Janet Jackson. We'll get there.

## At a glance

- **Era / release** — *33* (2022), track 5 of 10; released 2022-09-30 on Big Loud Rock. Roy names it among his very favorites — *"soul is a star is my second favorite song. sometimes it flips with the circle"* — and one of the songs he loves most to play live.
- **In one line** — two third-person portraits of souls the world consumed, framed by a second-person question turned on the listener; the falling reframed as a planting.
- **Who speaks** — [[Roy]] as the witness who *"met a Man"* and *"met a girl"* — and, when the chorus turns, the one *also* being asked. The witnessing seer inside the song reads as [[Mary]]-class (the function, not a named person). The industry — *"The Thieves"* — is the unseen antagonist.
- **Key symbols** — [[The Thread]] (the lineage that never broke) · [[The Banana]] (the *"bullet in her genes,"* inherited corruption that transmits cleanly) · the Star (the soul as inner light) · the Seed (*"we fall down as seeds on the ground"*).

## The song, mapped

The song is built as two witnessed portraits with a question wedged between and around them. Following its own shape:

| Section | In the song (short) | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **Verse 1 — the Son** | *"I met a Man / Who had a Son / But he lost him to The World"* · *"my Son was a Singer / He wrote songs you never heard"* | A **descending soul** introduced at second hand. The father carries a fragment of a song the world never received. The Son *"took his life… before Prophets ever turned"* — he died before recognition arrived. |
| **The request** | *"Can I sing you just one verse? / It goes…"* | The humble ask that opens the door to the chorus. One verse is all that survives the loss — the whole transmission narrowed to a single offered fragment. |
| **Chorus** | *"Your soul is a Star / It's alive / I can see it"* · *"Oh ah ha / Do you know Who you are?"* | The **pivot of the entire song.** The witness-frame (third person) breaks into direct address (second person). The one addressed now includes the one singing. |
| **Verse 2 — the girl** | *"I met a girl / Who was a gun / Kept a bullet in her genes"* · *"Papa was a sinner / Playing God behind the scenes"* · *"She wanted Love / But she got Fame / And Power from The Thieves"* | The second descending soul — lost not by self-sacrifice but by **poisoned inheritance and fame.** The *"dark father"* here reads as the industry patriarch, the star-maker, **not a literal parent** (see below). |
| **Chorus (repeated)** | *"Your soul is a Star / Who am I to believe in?"* | The same benediction, now doubting itself — the question folded into the answer. |
| **Bridge** | *"We fall down as seeds on the ground"* (×4) | The **center image.** Both falls — the Son's, the girl's — are reframed as a *planting.* Burial, not destruction; latency, not death. |
| **Outro** | *"(Know Who you are)"* threaded through the choruses; closes on *"Do you know Who you are?"* | The answer briefly **surfaces** as a statement — *Know* who you are — then the song slips back to the question and **ends there.** It reaches toward recollection and withholds resolution. |

Two textual notes worth banking, because earlier readings got them wrong: the lyric is **"Prophets,"** not "profits" (the *profits* / devil's-bargain meaning is almost certainly a deliberate homophone Roy is happy to let you hear, but the word on the page is *Prophets*); and Spotify's odd capitalization — **Man, Son, World, Singer, Prophets, Love, Fame, Thieves, Star, Who** — is intentional and preserved throughout. This is a *"you"*-dominant song by the numbers (see [[Pronoun Audit (I-You)]]), which is exactly *33*'s other-facing gravity: the album is talking *to* someone.

**Where it sits in the album.** Track 5 of 10 is the literal hinge of *33*. The attention-songs just before it (*Pay Attention*, *Happy Face*) are the *forgetting* — awareness pulled outward. This song is the *question*, posed at dead center. And the closer, [[The Circle]] (track 10), is the *answer* — *"the inside of the inside."* Forgetting → question → answer. Its sibling nodes along that arc are [[Down to You]] (*"is it fame? is it money?"*) and [[Another Way II Heaven]] (*"One, become Two"*).

## What it means

A few claims that cut across the whole song.

**It's an inheritance architecture.** The two verses are two halves of one bequest, packed into a single seed — *the dead thing that fell and the living thing that will grow.* The Son is the fragment lost; the girl is the fragment poisoned; the bridge tells you what the falling was *for.* As the reading goes: the falling **is** the planting. The catalogue's default, everywhere, is **burial over destruction** — what goes into the ground waits. The honest word is *latent,* not *failed* (calling it "failed transmission" smuggles in a pathology the seed itself refutes).

**Pain transmits more reliably than wisdom.** The song's quietly devastating asymmetry: *the bullet made it into the genes, but the songs didn't make it out of the room.* The wound inherits cleanly — *"a bullet in her genes"* — while the Son's actual gift, the *"songs you never heard,"* never crossed. Whether the song is *fighting* that asymmetry or *accepting* it is left genuinely open, and the ending (on the question, not the answer) is the tell that it doesn't settle.

**The pronoun turn is the whole move.** The verses are witness — *"I met a Man," "I met a girl"* — and then the chorus wheels around to *"do you know who you are?"* The question goes outward and inward in the same breath, which means Roy isn't the ferryman standing above the river naming the drowned. He's **among the buried** — one of the seeds, not the boatman. (There's a human floor under that: a typed note in Roy's own hand about a song he was *"really proud of… before being dropped by my record label,"* recognized as special *from the inside* even without outside validation. Handle that warmly; it's the wound the cleverness orbits.)

**The Star is relocated, on purpose.** Two tracks earlier, [[Pay Attention (feat. little luna)]] sells the fame-gospel — *stars are born from light,* the *A Star Is Born* machine, in the tempter's mouth. Here the correction arrives: *your **soul** is a star.* The star is moved off the marquee and back inside the person. That relocation is the same move the whole catalogue keeps making — the external star fails or betrays (*"stars out of place," "looked down at my stars," "stars conspired"*) and the true star turns out to be interior.

**The Joni thread is real, and it is load-bearing.** This is the part with a paper trail — and it's rich enough to have its own concept page, [[Joni and the Prophet Lineage]]. The short version below; the welding, the industry lexicon, and the enacted-repair reading are in the Advanced sections.

## The Joni Mitchell of it all

Here is what is *grounded,* in Roy's own delighted words: *"the fact that JONI MITCHELL gave me her blessing and is a cowriter is still mind blowing."* Not a sample cleared by lawyers and forgotten — a blessing, named by the artist, called mind-blowing by him. The specific Joni song behind the credit is **"Woodstock"** (*Ladies of the Canyon*, 1970), a song this one shares an uncanny number of beats with.

And the story of *how* the credit happened is one of the best in the catalogue. Roy has told it on record: Joni had pulled her whole catalogue off Spotify; the labels and publishers wouldn't even *ask* — *"There's no way — Joni Mitchell doesn't allow anybody to use samples or interpolations, we're not even going to ask."* Roy's response, roughly: *forget that — if Joni Mitchell hears this song, she will understand it, and she'll know it.* So he went around the entire industry. *"I found a friend who knew a friend who knew a friend who knew Joni Mitchell,"* got the song to her, and she *loved it and signed off easily.* He wrote her a note. Asked how rare that is — the interviewer pointing out she'd never do it as a *favor* — Roy delivered the flex of the decade: *"There are two people who have done it — me and Janet Jackson."* The reductive press tag *"Roy is a millennial Joni Mitchell"* gets quietly retired here: it turns out to be a real, chosen lineage rather than a lazy vibe-comparison, and the actual co-write is so improbable it stops being a joke.

One vivid claim you'll see attached to this song — that Roy used Joni's *actual voice* as a wordless, non-lyrical part — is **not verified here** and should not be stated as fact. What's grounded is the co-write and a borrowing; in the on-record telling Roy says he *"used this piece of music from Joni Mitchell,"* which is consistent with a cleared *musical* interpolation, not a vocal one. A lyric check confirms the song does **not** reproduce *Woodstock*'s famous lines (*"we are stardust… back to the garden"*) — the closest echo is the bridge's *"we fall down as seeds on the ground,"* a seed/garden rhyme, not a quotation. So: the borrowing is real; the *what* of it is most plausibly melodic; the actual-voice version is a beautiful reading resting on an open question, held as such.

There's a bittersweet coda Roy offered in the same interview, and it's a rare on-record statement of the exact industry-vs-art tension the song is *about:* it *"didn't really get a push — it doesn't fit a genre… if it doesn't fit a Spotify playlist genre, there's nowhere to put it and it goes unnoticed. But that song will have a life somewhere. It was such an important song to write, and I'm glad it exists."*

## Characters

- [[Roy]] — the witness who meets the Man and the girl, and, when the chorus turns, the one *also* being asked. Among the buried, not the boatman above.
- [[Mary]] — the witnessing **function** (the seer who *"came upon the child of God"* and still sees the soul *"after the world took him"*). A function, not a named figure in the lyric, and distinct from Roy's biographical mother — a *channel,* not the function.
- **The Man / the father** — sings *"just one verse,"* carrying a fragment of a song the world never received.
- **The Son / the Singer** — the descending soul; *"wrote songs you never heard"*; died *"before Prophets ever turned."* An **archetype** — the soul that descends into matter and is lost — not one biographical person.
- **The girl** — verse 2's soul lost through fame and poisoned inheritance. Breadcrumbed toward Joni herself as the song's non-substitutable witness (see Advanced reading), never a portrait of anyone's family.
- **The dark father** (*"Papa… playin' God behind the scenes"*) — read as the **star-maker / industry patriarch**, the one who made-and-exploited her — *not* a literal parent, and not Roy's mother.
- **[[The Crowd]] / The Thieves** — the fame-machine that hands out *"Fame and Power"* in place of *"Love."*

## Symbols

- The **Star** — the soul as a celestial body; relocated from celebrity to interior light. Roy sources the title to the **Egyptian Book of the Dead**, as he renders it: *"behold, thy soul is a living star."* (That phrasing tracks older translations; the attribution is his framing, and he treats it as a working creed — three weeks after the co-write message, to a fan who found the song at her lowest, he wrote that her soul *"felt the deep truth that YOUR SOUL IS A LIVING STAR."*)
- The **Seed** — *"we fall down as seeds on the ground."* Burial over destruction; the falling *is* the planting; latency, not death.
- The **Bullet** (*"bullet in her genes"*) — the ancestral-wound node; inherited corruption that transmits cleanly. It bridges to Joni by a homophone — *genes / jeans* — and lives on the catalogue's [[The Banana]] thread (real light in a sterile vessel: you can hear the song, you cannot inherit it).
- [[The Thread]] — the living strand of a lineage that never broke, tied to the inheritance that fell silent; the buried song **called back by name** rather than retrieved.
- The **Garden** — arriving through the *Woodstock* echo (she supplies *"back to the garden,"* he supplies the seeds that fall into it); Eden relocated inside (cf. [[Imagine A World]]).

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the *Woodstock* weld (the parallel inventory)
> The reading rests on a spine: *Soul Is A Star* rhymes with Joni's *"Woodstock"* beat for beat. Both lines exist on their own sides (that's grounded); that they *rhyme* is the interpretation. Joni on the left, this song on the right:
> - **Chance meeting with a stranger** — *"came upon a child of God"* ↔ *"I met a Man who had a Son"*
> - **The humble request** — *"can I walk beside you?"* ↔ *"can I sing you just one verse?"*
> - **The identity question / the mirror** — *"I don't know who I am"* ↔ *"do you know who you are?"*
> - **Stardust → star** — *"we are stardust"* ↔ *"your soul is a Star"*
> - **Garden → seeds** — *"back to the garden"* ↔ *"we fall down as seeds on the ground"* (he supplies the **Fall**; she supplies the **destination**)
> - **The bargain / trap** — *"caught in the devil's bargain"* ↔ *"gave it up before Prophets ever turned"* (the *Prophets* / profits homophone landing on Joni's own fame-bargain note)
>
> One fact worth banking: *"caught in the devil's bargain"* is **Joni's own published line** — so its echo in Roy's *"devil's bargain"* register is a borrowing from *her,* not from any cover. Two ends of one arc: *Woodstock* is the boy at dawn walking to *"get my soul free"*; this song is the same soul *after* the world took him. The fuller weld — the *genes/jeans* hinge, *"The Thieves"* as Joni's documented industry-as-thief lexicon (*"star maker machinery"*), the girl-carries-Joni breadcrumb trail, the *For Free* inversion where the son is the busker who *refused* the bargain — lives on [[Joni and the Prophet Lineage]], the page built to hold exactly this.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the interpolation as enacted repair (rests on an open question)
> This is the most beautiful reading available on the song, and it stands or falls on one unconfirmed fact: **is Joni's actual voice on the recording?** *If* Roy secured a gratuitous, non-lyrical Joni element the song didn't musically *need,* then the form becomes proof of concept — the song that **failed** to transmit (the Son's *"songs you never heard"*) healed by one that **succeeded** (*Woodstock,* audible for ~50 years). The lore enacted in reality, just sitting there on a streaming service. The honest brake: the co-write is the only hard floor. The on-record account says *"a piece of music,"* not *"her voice"* — so if the borrow is purely melodic, this climax collapses to a lovely idea. That he did it *in order to* enact the healing is intent — the strongest of strong reads, never his stated word — and the *"such an important song to write, and I'm glad it exists"* line partially grounds the *transmission* motive without confirming the voice claim. The witness-function is the reason a synthesized voice wouldn't do: Joni is the *"I"* who *"came upon the child of God"* in 1969, placed bodily inside the later song, still singing *"I can see it."* (This is the [[Mary]] / [[Source vs Reflection|witnessing]] function made literal — and it collapses the instant the voice isn't actually hers.)

> [!note]- Advanced reading — Da'at, gnosis, and *"angels and angles"* (the recognition-model)
> Under the transmission sits a recognition-model, and it reframes the Joni act away from *"her voice ferries a failed message across."* The cleaner frame is **anamnesis** — recovery by *remembrance,* not retrieval. The buried inheritance isn't *failed;* it's **latent,** and the song that still remembered itself *calls the buried one back by name.* In the esoteric register this is **Da'at** — the crossing-point where wisdom becomes lived experience, the Hebrew threshold that opens into Greek *gnosis* (the two are the same word: *knowledge*). Not the lost retrieved, but the forgotten *recognized.* A second compression carries the asymmetry: **"angels and angles."** Read through Kabbalistic letter-mysticism (see [[The Angels and the Angles]]), the **consonants** are the fixed architecture of a word — they descend intact, the way a wound inherits easily — while the **breath, the vowels,** are what's lost. *Pain transmits; wisdom doesn't; the bullet made it into the genes, the songs didn't make it out of the room.* **Two firewalls.** First: the angels/angles + consonants/vowels framework has **no public source** — it's an interpretive lens the work rhymes with, never a stated method. Second: that Roy himself uses the words *"Da'at"* or *"gnosis"* is **unverified** — keep those terms in the analyst's register, not his mouth.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — masculine/feminine descent, and the Sun/Son homophone
> The two verses split by polarity: the **Son** is the soul lost through *self-sacrifice*; the **girl** is the soul lost through *fame and poisoned inheritance* — a Hermetic gender polarity that feeds the catalogue's [[Duality]] (heart/mind). The descending soul is **every** soul — child of God = the Son = Jesus = Mozart = all of us, held at archetype, not biography. There's a homophone hiding under the release, too: on Roy's public *"I'm 33"* board the verse is drafted as *"who had a **Sun** / my **Sun** was a singer,"* cohering with the board's own gloss (*the sun = manifesting energy / externalized Life Force*) and the *Tracks* title *"Sun of God."* The released word is **Son** — record the board's *Sun* as an artifact detail; don't "correct" the recording. (A further *Christ / Logos / Sun* verse-annotation sometimes cited from the board is **not** in the vault's capture — pending, not asserted.)

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the tension the song refuses to resolve
> Two poles pull on the whole song, and it never picks one. The **Rosicrucian** pole says the world is *out of tune,* not evil — so the work is to re-tune, recover, repair (→ the song is *fighting* the asymmetry). The **Gnostic** pole (see [[Gnosticism - The World as Trap]]) says the corruption is *structural* — the parasitic loop, the bullet in the genes, the sterile [[The Banana|banana]] — so recovery is only ever partial (→ the song is *accepting* it). The corrected outro — ending on the *question* — confirms it doesn't settle. The Son still died; the girl was still consumed; the witnessing **sees** the soul but does not **save** the man. That refusal to resolve is the most honest thing in the song. (One more esoteric coordinate Roy has publicly engaged: **Steiner**'s redemptive *"illusion is a revelation"* is the optimistic pole this tension leans against.)

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the pre-album roots, and the "one song" thesis
> Two threads worth keeping. First, the father/son material predates the album: a 2021 poem Roy posted publicly carries *"A Darkness from my fathers Side / From long before he earthed"* and *"A stars someone that tells a truth / For better or for worse"* — the star/truth complex and the inherited-shadow already articulated a year before *33.* Second, the *"there's only one song"* thesis: in his Manifesto material Roy says *"the world needs music, but the world doesn't need more songs. There's only one song. I'm still learning how to write it."* Read through [[The Circle]], this song is one node in a catalogue that keeps trying to re-transmit that one song — and the Joni *"fifty-year transmission"* is another. A board aphorism glosses the bridge from the same angle: *"maybe a planet is one star's way of becoming another star"* — the seed/star/planet cycle stated as a board theory, rhyming with *"we fall down as seeds on the ground"* (a substrate echo, not proof the lyric points at the cell).
