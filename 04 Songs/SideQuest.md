# SideQuest

> **One-line reading.** A comic heartbreak song about false centrality — the speaker realizes he built his whole emotional plot around someone who never cast him as the main character, and the song lets him be both genuinely hurt and a little ridiculous about it.

**Release:** *SideQuest* EP, 2025 (Big Loud Records) · **Full lyrics:** [Genius](https://genius.com) · streaming

## What happens in the song

Game vocabulary structures the whole thing: *"side quest"* versus *"main one,"* *"you were playing a game,"* *"Baldur's Gate,"* *"you played me well."* The asymmetry is stated plainly — *"you didn't love me like I loved you"* — and so is the cost: *"sent me in the wrong direction," "wasted all my time."* The self-reference is a deflated swagger: *"I'm the biggest cat that you've seen thus far / Jaguar Swaguar 'til you broke my heart."* And the lightness keeps coming through wordplay — the *"s-s-s-side quest"* stutter, the astrology jokes (*"I should've seen the signs… you're a Libra"*).

## Its place in the mythology

The spine is false protagonist, not demotion. The hook is *"thought I was your main one"* — not *I was.* The song isn't about losing a status he held; it's about discovering his assumed centrality was never real. He mistook optionality for destiny. That's the characteristic wound here: not *you wronged the main character*, but *you didn't know what story you were in* — and the song implicates his own assumption, never proving he was entitled to the lead.

So *"you played me well"* is partly an epistemological gap, not only manipulation: the other person understood the game structure and he didn't, which is why *"I never saw it coming"* repeats. The shock is a map correction — he thought he was moving toward the center; he was being routed through optional content.

That makes SideQuest a personal, comic miniature of one of the project's recurring concerns: misorientation, false geometry, building a life around a false center (see [[Source vs Reflection]]). Rendered intimate and funny rather than cosmic — and it doesn't need to be about Lucius to matter. The deception-versus-asymmetry question is held open, which is emotionally true: after betrayal, unequal love feels indistinguishable from being played.

## Notes

The humor is survival, not proof of shallowness — the song metabolizes humiliation through wordplay, and *"I should've seen the signs"* turns the accusation inward. He knows the wound is real and a little humiliating at once, which lets him be hurt without being automatically right. One spelling detail is interpretively load-bearing and unresolved: *"Jaguar Swaguar"* (the animal, the proud cat humbled) versus a reported *"Jagwar Swagwar"* (the project-name pun, the act itself getting its heart broken).
