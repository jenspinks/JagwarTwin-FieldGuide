# SideQuest

> **One-line reading.** A comic heartbreak song about false centrality: the speaker built his whole emotional plot around someone who never cast him as the main character — and the song lets him be genuinely hurt *and* a little ridiculous about it at the same time.

> [!info] Album & credits
> **Release:** *SideQuest*, released **March 28, 2025** (Big Loud Records) · programmed & sung by **Jagwar Twin**
> **Written by** Roy English, Andrew Goldstein & Michael Morgan · **Produced by** Jagwar Twin & Ryan Daly
> **Mixed/mastered** by Ryan Daly (engineer) with Dale Becker et al. — Roy says the record is tuned to **444 Hz** and scored on a bespoke *"medieval synth"* by teenage engineering.
> **Full lyrics:** [Genius](https://genius.com) · streaming

This is the funniest heartbreak song in the catalogue, and one of the most disciplined. It takes a whole relationship, files it under *optional content*, and scores the humiliation with RPG vocabulary and astrology jokes. But under the swagger it's doing something the project does constantly — just, for once, at human scale and played for laughs instead of cosmos.

## At a glance

- **Era / release** — March 2025, the medieval *"Jagwar World"* / *"Realm"* rollout era (the [[dumbledore]] EP preceded it in Feb; [[MAGIK]] rides alongside). Heavy paratext, sword emojis, *"a loyal servant of the Realm."*
- **In one line** — He thought he was the main quest. He was the side quest.
- **Who speaks** — [[Roy]] at his most vulnerable and most comedic. Not the seer, not the shadow — just Roy, wounded and cracking jokes to survive it.
- **Key symbols** — [[The Jaguar]] (deflated) · the side quest / main quest (RPG geometry) · the zebra (black-and-white thinking) · astrology / *"the signs."*

## The song, mapped

Verse–chorus pop-breakup built entirely on game/RPG vocabulary. Following its own section tags (Spotify order):

| Section | Short excerpt | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **Intro** | *"(You a little baby)"* · *"(I was the side quest?)"* | Two parenthetical asides set the taunt/self-mock frame before a single full line lands. (A YouTube transcription hears the first as *"(You are little baby)"* — unresolved.) |
| **Chorus** | *"Thought I was your main one, but I was the (side quest)"* | States the thesis. Note the word: **thought**, not *was*. The asymmetry follows plainly — *"you didn't love me like I loved you"* — and it exits on the signature *"s-s-s-side quest"* stutter, the hook literally tripping over itself. |
| **Verse 1** | *"Baldur's Gate, you played me well, I never saw it coming"* | The grievance in questions (*"how many times did you walk away?"*) plus the game-reveal (*"I never knew you would play the game"*). Introduces the recurring couplet and closes on the load-bearing cost: *"sent me in the wrong direction, wasted all my time."* |
| **Pre-chorus** | *"Thought I was your main one, but I was the (side quest)."* | Single-line hinge — the thesis, restated bare. |
| **Chorus (2nd)** | *"Hey, engagement (side quest)"* | Repeats the thesis and adds the one outlier: **engagement** — the lone platform/algorithm word in an otherwise-intimate lyric. |
| **Verse 2** | *"The biggest cat that you've seen this far / Jaguar Swaguar, 'til you broke my heart"* | The **deflated-Jaguar verse**: persona inflation, punctured mid-line. Then the astrology wordplay (*"I should've seen the signs… you're a Libra / nothing's black and white… you're a zebra"*), then it **recycles Verse 1's back half verbatim** — same *"Baldur's Gate"* couplet, same *"wrong direction."* |
| **Chorus (final)** | *"…whoa (side quest)"* | Full restatement with the *"whoa"* variant. |
| **Outro** | *"Thought I was your main one, but I was the-"* | Cuts off mid-sentence. Twice. The thesis is **never completed** — the song formally enacts its own interrupted, optional-content status. |

**Three argument moves.** (1) State the false-centrality thesis [choruses]; (2) narrate the betrayal / game-reveal and the wrong-direction cost [verses, via the shared *"Baldur's Gate"* couplet]; (3) puncture his own persona and turn the blame inward through comedy [Verse 2: the Jaguar, Libra/zebra, *"I should've seen the signs"*].

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the sing-back production layer
> The recording carries its own crowd. Parenthetical background-echoes — *(side quest)*, *(my best)*, *(all my time)* — have the record answer its own claim back to itself. It's one instance of a project-wide production device: the mix stages a chorus of voices singing the hook back, the same trick heard across *Long Time Coming*, *Loser*, *Pay Attention*, *I Like to Party*, *City of Angels* and others. Here the effect is quietly cruel: the crowd he thought he was central to is now echoing his demotion.

## What it means

A few claims that cut across the whole song.

- **False protagonist, not demotion.** This is the interpretive engine. The hook is *"thought I was your main one"* — not *I was.* The song isn't about losing a status he held; it's about discovering his **assumed** centrality was never real. He mistook optionality for destiny — and crucially, the song implicates his *own* assumption. It never proves he was entitled to the lead. The wound isn't *you wronged the main character*; it's *you didn't know what story you were even in.*
- **"You played me well" is an epistemological gap, not only manipulation.** If he was never the main one, being *played* also means: *you understood the game structure and I didn't.* The other person knew where he fit; he didn't — which is exactly why *"I never saw it coming"* keeps repeating. The shock is a **map correction.** He thought he was moving toward the center; he was being routed through optional content the whole time.
- **The false center is the point.** *"Sent me in the wrong direction"* is load-bearing: the grievance isn't only lost love, it's having oriented a life around a **false center.** That's the catalogue's recurring concern — misorientation, false geometry, the wrong god / wrong audience / wrong mirror (see [[Source vs Reflection]]) — rendered personal and comic instead of cosmic. It does **not** need to be about Lucius to matter.
- **Deception vs. asymmetry, held open on purpose.** *"You didn't love me like I loved you"* (asymmetry, no malice) sits right against *"underneath your spell"* / *"you would play the game"* (manipulation). The song refuses to settle which it was — and that refusal is emotionally true, because after a betrayal, unequal love feels indistinguishable from being played. The tension **is** the song; don't resolve it.
- **The comedy lets him be hurt without being automatically right.** The *"s-s-s-side quest"* stutter, *"Jaguar Swaguar,"* the Libra/zebra dig — the lightness is a **coping mechanism, not proof of shallowness.** The song metabolizes humiliation through wordplay. And *"I should've seen the signs"* turns the accusation **inward** — hindsight shame, not just accusation. That's the tonal key: he knows the wound is real *and* a little embarrassing at once, which is what keeps him sympathetic instead of self-righteous.

## Characters

- **[[Roy|Jagwar Twin]]** — the sincere, wounded, *funny* Roy-register: vulnerable, not solemn. The narrator is Roy at human scale, not the seer who sees everything.
- **The deceiver / "you"** — a **human lover.** Explicitly *not* Lucius and *not* the Circus. Roy names her publicly only as *"the girl who SideQuested me,"* thanks her rather than attacks her, and insists she's *"an amazing person and not a Libra."* She stays un-named and un-investigated here — that's the point of how he framed it.
- **[[Michael Morgan]]** — a credited co-writer *and*, per the release post, *"the inspiration."* The person who prompted the song — distinct from the romantic subject. (Two different people behind the credit: the muse who sparked it, and the ex it's about.)
- **[[Sir Lucius]]** — **deliberately absent.** SideQuest is the vault's canonical example of a song that carries *no Lucius mapping* — the counter-example that keeps the Heart/Mind frame honest by proving it isn't force-fit onto every track. (More below.)

## Symbols

- **[[The Jaguar]] — deflated.** *"The biggest cat that you've seen this far / Jaguar Swaguar, 'til you broke my heart"*: the project's emblem of seeing and consciousness, brought down to human, wounded scale — still swaggering, but humbled enough to rhyme through the embarrassment. This is the catalogue's rare **comic Jaguar**, and it's valuable precisely because it keeps the symbol from being *only* grand.
- **The side quest / main quest (RPG geometry).** The governing metaphor: optional content versus the main storyline; false centrality; being routed away from the center. *"Baldur's Gate"* names the game outright.
- **The zebra.** Black-and-white thinking — Roy's own gloss (below) makes this explicit. The subject reduced to binary vision, and convenient rhyme-bait for Libra.
- **Astrology / "the signs."** Doubles as (a) the zodiac (Libra) and (b) omens he *"should've seen"* — a hindsight-shame pun. Autobiographically inverted: Roy is a Gemini *"who identifies as a Libra."*
- **"Underneath your spell."** Light enchantment/magik imagery, kept intimate — romantic thrall, not cosmic entrapment.
- **"Wrong direction."** A faint maze/labyrinth brush (see [[The Labyrinth]]) — but emotional misdirection, not spiritual entrapment.
- **The Realm / medieval Jagwar World + the sword ⚔️.** Not in the lyric — the *campaign skin.* The rollout's paratextual symbol-set (*"a loyal servant of the Realm"*), scored by that teenage-engineering *"medieval synth."*
- **444 Hz.** Roy says every JT song, this one included, is tuned to the *"healing frequency"* — a production-level symbol of intended benevolence quietly running under a breakup song.

## The joke, and the medicine

Two things worth holding side by side, because together they're the whole personality of the release.

**The joke is autobiographical inversion.** The astrology dig has no astrological content whatsoever — Roy admits in the release post that *"no other sign rhymes with zebra,"* and that the subject isn't actually a Libra at all. He then re-inflates the humbled cat with a wink: *"Jagwars eat zebra for breakfast."* On TikTok he ties himself directly to the song's gag — *"I'm a Gemini, but I identify as a Libra"* — and on Discord doubles down: *"i go by 12 signs."* The comedy runs cleanest as **maturity-cliché-turned-insult**: *the world isn't black and white* becomes *YOU are the black-and-white one* — you're the zebra.

**The medicine is the campaign inverting the song's own premise.** The lyric is the wound; the rollout is the cure. Where the song says *I was the side quest*, Roy spends weeks telling the audience the exact opposite: *"You're nobody's sidequest"* / *"youre no ones sidequest!!"* — and, in a perfect live beat, fires that off on Discord and immediately catches his own over-earnestness: *"oh shit haha."* He also universalizes it into a life-philosophy, the opposite valence of the lyric: *"Whole lifes been a series of SideQuests"* / *"ive been on so many sidequests to get here… so grateful for all the lessons."* The song files a relationship under optional content; the campaign reframes optional content as the whole point of the journey.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — Roy's own gloss turns the breakup into doctrine
> On Discord (April 2025) Roy re-reads the song's wound as a Heart/Mind reflection principle, converting a breakup track into a magik/manifestation teaching: *"if you think you're the sidequest youll be the sidequest"*; *"people come along to reflect who we think we are back to us"*; *"the way you think about yourself will create everything around you."* On this reading the false-centrality was **self-authored** — the geometry was never imposed from outside; he wrote himself into the wrong role. That's Roy's own authorial framing, and it dovetails exactly with why [[Source vs Reflection]] is the primary connection: *the mirror throws back the story you brought to it.* (He also logged an [[Alan Watts]] line as the release's epigraph — *"the attitude of faith is to let go, and become open to truth, whatever it might turn out to be"* — which grounds Watts as a named influence and threads the same *let-go / you-are-it* doctrine through the whole [[MAGIK]] era.)

> [!note]- Advanced reading — resonance vs. allegory (the hard guard)
> The game-world / role vocabulary genuinely resonates with the catalogue's interest in systems, roles, illusion and agency — but here it's **structural, not allegorical.** The deceiver is a human lover, not Lucius and not the Circus. *"Wrong direction"* lightly brushes the maze vocabulary of [[The Labyrinth]], but stays intimate: emotional misdirection, not spiritual entrapment. The one possible seam to the wider *Circus / Algorithm* system is the outlier word *"engagement"* in the second chorus — the single systemic term in an otherwise-personal song. Read it as a whisper, not a thesis; whether it's the platform word, a relationship-engagement pun, or a mishearing is unresolved. The discipline of this page is to *not* over-build the cosmic reading onto a song that is, on its surface and by design, about getting friend-zoned by an RPG metaphor.

## Source notes

- **Grounded (Roy's own words, released music, public posts).** The March 28, 2025 release on Big Loud Records; the credits (writers Roy English / Andrew Goldstein / Michael Morgan; producers Jagwar Twin & Ryan Daly), Spotify-verified; the *"visualizer"* on YouTube. The **keystone paratext is Roy's Instagram release post**, which supplies almost everything unique here: the thanks to *"the girl who SideQuested me,"* the zebra/Libra explanation (*"no other sign rhymes with zebra… Jagwars eat zebra for breakfast"*), the 444 Hz tuning claim, the teenage-engineering *"medieval synth,"* Michael Morgan as *"the inspiration,"* and the gratitude framing (*"you gave me a gift"*). Corroborated across the TikTok caption run (Mar 12 → Apr 8, incl. *"I'm a Gemini, but I identify as a Libra"* and *"You're nobody's sidequest"*), the public Discord #the-circle channel (Roy's reflection-doctrine quotes and the pre-release timeline), and the Alan Watts / *"loyal servant of the Realm"* reel.
- **Strong readings.** False protagonist rather than demotion; *"you played me well"* as an epistemological gap; the deflated Jaguar; humor-as-survival and the inward turn of *"I should've seen the signs"*; the deliberate open-holding of deception-vs-asymmetry.
- **Resonance (tradition / interpretation).** The RPG vocabulary's brush against the project's systems/roles/illusion themes and the [[The Labyrinth]] maze imagery — flagged as structural resonance, never as a claim that this is a Lucius or Circus song.
- **How we'd be wrong.** If *"engagement"* turns out to be a mishearing or a plain relationship pun, the one systemic-seam reading dissolves — kept as an open thread, not a load-bearing claim. And the real ex stays un-named and un-investigated by design: Roy identifies her only as *"the girl who SideQuested me"* and stresses she's *"an amazing person."*

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the open questions and the text history
> **Is it a single or an EP?** The vault's internal Master Source List catalogues *SideQuest* as a **5-track EP** (Shazam reported the title track as *"Track 1"*), but every analysis treats it as one song, and the other four tracks are **never named anywhere** — an unresolved gap. Whether any of those tracks relates to the [[Lucius Lullaby (album)|Lucius Lullaby]] arc is an internal open question, not established lore. **The intro asides** — *"(You a little baby)"* / *"(I was the side quest?)"* — could be taunt, sampled aside, comic insult, or phonetic artifact; a YouTube transcription hears *"(You are little baby),"* so even the wording isn't settled. **The text itself was corrected against Spotify (2026):** the canonical readings are *"Jaguar Swaguar"* (the animal) and *"the biggest cat that you've seen **this** far"* — superseding an earlier *"Jagwar Swagwar / thus far"* variant, and a phantom line (*"playing games with my head"*) that isn't in the released song at all. So the old *"is it the animal or the project-name pun?"* question is now closed to **the animal.**
