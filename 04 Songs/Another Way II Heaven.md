# Another Way II Heaven

> **One-line reading.** A first-person creation myth: a solitary "I," unable to know itself alone, speaks itself into two, births a mirror, then a Darkness and a Light whose collision makes a Sun — multiplying toward eleven, reunion promised and escape quietly forbidden.

> **Lyrics:** [Genius](https://genius.com) · streaming

The creation myth *underneath* *33*'s attention-economy critique — one of the core mythology texts in the catalogue. Roy wrote this cosmogony **twice**: the lyric is near-identical to a standalone poem on his public "I'm 33" board, so the creation-myth reading isn't a listener's projection — it's his own stated cosmology.

## The song is a count — One through Eleven

The song maps itself: it's a creation sequence told as a count, each number a stage of the One dividing in order to know itself. Following its own numbers:

| # | In the song | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | *"I was just me… but who was 'I' to know?"* | The solitary One. The problem is **sight** — it can't know itself alone (*"I wanted to see myself"*). |
| **2** | *"'One, become Two'… 'I' was in reverse… 'I' became 'You'"* | Division as self-knowledge. The **mirror is born**: "You" is "I" reversed. |
| **2 →** | *"a Darkness and a Light… a Sun was born inside"* | The first dyad collides and **ignites a Sun** — the union of opposites *makes* light. |
| **(3)** | *— skipped —* | Unnumbered. Possibly the Sun itself: the child of the union, not a division. |
| **4** | *"next there was 4, 'Process' and 'Time'"* · *"I opened up a Door"* | A structured world begins; a **door** opens and the self releases *"to cover the sky."* |
| **5–8** | *"split into Five, Six, Seven, Eight"* | Multiplication outward — the many proliferate. |
| **9** | *"when it came to Nine… I had to remind myself that 'I' was still 'I'"* | The point of **self-forgetting** — the one moment the self nearly loses itself in the many. |
| **10–11** | *"from 1:00 and through 11:00"* | The count is revealed as a **clock**; it runs to eleven and stops. |
| **(12)** | *— the missing hour —* | The unreached twelve loops back to One: the **deferred reunion**, *"one day we'll get back together and see The Great Design."* |

The refrain holds it all in place: *"I see it different every Time — another way to Heaven."* The song never arrives; it keeps **seeing differently.**

## What it means

A few claims that cut across the whole count:

- **The title *is* the argument.** *"To Heaven"* becomes *"II Heaven,"* the Roman two: the path *to* heaven is the path *of the two.* Multiplicity isn't the fall from unity — it's the route.
- **The founding dyad is the project's dyad.** *"A Darkness and a Light"* whose union makes a Sun is heart and mind, [[Roy]] and [[Sir Lucius]], placed at the very origin — and their meeting *generates* light rather than opposing the dark.
- **The captor's underside.** *"You'll never get away… don't you ever try to get away."* The One that divided itself also **forbids escape** — a closed system, not a free dispersal. This is where the cosmogony touches the [[Sir Lucius|Lucius]] closed-loop.
- **It's the charter for everything else.** Mirrors, doubles, twins, loops, fragmented selves, reunion — the whole JT vocabulary gets its **generating logic** here: unity divided itself to know itself, and every later song is a variation on that first act.

> [!note]- Deeper readings — the ninth station, the missing twelve, the Kabbalah *(tap to open)*
> **Nine as the "Lucius error."** In a Tree-of-Life frame the ninth station (Yesod) is where the self most risks mistaking the channel for the source. The lyric's lone moment of threatened self-loss landing exactly at nine is strikingly coherent with that. The song never says "Yesod"; the mapping is the reader's — but Kabbalah is demonstrably in Roy's toolkit (it's on the "I'm 33" board), so the *frame* is licensed even where the specific label is interpretive.
>
> **The missing twelve.** A clock has twelve; the count stops at eleven. The unreached twelve, which loops back to one, is the return — emanation runs 1→11, and 12→1 is deferred to *"The Great Design."* (One-through-eleven also reads as the ten Sefirot plus Da'at, the hidden eleventh.)
>
> **Emanation and return.** Read alongside Lurianic Kabbalah, the whole shape is procession-and-return: the One goes out into the many and is promised back.
