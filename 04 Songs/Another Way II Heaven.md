# Another Way II Heaven

> **One-line reading.** A first-person creation myth: a solitary "I," unable to know itself alone, speaks itself into two, births a mirror, a Darkness and a Light whose collision makes a Sun, then multiplies toward eleven — with reunion promised and escape quietly forbidden.

**Release:** *33* (2022) · **Full lyrics:** [Genius](https://genius.com) · streaming

This is Roy's solo-authored cosmology — the creation myth underneath the album's attention-economy critique, and one of the core mythology texts in the whole catalogue.

## What happens in the song

It begins in solitude that can't know itself: *"back long ago, I was just me, but who was 'I' to know?"* The motive is sight — *"I wanted to see myself."* Creation is a speech-act of division: *"I spoke the words 'One, become Two,' then 'I' was in reverse,"* after which *"'I' could see my Self, and 'I' became 'You'."* The new pair dances — *"a Darkness and a Light"* — and *"when we crashed and came together, a Sun was born inside."*

Then a count: one, two, no three, then *"next there was 4, 'Process' and 'Time'," then *"split into Five, Six, Seven, Eight, but when it came to Nine… I had to remind myself that 'I' was still 'I'."* Reunion is promised — *"one day we'll get back together and see The Great Design"* — and the refrain keeps perspective primary: *"I see it different every Time, another way to Heaven."* Underneath runs a captor's ad-lib: *"(you'll never get away)," and the close, *"don't you ever try to get away."*

## Its place in the mythology

This song is the metaphysical charter of the wider world. Mirrors, doubles, twins, loops, fragmented identities, reunification — the recurring JT vocabulary is given its generating logic here: unity divided itself in order to know itself, and everything after is a variation on that first act. The title encodes the argument — *"to Heaven"* becomes *"II Heaven,"* the Roman two, because the path *to* heaven is the path *of the two*; multiplicity is itself the route.

The dyad the whole project runs on — heart and mind, Roy and Lucius — is placed here at the origin, and the union of opposites generates a new luminous center. The mirror motif originates here too: *"One, become Two"* yields *"'I' was in reverse,"* a reversed self, before *"I became You." And the myth has a captor's underside. The One that divided itself also forbids escape — a closed system, multiplicity held inside the design rather than freely dispersing, which links to the Lucius closed-loop material.

## Notes

The creation-myth reading is unusually well-grounded, because Roy wrote this cosmogony twice. The lyric is near-identical to a standalone poem on his public "I'm 33" board — *"I spoke the words 'One become Two'… A Darkness and a Light… A Sun was born inside… Next there were 4, Process & Time… see The Great Design."* So this isn't a reader's projection onto a pop song; it is Roy's own intentional cosmology, composed in two forms, sitting inside the board's larger system of correspondences. The captor's ad-lib is the one thing the song adds that the board poem lacks.

The specific esoteric label-mappings stay interpretive. In a Tree-of-Life frame the ninth station is where the self most risks mistaking the channel for the source — the "Lucius error" — and the count's threatened self-loss landing exactly at nine is strikingly coherent with that; the song never says so. The numbers also render as a clock — *"from 1:00 and through 11:00"* — and a clock has twelve; the unreached twelve, which loops back to one, is the deferred reunion. Read alongside Lurianic emanation, the whole shape is procession-and-return: the One goes out into the many and is promised back. Those are the reservoirs the song draws on, named as frameworks rather than asserted as intent.
