# It's Your Time

> **One-line reading.** A remembrance song disguised as a motivational anthem — the soul came from elsewhere, forgot on arrival, and must find its way back — built around the catalogue's clearest statement of the integration thesis: a man is an in-between, animating Heaven and Hell.

**Release:** *33* (2022) · **Full lyrics:** [Genius](https://genius.com) · streaming

## What happens in the song

The spine is descent and forgetting, stated plainly: *"once you get to Earth you forget where you come from, gotta find your way back."* The movement is return, not advance; the destination is forgotten, not new. At the center sits the key couplet — *"a man is like an in-between, animating Heaven and Hell."*

The narrator calls himself the Fool — *"I ain't nothing but a Fool"* — and points to an interior infrastructure: *"within you got the Eye to see, all the stars under your Belt, and in your heart there is a Well, you can follow where it leads."* Pronouns appear in scare-quotes — *"as far as 'I' can tell," "who you are to 'me'," "your Self"* with a capital S. One line mutates across the two verses: *"if you don't know who you are to be, you still don't know you fell,"* then *"if you don't know Who You Are to 'me,' you still don't know your Self."* Over the top runs the plain motivational chorus: *"it's your time… learn to fly, don't give up, when it's tough, you got just one life to live."*

## Its place in the mythology

The motivational surface sells *becoming*; the verses say *remembering*. The Eye already exists, the Well already exists, "all the stars" are already "under your Belt," the path already exists — the only thing missing is memory. The disguise is the song. It is the earnest twin of the album's opener: same "from above" cosmology, opposite register. The opener is external capture (the system knows you, "they got my face"); this is internal amnesia (you don't know yourself, "you forget where you come from"). The machine remembers everything about you; you remember nothing about you.

The integration keystone is *"a man is like an in-between, animating Heaven and Hell."* Man is the living middle, and crucially he *animates both* poles — not choosing Heaven over Hell, not slaying the lower, but vivifying the whole polarity. This is the project's central move stated as ontology: the work isn't to eliminate the shadow (Lucius, the lower pole) but to be the channel that holds and animates both. The cosmic, near-innocent forgetting here later becomes personalized, traumatized, and named — the amnesia acquires a face.

## Notes

The doubled identity line teaches by mutating: same frame, deeper each pass — purpose and the Fall it obscured, then relational identity and the Self beneath it. Knowing your role to others isn't knowing yourself. Both renderings are intended variation, not transcription error.

The esoteric scaffold is inherited rather than Roy-stated, and is best read as the reservoir the song draws on. The descent–forgetting–return shape is the Neoplatonic and Gnostic pattern of anamnesis, the draught of forgetting and the return by recollection; *"you still don't know you fell"* names the Fall directly. The Fool is Tarot's Key 0, the unconditioned traveler who "can hardly spell" yet is the pure vessel — and the same line carries Roy's grounded "I'm no-thing" disposition, the Fool as the one empty enough to be filled. The Eye, the Well, and the in-between also track the middle pillar of Hermetic Qabalah. These are correspondences; it remains possible this is simply a motivational single conformed to the mythology, and that reading is held open.
