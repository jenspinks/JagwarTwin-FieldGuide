# Roy Voice Memo - S1 Arrowhead

> **One-line reading.** Forty-two seconds of the producer S1 talking Roy down off a fear — and, in doing so, naming the album's thesis in plain speech: build the vessel before the flood arrives.

**Release:** *Subject to Flooding* (2018), track 9 — a ~0:42 spoken interlude, not a song; a captured conversation left on the record

## What happens in the track

This is a real conversation, kept in the album rather than edited out. The producer S1 reassures Roy that the foundation is already in place — a *"super solid team,"* people who *"understand the vision"* — as opposed to *"the bubble bursting and then you're trying to find pieces."* His conclusion: *"in retrospect, you're already doing that… you're already preparing for it."* The memo's final line names the actual problem and stops, with no resolution: *"it's just the fear of… of when it happens."*

## Its place in the mythology

Watch the structure: S1 dismantles every *rational* fear in turn — you have a team, you have alignment, you have the vision, you're already preparing — and lands on what's left. The remaining obstacle isn't readiness, resources, or competence; it's the threshold itself. This is fear of *transformation*, not fear of failure — dread of crossing into becoming someone the current self hasn't met yet. It may be the earliest documented instance of the dread the whole catalogue later builds its monsters out of, caught raw, before it had any mythological costume.

S1's framing — *build the team, hold the vision, before the bubble bursts* — is the container principle, which is why a 42-second memo belongs on a record called *Subject to Flooding* (see [[The House and the Vessel]]). The flood — overwhelm, success, forces larger than the self — is coming; the only question is whether the vessel can *hold* it. Notably, the preparation S1 describes is relational, never commercial: he never says money, streams, or reach — he says *the team*, the people who get it. That makes the closing fear devastatingly simple: the future is fine; the remaining obstacle is the threshold in the psyche.

It is also the one unmasked track on the album. There's no character, no persona, no pop orchestration, no martial cadence — just a man being talked down off a fear by a friend. It reads as the precise inverse of [[Superhuman]] three tracks earlier, which performs invulnerability; here the fear is left audible and unarmored. Crucially the memo doesn't build the armor or generate the later persona — it shows the bare fear *before* any [[The Protector]] machinery exists. The wound, visible before it learned to defend itself.

## Characters

[[Roy]], with no persona — the bare threshold-fear. The other voice is the producer S1, talking him through it. (Roy released this conversation himself as a commercial album track, so it's published material.)

## Symbols

- The vessel — the team and vision as the container that must hold the coming flood; the album's thesis in plain speech
- Pieces — *"trying to find pieces"*: at the literal level a career reassembling, but the language of fragments and reintegration recurs across the catalogue

## Connected songs

- [[Superhuman]] — the inverse: *Superhuman* performs invulnerability; the memo leaves the bare fear audible
- [[Move To You]] — the same collaborator two tracks earlier; the memo's "vision and team" is that song's ethic in plain speech

## Notes

In Lurianic Kabbalah, the *Shattering of the Vessels* occurs when divine light pours into containers too fragile to hold it; the vessels break and the sparks scatter — and S1's anxiety, *the bubble bursting, then trying to find pieces*, is that catastrophe rendered in studio-session language (see [[Keilim - The Breaking of the Vessels]]). The honest floor: this is literally a pep talk about a music career — a team, a label situation, a bubble bursting — and the container-law and threshold-fear readings are interpretation laid over a friend's reassurance. The title's "Arrowhead" is genuinely unresolved (a studio, a place, a session name); it isn't guessed at here.
