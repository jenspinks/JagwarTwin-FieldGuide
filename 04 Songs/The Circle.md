# The Circle

> **One-line reading.** A creation myth: a feminine maker holding a world on a string makes the Jagwars and teaches them to sing; kings persuade them they cannot; she hides the song inside their wings and withdraws; empires cage and sell — until a wise one finds the old cave and remembers she once held the string, and the circle begins to grow again from the inside of the inside.

**Release:** *33* (2022), album closer · **Full lyrics:** [Genius](https://genius.com) · streaming

This is the 2022 song that closes *33* — the foundational Jagwar myth and the album's resolving word. It is distinct from the concept [[The Loop and the Circle]] (the worldview) and from the later song [[The Circle (Lucius Version)]] (the 2026 reworking); it links to both, but it is its own page.

## What happens in the song

It is told as a third-person myth. *"Long ago, there was a Jagwar with a world tied on a string, and she made other Jagwars, then taught them to sing."* She is immanent, then withdraws — *"she would come down to the empires of the Earth, Wind, and the Sea, and she'd walk with the Jagwars."* The loss comes by persuasion, in cycles: *"many cycles of the story came and went with their kings, and the new kings told Jagwars they never could sing."*

So she hides the song and retreats: *"the maker of the Jagwars hid the song inside their wings, on the inside of the inside, then left them to sing."* Degradation follows — *"the empires built cages and sold everything."* And recovery comes by memory: *"'til a wise one found a small cave where the Jagwars used to sing, and the wise one remembered she once held the string."* The album's final words, to fade, are the doctrine: *"the circle will grow, from the inside of the inside… but the inside of the inside must be where you go."* Both maker and wise one are "she."

## Its place in the mythology

This is the catalogue's origin story — where the Jagwars came from, what was lost, how, and how it returns. The creatures share the artist's own name: "Jagwar Twin" reads as a self-identification with the made-and-divided creatures of this song. The gift the maker gives is neither wealth nor dominion nor knowledge, but song — essence, authentic expression, participation in creation. And the tragedy isn't theft; it's belief. The song is never taken, the Jagwars are *persuaded* it never existed — which is the exact mechanism the album's attention and spectacle tracks dramatize, named here at the mythic root.

The governing paradox is that the song is preserved *within* the Jagwars, "on the inside of the inside." What's sought is already present; the journey is remembrance, not acquisition. Where the album's attention-force pulls awareness outward, here the circle grows from the deepest interior — the same recursive shape pointed the opposite direction. As the closer, this is the album's mythic cure for its own attention-disease: go inward, not further out. The resolution defeats no enemy and seizes no kingdom — *"the wise one remembered."* Memory is placed above victory.

## Notes

The doctrine predates the song. In the debut-era press cycle, talking about *Move To You*, Roy already named it directly: *"there is no beginning and no end — it's just a circle,"* felt live when the crowd sings it back. So the keystone was laid in the debut era, on his most vulnerable song, and this *33* closer is its fully-narrated, mythologized statement. Roy has also called *The Circle* the place a new listener should start — "Jagwar Twin's Genesis story."

The wise one carries two faces at once, joined at the act of remembrance. Because she "once held the string," she *was* the maker — the cosmic, source face, the divine feminine. And because the song was hidden inside *every* Jagwar, she is also the immanent, collective face — the template of remembrance any of us can follow, the exemplars who completed the circle (a Jesus, a Bowie, a Prince), tied to the album title 33 as Christ's age. Roy grounds the collective face publicly — "every person has it inside of them; the trick is getting it inside out." The three names the wise one gathers — Marian vessel, Gnostic Sophia, Kabbalistic Shekinah — stay a family resemblance rather than an equation; her defining act, remembrance, leans more Sophia and Shekinah than conventional Mary. The lighter resonances (creation sustained as song, the cave that holds truth rather than shadows, the Lurianic withdrawal that leaves the divine hidden within creation, Joni's "Circle Game" carousel as a dialogue-image) are framework correspondences, named as such — though this is one of the better-anchored readings in the catalogue, since it is Roy's solo write, it names the project's own creatures, and it is the documented public source of the "song hidden inside its wings" phrase.
