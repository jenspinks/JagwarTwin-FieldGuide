# dumbledore

> **One-line reading.** A volatile collision of martyr-magician grandiosity and algorithmic pop-nihilism — the artist claims the persecuted-truth-teller mantle and, in the same breath, tells the audience to *"go dumb,"* performing the very dumbing-down it describes.

**Release:** standalone release / *MAGIK* era, 2025 (with a "Dark Arts Version") · **Full lyrics:** [Genius](https://genius.com) · streaming

## What happens in the song

The song's engine is a piece of wordplay that enacts its own degradation: *"Dumble-Dumble-Dumbledore"* decays into *"I feel like dumb, dumb, dumb… go dumb."* A wisdom-name — hidden knowledge, the long-game sage — flattens into a chant. Around it the lyric builds a sincere martyr scaffolding (artist-as-persecuted-truth-teller, with Christ and resurrection imagery — *"caught thorns for this," "crucified me," "got out the grave"*) and a persecution roll-call placed *"in Hollywood,"* before bulldozing all of it with the club chant. It also subverts a nursery rhyme: *"if you're magic, and you know it / clap your hands."*

The sincere/ironic tension stays unresolved on purpose. The genuine pain of carrying hidden knowledge collides with an industry and audience that want a mindless party track, and the oscillation between the two is the song. Don't resolve it — *"go dumb"* is what the witch-burning looks like now: Hollywood doesn't burn truth-tellers anymore, it flattens them into content, and the song runs the persecuted wisdom-figures through the dumbing-down machine so the delivery is the thesis.

## Its place in the mythology

The title is sincerely Harry Potter-coded, and there's a grounded backstory that proves it. "dumbledore" was the codename for a release Roy fought the label to put out: the gatekeepers demanded TikTok virality first, so he did a blockchain end-run — *"the label has no rights there"* — and the release went out with a direct Dobby-freed-by-a-sock beat (announced as *"Master gave dumbledore a sock, dumbledore is finally free,"* February 2025). Roy publicly called it *"a love song,"* in the mentor/guide and freeing register, not a plot retelling. So the Dumbledore reference points to hidden knowledge, the long game, and the act of being set free — and ties the song to [[The Realm]] (the world past the gatekeepers) and [[The Circus]] (the gatekeeping system itself).

This is the only documented direct Harry Potter reference in the body of work; every deeper Potter resonance elsewhere is convergence through shared esoteric tradition, not Rowling's influence.

Read against the catalogue, *"go dumb"* aligns with Roy's own stance on cancel culture, stated in an interview: *"we've got to stop canceling people… if you're fighting something and lashing out against something, you become that thing without knowing"* — the burner becomes the witch (see [[Inversion]]). Whether the voice here is Roy's sincere wound or [[Sir Lucius]] mocking the martyr-complex by turning a crucifixion into a club banger, the song holds both, and the self-undercutting *"go dumb"* may be the tell that the spectacle-frame is operating.

## Notes

The lyric is sourced from an aggregator and is streaming-unverified, with high error risk on the proper names — so the persecution roll-call and martyr-mob reading, which rest on names like Galileo and Joan of Arc, are provisional rather than grounded; if any are mishears, those reads collapse. (The grounded core is the release saga, the "love song" caption, and the wordplay engine, none of which depend on the names.) Speculatively, *"eyes wide shut"* sits primarily on the Gurdjieff *asleep / dreaming-while-awake* axis the project tracks, with a secondary echo of the Kubrick film fitting the Hollywood setting; and the truncation of "Dumbledore" to "dumb" rhymes with the wise-fool who plays the idiot to survive the court — the degradation either imposed by the Circus or chosen as the fool's disguise.
