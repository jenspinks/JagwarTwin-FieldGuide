# Precious Time

> **One-line reading.** The moment the running stops working — not the flood's arrival but its recognition: the speaker discovers depth, breath, and time only from inside the water, already waist-deep, while the song's own looping form spends the very time it mourns.

**Release:** *Subject to Flooding* (2018), track 12 — penultimate, before the closer [[Shine]]  ·  **Full lyrics:** [Genius](https://genius.com) · streaming

## What happens in the song

The song's thesis is retrospective: *"we never see our depth till we're underwater / and we never need our breath till we're underwater."* It fuses time and water in a single line — *"time tickin and your head goin underwater / waist deep, wasting time"* — and circles a doubled refrain, *"we know we can't change / nobody else,"* which the outro sharpens to *"there's nobody else."* Two attempts to control time appear side by side: *"stocked up for the day, ready or not"* and *"walked up Heaven's gate, picking the lock."* A parenthetical marker, *"(the people),"* widens the scope just before a couplet about buffalo on the plains and blood in the veins.

## Its place in the mythology

There's no arrival event in the lyric — no wave hits, nothing breaks. The speaker is *already* waist-deep, and the thesis is explicitly backward-looking (*we never see… till we're*). So this isn't where the flood strikes; it's where the album's momentum-doctrine — [[Hell Of A Night]]'s *outrun it* — stops working long enough for the submersion to become conscious. A climax happens; this is a noticing.

Time is the flood. The title says *time*, but the lyric makes time behave like water — ticking, rising, submerging, wasting — and fuses them outright. The album's deluge turns out not to be only emotional: it is temporality itself, the awareness that life is being spent (see [[Subject to Flooding]], [[Machine vs Human]]). The refrain's line break is a genuine fork the song refuses to resolve: read on, *"we can't change nobody else"* (you can only spend your own time); stopped, *"we can't change / nobody else"* (locked in the pattern, and no one is coming). The relentless repetition lets both run at once.

The two control-strategies — *stockpile* for the day, or *force the gate* early — are seizure-logic, the same outlaw grammar as [[Hell of a Night Pt. 2 (The Hallelujah Stickup)]], now extended from money and joy to salvation and time. And *"ready or not"* concedes failure in advance: the day comes on its own schedule regardless. Most pointedly, a song *about* wasting time spends most of its runtime looping identical "nobody else" blocks — the structure performs the squandering it describes.

## Notes

The Vaughan writing credit is a sample — not interpolated lyrics but the spoken banter *before* she sings "Dream" on a 1957 live recording: a captured moment of someone else's spent time, preserved inside a song called *Precious Time* (see [[Inherited Substrate]]). The same recording feeds the album's earlier *Dream / Dream*, splitting one night's music and talk across the album's two halves. One line demands real care: the *"(the people)"* widening is a deliberate, grounded shift from "I" to a collective history of loss, but what the buffalo/Navajo couplet *means* stays open — ancestry, lament, metaphor, or sonic image — and it makes **no** claim about Roy's heritage. The safest reading routes through the household sound-world (Roy has described his mother listening to world and Native American flute music), keeping it music-in-the-blood rather than lineage.
