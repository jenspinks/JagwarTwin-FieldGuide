# Dream / Dream

> **One-line reading.** Not the first statement of a cosmology but an early, sincere participation in inherited dream-language — a 1944 standard reworked into a communal anthem — that the later catalogue spends years interrogating, fracturing, and rebuilding.

> [!info] Album & credits
> **Album:** *Subject to Flooding* (2018) — Jagwar Twin's debut · **track 2 of 13**
> **Written by** Johnny Mercer, Matthew Pauling, Roy English, Sarah Vaughan
> **Produced by** Roy English & Matt Pauling · **Mixed by** Adam Hawkins · **Mastered by** Chris Gehringer · **Exec. producer** S1
> **Interpolates** Johnny Mercer, "Dream (When You're Feeling Blue)" (1944)
> **Full lyrics:** [Genius](https://genius.com) · streaming
> **▶ Listen:** [Official audio (YouTube)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WHVkQQvCKh8)

The doubled title is the whole idea in two words. *Dream / Dream* names the song twice because it *is* two songs: Johnny Mercer's 1944 jazz standard underneath, and Jagwar Twin's new anthem laid over the top. An awakening-song that spends three minutes commanding you to dream turns out to be built on a dream someone else already dreamed — handed down from the American songbook, not authored here. That's not a flaw. In this catalogue it's the point, and the whole later arc grows out of it.

## At a glance

- **Era / release** — *Subject to Flooding* (2018), Jagwar Twin's debut; track 2. Built roughly 2012–2018 across Joshua Tree, Florence, Haiti, Ireland, and Lake Arrowhead.
- **In one line** — a sincere, pre-mirror awakening anthem that still believes the prison is entirely *external* — and whose vehicle of liberation is itself borrowed.
- **Who speaks** — [[Roy]], first-person sliding into collective "we." No persona is visible in the lyric yet; [[Sir Lucius]] is present only by retrospective implication.
- **Key symbols** — [[The Dream]] · [[The Stairway to Heaven]] · [[The Crowd]] · the spelled word (D-R-E-A-M) · the Lord's Prayer / Sunday

## The song, mapped

An anthemic pop song built on a **spelled-hook chorus + two verses + a bridge**, chorus recurring four times. Read by its own moves, it teaches a word, diagnoses a world, aspires upward, then lifts the individual into a crowd — an escape-vector the whole way, with no return-vector yet.

| Section | In the song | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **Chorus (hook)** | *"D-R-E-A-M / Dream, you gotta dream"* → *"Dream, dream, **out loud**, dream, dream"* | **Spells the word first**, letter by letter, before commanding you to inhabit it. Pedagogical: taught → chanted → ordered. The only spelled hook on the album. |
| **Verse 1** | *"If the red light roll, don't stick to one road"* · *"something inside, you know 'cause you feel it"* · *"tryna stop our climb and keep us in line / but something inside you knows you've been cheated"* | Names an **external oppressor** (society, pathing, being kept in line) *and* an **internal felt-truth** at once; resolves both to autonomy — *"doing everything our own way."* |
| **Chorus 2** | *"All you gotta do is…"* / *"I gotta dream"* answered by the *"(Dream, dream, out loud…)"* echo bed | The spelled hook returns, now interleaved with Mercer's instruction-formula. The record **carries its own crowd** at the production level — the parenthetical echo is the sing-back in seed. |
| **Verse 2** | *"When it comes Sunday, I'll say the Lord's prayer / and walk the stairway to Heaven like freedom"* · *"it's about showtime, we're kids in our prime / we feel it inside, the blood that we're bleeding"* · *"small crimes but we did our time"* | Adds **religion + a generational frame**: a Father-address, an ascent image, and a widening from "I" toward a collective "we" that bleeds together. |
| **Chorus 3** | spelled hook + *"all you gotta do is…"* / *"I gotta dream"* | Repeat and reinforce — the reprogramming-by-repetition craft running in real time. |
| **Bridge** *(the peak)* | *"Rise up, we gotta dream / take another one down, come and follow me / rise up, we gotta dream / come and dream that dream with me"* | The decisive turn from **individual aspiration into collective synchronization** — *"we,"* *"follow me,"* *"with me."* The consolation-lullaby becomes a communal anthem here. |
| **Final chorus + coda** | full echo bed → resolves to one bare word: *"Dream."* | Ends on the imperative stripped to its root. |

**The through-line:** *spell* the word → *diagnose* the external cage and *trust* the internal knowing → *aspire* upward and outward (Father, stairway, our own way) → *lift* the individual into the collective. The climb begins; the gate is never reached. This is a **pre-threshold** song.

## What it means

A few claims that cut across the whole song.

- **The dream is borrowed, and that's the deepest fact about it.** The awakening-anthem that orders you to dream is itself built on a *received* dream — Mercer's 1944 standard, not the speaker's invention. First *Dream* = the inherited standard; second *Dream* = the new song over it. Here the borrowing is benign; but it rhymes forward with the catalogue's later worry that liberation-language can be pre-scripted — that the [[Welcome to the Circus|Circus]] also hands the performer a script and calls it freedom (see [[Inherited Substrate]]).
- **What Roy keeps, and what he changes.** He keeps Mercer's dream-imperative (*"that's the thing to do"* → *"you gotta dream"*). He changes its **social posture**. Mercer's dreaming is private, interior, passive consolation — *"when you're feeling blue."* Jagwar Twin's is **vocal and collective** — the inserted *"out loud,"* plus *"rise up, we gotta dream"* and *"come and dream that dream with me."* The lullaby becomes an anthem, and *"dream out loud"* is the catalogue's later sing-back/communion gesture in seed. (External review judged this the *real* load-bearing find of the interpolation — the whole transformation turns on two inserted words. See the Advanced reading below, which fusses about exactly that.)
- **Felt truth precedes diagnosis.** *"Something inside you knows you've been cheated"* — the recognition is somatic. The body senses falseness before the conscious mind can map the system, and the line never says *who* cheated you or *how*. That establishes an early doctrine the catalogue keeps: feeling arrives before the concept.
- **Escape-vector, not return-vector — the line that ages strangest.** *"Doing everything our own way,"* *"rise up,"* *"stairway to Heaven"* are pure ascent-and-exit language. The later arc argues that escape *without* reconciliation becomes **orbit, not freedom** — transformation requires return, not only flight. And the figure who eventually embodies total optimized autonomy is [[Sir Lucius]]. So the song quietly contains the seed of the very worldview later revealed as incomplete. The speaker hasn't yet learned the difference between orbit and homecoming.
- **The moment before dreams become dangerous.** Here, dreaming *is* freedom, transcendence, collective ascent. Nothing has taught the speaker yet that dreams can be engineered, rebellion monetized, spectacle made to imitate awakening. The song occupies the instant *before* the dream-space itself can be colonized — and that innocence is exactly what gives the track its retrospective emotional weight.

## Characters

- **[[Roy]]** — the voice: first-person sliding into collective "we," before any persona is visible in the lyric. He is awakening *genuinely* and sensing falseness *correctly* — but still misunderstanding where the labyrinth ultimately resides. He assumes the oppressor is entirely external. No mask, yet.
- **[[Sir Lucius]]** — absent from the lyric, present only by retrospective implication. Not named, not masked. But the worldview this song sincerely holds — *escape = freedom* — is precisely the one Lucius later destabilizes, because Lucius becomes the *perfected* form of "doing everything our own way": total optimized autonomy, the protective intelligence calcified into a ruler. (Hold all three registers on him — manipulation, shadow, and protective love — and note the guard: nothing here claims Lucius was *intentionally encoded* in 2018.)

## Symbols

- **[[The Dream]]** — inherited from the songbook here (Mercer 1944), sincerely. Across the catalogue the relationship keeps shifting: *Subject to Flooding* inherits the cultural dream → [[Long Time Coming]] notices the disguise inside it → [[Ballerina Boy]] counts the cost of surviving it → [[Weirdness]] recovers the authenticity buried beneath it → [[Lucius Lullaby (album)|Lucius Lullaby]] and [[The Watchers]] turn it into simulation and loop (*"is it just a dream? wake up"*).
- **[[The Stairway to Heaven]]** — upward, outward escape imagery: *"walk the stairway to Heaven like freedom,"* the climb begun before the gate is met. Later inverts against [[City of Angels]]' descending ladder.
- **[[The Crowd]]** — collective-synchronization mechanics: repetition, entrainment, *"follow me,"* *"with me."* This is the same instrument the [[Welcome to the Circus|Circus]] later weaponizes as spectacle — and the seed of the [[The Circle|sing-back]] communion gesture. Not evil; the question is only what flows through the channel.
- **The Lord's Prayer / Sunday** — a Father-address, a vertical invocation, a reaching-toward-source: the earliest Father-vector in the arc, before the explicit Father interventions later (the reached-for Father's hand; the fatherly blessing in [[Ballerina Boy]]).
- **The spelled word (D-R-E-A-M)** — letter-by-letter construction as a transmission/teaching gesture; how a child learns a word. *(The one-letter-off echo of Wu-Tang's "C.R.E.A.M." was raised and ruled out — see Source notes.)*

## Connected songs

- [[Long Time Coming]] — the album's opener; shared awakening and anti-system language from the same era. The crowd is already singing, and *"the disguise on my face getting clearer"* is the album's earliest mask/mirror anchor.
- [[Welcome to the Circus]] — the clearest contrast: liberation mechanics *inverted* into spectacle (*"paint my face and they clap and smile"*). **Same crowd-channel, different current.**
- [[Precious Time]] — the sibling credit. The same 1957 Sarah Vaughan "Dream" recording appears to feed *two* tracks: the **song** here (Mercer's composition), the spoken **banter** there. Whether *Dream / Dream* uses that exact take or interpolates the composition isn't settled — but one Vaughan recording seems to underlie both, split across the album's two halves.
- [[Superhuman (Interlude)]] — the album's *other* credited interpolation (Chaplin's *Great Dictator* speech); the sibling inherited-substrate track, whose *"not in one man but in all men"* rhymes with this song's collective "we."
- [[Loser]] — the album's *other* relay-adjacent track (track 3), carrying a Beck-"Loser" echo and the same we-not-me humanism (everybody's all the same). The clearest reinforcement of the [[Inherited Substrate|inherited-substrate]] throughline that runs under this whole page: liberation-language handed down, not authored fresh.
- [[The Circle]] — the [[The Circle|sing-back]] communion device in full flower: the crowd-as-channel that this song seeds (*"come and dream that dream with me"*) becomes its own doctrine here. Same instrument, and here the operator is examined directly.
- [[Online]] — escape read as straightforward before escape is revealed as orbit; the later-era companion to this song's pure ascent-and-exit vector (*"doing everything our own way,"* *"rise up"*).
- [[Lucius Lullaby (album)|Lucius Lullaby]] — the dream-imperative destabilized into an ambiguous register: *"life is but a dream, I came to set you free"* reads as liberation *or* as Lucius's pitch.
- [[City of Angels]] — Mercer's hedge weaponized: *"they might come true"* becomes *"all your dreams are coming true / everyone looks just like you"* (fulfillment = uniformity). The inverted stairway.
- [[The Watchers]] — names this song as its interpolation *precedent*; the paranoid-alarm sequel to sincere dreaming (*"is it just a dream? … wake up"*).

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the four Mercer transformations (the full anatomy of the interpolation)
> This is the fullest map of what *Dream / Dream* does to its 1944 source. Mercer's original (public domain, quoted freely): *"Dream, when you're feeling blue / Dream, that's the thing to do,"* *"and they might come true,"* *"things never are as bad as they seem."*
> 1. **The instruction-formula is kept.** *"That's the thing to do"* → *"all you gotta do is… dream / you gotta dream."* The *command* survives intact.
> 2. **Triple-dream + one inserted word.** Mercer's *"so dream, dream, dream"* becomes *"Dream, dream, **out loud**, dream, dream."* Two words convert private consolation into collective performance. (The vault fusses endlessly that *"out loud"* is only two words while carrying the entire load-bearing transformation — and the external reviewer *agreed* it's the real find. The small word only carries weight because *"rise up"* and *"with me"* hold it up.)
> 3. **The hedge weaponized — later.** Mercer's *"and they might come true"* → [[City of Angels]]' *"all your dreams **are** coming true / everyone looks just like you."* The 1944 *maybe* becomes the Hollywood *certainly*, and fulfillment curdles into uniformity.
> 4. **The "seem"-line split — later.** Mercer's consolation *"things never are as bad as they seem"* → [[Lucius Lullaby (album)|Lucius Lullaby]]'s destabilizing *"nothing's what it seems."*
>
> **The one-source thesis:** the Mercer standard *already contains both poles the catalogue later splits.* *Dream / Dream* keeps the imperative and drops the **sedation** — and the sedation returns much later, as Lucius's voice. One source, divided across the whole arc.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — Source vs Reflection, and where this song sits
> The album's governing question is: *does a reflection return you to the source, or trap you in the image?* The **dream** is one of the mediating layers in that scheme — it can *guide and aspire* (here) or become *inherited programming, the engineered dream* (the Circus). *Dream / Dream* sits firmly on the **transmit** side of the split. In the album's hidden architecture — "the formation and defense of the Protector" — track 2 is a **pre-mirror awakening that still believes the prison is external**, whose vehicle of liberation is itself a transmission from 1944.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the crowd as channel (same instrument, different operator)
> The *"rise up, we gotta dream"* bridge uses the exact collective-entrainment mechanics — repetition, synchronization, emotional entrainment — that the Circus later weaponizes. The crucial nuance: this is **not** proto-Circus *versus* awakening. It is the *same mechanism* carrying a different current. **Same instrument, different operator.** The crowd is not evil; the only question is what flows through the channel. *Dream / Dream* is catalogued as a new instance of the [[The Circle|sing-back]] communion device in its **Roy-voiced** column (*"come and dream that dream with me,"* *"we gotta dream"*) — distinct from the later *Lucius-voiced* commanded/harvested echo. The device's direction tracks the narrative voice.
> **Open question:** does the bridge *structurally anticipate* the Circus's crowd-synchronization, or merely *share a formal similarity* with it? The honest answer is unsettled. A shared mechanism (entrainment is entrainment) doesn't prove a designed foreshadow, and reading the bridge as "proto-Circus" is the kind of backward-projection the page flags elsewhere. Best held as: the *resource* is the same; whether 2018 was *pointing at* 2020 is not something the song can tell us.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — esoteric resonances (framework, not authorial intent)
> These are structural harmonics the system permits, explicitly **not** claims about what Roy intended.
> - **Jungian** — an early individuation impulse, but with the shadow still *externalized*: the protector-shadow is active but not yet consciously encountered.
> - **Kabbalistic** — aspirational movement Malkuth → Tiferet without recognizing the occupied relay-space of Yesod; a *pre-encounter* song. The climb has begun; the gate has not.
> - **Gurdjieff / Fourth Way** — *"you've been cheated"* resembles the diagnosis of mechanical sleep; open question whether mass synchronization *awakens* or merely *redirects* collective hypnosis.
> - **Gnostic** — the soul senses captivity within a false ordering system; *"stairway to Heaven"* as a return-vector reached for *before* the prison's architecture is understood.
> - **Letter-mysticism** — a word built letter-by-letter and spoken *"out loud"* touches the "word spoken into being" figure. Resonant, not asserted.
> - **Alchemical phase** — the tempting label is *albedo* (solar, aspirational), but albedo follows nigredo, and the later collapse postdates the song. Better read as **pre-collapse aspiration, a naive solar orientation** — the climb before the fall.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the blood-census (album-local texture, not a cross-era thread)
> *"The blood that we're bleeding"* sits inside a blood-motif that escalates across *Subject to Flooding*: *"all of us bleed"* (Long Time Coming, solidarity) → *"blood on our knees"* (Superhuman, cost) → *"blood that we're bleeding"* (here, the shared-struggle midpoint) → *"blood in my mouth"* (Hell Pt. 2) → *"Navajo blood in the veins"* (Precious Time, inheritance). This is **album-local** texture, deliberately *not* elevated to a cross-catalogue thread.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — where the name comes from (the artist's own words)
> This is album-level paratext, not a statement about this track — but it grounds the mirror-motif the page leans on (the pre-mirror reading, the Source-vs-Reflection split) in Roy's own framing. Introducing the project (*Ones to Watch*, 2018), he glossed the name directly. The **jaguar** "looks into the souls of other people and then can look back into itself" — *"humans are just mirrors for each other."* The **twin** is *"the light and the dark, the yin and the yang."* And the whole thing sits inside a one-world humility: *"we were all just one little piece of the puzzle."* Note what this does for the page: the mirror-as-relation idea (people reflecting people) and the doubled/twinned structure aren't only analyst impositions — they're in the artist's stated concept from the start. It also quietly harmonizes with *Dream / Dream*'s doubling (two songs in one title) and its collective "we." Still: this is the *name's* concept, spoken album-wide; it is **not** Roy commenting on this song, and the page's per-track readings stay analyst-side.

## Source notes

- **Grounded** *(released music, credits, public interviews/posts).* The **interpolation** of Mercer's 1944 "Dream (When You're Feeling Blue)" — composer field credits Johnny Mercer, Matthew Pauling, Roy English, and Sarah Vaughan (Vaughan's credit most likely marking her specific recording/arrangement as the interpolated source). Production: Roy English & Matt Pauling; Adam Hawkins mixed (notably *not* Jeff Ellis, who mixed most of the album); Chris Gehringer mastered; S1 executive-produced. The **spelled hook** is a plain textual fact — the only one on the album. *Subject to Flooding* was first released independently **2018-09-21**, then redistributed/expanded by Atlantic in 2019 (streaming shows 2019; 2018 is the true first-release date). The album's stated mission (Roy, launch press): *"with the fragile state of the world… we should all let go of ego… After all, we are all Subject to Flooding."* And the craft statement behind the chanted hook (Roy, 2018): *"the more you tell yourself something out loud, the more you actually start to believe it… there's freedom in the repetition of that phrase."*
- **No artist commentary on this specific track.** There is **no** ingested Roy quote about *Dream / Dream* itself — the album-level statements above are the closest paratext. Every interpretive move on this page is analyst-side; the page does not imply an artist-stated reading of the song.
- **Strong readings.** The pre-mirror / pre-threshold placement; escape-vector-not-return-vector; felt-truth-before-diagnosis; the doubled title as encoded borrowing; the crowd-as-channel; the Father-vector; and the interpolation's "consolation → invitation" transformation. All grounded in the material, but interpretive — labeled as such.
- **Ruled out.** A one-letter-off echo of **Wu-Tang's "C.R.E.A.M."** (1993) was raised in the deep-dive and then killed on external review: no credit, no Roy statement, no cadence match, and likely *unhearable* — D-R-E-A-M is sung as letter-names in a bright anthem cadence, not as a word rhyming with "cream." Kept out of canon so it can't launder the solid Mercer reading; logged, at most, as a shared *tradition* (spelling a word out as a way of passing an ancestral song down), never a *lineage*. As the substrate law puts it: *co-participant ≠ source.* *(An earnest analyst really did build a whole "Dream Rules Everything Around Me" thesis on it before the ear-check falsified it. The kill is on file.)*
- **How we'd be wrong.** Every "pre-mirror / pre-Circus / pre-diagnosis" move is an arc-station read projected *backward* onto a 2018 track — and *"pre-X"* is still *X* projected backward. The flatter and possibly truer read: an **earnest 2018 indie-pop awakening anthem with a credited Mercer interpolation, no proto-anything.** Guards worth keeping: *"rise up / stairway to Heaven"* are maximally generic aspiration-clichés (and genericness is itself a mark of *inherited substrate*, not of encoded geometry); the heaviest readings once rested on then-unverified lyrics. Hold the heavier claims lightly.
- **Open question — how early the mirror-framework had formed.** The name-origin paratext (see the Advanced reading above) shows the *mirror-as-relation* and *twin/light-and-dark* ideas were already stated at the 2018 launch — so the mirror-framework is not purely a later retrofit. But "stated as the project's name concept" and "operating inside this specific song" are different claims; how *developed* that framework was during the *Subject to Flooding* era, versus how much crystallized afterward, is genuinely open. The page's "pre-mirror" placement is a reading of where the *song* sits, not a dated claim about Roy's thinking.
