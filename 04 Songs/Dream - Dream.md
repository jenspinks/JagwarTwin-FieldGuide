# Dream / Dream

> **One-line reading.** Not the first statement of a cosmology but an early, sincere participation in inherited dream-language — a 1944 standard reworked — that the later catalogue gradually interrogates, fractures, and reconstructs.

**Release:** *Subject to Flooding* (2018), track 2  ·  **Full lyrics:** [Genius](https://genius.com) · streaming  ·  **Interpolates:** Johnny Mercer, "Dream (When You're Feeling Blue)" (1944)

## What happens in the song

The song commands the listener to dream, repeatedly, and frames society as restrictive — *"tryna stop our climb and keep us in line,"* *"don't stick to one road."* It locates an intuitive recognition — *"something inside you knows you've been cheated"* — names collective suffering, and emphasizes autonomy: *"doing everything our own way."* Christian imagery threads through (*"I'll say the Lord's prayer,"* *"walk the stairway to Heaven"*), and the bridge lifts individual aspiration into collective synchronization: *"rise up, we gotta dream."*

## Its place in the mythology

This is one of the earliest awakening-anthems in the catalogue, and it documents a stage *before* the cosmology understands the recursive nature of the trap. Here, dreaming still means liberation, movement still means freedom, rebellion still feels authentic. The speaker assumes the oppressive force is entirely external — society, pathing, being "kept in line." Later works destabilize all of it: by [[Welcome to the Circus]], rebellion itself becomes commodified spectacle; the dream-space turns out to be colonizable. The song's innocence is exactly what gives it retrospective weight (see [[The Loop and the Circle]]).

*"Something inside you knows you've been cheated"* is load-bearing: the recognition is somatic, the body sensing falseness before the conscious mind can map the system. That establishes an early doctrine the catalogue keeps — felt truth precedes conceptual diagnosis. And *"doing everything our own way"* is the line that ages most strangely: it reads as pure liberation, but the later arc argues that escape without reconciliation becomes orbit, not freedom — and the figure who eventually embodies total, optimized autonomy is [[Sir Lucius]]. The song contains the seed of the very worldview later revealed as incomplete.

The deepest structural fact is that even the dream is borrowed. The song interpolates Johnny Mercer's 1944 standard — hence the doubled title: the old *Dream* and the new one layered over it. An awakening-anthem that tells the listener to dream is itself built on a received dream, the language handed down from the American songbook rather than authored by the speaker (see [[Inherited Substrate]]). What Roy *keeps* from Mercer is the dream-imperative; what he *changes* is its posture — Mercer's dreaming is private consolation *"when you're feeling blue,"* while Jagwar Twin's is vocal and collective: *"dream out loud,"* *"rise up, we gotta dream,"* *"come and dream that dream with me."* The lullaby becomes a communal anthem.

## Characters

[[Roy]], first-person and collective ("we"), before any persona is visible in the lyric. The speaker is awakening genuinely and sensing falseness correctly — but still misunderstanding where the labyrinth ultimately resides.

## Symbols

- The dream — inherited from the songbook here, sincerely; later it becomes a mirror, then a loop, then a system of capture
- The stairway to Heaven — upward, outward, escape-vector imagery: the climb begun before the gate is encountered

## Notes

The hook spells the word — *"D-R-E-A-M"* — making DREAM something taught, chanted, then commanded to inhabit, which fits the album's own repetition-as-reprogramming craft (Roy in 2018: *"the more you tell yourself something out loud, the more you actually start to believe it"*). A possible echo of Wu-Tang's "C.R.E.A.M." was raised and deliberately set aside: there's no credit and no cadence match, so it's kept out as a rejected resonance rather than a source. The fair counter-reading is that "pre-mirror / pre-Circus" framing is still an arc projected backward — the plainest read is an earnest 2018 awakening anthem with a credited standard, no proto-anything. The lyric source for this track also remained unverified longer than most, which is reason to hold the heavier readings lightly.
