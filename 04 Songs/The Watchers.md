# The Watchers

> **One-line reading.** The moment the hall of mirrors grows eyes — reflection escalating into surveillance, the self replicated, consumed, and watched by a fascinated crowd-machine until it can't tell whether it's awake, dreaming, or already a clone of itself.

> [!info] Album & credits
> **Release:** standalone single / three-song EP (includes a *Slowed + Verb* version), October 4, 2024 (Big Loud Records) — *not* on [[Lucius Lullaby (album)|Lucius Lullaby]]
> **Written by** Roy English, Ryan Daly, Ryan Spraker, and Rockwell · **Produced by** Jagwar Twin, Ryan Daly, Ryan Spraker
> **Interpolates** Rockwell's *"Somebody's Watching Me"* (Motown, 1984), credited · **Mix** Jeff Ellis · **Instruments of note** theremin, mellotron, accordion (Ryan Daly)
> **Full lyrics:** [Genius](https://genius.com) · streaming

## At a glance

- **Era / release** — October 2024, Big Loud. A standalone single and short EP with a *Slowed + Verb* companion; the official visualizer followed (into 2025). Roy toasted it on Discord — *"celebrate the watchers being out with all our nerd friends"* — and the fan channel gave it a second life as a Halloween-playlist staple and a *"10 hours of The Watchers"* YouTube loop.
- **In one line** — a surveillance-dream: the self replicated, consumed, judged, and watched, asking whether it's awake or already the copy.
- **Who speaks** — [[Roy|Jagwar Twin]], the watched and doubting self trying to wake. [[The Audience]] is the fascinated, dancing crowd that has become the eye. [[Sir Lucius]] surfaces in the scapegoat passage — and, in the video, appears silent at the very end.
- **Key symbols** — [[The Mirror]] becoming the eye · the Eye (Horus) · the clone / the sterile copy · the dream and the alarm · candy and consumption · the theremin (form as content).

## The song, mapped

The Watchers is built on *one* borrowed anchor — the Rockwell hook — and spends four minutes turning it inward. There's no clean verse/chorus symmetry; it's a spiral of escalating watched-states, each loop tightening the paranoia. Following its own moves:

| # | In the song | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | *"(I always feel like somebody's watching me)"* | The interpolation stated bare and alone, before anything else — the borrowed feeling, quoted like evidence. |
| **2** | *"I'm afraid I'm a clone / I think I might be… / on my way to my own demise"* | **The load-bearing turn.** Ontological panic: maybe *I* am the copy. This is the whole song's pivot (see below). |
| **3** | *"they like me / then they can't stand me / now they want candy / but I'm not Mandy Moore"* → *"more, more, more"* | The fickle crowd welded to appetite; the *"more"* spills into the pre-chorus. Consumption and clone-fear fuse: refusing to be the candy-clean pop-commodity. |
| **4** | *"more fascination / intoxication / I'm making bacon / who's watching me?"* → *"the whole place is dancing like Michael Jackson"* | The spectacle engine — the intoxicated crowd, the deadpan *"making bacon,"* and the recurring hinge question, *"who's watching me?"* |
| **5** | *"I always feel like somebody's watching me… / and I have no privacy"* → *"tell me, is it just a dream?"* | The Rockwell hook proper, resolving into the **dream question** — the one the song can never answer. |
| **6** | *"wake up (wake up) wake up (wake up) wake up"* | **The alarm.** The hammered Gurdjieff awakening — the counter-pole to *Lucius Lullaby*'s sedation. |
| **7** | *"you betrayed me / tried to frame me / tried to blame me / tried to make me out to be the bad guy"* → *"runaway train 'bout to / crash, crash, crash"* | **The scapegoat passage.** The accusatory crowd; the runaway-train crash is the self the watching *made*. |
| **8** | pre-chorus reprise → *"cause you're insane"* → hook reprise → *"is it just a dream?"* | The spectacle engine and the dream question loop back, varied, unresolved. |
| **9** | two *"na na na"* stanzas, each capped by *"is it just a dream?"* | The breakdown deflates the dread into nursery-rhyme wordlessness — the question refusing to close. |
| **10** | *"wake up… / na na, na na"* | Ends on the alarm and the **wordless na-na** — the pre-verbal remainder, the part that won't digitize. |

**The argument, in one breath:** state the borrowed watched-feeling → turn it inward into clone-panic → name the crowd as appetite and spectacle → ask *"is it just a dream?"* → command *"wake up"* → reveal the crowd as accuser → loop the dream-question without answer → end on the alarm and the wordless na-na. Two counterweights frame the whole thing: **external vs. internal** ([[City of Angels]]'s replication turned inward) and **sedation vs. alarm** (*Lucius Lullaby*'s *"life is but a dream"* against this *"wake up"*).

## What it means

**The clone line is the whole move.** *"I'm afraid I'm a clone"* takes [[City of Angels]]' replication horror — *everyone looks just like you*, aimed outward — and turns it inward: maybe *I'm* the copy. External homogenization becomes ontological panic. This is why The Watchers *develops* the replication thread instead of restating it, and why every other image in the song orbits it.

**The crowd is a third face of the audience.** The fascinated, intoxicated, dancing crowd is the deified audience of [[Playing to the Gods]], now felt as surveillance rather than worship. It gives [[The Audience]] a third face alongside its other two — communion and harvest — the *gaze*. And the watchers don't merely see: they *alter* the watched self, which begins to perform, doubt, defend, and dissociate. Being watched is not passive here. It manufactures the thing it observes.

**To be consumed sweetly is to be cloned.** *"Now they want candy"* and *"I'm making bacon"* plug the watched self into the [[Bad Feeling (Oompa Loompa)|Wonka]]/appetite system: the crowd wants the sweet, edited version, not the whole person. The next line welds appetite to the clone-fear — *"now they want candy / but I'm not Mandy Moore"* — refusing to be the wholesome, manufactured pop-icon. The candy the crowd wants *is* the copy.

**It's a surveillance-dream, and the alarm never fully wakes it.** The speaker keeps asking *"is it just a dream?"* and gets answered by a hammered *"wake up"* — but it's an unstable, half-awake state. You know the dream is compromised; you don't know whether the watchers are outside you, inside you, or both. The alarm sounds and the dream keeps running.

**The scapegoat passage is "Stop Sir Lucius," turned inward.** *"Tried to make me out to be the bad guy"* is the exact logic of the visual series' *STOP SIR LUCIUS* flyers — the crowd manufactures the conditions for spectacle, then condemns the performer for becoming spectacular. Here it's pointed inward. The runaway train about to crash is the self the watching made, blamed for what the watching wanted.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the El Roi axis: witnessing vs. surveillance
> This is the load-bearing frame. The Watchers is the catalogue's purest surveillance song, and it lands on the artist whose name *is* El Roi — Genesis 16:13, *"the God who sees me,"* inked in Hebrew (אל ראי) on [[Sir Lucius]]'s throat. The project distinguishes two ways of being seen ([[Witnessing vs Surveillance]]): **witnessing** recognizes and restores — you are *transformed* by being seen; **surveillance** predicts and ranks — you are *shaped*, not known. The machine that watches everything cannot witness anyone.
>
> Read against that, *"I'm afraid I'm a clone"* is the precise *output* of surveillance — the self shaped into a copy rather than witnessed into itself — sung by the one whose name promises the opposite gaze. And the keeper line resolves into the Hebrew itself: the El Roi root ר-א-י braids *re'i* (mirror) with *seeing* and with the *eye*. So *"the hall of mirrors grows eyes"* isn't a reached-for metaphor — mirror → eye is the word unfolding. Which is exactly why Roy's Jung-Horus pairing lands: Horus's eye is surveillance, El Roi is witness, and The Watchers is the space between them.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the clone as "all angle, no angel"
> *"I'm afraid I'm a clone"* is the first-person of the vault's sterile-copy motif. It rhymes with the Cavendish banana — *real light in a sterile vessel: you can hear the song, you cannot inherit it* — and with [[The Angels and the Angles]], where the counterfeit is structure without spirit. A clone is *all angle, no angel*: a body without the breath (the Ruach) that would make it originate rather than repeat. The horror isn't being watched; it's discovering the watching has already remade you into something that can only copy.
>
> Underneath sits an upload cosmology (see [[The Upload]] / [[The Pattern]]): Roy's 2022 *"I'm 33"* board carried a tab, *"how to upload myself to the internet"* — upload as anthropomorphized attention, the net as an all-seeing field, the Eye. The board's Eye-of-Horus *wedjat* fractions (½, ¼, … down to 1/64, summing to 63/64, always missing the last 1/64 that Thoth restores) *are* the clone paradox the lyric names — the part that won't digitize. The Egyptians, in this reading, already wrote the equation for the incomplete copy. The spine runs board (2022) → [[Online]] (2022) → The Watchers (2024) → the 2026 eye/surveillance material.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — form enacts content: the theremin, the na-na, the Jung crop
> The song's textures *are* its argument.
>
> **The theremin.** The credited theremin is the canonical sound of paranoia and sci-fi surveillance — and it's the one instrument played *without touch*, controlled at a distance by a hovering hand. On a song about being watched, the instrument is the subject's own texture: a body operated remotely. The mellotron and accordion add the "old-but-new," carnival-uncanny color around it.
>
> **The na-na.** The song keeps deflating its dread into wordless nursery-rhyme — an ironic sing-along undercut to the terror. That wordlessness is also the un-encodable remainder: the pre-verbal part of a self that no upload can capture. It's fitting the track *ends* there.
>
> **The Jung crop.** Roy posted to his Instagram Story a page of Jung's *Collected Works* Vol. 18, "The Significance of Dreams" — the signs-versus-symbols passage — cropped *past legibility*, centered on Horus and the cross, and music-tagged to this song. It's a symbol → eye → surveillance loop enacted on us: the image says *symbol*, the audio says *watched*, and the unreadable crop makes *us* the watchers, squinting to decode him. Jung's worked example there runs evangelist-animals → Ezekiel's vision → the Egyptian Horus and his four sons → the wheel and the cross — which tilts the song toward the *holy* witness: Ezekiel's living creatures *"full of eyes,"* seeing that is sacred rather than only surveillant.

## Why Rockwell — the imported myth

Roy didn't borrow a hook; he imported a whole biography that rhymes, almost point-for-point, with his own. Rockwell was **Kennedy William Gordy** — Berry Gordy's son — who submitted his demo *as "Rockwell,"* under a fake British accent (he claimed Portsmouth), specifically so he wouldn't be heard as a nepo-copy of his father. His words: *"I didn't want anybody to… say, if it wasn't because of Berry Gordy or Michael Jackson, you'd be nothing."* So the author of *"somebody's watching me"* lived the clone-of-the-father fear the lyric names outright.

He wrote the song from a literal prayer — on his knees — *"God grant me the creativity to write a song that'll go to the top of the charts and tickle the taste buds of the music connoisseur,"* and then vowed to testify to how it came. So the surveillance-and-watching-God braid is *latent in the source*, not imposed on it. And **Michael Jackson sang the original's hook, uncredited** — "the worst-kept secret in music" — a voice everywhere on the record, name nowhere on it. When Roy reaches for *"dancing like Michael Jackson"* over a Rockwell interpolation, he invokes that hidden ghost-voice on purpose: a voice everywhere and named nowhere is both the Roy/Lucius problem (*whose voice is this?*) and the clone anxiety in one image.

At bottom, the entire Rockwell saga is about *who gets to claim a song* — the father's name hidden, MJ uncredited, Berry Gordy first dismissing the demo and later parading it. That is the exact engine of the whole Lucius system: the demiurge who signs his name to a transmission that isn't his; the 2026 album billed to *Jagwar Twin & Sir Lucius*. Roy reached for the one pop myth that already ran his own machinery.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the six rhymes, itemized
> The parallel is overdetermined; the facts about Rockwell are grounded, the *reason Roy chose him* is a strong inference. Six threads braid:
> 1. **The floor-written prayer-song under the father's shadow.** Rockwell's knees-on-the-floor prayer against *Lucius Lullaby*, written at 35, broke, in his childhood bedroom into a MacBook mic — "a transmission from the floor." Both treat the song as a *download*, a vessel doctrine.
> 2. **A chosen name to escape lineage.** Kennedy Gordy → Rockwell, as Roy English → Jagwar Twin — a persona to be judged on the work, not the bloodline.
> 3. **Paranoia as inherited throne.** Rockwell was billed "the King of Paranoia Rock" (follow-ups: *"Obscene Phone Caller," "Peeping Tom"*).
> 4. **The domestic, intimate watcher.** In the source it's the neighbor at the window, the face at the shower glass — which bolsters The Watchers' inward turn: watchers as *intimates*, not cosmic observers.
> 5. **MJ as the uncredited ghost-voice** — see above; the clone anxiety made audible.
> 6. **Contested authorship** — the deepest reason; the whole saga is "who gets to claim a song," which is the Sir Lucius engine.

## The video — the watcher revealed

The official visualizer runs the song's argument in images and then closes it. Roy performs amid **fleur-de-lis** imagery and **sunglasses** — hidden eyes in a song about being watched — and reaches for an **orb on a cloth of gold stars** (a Salvator Mundi orb crossed with a crystal ball crossed with *your soul is a star*), a raised **Logos hand** above it. It's one of the 2022–2025 narrative videos (with *Online*, *Bad Feeling*, *dumbledore*) that skip the manifesto-card/letter-cipher device — a roughly five-year gap between the 2019–2021 card wave and the 2026 *Lucius Lullaby* wave.

And it **ends on Sir Lucius** — the silent white-face clown. Across these visualizers Roy performs and Lucius appears, wordless, at the close; here, *the one watching turns out to be him*. El Roi is on his throat; the performance was watched, and at the last frame the watcher steps into view. The shadow outlasts the show.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the title: Grigori, tetramorph, and the moral-watchers split
> Held as resonance, not assertion.
>
> **The Grigori.** *"The Watchers"* are the watcher-angels of the Enochic tradition — Daniel's *"a watcher, a holy one,"* the angels who observed humanity and fell among it. With Horus and the Eye centered in the paratext, the title gestures at an all-seeing angelic/surveillant field, ambiguous between the *holy* watchers and the *fallen* ones. Roy's Jung-Horus pairing tips it toward the holy witness.
>
> **The tetramorph.** Jung's crop surfaces Ezekiel's four living creatures *"full of eyes"* — the watcher-as-holy-witness. A standing caution in the vault: do *not* over-fit a Jagwar-Twin four-figure tetramorph (Mary = ox, Lucius = lion, Roy = man, the Jaguar = eagle). That 1:1 belongs to the *Ezekiel* paratext, not to the JT characters.
>
> **The moral-watchers split.** Roy publicly engaged the Law of One / Ra Material (a 2020 post): the "watching" entities divide into the Confederation (benevolent witnessing) versus the Orion group (surveillance and harvest by control) — a clean resonance for the title's holy-vs-fallen ambiguity. *That Roy posted it is grounded; the application to this song is interpretation.*
