# The Rabbit

> **One-line reading.** The rabbit is the searching, vulnerable self before the split — and the clown-makeup rabbit who slides into the passenger seat is what that self becomes when the mind grabs the wheel and starts answering questions it hasn't earned the right to answer.

## At a glance

- **What it does** — names the seeker (instinct, vulnerability, survival, the self with no fixed name yet) — and then, worn by a second rabbit in clown makeup, names the guide who arrives too early with true answers.
- **Carried by** — [[Roy]] (the dream-rabbit, the seeker) · [[Sir Lucius]] (the clown-rabbit, the counterfeit guide) · [[Sir Rabbit]] (the seeker before the split, the poem's protagonist).
- **Appears in** — [[Good Time (1-08)|Good Time (1:08)]] · the *Sir Rabbit* poem · the 2026 theatrical / [[Lucius Lullaby (album)|Lucius Lullaby]] era · [[Weirdness]] · the *Playing to the Gods* / *City of Angels* visualizer.
- **Confidence** — grounded fact (Roy narrates being a rabbit; the clown-rabbit names himself Sir Lucius; the rabbit-ear look is documented) braided with strong reading (self-vs-self) and openly framework-tier esoterica.

## What it does

The rabbit does one job that splits into two. It marks the **seeker** — and then it marks the thing the seeker turns into when it stops searching and starts pretending it already knows.

Roy tells the origin of [[Good Time (1-08)|Good Time (1:08)]] as a dream, recounted on TikTok around the 2023 release. He keeps a dream journal — *"I write my dreams down every night, and every dream comes true"* — and in this one he leaves a party, gets picked up by Drake in a white minivan, takes some fame-advice, and is told *"it's your turn to drive."* Drake exits. Roy drives the highway alone, *"looking for something, but I didn't know what,"* and — here is the load-bearing line — *"somehow I know that — I'm a rabbit."* And, deadpan: *"Drake wasn't a rabbit."* The rabbit, at this stage, is pure register: instinct, exposure, survival, a self searching without yet knowing its own name.

Then a *second* rabbit drops into the passenger seat. *"He's a rabbit, but he's got like clown makeup on, and he says, I'm Sir Lucius… I'm here to help you."* He has all the answers; he's factually correct about every turn — *"but I had a weird feeling in my heart… he was telling the truth, but he was manipulating me in some way, and I couldn't explain why."* The dream ends in a white-semi-truck-with-a-lightning-bolt crash; Roy wakes at **1:08** — which becomes the song's title — and the crash then plays out in waking traffic that same day.

That two-rabbit structure is the entire operation in miniature, and the detail that makes it work is **species**. Drake isn't a rabbit. Roy is. [[Sir Lucius]] is. The encounter is therefore *self against self*, not hero against monster: Lucius isn't an invader, he's an **adaptation of Roy's own rabbit-nature with clown makeup added** — the same instinct, made up like a performer, given the guide's seat. The shadow grows *from* the self, which is precisely why it can't be cast out. (This is the logic of [[The Dweller on the Threshold|the Dweller on the Threshold]]: the shadow accumulates, it doesn't arrive.) The rabbit is the self before the split; the clown-rabbit is what the mind becomes when it takes the wheel too early.

And the song is sung from *inside* that dream. [[Good Time (1-08)|Good Time (1:08)]] carries near-verbatim callbacks — *"Just a rabbit in a road rage"* and *"I don't know what I'm looking for,"* the dream's exact phrase. (That last line is, quietly, the most anti-Lucius thing in the song: honest not-knowing is the one stance the answer-machine can't colonize. Lucius arrives the instant Roy both takes responsibility for navigating *and* admits he doesn't know what he's looking for — he is, structurally, a solution to uncertainty, the part of the psyche that cannot tolerate not-knowing.)

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the mechanism: arrives too early with true answers
> The sharpest gloss of the clown-rabbit comes from setting the dream beside the poem (below). In the *Sir Rabbit* poem, truth arrives *only after* the box and the recovered heartbeat — the initiation has to be endured before the answers mean anything. Lucius occupies the guide-seat *before* any of that is suffered through. His answers are factually correct but bypass the heart entirely. So the formula isn't quite "truth that deceives" — it's subtler: **Lucius does not only deceive by lying; he deceives by arriving too early with true answers.** Correct, premature, and therefore hollow. The clown-rabbit is the mind in the guide's seat, narrating the map before you've walked the road.

## Associations

Crucially **not a proto-Lucius and not a third figure**: in the grounded dream Roy *is* the rabbit and Lucius is the *second* rabbit, so "pre-Lucius" means the self before it divided — the Roy-register, the seeker who hasn't yet hardened into a guide.

- **[[The Jaguar]]** — the food-chain pairing (see below): the jaguar eats rabbits. The rabbit, in this register, is prey — the foolish, exposed self the predator-self outranks.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the full character chain, and why Sir Rabbit stays the Roy-register
> Read across the framework, the rabbit anchors a developmental sequence rather than a single image: the original **wound** produces a **buried child** ([[Ballerina Boy]]), who becomes a **searching rabbit** with no fixed identity ([[Sir Rabbit]]), who is then overlaid with **armor** ([[Sir Lucius]] — and behind Lucius, [[The Protector]], the survival-self that calcifies into him). If evidence ever forces a lock, that can change; for now he stays the seeker-pole.

## The other rabbit: the silly one (and we're all it sometimes)

The dream gives you the seeker and the false guide. Roy's own posts add a third, funnier face: the rabbit as **fool and prey**. When online critics got mean, Roy reframed them through the food chain — *"jagwars eat rabbits and theyre just being silly,"* *"the silly rabbits on the internet dont know whats coming,"* *"down the rabbit hole. rabbits are below jagwar on the food chain."* The jaguar is the apex self; the rabbit is what gets eaten for commenting from the cheap seats.

But — and this is the turn that keeps it from being mere shade — Roy folds himself straight in: *"yes! and me! and you! We're all silly rabbits sometimes,"* and *"ill catch myself being a silly rabbit when i get mad."* The rabbit isn't only the enemy; it's a *register everyone drops into*, Roy included, usually right when anger takes the wheel. (Which rhymes, of course, with *"a rabbit in a road rage."*) And then the magician's hat shows up to manage it: *"dude. put that silly rabbit back in the hat. love you peypey…"* — affectionate scolding via the conjuror's trick, the same trick as *"I can pull a rabbit out of a hat."* The rabbit you pull out of a hat is a *conjured illusion*; the move is to make the silly-self disappear back into the hat it came from.

So the symbol carries two weights the dream alone doesn't show: the rabbit as **foolish, reactive prey**, and the rabbit as **conjured illusion** — both of them things to be eaten, or pocketed, or made to vanish.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — Year of the Water Rabbit, three burrows, and the chaser/chased inversion
> 2023 was the Chinese zodiac **Year of the Water Rabbit**, and Roy leaned in: *"Year of the water rabbit is really coming in strong in LA"* and *"a crafty rabbit has three burrows"* — the latter a Chinese proverb about keeping multiple escape routes. (In the Mirror Sessions he runs the bit as a joke against itself: *"I did hear it was the year of the water rabbit… I've seen a lot of water, I haven't seen any rabbits yet."*) Two more inversions worth holding: *"Idk. Maybe the rabbits chase us"* (2023) flips predator and prey before *Good Time* even drops — the hunted self becomes the haunting one — and **"Sir Lucius Rabbit"** is Roy's own compound name for the figure, used publicly in 2023 (*"Sir Lucius Rabbit says thank you. - El Roy"* / *"Sir Lucius Rabbit is coming"*). That fused name is the cleanest grounded proof that the seeker-rabbit and the clown-guide are the *same animal seen twice*.

## The Sir Rabbit poem: the authentic version of the dream

The dream is the hijacked arc. The poem is the **authentic** one — the same initiation, run by the right guides instead of the wrong one. Dated 2022 and held in [[Hall of Mirrors]] Room 51, it follows Sir Rabbit — a rabbit with a home in *"a garden full of sound"* — through a full sequence: a Cat leads him to the **Violet Moon**, which turns out to be *"a machine / With whirling gears and funny ears"*; **Mr Fox** traps him in a box; inside the box he recovers a forgotten heartbeat (*"the key is hidden in yourself / Just listen for the knock"*; *"Now you must use your other mind… it's been mapped"*); a **Dove**, a lamp-bearer in an emerald garden tree, releases him; and a golden-caped, grinning **Spider** meets him *"at The Divide"* — *"I'm here to be your guide"* — where Sir Rabbit makes a *chosen* leap *"straight through the Mourning Sun,"* and *"in a flash of lighting he was back where he begun."*

The seeker's defining moment is the Spider's interrogation. *"Who are you Rabbit?… And where does You come from?"* — and Sir Rabbit answers: *"I do not know… but I must be someone."* Identity held open as a question, not yet sealed. That line *is* the rabbit-register: searching, unfixed, but refusing to conclude it's nobody.

Set against the dream, the symmetry is exact and devastating. Both are the same seeker at the same threshold. In the poem the guide is true (the Spider, after the heartbeat is earned) and the ending is a *chosen* leap that transforms him. In the dream the guide is false (Lucius, *before* anything is earned) and the ending is an *involuntary* lightning-crash. Same position, opposite outcome — one initiation completed, one intercepted.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — what the poem grounds: the Fox, the Circle, the box, the other mind
> The *Sir Rabbit* poem is unusually generative — it anchors several framework distinctions on its own:
> - **The Fox is a redeemed trickster, and he is *not* Lucius.** Mr Fox is manipulative in *form* (he traps Rabbit in a box) but medicinal in *result* (the box forces Rabbit inward until the heartbeat returns; he carries a bread the machine can't generate). Lucius is the **counterfeit of the Fox**: helpful-form, parasitic-result. So the cosmology has a trickster *mode* that is benevolent — don't flatten all cunning onto Lucius.
> - **The sharpest [[The Circle|Circle]] / Loop line in the whole system comes from here.** *"Loop = return without transformation; Circle = return with memory restored."* Sir Rabbit ends *"back where he begun"* — the *same point*, the *subject transformed*. That's the Circle, not the Loop.
> - **The box is a missing container-type.** In the [[Keilim - The Breaking of the Vessels|keilim]] framework it supplies a fourth sub-type between *prison* and *vessel*: the trap that becomes the **womb of recovery**.
> - **"Your other mind" = the mind reoriented through the heartbeat** — not a different faculty, the same mind put back *under* the heart.
> - **The Violet-Moon-as-machine** grounds [[Source vs Reflection]]: beauty conceals apparatus (*"the moon… was a machine"*), which mirrors Lucius's helpfulness-precedes-manipulation move exactly.
> - **The Dove is a [[Mary]] / Sophia-*function* guide** — lamp-bearer, emerald garden, helps and then *releases* (the opposite of Lucius, who clings). Distinct from the faceless rabbit-woman of the visualizer; do not conflate the two.
> - **The Divide** is the named edge where the Spider stands and the leap is chosen — see [[The Divide]]. (The poem's Spider is also the [[Hall of Mirrors]] I'M THE SPIDER room.)
> - One spelling flag, held honest: *"Mourning Sun"* is transcribed as written. If intentional, it reads grief-as-solar-passage (mourning, not dawn) and puns *mourning / morning* → the Morning Star → Lucifer / Lucius. Treat as unverified against the artifact image.

## The name research: Janus, the March Hare, and the Gospel of Luke

The rabbit isn't just an image Roy uses — it's one he's *researched out loud*. *"The year begins with Janus and the March Hare"* pairs **Janus**, the two-faced Roman god of thresholds and the new year, with the **March Hare** (the rabbit-ears look) — duality and the rabbit, named in one breath. The same day, he posted a number-correspondence — *"3 = 🪐 / 2+7 = 🌕 / 2+2+6 = 🐇"* — slotting the rabbit (🐇) into a gematria-style scheme alongside the planet and the moon.

And the rabbit-research runs straight into the *name*. On a single day in 2023 Roy wrote that he was *"looking into clues… in a Rudolph Steiner rabbit hole with the Gospel of Luke"* — and **Luke → Luc-ius**. The rabbit hole he says he went down to research the figure is literally a *rabbit hole*, and the gospel at the bottom of it is the one whose name his villain shares. Whether that pun is engineered or found, Roy put it on the page himself.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the maze itself is a rabbit hole
> Worth distinguishing the rabbit-as-*character* from the rabbit-hole-as-*structure*. The [[Hall of Mirrors]] — the web3 puzzle-maze that holds the Sir Rabbit poem — is repeatedly called a "rabbit hole" in its own framing: a culture3 interview describes it as *"a rabbit hole of red 1/1 NFT artifacts,"* and the community talks about *"the rabbit holes we fell down deciphering the videos."* So 🐇 does double duty in the LOOP-era symbol grammar: a *figure* (the seeker / the clown-guide) and a *topology* (the Looking-Glass descent, the maze you fall into). The Room 51 poem is, fittingly, triple-sourced — the Room 51 pages plus three *I'm 33* Notes-app screenshots — and travels with a cat-spirit drawing and a child-knight photo as visual companions; that child-knight image reads as the biographical genesis of [[The Protector]].

## Related traditions

The deepest root is **Lewis Carroll**. The rabbit is **Alice's White Rabbit** — the harbinger you follow down the hole into a surreal, inverted world (here, the descent toward [[The Circus|the Circus]]). The rabbit-ears tie Lucius to the **March Hare**, the one who is *mad* — "mad as a March hare" — which Roy names directly. And the thread keeps going **Through the Looking-Glass**: the *Bad Feeling* circus staging uses Alice's chess pieces and playing cards, stepping the audience through the mirror, with the **White Knight** rhyming against the poem's protective-but-absurd guides and the child-knight companion artifact.

Beyond Carroll: across folklore the rabbit reads as **instinct, vulnerability, survival — *not* power**, which is exactly the register the work assigns it. The **Chinese zodiac** supplies the 2023 Water Rabbit and the *"crafty rabbit has three burrows"* proverb. The **magician's hat** trope ("pull a rabbit out of a hat" / "put it back in the hat") frames the rabbit as conjured illusion. A **trickster / redeemed-trickster** lineage runs through the poem's Fox (the Aesopic fox-with-a-box, medicinal where Lucius is parasitic). And at the esoteric end sits the **Dweller-on-the-Threshold** (Theosophical, via Bulwer-Lytton and Steiner) — the shadow-self met at the gate, which is exactly the rabbit-vs-rabbit, self-vs-self structure the dream narrates. The **Gospel-of-Luke** pun (Luke → Lucius) and **Janus** round out the named set.

## Open questions and one guarded resonance

- **The faceless white-rabbit-masked woman** (*Playing to the Gods* / *City of Angels*) — held explicitly open. She is faceless like [[Mary]], but passive, masked, and led-then-dropped — which reads as *the soul stripped of sovereignty by the spectacle*, or possibly Lucius's own benched anima (rabbit-vs-rabbit extended). **She is not identified as Mary here** — that equation would need a Roy anchor, and there isn't one.
- **Is the rabbit / March-Hare imagery a fixed cipher** in the LOOP-era sigil grammar (🐆🐇 appended to captions), or open thematic recurrence? The vault stance: no cipher asserted yet.
- **The exact date of the *Good Time* dream** precedes the 2023 release; the TikTok telling is later that year; the precise dream-date is unpinned.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the leopard-rabbit (resonance only, do not canonize)
> A striking convergence, flagged precisely because it's *not* Roy's design: **angelbaby**, an AI "metastar" artist, collaborated with Roy on *life is good* (2023), and angelbaby's avatar is a **leopard-patterned rabbit** — fusing Jagwar Twin's two animal motifs (jaguar + rabbit) about two years before the rabbit-eared Lucius. Genuinely arresting. But the avatar is the platform's design, not Roy's, so it stays a *resonance* — noted, never folded into the iconography canon, and no claim that Roy chose the collaboration for it.
