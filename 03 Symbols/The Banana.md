# The Banana

> **One-line reading.** The banana is Sir Lucius's prop — the offering he lifts from the altar and turns into a microphone for his own voice; a sterile clone that carries real light but can't pass it on.

## What it does

In the Roy-versus-Sir-Lucius theatrical series, Lucius repeatedly **plays his songs off a banana** lifted from the set's fruit bowl — a browning one before *Not Your Homie* (*"It's nonsense. It's all nonsense"*), another before *Welcome to the Circus* (*"the algorithm, baby"*). The fruit bowl of green apples and bananas sits on a small altar with red candles, an Egyptian statue beneath, and Tree-of-Life pillars. So the banana is a stable device, not a one-off.

Read against that altar, it does something specific: it is a **hijacked offering**. The altar reads as a site of the concealed feminine — [[Mary]], the Mother — and Lucius takes the gift meant for the sacred and uses it as a PA to broadcast *his own* voice, then sets it back as if untouched. The altar looks intact; inside, it's been repurposed into a delivery system for him.

There's a second layer in the fruit itself. The supermarket banana — the Cavendish — is a **sterile clone**: every plant genetically identical, propagated only by human cutting, unable to vary, and now being wiped out worldwide by a single fungus precisely because they are all the same. That is plain botanical fact, and Roy has glossed it publicly, commenting on the *Bananas* reel that *"most bananas are sterile and cannot reproduce by seed ;)"*. It maps cleanly onto Lucius's loop — recurrence without variation — and onto *City of Angels*'s *"everyone looks just like you"*: real light in a sterile vessel. You can *hear* the song; you cannot *inherit* it.

And Lucius never takes the fruit at its **right time**: the green apple bitten *early* (*"I'm your weirdness"*) is the premature mind; the browning banana scavenged *late* is the overripe id. Both are the proper exchange the altar was built for, performed wrongly. The album's own word for its energy names the same object — *"go bananas,"* go crazy, the comic and consumable thing that you also slip on.

## Related traditions

The altar staging draws on the offering made to a hidden divine feminine — the Mother, the Shekhinah of Kabbalah — whose gift is meant to pass upward and instead gets rerouted. The sterile-clone reading needs no esoteric source; it's modern agriculture's actual problem (the Cavendish and the fungus called TR4), used here as a ready-made image of identical copies that look healthy and cannot reproduce.
