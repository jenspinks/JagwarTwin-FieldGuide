# The Crow

> **One-line reading.** The crow is [[Sir Lucius]]'s bird — **Mercury's** bird: the sharp, clever, scavenging intelligence, the mind's cunning given a familiar. Roy said it himself: *"I'd map a crow onto Mercury."*

## At a glance

- **What it is** — the emblem of the mind-pole: intellect, cleverness, cunning, the scavenger that takes. The crow (constellation **Corvus**) to Roy's [[The Dove|dove]] — Mercury against Venus, mind against heart.
- **Carried by** — [[Sir Lucius]] (Hod / **Mercury**, the analytic intellect), in the simple character-symbol key.
- **Appears in** — Roy's *"I'd map a crow onto Mercury"* post · his signing to **Mercury** Records · the crow-call cued into [[Sir Lucius|Lucius]]'s scene in the theatrical *[[The Interview]]* · Roy's *"midnight crows above"* and *"that was a real crow. no AI!"* posts.
- **Confidence** — grounded fact (Roy's own crow-to-Mercury mapping, the crow-call in the lore) braided with the universal crow-tradition, labeled as such. Note Roy's own hedge (below).

## What it is

The crow is the [[The Dove|dove]]'s exact opposite, and the pair is the point. The dove *descends and gives;* the crow *watches and takes.* Crows are the famous problem-solvers of the bird world — tool-users, face-rememberers, hoarders, opportunists — which makes them the perfect familiar for [[Sir Lucius]], the mind that *"only ever tells the truth"* and deploys it to win. Clever, quick, unsentimental, and always working an angle: that's the crow, and that's Lucius.

It lands where it does because of **Mercury.** Mercury / Hermes is the god of the intellect, of speech and cleverness and trickery, the messenger who moves between worlds — the exact register of Lucius (Hod / Mercury on the [[Kabbalah - The Tree of Life|Tree]]). The crow is Mercury's bird, so the crow is Lucius's bird. And there's a scavenger's edge to it the softer symbols don't have: the crow is a carrion bird, feeding on what's already dead — which rhymes with the *sterile,* second-hand quality the project keeps assigning the shadow (the seedless [[The Banana|banana]], the copy that can't beget, the algorithm reheating what already worked).

## Where it appears

**Roy's "crow onto Mercury" post.** The anchor. Roy has written, in his own voice, *"I'd map a crow onto Mercury"* — his personal correspondence between the bird and the planet of intellect. It sits inside a whole Mercury cluster he's built: he is (was) signed to **Mercury** Records; the [[Hall of Mirrors]] spider is decoded as **Mercury / Hermes** the messenger-psychopomp; and the Hermes / **Mercurius** thread runs through [[Sir Lucius]]'s whole name-and-face research (*"Did Hermes paint his face?"*). The crow is one more node in Roy's Mercury web.

**The crow-call in *[[The Interview]]*.** In the theatrical series, as [[Sir Lucius|Lucius]] runs his manipulation — reminding Roy of the ballad that *"doesn't stream,"* steering him back toward the hit — the audio cues a **crow calling** in the background. The bird is scored *into* Lucius's scenes: the sound of the clever, circling mind.

**"Midnight crows" and "a real crow."** Crows recur across Roy's public posts — *"midnight crows above"* on one artifact-tied post, and, tellingly, *"that was a real crow. no AI!"* on another. The bird is real to him and around him, not just a decode.

## The crow and the dove

Roy's own simple character-key draws the split as a matched pair of birds:

- **[[The Crow|Crow]] → [[Sir Lucius]] → Mercury / Hod** — intellect, cunning, the mind that takes and keeps.
- **[[The Dove|Dove]] → [[Roy]] → Venus / Netzach** — love, beauty, the spirit that descends and releases.

It's the same move as [[The Green Apple]] (mind grasping) against [[The Rose]] (heart revealed): the central heart/mind split, drawn again — this time with wings.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the crow in the traditions (framework, not authorial intent)
> The universal crow braids cleanly into the Lucius reading:
> - **Mercury / Hermes.** The crow is the bird of the trickster-intellect, the psychopomp who crosses between worlds — the same Hermes / Mercurius already threaded through Lucius's face-painting and name research. This is the load-bearing strand, and it's the one Roy states.
> - **Apollo's crow.** In myth the crow was once white and was turned **black** for carrying bad news to Apollo — the truth-teller punished for the truth, the messenger blamed for the message. A neat rhyme for a figure who *"only tells the truth"* and is hunted for it (*STOP SIR LUCIUS*).
> - **Odin's ravens.** Huginn and Muninn — *Thought* and *Memory* — the corvids as the very faculties of mind, sent out to gather and report. The crow as intellect made a bird.
> - **The omen / carrion register.** The crow as harbinger and carrion-eater — decline, the feeding-on-the-dead that shadows the project's *sterile / second-hand* imagery.
> - **Corvus the constellation — the thirst that never ends.** Roy named the *constellation,* Corvus, and it carries its own fable. Apollo sent his crow with a cup to fetch water; the crow *dawdled* (waiting on a fig to ripen), came back late, and *lied* about why. So Apollo fixed it in the sky beside **Crater, the cup** — set forever within sight of the water it was sent for, and never able to drink. Intelligence condemned to unquenchable thirst by its own deceptive framing of the truth: *"the shell is always hungry,"* written into the stars. Its exact counter-image lives on [[The Dove]] — the white **dove on a chalice** artifact, the bird that *reaches* the very cup the crow is denied.
> - **Noah's raven — the bird that never comes home.** In Genesis 8, Noah sends out two birds. The **raven** *"went to and fro"* over the drowned world and **never returned;** the [[The Dove|dove]] returned, then returned bearing the olive leaf, then finally stayed away *because the world was dry and she had arrived.* Read forward onto Roy's own crow/dove split, the two Ark birds already divide on the catalogue's core axis: the raven is **orbit** ([[The Protector|the Protector]]'s velocity, perpetual not-landing, the flight that outruns the wound rather than metabolizing it), the dove is **return** (homecoming). The crow, fittingly, is the twin that keeps moving and never lands — a neat rhyme, given the debut is named *[[Subject to Flooding]].*
>
> **Roy's own hedge, kept in view:** when he mapped the crow to Mercury, he added *"but that's just personal. I don't recommend you do it."* So the crow is real and it's *his* mapping — but he flagged it as personal rather than a rule for readers. His later putting it forward as a Lucius fundamental is what elevates it to the public starter-key; the hedge is why we cite it as a named correspondence, not a decoded law.
