# Pronoun Audit (I-You)

> **One-line reading.** Count the pronouns across two albums and a real pattern appears — *33* is built on "you," *Lucius Lullaby* on "I" — but the sharper truth is that it's the "you" that drains away, not the "I" that hardens.

This is a working note that tests a simple hypothesis by counting: does *33* run on the second person and *Lucius Lullaby* on the first? The answer is yes, and the numbers are clear enough to be worth recording — but the interesting part is *why* the ratio shifts, which the raw count only points toward.

## The count

Whole-word counts across the lyrics of both albums, grouping the pronoun families (I / I'm / my / me / mine against you / you're / your / yours):

| Album | I-cluster | you-cluster | I : you |
|---|---|---|---|
| **33 (2022)** | 166 (3.7%) | 287 (6.3%) | **0.58 — "you"-dominant** |
| **Lucius Lullaby (2026)** | 134 (4.0%) | 82 (2.5%) | **1.63 — "I"-dominant** |

The shift is real and large. The ratio nearly triples. But look at where it comes from: the *you*-density roughly halves (6.3% to 2.5%) while the *I*-density barely moves (3.7% to 4.0%). So the change isn't mainly the "I" getting louder — it's **the "you" collapsing**. The second person — relation, address, the other — drains out of the later record.

## The texture underneath

The averages hide a vivid per-song spread:

- ***33*'s "you"-engine** is built on songs addressed outward: *Down to You* (almost pure address to another person), *Imagine A World* (a collective "you"), *It's Your Time*, *Soul Is A Star*. The album's center of gravity is the second person — the *other* being called toward.
- ***Lucius Lullaby*'s "I"-engine** is self-assertion and performance: *#1 Champion*, *Bananas*, *Playing to the Gods* (the survival-clutch *"if I make it out alive"*), *Bounce*.
- ***Lucius Lullaby*'s leftover "you"** isn't the beloved — it's the audience, and often a refusal: *Not Your Homie* (*"you don't know me"*), *City of Angels*.
- A few late songs **evacuate the pronoun entirely**: *Moths to the Flame* (pure image, no I or you), *The Circle (Lucius Version)* (a one-and-one mantra).

## What it suggests

A reading the count supports but doesn't prove: the project's "I-and-you machine" doesn't just tilt toward the self — it **loses its second person**. *33* faces an other; *Lucius Lullaby* turns that address into spectacle (the "you" becomes the crowd, then gets refused), and the self swells to fill the space the other left behind — the reflection claiming the place of the source, right down at the level of grammar. The album-closer *SOL* then voids the whole axis: it's wordless, so person dissolves completely — no I, no you, only breath.

The honest version of the finding isn't *"the I hardens"* — it's *"the you drains."* That's the sharper and truer shape.

## Caveats

The count is mechanical. It can't tell a "you" that means *beloved* from one that means *crowd* or *God*, or an "I" that's a confession from one that's a brand. That disambiguation has to be done by reading, song by song. And this is only two albums; the 2018 record is an untested third point that would show whether the second person was always there to be lost.
