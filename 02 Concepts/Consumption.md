# Consumption

> **One-line reading.** Consumption is the Circus's digestive system — performers, audiences, and meaning taken in, metabolized for fuel, and spat back out as waste. [[Sir Lucius]] says the quiet part out loud in [[Welcome to the Circus]]: *"I'll chew you up, spit you out."*

## At a glance

- **In one line** — the Circus doesn't house you, watch you, or sell to you. It *eats* you, and the algorithm does the digesting.
- **Where it shows up** — [[Welcome to the Circus]] (*"I'll chew you up, spit you out"*) · [[I Like to Party]] (the force-feeding video) · [[Bad Feeling (Oompa Loompa)]] (the attention economy as a mouth).
- **Connected ideas** — [[The Circus]] · [[The Algorithm]] · [[The Audience]] · [[The Spectacle]] · [[Flattened Identity]] · [[Inversion]] · [[The Mouth]].
- **Confidence** — the naming line and the video are grounded fact; the digestion is a strong reading; the biblical and esoteric apparatus is framework, labeled throughout.

## What it is

Most critiques of fame reach for the same nouns — the machine, the spotlight, the marketplace. The Jagwar Twin world picks a ruder one. The Circus is not neutral architecture and it is not a shop. It is a *stomach*. Things go in, get broken down for energy, and come out the other end no longer useful. Lucius doesn't threaten to defeat you or sell you out. He threatens to *digest* you: *"I'll chew you up, spit you out."* Cheerful, dental, final.

The handy thing about a digestion metaphor is that it scales in every direction. The industry consumes the artist. The Circus consumes the audience. The algorithm consumes attention. The mask consumes the face. The figure on the menu changes from song to song — sometimes it's Roy, sometimes it's the crowd, sometimes it's *you*, the person reading the lyric — but the mechanism never does. Something is taken in, metabolized, and ejected. Consumption is the name for that one move, performed over and over on whoever's closest.

And it's been on the menu since the start. This isn't a Circus-era invention; the earliest node is [[Good Day]] back on *Subject to Flooding* (2018) — consumption as *anesthetic*, the post-scarcity high (*"now we're getting to the peak years,"* *"spending the money that I should save"*) spent on numbing rather than transforming. Not transmutation. Distraction. The Circus didn't teach the catalogue to consume. It just industrialized it.

## How it works

The single most useful thing to know about Consumption is that it is a *counterfeit* of something real — and the real thing is everywhere in the same body of work. The whole drama of the project runs on one fault line: the heart versus the machine that was supposed to serve it. A machine kept under the heart is creation. The same machine cut loose becomes [[The Algorithm]] — optimization severed from feeling, now optimizing *you* into fuel. So Consumption isn't "eating is bad" or generic anti-consumerism. The villain is the *severing*, not the mouth. (More on that guardrail below — it's what keeps this page from collapsing into a poster about screen time.)

Once you have that frame, the catalogue lights up as variations on a single mouth:

- **The crowd as a mouth.** [[Bad Feeling (Oompa Loompa)]] takes Lucius's chew-you-up line and turns it into a buffet: *"carnivores and cannibals, I want it on demand-able / mandible, jaw split."* The attention economy rendered as appetite — people consumed as content, scandal, outrage, on demand. (The internal rhyme literally has words eating each other. The joke is load-bearing.)
- **Attention as the meal.** [[Pay Attention (feat. little luna)]] personifies Attention as a deity that offers you everything you want in exchange for the one thing it actually eats: your awareness. The pun does the whole job — *"Everybody's gotta pay me / You gotta pay Attention."* The currency you spend and the resource it harvests are the *same substance.*
- **The web that eats the spider.** [[Online]] flips the obvious metaphor: *"Internet is like a web and we're the Spider's food."* You think you're navigating; you're being digested like prey. And the system's *"Buy! Buy! Buy!"* turns, by homophone, into *"Bye! Bye! Bye!"* — purchase and farewell as the identical sound.
- **The parasocial bite.** [[Not Your Homie]] (*"Stick it in your eye, bury you alive"*) and the [[Bounce]] line *"you just love me for my lore"* name the consumption that lands closest to home: the audience devours the mythology, the wound, the *content* — and never meets the person. Roy's own framing of *Not Your Homie* as *"the ultimate introverted extrovert anthem"* is the polite version. The bite is that the line is *accurate.*
- **The cheer-machine that eats the exit.** [[Great Time to Be Human]] gives you *"bloodsuckers with crocodile shoes"* throwing *"a party with diamonds and spoons"* — predator and consumer in one figure, death worn as status. And even leaving gets eaten: the *"goodbye"* is swallowed and reabsorbed by the refrain. *"The crooks and believers all sing the same song."* You can't even quit the show; the show consumes your resignation letter.
- **The pitch you swallow and re-transmit.** [[City of Angels]] runs a pronoun rotation — *your* dream → *my* dream → *your* dream — as the city's sales pitch is aimed at him, swallowed (*"my"*), and re-aimed at the next walker. He doesn't escape the beautiful stranger; he *becomes* one. The promise — *"never gonna die… live forever in the bright lights, godlike"* — is the upload/immortality counterfeit, resurrection downgraded to taxidermy.
- **The sweet that clones you.** [[The Watchers]] welds consumption to the clone-fear: *"now they want candy / but I'm not Mandy Moore."* The crowd wants the sweet, edited, manufactured pop-commodity — and *to be consumed sweetly is to be cloned.*

What ties these together, and what keeps the concept honest, is the contrast pole. The Circus doesn't have the only mouth in the catalogue. There's also the mouth that *testifies* — Roy's song, Ballerina Boy's voice, the Song of Love (see [[The Mouth]]). [[Welcome to the Circus]] is brutal precisely because it packs three mouth-*failures* into one song — *"teeth falling out," "screaming, no words come out," "shut your mouth"* — alongside the chew-you-up line. The body that should sing is the body being eaten.

## Where it appears

- **[[Welcome to the Circus]]** — the primary naming. *"I'll chew you up, spit you out"* (the line lands twice). The Ringmaster's fourth-wall snark — *"did you like the show?"* — asks the audience to applaud the harm they just consumed, and *"they love it when you lose control"* is the thesis: the spectacle is powered by the performer's collapse. *"Step right up, shut your mouth"* is the participation-and-suppression contract in five words. Released as a single Jan 16, 2026; track 4 of 15 on [[Lucius Lullaby (album)|Lucius Lullaby]] (March 27, 2026); writers Cody Tarpley, Edan Dover, Rachel Kanner, Roy English; vocals credited to Roy English *and* Sir Lucius.
- **[[I Like to Party]]** — *the cake before the Circus.* The 2021 video (dir. Kyle Vorbach) shows Roy force-fed cake by a jeweled, ringed hand from off-screen, escalating until it's smashed across his face and body — the consumption mechanism rendered *visually* ~five years before *Welcome to the Circus* names it lyrically, with Roy cast as the thing being eaten. The lyric does the recipe: *"Devil's Food is a cake / bake a truth with a lie / then we sell 'em the cake,"* and the kicker, *"we don't want 'em awake."* The whole upbeat party-anthem surface is the camouflage — a clap-along banger about people who *"stay in and pretend that we're in the club."* The cheerfulness *is* the force-feeding. (*33*, 2022; track 4 of 10; the album's most-streamed.)
- **[[Bad Feeling (Oompa Loompa)]]** — on-demand cannibalism; the attention economy as a mouth. *Lucius Lullaby* track 5, originally a Dec 2023 single timed to the *Wonka* film. The nursery bounce is the chocolate over the dread; the one un-jingled rupture is the line *"Are you fucking serious?"* Roy's own modest gloss: *"a chocolate covered reminder to trust your gut."*
- **[[Pay Attention (feat. little luna)]]** — Attention as the consuming deity. *33* (2022), track 3. *"Everybody's gotta pay me / You gotta pay Attention."*
- **[[Online]]** — the spider's food; *"Buy! → Bye!"* The song is itself the sedation it diagnoses — an earworm about being metabolized by earworms.
- **[[The Watchers]]** — the consumed-and-cloned self; *"they want candy."*
- **[[Great Time to Be Human]]** — bloodsuckers; the swallowed goodbye.
- **[[City of Angels]]** — swallowing the pitch; the upload-immortality counterfeit.
- **[[Good Day]]** — the earliest node; consumption-as-anesthetic.
- **[[Not Your Homie]]** / **[[Bounce]]** — parasocial consumption; *"love me for my lore."*
- **The manifesto cards** ([[The Manifesto Cards]]) — the consumerism critique stamped on paratext since ~2020. The Happy Face card frames an infomercial parody: *"ARE YOU CONTENT? CALL NOW"* under a *"BROUGHT TO YOU BUY"* banner (with a ® and a *(TICK)…(TOCK)*), carrying a *"death of my generation"* payload in TV-pastiche register. The literal word *"consume"* — alongside *"BUY"* — appears on the cards.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the "ARE YOU CONTENT?" triple pun
> One infomercial line holds the entire consumerism-versus-seeker critique. *Content* = happy/satisfied (the seeker's question). *Content* = media-to-be-consumed — *are you the content?* — which is exactly what the *"BROUGHT TO YOU BUY"* frame implies. *Content* = complacent, contained, pacified. The card asks all three at once and means all three: are you at peace, are you the product, and are you sitting still while it happens? Routes straight to [[The Algorithm]], Consumption, and [[The Spectacle]].

> [!note]- Advanced reading — Bad Feeling's two-way digestion (the crowd that sings is the crowd that's eaten)
> *Bad Feeling* eats in both directions. V2 turns the audience into appetite — *"carnivores and cannibals… mandible, jaw split… gossip"* — but the Oompa-Loompas doing the singing are *themselves* consumed labor: imported workers paid in cacao beans, used as test subjects, one of them turned into a blueberry. The chorus that sings the warning is the factory's own product and victim. The crowd that sings is the crowd that's eaten. (The chorus phrase *"Everything's Perfect Here"* later became the title of Roy's first gallery show and pop-up — Lauren Powell Projects, LA, opened Jan 20, 2024.) A counter-flag worth keeping: not every devouring image encodes Consumption. The candy-horror wordplay may just be vivid candy-horror wordplay, and Roy's own read is the modest *"trust your gut."* Hold the systems-critique and the gut-feeling at once — the gap between them is itself a wink.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the Saturn-cake (the mythic key to the force-feeding)
> The official *I Like To Party* cover art plates a cake as **Saturn / Cronus the devourer** — the god who eats his own children — ringed by a circle of skulls (*memento mori*). The tagline reads *"A NEW DIRECTION AFFECTING THE STATE OF YOUR MIND"* in flat infomercial register. This is the decoder for the video: the force-feeding isn't random cruelty, it's the *devouring-parent / time-the-devourer* archetype made literal. A birthday cake plated as the child-eating god and garnished with skulls is also, deadpan, the best one-line summary of the whole concept: *the party that eats you.*

> [!note]- Advanced reading — consumed-and-discarded versus consumed-and-transformed (the central device)
> This is the interpretive spine of the page, and it works as a *framework comparison* — something the songs invite, not something they assert. The biblical tradition is full of being *swallowed*: **Jonah** (Jonah 1–2), taken down into the dark for three days and returned *changed*; the **Eucharist** — *"this is my body, broken for you"* (Luke 22:19) — a consumption that is voluntary and nourishing. Those are descent-and-return structures: you go in, and you come back transformed. The Circus uses the same verbs and reverses the outcome. *"I'll chew you up, spit you out"* is the **anti-Eucharist**: the Christ-speech *form* (this is my body) loaded with predatory *content* (chew / spit). Predatory consumption, ejection without nourishment, the body broken *for nothing.*
>
> | The descent-and-return (Jonah) | The Circus |
> |---|---|
> | Swallowed whole | Chewed — active mastication |
> | Three days in darkness | No pause; immediate ejection |
> | Prayer from inside the belly | No prayer noted |
> | Returned to the surface | Spit out |
> | Returns transformed | Returns as waste |
> | Obeys his mission after | Discarded; show over |
>
> The two rows people skip are the load-bearing ones: the **prayer in the belly** and the **obedience after.** Jonah's descent works because something happens *in* the dark and *changes him on the way out*. The Circus offers neither — no pause to pray, no self to send back on a mission. That's the difference between an initiation and a meal.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the expanded tradition (Sheol, the beasts, Moloch, Marx)
> The image of consumption-as-adversary runs deep and wide:
> - **Sheol as a mouth** — *"Sheol enlarges its appetite and opens its mouth beyond measure"* (Isaiah 5:14); the underworld as the consuming void.
> - **The roaring lion** — *"the devil, as a roaring lion, walketh about, seeking whom he may devour"* (1 Peter 5:8); predation as the adversary's mode.
> - **The beasts that consume kingdoms** — Daniel 7 / Revelation 13; empire itself as a consumption-system.
> - **Moloch** — the god children were sacrificed to (2 Kings 23:10; Jeremiah 7:31); consumption as false-god worship. Allen Ginsberg's *Howl* revives Moloch for industrial capitalism — the machine-god fed on the living.
> - **Capital / commodity** — Marx's labor consumed and expelled as surplus value; the worker chewed up and spat out with minimal return. The Circus is that factory floor with better lighting.
> - **The Roman arena** — *Panem et Circenses* (Juvenal, *Satires* X, c.100 CE): the crowd is sedated not by force but by pleasure, and the literal Christian martyr is consumed by the entertainment apparatus for the spectators' enjoyment. The chains are fun. See [[Panem et Circenses]].

> [!note]- Advanced reading — attention as a deity (Pay Attention, and the Marduk note)
> *Pay Attention* is the attention-consumption prototype, and the structure is exact: Attention is the hidden god of the modern world, and what it consumes is *awareness* — the very faculty you'd need to notice it eating you. The currency you *spend* (*"pay me"*) and the resource it *extracts* (*"pay Attention"*) are one substance, which is why the temptation works through consent: the mark already wants what's offered. The grounded vocal roles read Roy as **Attention** and little luna as the guide/introducer. And the working method is on the record: Roy's *33* board, in his own hand, asks *"How to anthropomorphize attention? → Marduk"* — personifying Attention-as-deity (after the Babylonian creator who orders the cosmos by *word*) was an explicit move, not a critic's projection. (The same board's *"Let Them Eat Cake"* tracklist line was later realized as the Hall of Mirrors artifact *"Caution, All Kings End,"* code **CAKE** — tying the board back to *I Like to Party*'s cake.)

> [!note]- Advanced reading — nigredo without completion
> In alchemy, the **nigredo** — the blackening, the rot, the dissolution — is the *first* stage; the prima materia is broken down *in order to* be reconstituted as gold. The Circus specializes in the blackening that never completes: prima materia endlessly processed, exhausted, and never reconstituted. *"I'll chew you up, spit you out"* is dissolution with the gold-step amputated — Jonah digested but not returned, the Eucharist inverted, nigredo arrested mid-rot. Consumption *without* transformation is the whole signature. See [[Nigredo]].

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the consuming fire and the bush that doesn't burn
> Two fires sit at the edge of this concept. *"Our God is a consuming fire"* (Hebrews 12:29) — the same fire that purifies *also* consumes whatever can't endure it; consumption isn't always the adversary's, sometimes it's the refiner's. Against it, the burning bush of Exodus 3 *that is not consumed* — presence that radiates without being used up. The Circus's fire only ever takes the first form and never the second: it burns, and there's nothing left. See [[Fire]] and [[Moths to the Flame]], where the moth gets the death without the rebirth — the counterfeit of the phoenix.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the severing, not the eating (the guardrail that keeps this from being a screen-time poster)
> Consumption is the Circus's mode of a much larger split — Source vs Reflection, Machine vs Human. The antagonist is **optimization severed from the heart**, not machinery and not appetite per se. A machine subordinate to the heart is creation; the same machine cut loose becomes the Algorithm that consumes. This matters because it stops the concept from flattening into generic anti-consumerism: the project is not against *eating*, or *attention*, or even *the internet*. It's against the moment the tool stops serving feeling and starts feeding on it. Lose this guardrail and Consumption becomes a poster about putting your phone down. Keep it and it stays a diagnosis of a *severing*. See [[Machine vs Human]].

## Source notes

- **Grounded (released music, public posts, official videos).** The naming line — *"I'll chew you up, spit you out"* — in [[Welcome to the Circus]] (appears twice), with its release facts and co-credit to Roy English and Sir Lucius. The [[I Like to Party]] force-feeding video (dir. Kyle Vorbach, 2021) and its lyrics (*"Devil's Food is a cake," "we don't want 'em awake"*). The *I Like to Party* Saturn-cake cover art and tagline. The [[Bad Feeling (Oompa Loompa)]], [[Pay Attention (feat. little luna)]], [[Online]], [[The Watchers]], [[Great Time to Be Human]], [[City of Angels]], [[Good Day]], [[Not Your Homie]] lyrics quoted here. The manifesto-card text (*"ARE YOU CONTENT?," "BROUGHT TO YOU BUY," "consume," "BUY"*). Roy's *"the algorithm baby"* and *"chocolate covered reminder to trust your gut"*; his *"introverted extrovert anthem"* and *"co-creators"* framings; the *33* board notes (*Marduk*, *Let Them Eat Cake*) ([[Public Posts]]). Biblical quotations are verifiable text.
- **Strong readings.** The Circus-as-digestion; the cake video as the consumption mechanism rendered before it's named; the multi-directional reach (industry / audience / algorithm / mask); the attention-as-deity, spider's-food, and parasocial readings; the Saturn-cake as the mythic key; *to be consumed sweetly is to be cloned.* Built on grounded anchors, but interpretive — read as such.
- **Framework (esoteric / scriptural — not authorial intent).** The Jonah / Eucharist contrast table as the central device; the anti-Eucharist and Inversion engine; the expanded tradition cluster (Sheol, Daniel/Revelation, Moloch + Ginsberg, Marx); the nigredo-without-completion reading; the consuming-fire pole. The tradition is the lens this guide brings; the songs invite the comparison, they don't assert it.
- **What's still open.** The [[Bounce]] line *"you just love me for my lore"* is a strong read on a word pending audio verification. And see the open question below on *who is actually doing the eating.*

> [!note]- Advanced reading — open questions
> - **Who is the actual consumer?** The song pins the eating on Lucius-as-ringmaster, but behind Lucius is a Boss, and behind the Boss may be the industry, capital, or the Algorithm itself ([[BMRC]] is the candidate production-house-and-weapons-brand — but its decode is unconfirmed). The consumption may be further upstream than it looks.
> - **Is being chewed up the *start* of something?** Is there a transformation-through-consumption reading the *Welcome to the Circus* timeframe simply hasn't reached yet — is this the Jonah-moment *before* the prayer in the belly?
> - **Diagnosis without exit?** Juvenal diagnosed *panem et circenses* but prescribed nothing. Is the project the same — naming the Circus without naming a way out — or is there a way to *bear witness within* it rather than escape or be eaten? ([[Down to You]]'s *"bread alone"* is the cleanest candidate for a built-in answer.)
> - **Audience: failure or feature?** Is the crowd's love of watching you lose control a *moral failure* of the audience, or a *designed feature* of a system the audience can't be blamed for?
> - **What can't be metabolized?** Is there a song built to *fail* by the Algorithm's metrics — anti-consumption by design? And the standing puzzle: why can't Lucius absorb the Song of Love? An unconsumable object would be the exact shape of the answer.
