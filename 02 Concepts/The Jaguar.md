# The Jaguar

> **One-line reading.** The jaguar is the project's emblem of seeing — the creature that looks into other beings and, in turn, looks back into itself, which makes the whole project a mirror of consciousness.

The name [[Jagwar Twin]] is built on an image, and [[Roy]] has explained it the same way across years of interviews: the jaguar is *"the creature who looks into the souls of other people and then, in turn, can look back into itself."* His gloss is that *"humans are mirrors for each other."* So the jaguar isn't a mask in the story or a character on the stage — it's the *frame* the whole project happens inside: reflexive seeing, looking outward and back at once.

## What it is

Roy has given this etymology publicly in at least three interviews (2018, 2021, and 2024), and the 2024 telling adds that the jaguar *"pulls people out of the darkness… into the light."* The "Twin" half completes the picture: *Jagwar* (the seer / the mirror) plus *Twin* (the paired self) — observer and observed in one name. The jaguar names the seeing; the twin names the doubling.

Two things keep it from flattening into a single idea:

- **Seeing has no fixed starting point.** Across different tellings the order flips — sometimes the jaguar looks into others first and then back into itself, sometimes the reverse. That isn't a contradiction; the mirror-loop is the doctrine, and a loop has no first step. The reciprocity *is* the meaning.
- **The field sings as well as sees.** The etymology centers vision, but the jaguar-figure's defining act elsewhere in the work is *teaching the song*. So the emblem is voice as much as sight — one capacity with two faces, seeing-into-beings and sounding-the-world. It's worth guarding against reading the seeing-field as pure surveillance: it's also the original singer.

Roy has kept restating the mirror idea in recent posts — *"relationships are fundamentally about two people reflecting to each other what they need to know to become more of who they are,"* and *"to love is to meet the hidden part of yourself in someone else."* The jaguar's *"humans are mirrors for each other"* is a standing, repeated idea, not a one-off bit of name-trivia. It is, in effect, the [[Source vs Reflection]] master-frame stated as a creature.

## Not a fourth character

It's tempting to read the jaguar as a fourth figure standing alongside Roy, [[Sir Lucius]], and [[Mary]]. It isn't. The jaguar is the *field* — the seeing the whole project takes place within — not a peer who acts inside the story. Pushing it into a neat four-way scheme (one figure per symbolic beast) overfits the work; that kind of one-to-one belongs to a specific biblical paratext, not to the core figures.

## An open question

What stays genuinely unsettled is how the jaguar relates to [[Mary]], the project's feminine source-figure and rightful seer. Two readings sit side by side. On one, the jaguar is a *genderless capacity* — the seeing-field — distinct from Mary, who sees rightly *inside* it. On the other, the project deliberately collapses the two: the seeing and the seer as one function with two faces. The work's own mythology actually personifies the jaguar as a feminine maker who *"made other jaguars, then taught them to sing"* — which leans toward the second reading. But Roy hasn't equated them by name, so the question is best left open rather than locked. Within the field, the two *modes* of seeing are the project's real fork: [[Sir Lucius]] as surveillance, Mary as witnessing.
