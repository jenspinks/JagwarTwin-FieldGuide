# Hermeticism - As Above So Below

> **One-line reading.** Hermeticism is the opposite of treating the world as a trap: it says the cosmos is one coherent, legible law, the same pattern repeating at every scale — and Roy not only studies it, he printed its central maxim, *"as above, so below,"* on a music video years before anyone decoded his sources.

This is the most directly documented of the three esoteric traditions the project draws on. The seven principles of *The Kybalion* — a popular Hermetic primer — are transcribed in full on Roy's "I'm 33" board, and the phrase **"AS ABOVE SO BELOW"** appeared publicly on the end-card of his 2021 *Down to You* video. So the engagement is real and on the record. What follows is the tradition in plain terms, then how the work resonates with it. The specific song-by-song mappings are an interpretive lens — a frame the songs fit — not a claim about how they were written.

## What Hermeticism is

Hermeticism is a Western esoteric tradition rooted in late-antique texts attributed to a legendary sage, Hermes Trismegistus. Its single most famous idea is the maxim **"as above, so below"**: whatever pattern holds at the largest scale (the cosmos, the divine) repeats at every smaller one (the world, the body, the mind). The universe is not a trap to escape but a coherent, lawful order you can *read* and *climb* — one design echoing through every level.

Much of Hermeticism's modern popularity comes from *The Kybalion* (1908), which boils the tradition down to **seven principles**:

1. **Mentalism** — "The All is Mind." Reality is fundamentally mental; the universe is a thought.
2. **Correspondence** — "as above, so below." The same pattern recurs at every scale. (This is the famous one.)
3. **Vibration** — nothing rests; everything is in motion, everything is a frequency.
4. **Polarity** — everything is dual; opposites are two ends of one thing.
5. **Rhythm** — everything swings, like a pendulum, between its poles.
6. **Cause and Effect** — every cause has its effect; nothing is mere chance.
7. **Gender** — a masculine and a feminine principle operate in everything.

The throughline is *correspondence* and *coherence*: a legible cosmos, not a deceptive one.

## How the work uses it

Several of the seven principles surface in the catalogue — most clearly where Roy has restated the underlying idea publicly.

**Mentalism** — "The All is Mind" — sits under the project's consciousness-and-upload imagery: a reality that is, at bottom, mind.

**Correspondence** — "as above, so below" — is the principle Roy engages most directly. He has his own name for it, *the Pattern* — the idea that the same shape is present on every level. The mirror runs on the same logic (what's above reflected below), and the maxim itself was printed on the 2021 video end-card.

**Vibration** is the one with the most public anchors. Roy states the doctrine plainly in interviews — *"Everything is a vibrational frequency. Light, matter, sound, it's all the same thing at variable speeds"* — and the released image is *"My head's an antenna"* ([[Life Is Good]]): mind as a receiver tuned to a frequency. The principle has a darker register too. The same idea of mind-affecting-mind shows up as surveillance and intrusion in [[Online]] (*"they infiltrate my brain"*) — the doctrine of induction inverted from a gift into a violation. And it has a destructive register: *"Jericho fell like a wave"* ([[Loser]]) is frequency turned to collapse — vibration that dismantles walls.

**Polarity** shows up as the project's deep concern with duality — the Roy/Lucius split, the "one becomes two" shape of the whole story. The Kybalion's Polarity principle teaches that opposites are two ends of one thing, which is why the resolution is never to defeat one pole but to re-order the pair. A song like [[Life Is Good]] even enacts the principle's refusal to pick a side: *"keep a smile on my face, too happy to choose"* declines the this-side/that-side split.

## Three traditions, kept distinct

Hermeticism is one of three esoteric streams the project draws on, and it's worth not blending them. Hermeticism is **world-affirming**: the cosmos is a coherent law to read and climb. That is the opposite of [[Gnosticism - The World as Trap]], which treats the cosmos as a false copy to flee — even though the two can share imagery (descent, ascent, the spark). And the Hermetic stream's tarot-and-correspondence material is a *different* lineage from the Jewish Kabbalah of [[Kabbalah - The Tree of Life]], even though both use a "Tree." The same symbol can carry opposite meanings depending on which tradition you're standing in, so it's cleaner to name the stream each time than to merge them.

## A note on scope

Two honest cautions. First, that Roy transcribed the seven principles onto his board proves he *read and collected* the Kybalion — it's a reading-list, not a proven compositional grammar. The leap from "he wrote it down" to "the songs run on it" is interpretive. Second, seven principles this broad — duality, rhythm, cause and effect — fit almost any body of work, which is exactly the risk a correspondence-frame has to watch: a lens that matches everything explains nothing. So the mappings here are offered where they genuinely illuminate, not as proof. Roy himself supplies the right caution: speaking of his own correspondence-making, he said *"I would map a crow onto Mercury but that's just personal. I don't recommend you do it."* The author disowning the method as idiosyncratic is the ceiling on any claim of intent — and worth honoring.
