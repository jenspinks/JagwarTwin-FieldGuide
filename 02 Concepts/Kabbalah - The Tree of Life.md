# Kabbalah - The Tree of Life

> **One-line reading.** Kabbalah's Tree of Life is the map the Jagwar Twin world keeps drawing on without naming: a single source of light and a row of bodies that only reflect it — which is exactly the shape of Roy, Lucius, and Mary.

This is a reference page. The Tree of Life is the structural backbone behind a lot of the project's imagery, so it's worth setting out plainly once and linking back to. Roy openly studies Kabbalah — his "I'm 33" board lists the Tree of Life and its *sephirot* among his sources, and he wears the Hebrew phrase *El Roi* as a tattoo — so this is a tradition he genuinely engages, not one imposed from outside. What follows is the tradition first, then the ways the work resonates with it. Treat the mappings as a lens the songs fit, not as a doctrine the artist has stated.

## What the Tree of Life is

Kabbalah is the mystical tradition of Judaism. Its central diagram is the **Tree of Life** — a map of how a single, infinite divine source pours itself "downward" into the created world through ten stages. Those ten stages are the **sefirot** (singular *sefirah*): vessels or filters, each catching a measure of the original light and passing it on. Think of one sun and a series of mirrors arranged below it: only the sun makes light; everything beneath it shines by reflection.

The ten sefirot most relevant here:

- **Keter** — the Crown; the highest, the point nearest the infinite source.
- **Binah** — Understanding; the great mother, the womb the lower world is born from.
- **Tiferet** — Beauty; the heart of the Tree, its balanced center. In a sun-and-mirrors picture, **Tiferet is the sun** — the source the lower vessels reflect.
- **Netzach** — Victory; associated with the planet Venus and with display, brilliance, the herald.
- **Hod** — Splendour; associated with intellect and strategy.
- **Yesod** — Foundation; the **reflective sphere**, the realm of images, sometimes called the Treasure House of Images. It generates no light of its own; it relays what's above it downward.
- **Malkhut** — Kingdom; the lowest sefirah, the created world we live in. It is also called the **Shekhinah** — the indwelling divine presence, the feminine face of God *in exile*, a moon reflecting a light that isn't its own and longing to be restored.

The sefirot are also arranged in **three vertical columns, or "pillars."** The right pillar is Mercy, the left pillar is Severity (judgment), and the central pillar is the pillar of balance and return — the one that holds the others in equilibrium.

## How the work uses it

Read through the Tree, the project's central figures fall into place as one source and its reflections.

**The sun is the heart.** In this reading, **Tiferet — the balanced center, the sun of the Tree — is the heart-pole** the whole catalogue orbits, the place the song [[SOL]] (Spanish for *sun*) points to. Everything else on the axis is a body that makes no light of its own.

**Roy is the moon.** Roy occupies the lunar position — Malkhut and Yesod, the reflective spheres. The recurring image of *"the moon trying to become a sun"* is his arc: the reflected vessel reaching to be restored to full light. This is the structure under his phrase *"reclaim your crown."* In the Kabbalah, there is a strand where Malkhut — the lowest, most diminished sefirah — is itself raised to become a crown on the return journey. Roy's "reclaim your crown" rhymes precisely with that ascent.

**Mary is the Shekhinah.** [[Mary]], the divine-feminine presence of the songs, reads cleanly as the Shekhinah — the reflected divine light in exile, longing to return. Where Roy holds the moon's *position*, Mary holds its *theology*: the face-hidden presence promising a light that is coming. Her line *"saw a halo but I didn't see a head"* reads as the crown glimpsed but not yet possessed.

**Lucius is the morning star.** [[Sir Lucius]] maps to Venus — Netzach, the morning star that heralds the dawn and is routinely mistaken for the sun it precedes. (*Lucifer* is simply the Latin name for that morning star.) His method works through Hod and Geburah — intellect, strategy, severity — and his *operating theater* is Yesod, the realm of images. That is why the project's broken or counterfeit imagery (an artificial apple, a sterile copy) reads as **Yesod gone wrong**: the image-realm producing reflections that have forgotten their source.

So the reflecting side of the Tree holds two bodies — the Moon (Roy and Mary) and the Morning Star (Lucius) — and the Sun at the center is the light all three orbit and **none of them is.**

The pillars sharpen the relationships. On this reading Mary sits at the head of the left pillar (Binah, the source of judgment) while Lucius sits lower on that same pillar — so when Mary "drops Lucius dead" in [[#1 Champion (la Conquistadora)]], it reads as the origin of judgment overruling the shadow-intelligence beneath it. And Roy stands alone on the central pillar of balance — the structural reason he is the one who passes through the pressure and can still say *"I am still I."*

## Where the Tree breaks

There is a companion idea worth naming, because the project leans on it just as hard. In one strand of Kabbalah (the Lurianic), the original vessels could not hold the light that was poured into them and **shattered** — a primordial catastrophe called the *breaking of the vessels*. The repair of that break, the gathering-up of the scattered sparks of light, is **tikkun**: the healing of the world.

That is the deep shape under *"my mind must serve my heart."* The shattered Tree is a set of faculties that have come apart and stopped cooperating; the repair is re-ordering them — the mind (the morning star's intellect) brought back under the heart (the sun), the moon restored to the sun's light. The work isn't to destroy the broken parts but to put them back in right relation.

One honest caution lives here. Lurianic Kabbalah is **world-affirming** — the broken world is to be *redeemed*, the sparks raised where they fell. That is a different impulse from the *escape*-the-world frame the project sometimes sounds (see [[Gnosticism - The World as Trap]]). The two traditions share a lot of imagery — descent, exile, return — but they point in opposite directions, and the work holds both in tension rather than choosing. It's worth keeping them distinct rather than blending them into one.

## A note on scope

These mappings are a framework lens. They are licensed by Roy's real, documented engagement with Kabbalah — the board, the *sefirot*, the *El Roi* tattoo — but they are not claims that he composed the catalogue by laying it on the Tree. The fit is genuine and recurring; that is reason to find it illuminating, not reason to call it intent. The alignment with neighboring traditions (Hermeticism, Neoplatonism) follows from the shared esoteric heritage Roy draws on, not from deliberate cross-encoding.
