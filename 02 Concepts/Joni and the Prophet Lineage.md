# Joni and the Prophet Lineage

> **One-line reading.** Joni Mitchell is a genuine, credited presence in the work — but the leap from "Joni is in the songs" to "Roy positions himself as a prophet in her lineage" is a leap, and this page keeps the two firmly apart.

This is a working note, not a finished claim. There's a real Joni Mitchell thread running through the catalogue, and there's a *temptation* to extend it into a self-mythology — Roy as prophet-heir. The first is grounded; the second isn't. The discipline here is to hold them at different levels and not let the solid part vouch for the speculative part.

## What's grounded

- **Joni Mitchell is a blessed cowriter on *Soul Is A Star*** — stated by Roy directly. That's the anchor, and it's solid.
- **A Joni substrate runs through the work** more broadly — the kinds of songs and themes associated with her (*"Woodstock," "The Circle Game,"* the *Ladies of the Canyon* register) surface across the catalogue. Wherever there's a credit or a statement, Joni belongs in the reading as a real influence.

## The leap to avoid

The move from there to *"therefore Roy consciously identifies as a prophet in the Joni lineage"* is a jump, not a finding. The word "prophets" does appear in *Soul Is A Star* (*"died before Prophets ever turned"*) — but a lyric using a word is not the artist identifying with it. A single occurrence in a song doesn't establish a self-image.

There's a specific trap underneath this, too: a "prophets" quotation got mis-sourced in earlier passes, its true origin unsettled. No "prophet" argument should be built on a quotation whose source isn't pinned down. Get the provenance right first; build the reading second.

## The rule for this lead

- **Joni counts as an influence** wherever a credit, a caption, a lyric, or a statement pins it — and the *Soul Is A Star* credit does exactly that.
- **The "prophet" alignment stays speculative** unless something direct — a credit, a caption, a lyric, or a statement from Roy — actually names him as prophet or heir. Until then it's a *possible* lineage, logged and held, not asserted.

This is the honest shape of the thing: a confirmed influence, and an unconfirmed self-positioning that may simply be available as a theme without ever being claimed.
