# Joni and the Prophet Lineage

> **One-line reading.** Joni Mitchell is a real, credited, *blessed* presence in the Jagwar Twin world — and "Roy positions himself as a prophet in her lineage" is a much bigger claim that the credit does not pay for. This page holds the two apart on purpose.

The temptation is irresistible and you should resist it. There's a genuine Joni Mitchell thread running through the catalogue — credited, on the record, mind-blowing by Roy's own account. And then there's the *story* you want to tell on top of it: Roy as prophet-heir, anointed by the high priestess of confessional folk, carrying the torch down a sacred line. The first is grounded. The second is a vibe wearing the first as a costume. The whole discipline of this page is to keep the solid part from vouching for the speculative part — to enjoy the leap without pretending we've landed it.

## At a glance

- **In one line** — A confirmed influence (Joni co-wrote and *blessed* a song on *33*) braided with an unconfirmed self-mythology (Roy as "prophet" in her line) that may simply be available as a theme without ever being claimed.
- **Where it shows up** — [[Soul Is A Star]] (the grounded anchor — Joni's co-write, the "Prophets" lyric) · [[Down to You]] (a candidate second Joni dialogue on *33*) · [[SOL]] (where the grounding deliberately stops)
- **Connected ideas** — [[Inherited Substrate]] · [[The Thread]] · [[The Circle]] · [[Sir Lucius]] · [[Witnessing vs Surveillance]] · [[Mary]] · [[Duality]]
- **Confidence** — One hard grounded floor (the co-write + blessing, Roy-stated); a rich layer of strong interpretation (the *Woodstock* weld); and a clearly-marked speculative ceiling (the "prophet" alignment), kept firmly above the floor.

## What it is

Two things are true at once, and most of the work here is not confusing them.

**Thing one, solid:** Joni Mitchell is a credited co-writer on [[Soul Is A Star]], track 5 of *33* (2022). Not a sample cleared by lawyers and forgotten — Roy has said, in his own words, that she *gave him her blessing.* That is about as good as grounding gets: the artist himself naming the collaboration and calling it mind-blowing.

**Thing two, slippery:** the released lyric of that same song contains the word *"Prophets,"* and the air around the whole catalogue hums with a tempting narrative — that Roy sees himself as a prophet, an heir, the next link in a line that runs through Joni. That story is *seductive*, partly true-feeling, and not actually established by anything. A song using a word is not an artist claiming a crown.

So the shape is honest and a little funny: a real anointing, and a self-coronation that nobody has confirmed actually happened. We keep the anointing on the record and the coronation in the "maybe" pile.

## How it works

What the Joni thread *does* across the work is supply a method, not just a name-drop. Roy's whole creative engine runs on **inherited substrate** — old material consciously carried forward, credited, transformed. Joni sits in that roster of credited transmissions alongside the likes of Johnny Mercer, Charlie Chaplin, and Norman Rockwell (see [[Inherited Substrate]]). She isn't a poster on the wall; she's load-bearing structure.

The grounded home of all this is [[Soul Is A Star]], and the specific Joni song behind the credit is **"Woodstock"** (from *Ladies of the Canyon*, 1970). Once you know that, the song reveals itself as a careful **weld** to Joni's — two writers building one inheritance. The borrowing, importantly, is *not* a quotation: Roy's lyric never reproduces Joni's famous "we are stardust / we are golden / back to the garden." The closest textual echo is his bridge, *"We fall down as seeds on the ground,"* sung four times — a seed/garden rhyme to her destination, not a lift. The credit grounds that *something* of Joni's is in there; the borrowed element is most plausibly musical or melodic. (Whether her actual *voice* appears on the track is unconfirmed — and that open question turns out to carry surprising weight; see the Advanced reading on the interpolation.)

The thesis the song builds, and the reason the Joni thread matters beyond trivia, is **transmission**: a lineage handed down corrupted but recoverable, wound and wisdom inherited together, then called back by name. That's the engine. Joni is the proof-of-concept and the co-author of it at once.

And the "prophet" piece? It stays a guest, never a resident. The word *"Prophets"* appears once, in one line, in one song. There is no caption, no credit, no lyric elsewhere, and no statement from Roy that names him prophet or heir. A single occurrence does not build a self-image. So the alignment is **logged and held** — a possible lineage, available as a theme — not asserted as a finding. (There's also a provenance snag in the neighborhood worth knowing about; see the Advanced reading on the "prophets quote.")

## Where it appears

**[[Soul Is A Star]]** — *33* (2022), track 5 of 10, 3:17. The grounded center of everything. Writers: Roy English, Joni Mitchell, Matt Pauling. The opening sets the whole transmission frame:

> *I met a Man / Who had a Son / But He lost Him to The World / He said My Son was a singer / He wrote songs you never heard.*

And the line that lends this page its name, the released text (Spotify-stable):

> *He took his life / He gave it up / Before Prophets ever turned.*

Two notes on that couplet. First, an earlier vault draft misread it as *"profits"* — corrected; the release reads *"Prophets."* The *profits*/devil's-bargain sense is almost certainly a deliberate homophone Roy is happy to let you hear, but the literal word is *Prophets.* Second, the title itself is sourced — Roy has said it comes from the Egyptian Book of the Dead, *"behold, thy soul is a living star."* The album's governing question lands in this same song, at the literal midpoint of the record: *"Do you know Who you are?"*

**[[Down to You]]** — *33* (2022), track 7. A *candidate* second Joni dialogue, not a confirmed one. Joni has her own "Down to You" (*Court and Spark*, 1974), and the verbatim hook — *"it all comes down to you"* — is shared, on a duality text both songs are running. Tempting. But the phrase is ordinary idiomatic English, Roy's track carries no Joni credit, and the song-essay itself still carries an older "no Joni bridge" caution. So it stays a candidate. (The ledger rule that keeps it honest is in the Advanced reading.)

**[[SOL]]** — *Lucius Lullaby* (2026), the wordless closer. This is where the Joni grounding *stops.* No credit, no caption, no melodic match makes SOL Joni-grounded, and influence-bleed forward is explicitly blocked. SOL and Soul Is A Star clearly rhyme as ideas — both are a transmission surviving partial loss — but reading SOL as "the Joni-borrowed song surfaced as the breath beneath all the verses" is an interpretive collapse, held as one analyst's position, never as grounding.

**The childhood floor.** Per the Masquerade Atlanta venue bio, Roy "grew up serenaded by the lucid new romance storytelling of Joni Mitchell and Bob Dylan, who the singer's mom played around the house." (That's published PR copy, third-person — citable as a statement, not as Roy's own first-person words.) And in Discord, Roy has named Joni among his current listening (2022) and acknowledged a fan's Joni reference. The influence is neither invented nor recent.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the *Woodstock* weld (the parallel inventory)
> The cleanest way to see the welding is line by line. The lines exist on both sides (grounded); that they *rhyme* is the reading. Joni's *Woodstock* on the left, *Soul Is A Star* on the right:
>
> - **Chance meeting with a stranger** — "came upon a child of God" ↔ "I met a man who had a son"
> - **The humble request** — "can I walk beside you?" ↔ "can I sing you just one verse?"
> - **The identity question** — "I don't know who I am" ↔ "do you know who you are?"
> - **Stardust → star** — "we are stardust" ↔ "your soul is a star"
> - **Garden → seeds** — "back to the garden" ↔ "we fall down as seeds on the ground." (He supplies the *Fall*; she supplies the *destination.*)
> - **The bargain / trap** — "caught in the devil's bargain" ↔ "gave it up before Prophets ever turned" (the *Prophets*/profits homophone landing on Joni's own fame-bargain note)
>
> One correction worth banking: *"caught in the devil's bargain"* **is Joni's own line** — it's in her official published *Woodstock* lyrics, consecutive with "billion year old carbon" in the final chorus. An earlier dossier claim that it was a Stills/CSNY-only alteration was wrong and is retired. The bargain belongs to Joni, which makes its echo in Roy's "devil's bargain" register a borrowing from her, not from the cover.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the girl carries Joni (verse 2)
> The second verse hides Joni inside it without ever flatly naming her — the discovery is meant to be *walked*, breadcrumb by breadcrumb, and every crumb is grounded:
>
> - Joni co-wrote the song (the only [credit-level] fact you need to license the rest).
> - The Witness who "came upon the child of God" in 1969 is literally her *Woodstock.*
> - The verse-2 girl with a *"bullet in her genes"* rhymes against Joni's *"the cleaner's press was in my jeans"* (*The Boho Dance*) — the **genes/jeans** hinge.
> - She *"wanted Love / But she got Fame / And Power from The Thieves"* — pure Joni fame-bargain lexicon (more below).
> - Joni is the childhood voice the mother "played around the house."
>
> The verse's *"dark father"* — *"papa was a sinner playin God behind the scenes"* — reads as the **star-maker / industry patriarch** (compare Joni's own Geffen portrait in *Free Man in Paris*), **not** Joni's literal father. That keeps the whole adoption non-defamatory: we're mapping the music-industry myth she lived, not slandering anyone's dad.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — "The Thieves" and "For Free" (Joni's industry lexicon)
> The capitalized *"Power from The Thieves"* maps onto Joni's documented commodification vocabulary — the "star maker machinery," the "golden egg tossed by the company," the thieves who "buy and sell" (across *For the Roses*, *Free Man in Paris*, *The Boho Dance*, *Taming the Tiger*). Because Joni co-wrote the song, that lexicon plausibly enters through *her* register rather than being independently reinvented.
>
> *For Free* maps by **inversion**: Joni's busker who plays "real good, for free" and goes unheard is the soul that *refused* the bargain — and that lines up with the **son** ("songs you never heard," the unheard player). The girl is the soul that *took* the bargain. Two halves of one inheritance: the one who wouldn't sell, and the one who did.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the interpolation as enacted repair (rests on an open question)
> This is the most exposed and most beautiful reading on the page, and it stands or falls on a single unconfirmed fact: **is Joni's actual voice on the recording?**
>
> *If* Roy used Joni's real voice for some non-essential, non-lyrical part, then the choice is pure transmission, not craft — a song that *failed* to transmit (the son's "songs you never heard") healed by one that *succeeded* (Woodstock, audible for ~50 years). Joni becomes the non-substitutable Witness who "came upon the child of God" in 1969, placed bodily inside the later song, still singing *"I can see it."* The lore leaking into reality with a shattering subtlety — just sitting right there on Spotify.
>
> The honest caveat: the co-write is the only hard floor. If the borrowing is purely melodic and her voice isn't actually on the track, this climax collapses to a lovely idea. Pinning the production docs would decide it.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the "prophets quote" and a provenance knot
> Nine months *after* Soul Is A Star, Roy curated a quote on X that retells the song's exact thesis in found language: *"The realist crucifies genius, reaps the fruit of his vision and then immortalizes him… Prophets, philosophers, saints…"* — condemned-in-life, vindicated-after. It's the song's argument wearing someone else's words.
>
> The knot: tracing that quotation's true author has been genuinely fiddly (candidates floated included Russell, Kaufman, Ingersoll), and the governing rule is sound — *no "prophet" argument should be built on a quotation whose source isn't pinned.* The current best resolution attributes it to **Walter Russell**, *The Universal One* (1926), as a second public Russell receipt for Roy. Two cautions: (1) some internal notes still carry the older "unsettled" disposition, so treat the attribution as strong-but-reconcile-before-quoting; and (2) this X post is a *separate artifact* from the song — they share the condemned-then-vindicated motif, but the post is not the lyric. Don't let one launder the other.
>
> The methodological scar that informs all of this: a past attribution flipped only when an archaic-spelling / curly-apostrophe search finally matched — proof that "ruled out" must itself be primary-source-grade before you trust it.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — Da'at, "angels and angles," and the seed
> The transmission has a recognition-model under it: **Da'at** — the crossing-point of knowledge, Hebrew threshold into Greek *gnosis* — paired with **anamnesis**, recovery by *remembrance* rather than retrieval. The buried inheritance is not *failed*; it's **latent**. The song that still remembered itself "calls the buried one back by name."
>
> Two compressions carry the whole thesis. First, *"angels and angles"*: the consonants of a word are its fixed architecture — they descend intact, the way a wound inherits easily — while the breath, the vowels, are what's lost. *Pain transmits more reliably than wisdom; the bullet made it into the genes, the songs didn't make it out of the room.* Second, the seed: *"We fall down as seeds on the ground."* The falling **is** the planting. The dead thing that fell and the living thing that will grow are the two verses — one inheritance packed into one seed.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — Joni's Art Nouveau persona ↔ Sir Lucius (structure only)
> Joni built a male alter-ego — "Art Nouveau," sometimes "Claude" — used as character-acting, disguise, and armor against a press that kept "genderizing" her ("I don't want to be vulnerable anymore!"). That is the **structural template** of [[Sir Lucius]]: alter-ego-as-armor, anti-vulnerability, duality.
>
> **Hard scope guard:** Joni's persona was a racial-impersonation costume, and that content is Joni-specific. Map only the **function** (the mask as armor against exposure) — **never** the costume, and never onto Roy or Lucius. The template is the mechanism; the makeup stays hers.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — Joni's own "empty mirror" doctrine
> The project's [[Empty Mirror]] / speaker-problem stance turns out to be articulated by Joni herself. On the 1988 TV show *Wired*: her songs "were not intended as a portrait of myself… They were intended to be a mirror of human nature… the listener sees themself in them, not Joni doing something." That is the project's whole doctrine — the song as a mirror you see *yourself* in, not a self-portrait — stated by a confirmed influence, decades early. (Verified on Joni's official channels.) It also bears on why the "prophet/heir" self-positioning is shaky: the artist Roy is borrowing from explicitly *refused* to be the subject of her own songs.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — Joni as a serious esoteric lineage (not a vibe)
> Aligning with Joni is not aligning with a pop reference; it's aligning with a genuine esoteric artist, which is part of why the lineage reading is *tempting* in the first place. Joni is self-avowedly **Jungian** (synchronicity, the four-faculty "Medicine Wheel," the subconscious-beyond-time), reads **Nietzsche** (her "maker of individuals" sits beside Roy's "camel" metamorphoses from *Zarathustra*), called herself a **"Buddhist-Gnostic hybrid"** ("God is within"), and had a Chögyam Trungpa awakening. On her own word she's "interested in the prophets of all religions, but the religions themselves don't make any sense to me" — the closest grounded tie between *Joni* and the literal word *prophet*, and notably it's about **her**, not about Roy. The point: the substrate Roy is drawing from already runs on the same Jung/Hermetic/Gnostic furniture the rest of the catalogue does. The shelves match.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the mother as first transmission channel (firewall inside)
> The "song handed down" doctrine has a biographical floor in Roy's **mother**, resting on two *public* anchors only: the 2018 Atwood interview (she "listened to pretty much only weird world music") and the Masquerade bio ("Joni Mitchell and Bob Dylan… his mom played around the house"). So the inherited-substrate method literally originates in her records.
>
> Two firewalls keep this clean. **(1)** The mother is a *channel*; [[Mary]] is a *function* — do not collapse the biographical mother into the symbolic Mary. **(2)** There is also a Roy-exposed note that *"dad sent me Woodstock,"* his favorite Joni song — note that's **dad**, not mom; keep it distinct from the "mom played them" bio rather than merging the two. The public mother-channel claim stands on the two interview anchors alone.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the album-wide *33* ↔ *Ladies of the Canyon* dialogue
> Beyond Soul Is A Star, *Ladies of the Canyon* (1970) keeps surfacing as substrate, the lines existing on both sides while only the *intent* of any single mapping stays speculative:
>
> - **"The Arrangement"** — "thirty-third floor in the air… more than a consumer" ↔ the album titled *33* and its consumerism critique (cf. [[Online]] / *Pay Attention* / *I Like to Party*). The one genuine leap is whether Roy titled the album *33* *because* of Joni's 33rd floor — that specific intent stays [interpretation].
> - **"The Circle Game"** — "captive on the carousel of time" ↔ [[The Circle]].
> - A pervasive **father motif** across the album (Willy, *Rainy Night House*, *The Priest*) that rhymes with the song's "dark father."
>
> Also on the board, in Roy's own hand: the lyric laid out as *"Who had a Sun… My Sun was a singer,"* spelling **SUN** where the release reads **Son** — a Sun/Son homophone cohering with the board's "the sun = manifesting energy / externalized Life Force." Record it as an artifact detail; the *released* word is **Son**, and we don't "correct" it.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — where this stands in *33*'s arc, and the buried-then-recognized note
> Soul Is A Star (track 5) poses the album's governing question — *"do you know who you are?"* — at the literal midpoint; [[The Circle]] (track 10) answers it ("the inside of the inside must be where you go"). The anamnesis arc **oscillates and ends unresolved**: the song's final line is the *question*, not a settled statement.
>
> There's a human floor under the transmission reading, in Roy's own typed board note: *"I was really proud of this song before being dropped by my record label. I think it shook my confidence… Maybe I shook my own confidence. I love what I create now. And it's okay if it doesn't get outside validation. I know it's special."* The song that "failed to transmit" is recognized from the *inside* — which is exactly the move the whole concept is about. (Handle this one warmly; it's the wound the cleverness orbits.)

## Source notes

- **Grounded (Roy's own words, released music, public posts).** Joni Mitchell's co-write credit on Soul Is A Star (with Roy English and Matt Pauling); Roy's stated *blessing* ("JONI MITCHELL gave me her blessing and is a cowriter is still mind blowing"); the Egyptian Book of the Dead as the title's source ("behold, thy soul is a living star"), Roy-stated; the released lyric reading *"Prophets"*; the seed/bridge line; the album's *"do you know who you are?"*; "Woodstock" as the specific Joni song (and "caught in the devil's bargain" as Joni's own published line); Roy naming Joni among his listening; the board artifacts (the Sun/Son layout, the "dropped by my record label" note, the "dad sent me Woodstock" note); the Masquerade childhood-influence bio (published PR copy, third-person). Joni's "prophets of all religions" and "mystical thread" lines, and the 1988 "mirror of human nature" doctrine, are her own public statements.
- **Strong readings.** The full *Woodstock* weld and parallel inventory; "the girl carries Joni" (the genes/jeans hinge, the Thieves/For Free maps, the industry-patriarch read of the "dark father"); the transmission/Da'at/anamnesis thesis; "angels and angles"; the Art Nouveau ↔ Lucius **structural** template; the mother-as-channel floor; the album-wide *33* ↔ *Ladies of the Canyon* dialogue. Built on grounded anchors, but interpretive — labeled as such.
- **Candidate / open.** *Down to You* as a second Joni dialogue (verbatim hook match, but uncredited and idiomatic — a candidate, with a live "no Joni bridge" caution to reconcile); whether Joni's *actual voice* is on the recording (the "enacted repair" climax depends on it); the *"prophets quote"* attribution (strong toward Walter Russell, reconcile before quoting); a possible *City of Angels* ↔ Joni's "Paprika Plains" link (no evidence — open).
- **The speculative ceiling, kept above the floor.** "Roy consciously identifies as a prophet/heir in the Joni lineage" is **not** established — no credit, caption, lyric, or statement names him as such. It is a *possible* lineage, logged and held, available as a theme. The reductive press tag "Roy is a millennial Joni Mitchell" is recontextualized here as a real, chosen lineage rather than a vibe — but the *self-positioning* stays unclaimed.
- **Confirmed negative (so it isn't re-litigated).** Joni is a *33* phenomenon, not a *Lucius Lullaby* one — on LL she survives only as the SOL ↔ Soul Is A Star bridge.
