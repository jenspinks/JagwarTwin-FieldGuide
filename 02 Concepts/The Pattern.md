# The Pattern

> **One-line reading.** Roy's own name for the single law that repeats at every scale — "as above, so below," the same formula from the solar system to the atom.

The Pattern is one of the few framework terms Roy names himself, rather than something readers map onto the work. It's his word for the idea that reality is one fractal correspondence: the same structure recurring from the cosmic down to the microscopic, encoded in myth, film, music, biology, and number.

## Roy's own term

This is grounded as Roy's framework, not an interpreter's projection. The term is his, written on his own "I'm 33 this year" board — *"The Pattern is present on all levels,"* set beside a Hermetic-correspondence note, alongside a list he titles *"The Pattern Represented in Movies."*

He also runs the correspondence-mapping *and* disclaims it in the same breath, which is the important guardrail. *"I would map a crow onto Mercury, but that's just personal. I don't recommend you do it,"* he wrote in 2026. So holding the Pattern as a lens is licensed by Roy; treating it as a master key that decodes every song is not — and Roy himself flags that risk in his own voice. The honest reading is: Roy holds a Pattern-framework; whether any given song "encodes the Pattern" needs its own evidence, song by song.

## What it is

On his board, Roy assembles a unified esoteric-and-scientific system from named sources — Alice Bailey, Walter Russell, the Kybalion, the Kabbalah, Jung, Plato — around one claim: reality is a single fractal correspondence, and the self can be *read* through it. The operating rules he transcribes are the Kybalion's seven principles (mentalism, correspondence, vibration, polarity, rhythm, cause-and-effect, gender), summed in the line *"the All is Mind… Mind woven into matter."*

The same axiom surfaced publicly years before the board was known. The 2021 "Down to You" end-card prints **"AS ABOVE SO BELOW"** as a sigil, beside a manifesto paragraph: *"to change the world one must first change the concept of the world. To do that one must change the concept of one's self, which makes one see the world as they do."* That passage is near-verbatim Neville Goddard — a named-influence lead for the concept-of-self thread that also runs through [[Source vs Reflection]].

## One ascent, many encodings

The board reads less like a loose collage than like *one* schema rendered in parallel alphabets. A single ten-stage ascent (descent and return) is written out in tone (a musical scale), color (a light scale), virtue and vice (positive/negative traits in ten stages), and the body (feet to crown). The same arc gets container-figures: the Tree of Life, Jacob's Ladder (Earth to Heaven), the Hero's Journey wheel (ordinary world, the rabbit hole, master of two worlds, a new cycle begins), all mapped onto *"cycles of human consciousness throughout history."* The strong inference is that these aren't separate systems but one structure shown many ways — which is exactly what "the Pattern" would predict.

The board also carries a cosmogonic poem — *"I spoke the words 'One become Two'… a Darkness and a Light… a Sun was born inside… split into 5, 6, 7, 8, 9… 'I' was still I"* — that reads as the [[Sir Lucius|Roy/Lucius]] split written as a creation myth, the artist-bio "there's two sides to every story" given a cosmology. See [[Duality]].

## The film list

The board's *"Pattern Represented in Movies"* is a list of twenty-two titles. Read as a whole, it's Roy's own concept-index — each film a worked example of "the Pattern," chosen from his side of the glass. The strongest fits are the specific, hard-to-copy ones:

- **Groundhog Day** — the loop escaped through love (the Circle/Loop hinge, named in a film).
- **The Giver** — remembrance at collective scale (one keeper remembers, like the Wise One).
- **Pinocchio** — the wound, the strings, the realness-blessing.
- **Wings of Desire** — watcher-angels who long to feel.

The crucial caveat is the one Roy supplies himself: twenty-two films can be mapped onto almost anything, so the list does not *prove* the project's concepts. It only shows that Roy curated a film-set that indexes his own Pattern. The same anti-overfit guard applies as everywhere else — license the lens, not a master key.

## In framework terms

The Pattern is openly a syncretic frame, and Roy assembles it from a named lineage: Alice Bailey, Walter Russell, the Hermetic Kybalion, the Kabbalah, Jung, and Plato — a theosophical, perennialist synthesis. Two public receipts ground the influence outside the board: a 2023 post quotes Walter Russell's *The Universal One* verbatim, and the public line *"the rose blooms in the center of the cross"* anchors the board's Rosicrucian rose-cross cluster. Because the Pattern braids all these traditions together, the project's other concepts naturally *rhyme* with it — [[Source vs Reflection]] echoes "as above, so below," [[Duality]] echoes polarity, the [[The Loop and the Circle|Circle]] echoes the cosmic cycle. That rhyme is a property of the shared root, not in itself proof of deliberate cross-encoding. The right move is to attribute the contributing thinkers in passing and let the *concept* be the thing — not to build the work into a secret cipher.

## The Sun/Son pun

One small, grounded instance: the board drafts the song "Soul Is A Star" with *"Sun"* — *"who had a Sun, my Sun was a singer"* — against the released spelling *"Son."* It's a deliberate homophone, coherent with the board's *"the sun = symbol of manifesting energy, externalized life force"* and its *"Sun of God."* The released lyric is "Son"; the board's "Sun" is best kept as an artifact detail rather than a correction.

## How to hold it

Roy *holding* a Pattern-framework is grounded. The open question — and it's a real one — is whether the Pattern *generates* the catalogue or is Roy's after-the-fact map of it. Per-song mapping needs per-song anchors; the lens should never be back-fitted onto a song that doesn't carry it on its own. Read the Pattern as the substrate Roy works *from*, not a key that unlocks everything.
