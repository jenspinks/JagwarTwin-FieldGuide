# The Angels and the Angles

> **One-line reading.** A letter-mysticism lens, drawn from Kabbalah, that hears the project's wordless "la-la / na-na" syllables as technique rather than filler: the consonants are *angles* (sounded structure), the vowels are *angels* (breath, spirit), and the singer is the instrument that spirit is sung through.

A clear caveat belongs at the top of this one. **Roy has never stated this as his method** — there is no interview, post, or video where he frames his singing in these terms. So this page is offered purely as an *interpretive lens*: a documented Kabbalistic tradition that the work happens to embody, with the songs and iconography as the evidence. It is a correspondence, not a claim about how Roy writes. Read it as "here is a tradition the work rhymes with," and nothing more.

## The pun at the center

The lens turns on a pun in English — *angels* and *angles* are one letter apart — laid over a real idea in Kabbalah. Hebrew is written with consonants only; the vowels are unwritten marks (the *niqqud*) added "into" the consonants afterward, like breath filling a frame. The reading hears that structurally:

- **Consonants = angles.** The hard, sounded, written structure — the skeleton of a word.
- **Vowels = angels = breath, spirit.** The unwritten life breathed into the structure to animate it.

So a sung vowel is *spirit carried on structure* — the angel sung through the angle.

## The tradition it draws on

This isn't invented; it assembles several genuine strands of Jewish mysticism, which is why the lens has teeth even though Roy never names it.

- **Letters as the building blocks of creation.** The early mystical text *Sefer Yetzirah* describes the cosmos as formed out of the 22 Hebrew letters — the letters are the formative powers of existence itself.
- **Vowels as the soul of the letters.** The *Zohar*, Kabbalah's central work, states it almost exactly: *"the letters are the body and the vowel-points the soul."* In a related passage the 22 letters exist before creation as *"a body without a soul"* until breath enters and the vowels settle in — described as *"souls entering bodies."* Other medieval kabbalists call the vowels *"the lifeblood of the letters."* Since the vowels go unwritten in a plain Hebrew text, they are quite literally the unseen spirit added to visible structure.
- **Letters as angels.** In Kabbalah the letters can *be* angels — there is a tradition of deriving 72 angelic names from the Hebrew letters of a verse in Exodus. The letters and the angels aren't merely linked; in this strand they're the same.
- **The method — Abulafia.** The ecstatic Kabbalist Abraham Abulafia taught a practice of *permuting the letters with chanted vowel-tones* to reach visionary states — sound and letter combined into "a ladder for the mind to climb." A singer chanting pure vocables is doing something structurally close to that technique: the mouth as the instrument the spirit plays.

## How the work resonates with it

The reason the lens is worth offering at all is that the songs supply the evidence — the tradition lines up with things that are actually there in the catalogue.

**The wordless syllables.** The *"la-la" / "na-na" / "ba-ba"* textures that run across the catalogue (and the *"go bananananana"* of [[Bananas]]) are, on this reading, pure vowel-spirit carried over consonant-structure — the angels sung through the angles. What sounds like childish nonsense becomes, in this frame, technique: the breath foregrounded over the word.

**"Angels" named, "angles" spoken.** [[City of Angels]] names the angels outright. And here the grounded layer is genuinely Roy's: speaking about reworked versions of [[The Circle]], he used the word *angles* in his own voice — *"I hope with the reimagined 'angles' of the song that everyone can find their own 'home' in one of the spaces."* That *angles-as-spaces* sense is documented and public. (The angel/angle *method* is the part that is not — keep them separate.)

**Hebrew on the body and the set.** The Hebrew phrase *El Roi* is tattooed on Lucius's throat — Hebrew letters placed on the gate of the voice. The stage set has used Tree-of-Life pillars. And Roy's public interest in the Hebrew language is on the record: in 2019 he wrote about watching *"a documentary on the Hebrew language… Everything is a mirror."* These don't prove the letter-method, but they show the surrounding material — Hebrew, the Tree, the mirror — is genuinely in the project's world.

## Why the lens is interesting

If you accept it as a lens, it reframes the album's "childish nonsense" as **technique, not decoration**: the vocables are vowel-spirit (the angels) carried on consonant-structure (the angles), with the mouth as the instrument the spirit is sung through. It also tightens two other clusters in the work — the **mirror** (Hebrew can be read in both directions; the Hebrew word for mirror, *re'i*, sits inside the *El Roi* tattoo; *"everything is a mirror"*) and the **counterfeit** (Lucius produces *structure without spirit* — angles without angels — which is the logic behind the project's sterile-copy imagery).

But the caveat stands and shouldn't be softened: this is **tradition-correspondence, not Roy's stated method.** The tradition is real and documented; the songs really do embody it; and Roy really does assemble Kabbalah among his public sources. What's missing is any statement from him that *this letter-and-breath idea is how he works.* Until that exists, the page cites the tradition, not the man.
