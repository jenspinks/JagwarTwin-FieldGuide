# Jagwar Twin and Harry Potter

> **One-line reading.** There is exactly one documented Harry Potter reference in the entire Jagwar Twin catalogue — a 2025 song called "dumbledore." Everything past that single anchor is *convergence,* not influence: both bodies of work drink from the same well of Western esoteric tradition, so the parallels are real, dense, and uncanny — but neither side is borrowing from the other.

## At a glance

- **In one line** — One song ("dumbledore") is the only direct Potter reference on the record; every deeper parallel is two works inheriting the same tradition, never Potter feeding Jagwar Twin.
- **Where it shows up** — [[dumbledore]] (the song + the "DARK ARTS VERSION") · the public Gatekeeper Saga on Discord · Lucius's "one minute a twisted circus, the next it's Dumbledore" jab in [[The Interview]].
- **Connected ideas** — [[Sir Lucius]] · [[The Realm]] · [[The Circus]] · [[Nigredo]] · [[MAGIK]] · [[The Mask]].
- **Confidence** — one grounded anchor (the song and its release saga) braided with strong tradition-level readings and a clearly-labeled esoteric framework; the deep parallels are interpretation, flagged throughout.

## What it is

Here is the whole situation in one breath: Roy is, by his own admission, a "potter head." Harry Potter sits on his public list of favorite *documentaries* (an endearing miscategorization he made in 2022, alongside *The Wizard of Oz*, *Pinocchio*, *The Lord of the Rings*, and *The Fountain*). And in 2025 he released a song literally named **"dumbledore."** That is the entire documented footprint — one fandom, one favorites-list mention, one song. No Malfoy, no Hogwarts, no Horcruxes, no Voldemort anywhere on the record.

So why does this page exist at all? Because once you start reading Jagwar Twin against Harry Potter, the parallels pile up fast and start to feel like influence: alchemy, mirrors, shadows and doubles, hidden knowledge, word-magic, death and rebirth. The temptation is to draw a line from Rowling to Roy. **Don't.** Both works are running the same ancient operating system — Western esotericism — and that shared inheritance is doing all the work. The page's whole job is to keep you from mistaking a fellow traveler for a source.

The rule, stated once and obeyed everywhere below: **route every parallel through the shared *tradition,* never "Potter to Jagwar Twin" directly.** Sir Lucius and Lucius Malfoy share a Latin light-name. That is a resonance. It is not a derivation. Keep that distinction and the page stays honest; lose it and you've turned a co-participant into an origin — the same error the archive forbids everywhere else.

## How it works

The one place Potter is genuinely *on the record* is the song, and the song is the only place the influence runs one direction. Everywhere else, the move is the same: name the motif, name where it lives in each work, and then — this is the load-bearing part — name the **tradition** that explains both. The explanation always lives in that last clause. That clause is the firewall.

Read the parallels this way and they stop being evidence of borrowing and become evidence of a shared library. Potter has Rowling-confirmed alchemy; Jagwar Twin has [[Nigredo]]. Both inherit the Great Work. Potter has the Mirror of Erised; Jagwar Twin has [[Source vs Reflection]] and [[The Empty Mirror]]. Both inherit the speculum, the mirror-as-knowing. Potter has the unintegrated shadow stalking its hero across the houses; Jagwar Twin has [[Sir Lucius]], the mind split from the heart, and [[The Dweller on the Threshold]]. Both inherit the Jungian double. The pattern holds for word-magic, for awakening-as-remembrance, for death-and-rebirth, for the martyred sacrificial light. Seven motifs, seven shared traditions, zero arrows from Rowling to Roy.

The song "dumbledore" is where all of this gets *funny,* and the comedy is the insight. The name enacts its own degradation: the hook "I feel like Dumbledore / Dumble-Dumble-Dumbledore" decays in real time into "I feel like dumb, dumb, dumb… go dumb." A wisdom-name — the long-game sage, the keeper of hidden knowledge — flattened into a club chant. That's not a lapse; it's the thesis performed. The song doesn't *argue* with the wise-elder archetype, it memes it into mush, because that is precisely what the [[The Algorithm|algorithm]] and the [[The Circus|Circus]] do to anything profound. "Go dumb" is the wisdom-figure run through the spectacle-grinder, live, on the track.

And underneath the joke, a real wound. The song builds genuine martyr-scaffolding — crucifixion imagery, thorns, getting out of the grave — and then bulldozes it with the party chant. That collision *is* the song: the sincere pain of carrying something true, slamming into an industry and an audience that just want a mindless banger. Don't resolve the oscillation. The oscillation is the point. (The deeper read of that tension is in the Advanced reading below.)

## Where it appears

- **"dumbledore" (the song, 2025).** The one documented anchor — a release named for the Potter character, complete with a **"DARK ARTS VERSION."** Roy framed it publicly as *"a love song"* — the mentor/guide, *freeing* register, not a plot retelling. Credits are worth knowing: written by Roy English, Ryan Daly, and Dustin Steven Rutkowski; produced by Roy English, OmegaSign, and Ryan Daly; released via Big Loud Records (2025). The performer credit includes **Laboratorium Pieśni,** a Polish folk vocal ensemble — a genuinely strange and lovely detail for a song about a wizard. See [[dumbledore]].
- **The Gatekeeper Saga (public Discord, Nov 2024 → Feb 2025).** This is the grounded heart of the whole concept, and it is a sustained Harry Potter bit between Roy and Michael Morgan. "dumbledore" was the codename for a release Roy was fighting his label to put out; the gatekeepers demanded TikTok virality first. Roy's running commentary: *"I'm trying to release dumbledore but the gatekeepers are blocking me from getting it to the realm"*; *"they said the song needs to go viral on tiktok for them to release the song and im like wtf haha"*; and the Arthurian kicker, *"they couldnt even pull the sword out of the stone."* Then, plainly: *"idk about you but i feel like fucking dumbledore."* See [[Gatekeeper Saga]].
- **The blockchain end-run.** Roy's solution was to route around the label entirely: *"dropped it on the blockchain becuase the label has no rights there! Haha go cop it"* — posted via Michael with a [[The Realm|Coop Records]] link. The label clearance was separately delayed by the January 2025 LA fires; in the meantime Michael told the Circle that *"the more you all comment and ask for it, the more ammo we have to prove to the label that it should be released."*
- **The resolution (the Dobby beat).** When it finally dropped, Michael announced it in pure Potter: *"Master gave dumbledore a sock, dumbledore is finally free"* — casting the label as Lucius Malfoy and the freed song as Dobby the house-elf. Chef's kiss.
- **"dumbledore [DARK ARTS]" (visualizer, ~2024).** An ornate priest-king robe with **no one inside it** — the hollow pole of [[The Empty Mirror]] made literal — eyes-wide-shut, holding a dead-branch wand. The mask that finally replaced the face. See the Advanced reading below.
- **[[The Interview]] (theatrical EP).** [[Sir Lucius]] weaponizes the Potter pivot as a roast: *"One minute your music is a twisted circus, the next it's Dumbledore. You don't stand for anything. The darkness or the light?"* — using the turn toward the wise-elder song as evidence of Roy's incoherence (darkness-or-light being the album's governing binary).
- **The wider fan-world.** Roy self-IDs with the fandom — *"we just got to get it to the fellow potter heads and wizard nerds like us"* — and the Circle minted a bespoke **"Wizard" emoji** (March 2025) that Roy then sprayed everywhere, including to unveil the paid Inner Circle: *"Behold the Inner Circle 🧙."* Earlier still, in 2023, Michael posted *"You're a wizard Luke"* over a literal *Harry_Potter.mp4* — a Hagrid riff with Roy's name swapped in (and note that "Luke" ties to the documented Gospel-of-Luke name thread on the [[Sir Lucius]] page).

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the seven shared traditions, mapped
> Every parallel below is convergence routed through a tradition node. The tradition is the explanation; Potter and Jagwar Twin are two of its readers.
> - **Alchemy** — the Great Work, *nigredo → albedo → rubedo,* the red and the white, the Philosopher's Stone. Rowling has said she "learned a ridiculous amount about alchemy" to build the wizarding world; the red/white colour-coding even links Rubeus Hagrid (red) and Albus Dumbledore (white) as the two halves of Harry's ideal father-figure, and Nicolas Flamel is literal book-one canon. Jagwar Twin's side is [[Nigredo]] — dissolution before rebirth. *Shared tradition, not Potter-sourced.*
> - **The double / the shadow** — Potter's unintegrated shadow and house-polarity (Gryffindor lion vs Slytherin serpent); Jagwar Twin's [[Sir Lucius]] (mind split from heart) and [[The Dweller on the Threshold]]. *Shared tradition: the Jungian shadow.*
> - **The mirror / speculum** — the Mirror of Erised (desire vs truth); [[Source vs Reflection]], [[The Empty Mirror]], the "magic mirror." *Shared tradition: the mirror as a way of knowing.*
> - **Anamnesis** — Potter turns on hidden knowledge and a long game; Jagwar Twin's [[The Circle]] runs on remembrance-not-acquisition. *Shared tradition: Gnostic/Platonic recollection.*
> - **Logos-magic / word-magic** — Latinate spells, true-names with power; the "magic of words," letters-as-creative-force ([[The Angels and the Angles]]). *Shared tradition: the word as creative power.*
> - **Death–rebirth** — Fawkes the phoenix and the sacrificial mentor; the Circle's recurrence and survival-through-rock-bottom. *Shared tradition: the death-rebirth mystery.*
> - **Christ-mythos / martyrology** — Potter's whole climax is a willing sacrificial death-and-return (the king-sacrifice the alchemy is pointing at all along); "dumbledore" runs its own martyr-scaffolding — crucified, the crown of thorns, climbing out of the grave, the persecuted-truth-teller roll-call. *Shared tradition: the Christ-passion / martyrology — the sacrificed light, not Potter-sourced.*
> The deeper Potter-side architecture (the Black-family astronomy names — Sirius, Regulus, Bellatrix; the Deathly Hallows triangle-circle-line sigil; the sevenfold recurrence of seven books and seven Horcruxes) is real *in Potter* and Rowling-confirmed — but it is context about Potter, never evidence about Roy. Treat it as a neighbor's bookshelf, not a citation.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the Lucius name (resonance, never derivation)
> [[Sir Lucius]] and Lucius Malfoy share the Latin light-field — *lux,* adjacent to Lucifer-as-light-bringer. In Potter it lends an aristocratic villain his irony; in Jagwar Twin it suits the brilliant intellect-shadow who brings a *dangerous* kind of light. But **Malfoy is explicitly the weakest strand of the entire name-field.** No derivation from Potter is documented. The stronger lineage — Lucifer / *lux* / the morning-star / the Gospel-of-Luke-via-Steiner thread / Paul Foster Case's Devil card — runs entirely outside Potter and is handled on the [[Sir Lucius]] page. Mention Malfoy here only to set him aside.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the song's core move, and the survival read
> The reading that holds the song together: **the name enacts its own degradation.** "Dumble-dore" sliding into "dumb… go dumb" is a wisdom-figure dumbing *itself* down in real time — the bit *is* that it disintegrates as you listen. Set that against the song's sincere scaffolding (the artist-as-martyr cluster: caught thorns, crucified, got out the grave) and you get performed cognitive dissonance: genuine pain colliding with an audience that wants a party track, and the collision left unresolved on purpose.
> The synthesis: **modern persecution is meme-ification, not the stake.** Hollywood doesn't burn its truth-tellers anymore — it flattens them into content. "Go dumb" is what witch-burning looks like now; the *delivery* is the thesis. This sits on Roy's own words elsewhere about cancel culture — that "if you're fighting something… you become that thing without knowing," the burner becoming the witch (the Inversion).
> And a gentler third reading, the one that touches the wound: "going dumb" as **dissociation and survival** — ecstatic ego-death, intoxication, numbing the mind so the body can still perform. "I'm already gone" carries all three at once — the meme-flattening, the party-nihilism, and the quiet exhaustion underneath. Be witty about the first two. Be careful with the third.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the empty robe, the wise fool, and who is even singing
> **The DARK ARTS visualizer** reads as a single icon: a priest-king robe with *no body in it* ("no body, I'm already gone") — the sage turned dark-magician (the Lucius register), eyes-wide-shut as a blindfold-initiation, a dead branch where the wand should be. The mask that finally replaced the face — which ties straight to [[Welcome to the Circus]]'s "who are you without the mask?"
> **"Eyes wide shut," read straight.** The phrase sits *primarily* on the Gurdjieff frequency — **"man is asleep, he must wake up"** — the dreaming-while-awake state Gurdjieff is Roy's most-named public influence for naming. The eyes are open and the man still does not see. The Kubrick film is a real but *secondary* echo, fitting the Hollywood-occult setting; the blindfold-initiation iconography rides on top of the Gurdjieff sleep, not instead of it. So the empty robe is also a sleeping figure: a body that performs the rites with the lights on and nobody home.
> **The wise fool.** Truncating "Dumbledore" to "dumb" rhymes with the holy fool — and, in the deck the rest of the cosmology already leans on, the **Tarot's Fool (card 0):** highest wisdom appearing as idiocy to the profane world, the zero that contains everything, the jester who plays the idiot to survive the court. So the degradation cuts two ways at once: it could be *imposed* by the Circus, or *chosen* as the fool's disguise. Hold both.
> **Whose voice is it?** The unresolved authorship question: is "dumbledore" Roy's sincere wound, or [[Sir Lucius]] mocking the martyr-complex by turning a crucifixion into a club banger? The self-undercutting "go dumb" may be the tell. The Gatekeeper Saga grounds the *title* as sincerely Potter-coded — Roy genuinely "feels like dumbledore" fighting the gatekeepers, hidden-knowledge and long-game and the freeing — but the *song* holds both registers at once. Don't pick.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the gatekeeper double-bind, and the comedy of the saga
> The saga's sharpest reading is its **double-bind:** to escape the [[The Circus|Circus]], the Circle had to *run* the Circus — the comment campaigns, the streaming asks, the manufactured demand "to prove to the label it should be released." Freeing dumbledore meant playing the gatekeepers' own game well enough to beat it. That's the pathos under the joke.
> And the joke is genuinely good. The whole saga is a sustained Potter bit: Michael teeing it up months early (*"U sure you're not feeling like dumbledore?"*), the realm that *"needs to be ready for dumbledore before it can drop,"* the gatekeepers who *"couldnt even pull the sword out of the stone,"* Roy and Michael bonding over their own target demographic (*"fellow potter heads and wizard nerds like us"*), and the perfect Dobby-sock landing. A real fight with a label, narrated entirely in house-elf.

- **Grounded (released music, public posts, public interviews).** "dumbledore" is a 2025 release named for the Potter character, with a DARK ARTS version; its credits (writers Roy English / Ryan Daly / Dustin Steven Rutkowski; Big Loud Records; Laboratorium Pieśni) are verified against streaming metadata. That Rowling used alchemy, that the red/white alchemical colour-naming links Rubeus Hagrid (red) and Albus Dumbledore (white) per Pottermore (as summarized by *Time*), and that the Black-family astronomy names and Hallows sigil are canon — all verifiable, Potter-side only.
- **Strong readings.** The seven tradition-routed parallels (alchemy, the double, the mirror, anamnesis, word-magic, death-rebirth, and the Christ-passion / martyrology) as *convergence;* the "name enacts its own degradation" core move; the meme-ification-as-modern-persecution synthesis; "going dumb" as survival; the empty-robe and wise-fool reads; the Lucius/Malfoy etymology as resonance-not-derivation. Built on grounded anchors, but interpretive — and labeled as such.
- **Lyric-level caution.** The song's deepest martyr/persecution thesis leans on a lyric sheet (AZLyrics) that streaming has *not* verified — Spotify carries no lyrics for this track. The persecution roll-call in particular (Galileo, Joan of Arc, and a "Constantine" that forks between the Emperor and the DC occultist) rests on three proper nouns that have never been confirmed against an official source. If any are mis-transcribed, the reads that depend on them soften. Treat those specific names as provisional.
- **Open questions.** Is "dumbledore" a single, a track on [[MAGIK]], or its own 6-track EP? The archive contradicts itself (Big Loud and the lyric source say single; the master log says a 6-track EP dated 2025-02-28) — so don't assert any track-sequence meaning yet. And the governing absence: there is **no** documented Roy reference to Malfoy, Hogwarts, Voldemort, or the Horcruxes anywhere. That absence is exactly why everything past "dumbledore" stays convergence — and the moment one surfaces, that item graduates from resonance to documented.
- **A fabrication-hygiene note.** Lyrics about *"Horcruxes / why have you abandoned me"* circulate online and are sometimes pinned to this song. **They are not Roy's** — they belong to a different wizard-rock track. Do not attribute them to "dumbledore."
