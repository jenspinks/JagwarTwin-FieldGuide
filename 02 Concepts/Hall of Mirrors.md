# Hall of Mirrors

> **One-line reading.** Hall of Mirrors is the Jagwar Twin metaverse — a web3 puzzle-world built on the fiction that the artist uploaded his consciousness to the internet and scattered the pieces of himself behind locked doors, so that the fans who decode the clues are the ones who bring him back into the light.

It started, like a surprising amount of this project, as a joke. Roy English told the studio building the thing: *what if I uploaded my consciousness to the internet so there could be a 24/7 social-influencer version of me?* Everyone laughed. Then they built it, played it straight-faced, and the joke turned out to have a whole cosmology inside it.

Here's the fiction. An artist's consciousness gets *uploaded* to the internet and shatters into fragments "lost in the code" — drawings, voice memos, family heirlooms, old tweets, scribbled cosmology pages. Each fragment is sealed in a room behind a code. Find the code, open the room, recover the piece. It is the single biggest thing to explore in the entire Jagwar Twin world, and it is not a gallery you stroll through.

It is a maze you *solve*.

## What it is

Roy has named it on the record, almost as a footnote, while talking about something else: *"Not to be confused with the Hall of Mirrors — which is the Jagwar Twin metaverse."* That's the whole thing in one parenthetical. It lives today at **mirrors.jagwartwin.com**, and it was built with the studio CTHDRL across a six-drop campaign in 2022.

The public framing is its own **Manifesto** — a complaint about an internet that swapped the things that matter for their metrics. Roy lays it out as a substitution table: *immersion* traded for **volume of content**, *purpose* traded for **reach**, *feeling* traded for **algorithm**. Against that, the manifesto wants the opposite of a follower count. *"I don't desire more followers. I desire to see more leaders, more free thinkers, more truth seekers."* It signs off with the line that becomes the project's whole theology of the self — *"We are not defined by what we do, but by who we are. All I am is that I am"* — a riff on God's name to Moses in Exodus, *I am that I am*, which is roughly the level of ambition we are dealing with.

So the artifacts you uncover aren't collectibles. They're fragments of one person's symbolic operating system, turned into a game you play with other people. Opening the maze is, in effect, learning to read the project's private language well enough to speak it back.

## How it works

Two kinds of object live in the architecture. **Crowns** are the keys: free, minted when you connect a wallet, composable access passes whose look and rarity reflect how much you've actually participated — *"your keys to the universe."* **Artifacts** are the prizes: one-of-one pieces "lost in the code" during the upload. Discover one and the room it unlocks opens for *everyone* — but the artifact itself stays yours alone. Community access, private ownership; the door swings open for all, the object goes home with the finder.

The governing ethic is the part Roy is proudest of: **attention over money.** Every one-of-one artifact is *free*. You cannot buy your way to a rare one. As he put it during a launch-week Space, *"we're incentivizing attention more than money… It doesn't matter how much money you have."* This produced the maze's best running gag: when it launched, the deep-pocketed NFT collectors flooded in — thousands of mints in hours — and got comprehensively out-solved by broke superfans with spreadsheets, who'd been reading the clues for months and stayed ahead the entire time.

Because the codes are seeded out in the actual world — tucked into campaign drops, old tweets, video frames, lyrics, the artifacts themselves — and no single person ever sees every clue, the maze is built to be solved *together*. A crowd comparing notes, testing guesses, sharing what opens. One community solver named the method on launch night: *"the codes are hidden within stuff we've had over the past like six months."* Roy confirmed it — solvers were *"creating spreadsheets, organizing — okay, Roy tweeted this two years ago, maybe it's a clue to this."*

That is the heart of it. **Participation is the art.** The maze converts an audience into a community of decoders, and the act of collectively recovering a hidden piece *is* the meaning — many separate people returning the same lost song. There's a shadow to that too, which is where the deeper readings live (below). But the surface is clean and generous: a hidden thing comes back because a crowd of strangers cared enough to read closely.

The maze is **meant to be solved.** Not cracked open to steal a secret answer key — the reward is the experience of reading closely, in good company, and watching a buried thing surface.

## What's inside

The recovered fragments read like a tour of the project's underground. A few of the named ones:

- **"How to Live Forever?"** — a hand-drawn cosmology page in Roy's own hand: a hopscotch ladder from Earth to Heaven, a Lo Shu magic square, Eye-of-Horus sense-fractions, a Fibonacci daisy labeled "Ways of Growing," golden-ratio math, and Genesis citations in the margins. It is, essentially, a creation myth that is secretly also a clock.
- **"Coding Consciousness"** — a handwritten note: *"Patterns of right Living are Formed in the unconscious mind of a race so that it becomes easier to follow for generations after."* Culture as software, written down.
- **"Egg"** — a 2021 tweet whose *entire text* is the single word "Egg," posted over Vladimir Kush's painting of a cracked world-egg with the sun rising between the shell-halves. The only authorial content is one word. The deadpan minimalism is the joke and the meaning at once.
- **A test-pilot-school lighter** — a silver flip-top with a USAF Test Pilot School emblem, part of a whole father-and-grandfather aviation archive woven through the maze (a grandfather's WWII plane, a grandfather's mask, a biplane, "Dad's Clock," "Dad's Wooden Horse"). Grounded directly in Roy's own line about what's hidden in the rooms: *"3D scans of childhood items or my grandfather's World War II plane."*
- **A third-eye watcher** — a white folk-icon face with three eyes and the Jagwar Twin heart-monogram on its chest, set in a field of mirror-doubled roses.

And the maze leans hard into the absurd-domestic alongside the sacred: "Potato Chip," "Sleeping on Carrot Juice," "Chuckster's Mummy 2," "Dad's Clock 4 BB Roy," and a "List of Roy's Favorite Things" whose unlock code is, perfectly, **WHAT DOES A MANAGER DO?**

What the artifacts *are*, in Roy's own inventory: *"some are unreleased songs… the voice memos of me first recording songs that are on the new album… 3D scans of childhood items or my grandfather's World War II plane."* Read together they form a public **concordance** — the symbolic operating system behind the music, laid out as rooms you can walk into one at a time. The artifacts aren't trivia. They're the building blocks of the world, made findable.

## Where it appears

- **mirrors.jagwartwin.com** — the live site: a public **gallery** of ~149 substantive unlocked artifacts (each card showing the unlock code, the discoverer's handle, and the timestamp — open to view, no wallet needed), a leaderboard, and crown-gated rooms.
- **The Manifesto-FAQ** — the public-facing statement of intent and the rules of crowns vs artifacts.
- **The 2022 campaign** — six CTHDRL drops across the year: *Going Dark* (3/30), the *Spreadsheet Map* (4/08, where Roy tweeted *"I'm dropping a spreadsheet with a roadmap and music nft hidden in it"*), a *Body Parts* auction (5/02), *"The Upload"* live desktop stream (5/16–18), the *Genesis of SARAVI* (6/11), and the **Hall of Mirrors homepage takeover** (11/11) — the moment the maze stopped being a side-page and became the front door.
- **Roy's 2022 tweets** — the seeded clues themselves. He all but announced the game while playing it: replying to a collaborator on 4/10 with *"It's all code!"*, and ending an April tweet with a run of macOS Option-key glyphs that decode to **PAY ATTENTION**.
- **The launch event** (Aug 11–13, 2022) — roughly 120 artifacts cracked in about 72 hours. *"We got over 3,500 mints the second day… in a matter of a few hours,"* yet the day-one decoders stayed in front.
- **Twitter/X Spaces** (Aug 15 "DoinGud," Aug 16 "The Listening Room") — the earliest spoken framing, including the line that became its own artifact: *"the truth is a sphere and we're all going to see our different perspectives… the only way that we get to the truth is by seeing them all"* (minted as the *Enlightened Being* artifact, unlock code **THE TRUTH IS A SPHERE**).
- **The Discord** (`#hall-of-mirrors`) — the live solving floor, run alongside the community by Michael Morgan, where Roy dropped warnings like *"keep track of what you typed because things tend to flip when inside the Hall of Mirrors"* and pointed solvers at the channel that *"gets the alpha cheat codes."*
- **Predecessor microsites** — the lore runs back further than the maze: *deathofmygeneration.fun* (the Happy Face Experiment, Dec 2020, with its smile-gate and *"Are you content?"* prompt) and *thechaosorder.academy* (Down to You, 2021, *"What is it that you seek?"*).

## Advanced reading

> [!note]- Advanced reading — why it's a *Hall* and not a single Mirror
> The name does specific work. A mirror returns one true reflection; a **hall of mirrors** is a funhouse — it stretches, multiplies, and inverts. Read as a lived structure, the sequence is: **mirror → flip** (One becomes Two; Roy becomes [[Sir Lucius]]) **→ distortion** (the funhouse multiplies the reflection into masks) **→ the work** of seeing through the distortions and flipping back to the one true face. The artifacts are fragments and distorted reflections of the maker's psyche; the journey is self-recognition *amid the warping*. That's precisely why it's a maze you walk rather than a mirror you glance into — and Roy's own launch-week warning, *"things tend to flip when inside the Hall of Mirrors,"* is the reversal mechanic stated outright (some codes are answers spelled backwards: RORRIM = mirror, reversed). See [[The Flip and the Distorting Mirror]].

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the maze as memory palace, and its surveillance shadow
> Grounded: the Hall is the project's **externalized memory palace.** It takes private archive material — drawings, diary pages, family objects, old tweets, board labels — and turns it into a *public* concordance of one artist's symbolic vocabulary. That is the load-bearing point: the artifacts are building blocks of the universe, not bonus trivia.
>
> The shadow (a strong reading, the vault's framing, *not* a Roy claim): a solving crowd is also a crowd that can be **watched, ranked, leaderboarded, measured.** The same gesture is communion or surveillance depending on who's keeping score. This is decoder-participation read through two of the work's own forks — [[Communion vs Spectacle]] (decoding that *remembers* vs decoding that is *performed*) and [[Witnessing vs Surveillance]] (decoding that holds vs decoding that harvests). "Decoder culture" isn't a separate doctrine; it's HoM-participation seen through those existing splits. It also sets up [[The Watchers]], where *"the Hall of Mirrors grows eyes"* — reflection tipping over into being-watched. (The Hebrew root *re'i* means both *mirror* and *seeing*, so mirror-becoming-eye is the word itself doing the turn.)

> [!note]- Advanced reading — concordance, not master cipher (the empirical version)
> The honest frame: this is a **concordance of an existing vocabulary, not a single master cipher.** Resist the pull to treat every artifact as a deliberately cross-encoded clue. Forensic work on the open rooms bears this out empirically: most open rooms are **reveal-art** (tweets, illustrations, photos, color swatches) whose codes were seeded *elsewhere* — not on the artwork itself. A clean tweet reading "My favorite color is information" does not hash to its own room. So deeper OCR of the art won't crack those rooms; the frontier is partly forensic (largely mined) and partly a seeded-clue gap. The maze rewards reading each piece as *part of a language already in use*, which is also why much of it draws on older cultural currents rather than a bespoke invented code (see [[Inherited Substrate]]).

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the emergent map (there is no authored path)
> A common misread is to expect a spatial path with the rooms arranged to mean something. There isn't one. The map is **emergent and collaboratively built**: any valid answer key can be entered at any open field, and entering it *manifests* that artifact at that spot — and the position then locks permanently. Roy's framing: *"the fans are creating a new world or creating a map within Saravi's Hall of Mirrors… there's really infinite pathways."* The practical consequence for a solver: room-*position* carries no clue information — the topology only records *who* solved *what*, *when*. The community boards are finding-aids, not solving keys.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the anatomy of the uploaded self (body-part codes)
> A cluster of unlock codes reads like a dissection: LEFT HIP OF A DOVE, TEETH OF A WHALE, RIGHT SHOULDER OF A HORSE, CRANIUM OF A SWAN. These aren't a random code-set — they're the **anatomy of the uploaded self, minted piece by piece**, grounded in the upload manifesto's own instruction to *"mint body parts to immortalize little luna and I."* The self is reassembled limb by limb, and the answer-keys themselves form chains: a SARAVI loop where **RA-LINK → SA-LINK → VI-LINK** assembles *SA-RA-VI* out of its own links — a name that builds itself from the codes that point to it.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — "The Upload" recipe, and the senses as fractions
> The cosmology the rest of the maze only gestures at gets a concrete decode in the **Complete Upload Recipe** artifact. It's a literal recipe of the self, assigning each sense the exact Eye-of-Horus *wedjat* fractions: ½ smell, ¼ sight, ⅛ thought, 1/16 hearing, 1/32 taste, 1/64 touch — plus a *Ra-link* (the SARAVI component) and *Sacrifice / Love* (the cost). The Egyptian fractions famously sum to 63/64, never quite reaching one — the missing 1/64 is the part magic (or breath, or the divine) has to supply. A recipe for a self that is, by design, slightly incomplete without something added from outside.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the immortality fork ("How to Live Forever?")
> The "How to Live Forever?" page is where the project's whole notion of immortality forks. Its implied answer is that *forever* means **entering the Pattern** (recursion, the living form) — **not** never-dying. Which splits two versions of the upload, held against each other across the work: a **true** immortality (the self enters a living pattern and is *transformed*) versus a **false** one (the self made into reproducible content — the upload as a data-copy, the influencer-clone, the algorithm's product). The maze stages both at once: a genuine memory palace *and* a satire of immortality-by-content. Whether that true/false fork is Roy's intent or imported from the same theme in [[City of Angels]] is an open question.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — "Coding Consciousness" cuts both ways
> The "Coding Consciousness" note — *"Patterns of right Living are Formed in the unconscious mind of a race…"* — reads as **encoding patterns into the collective/ancestral mind: culture as software.** Crucially it cuts both ways. It's [[The Circle]] when it encodes *truth* (pedagogy, remembrance, patterns of right living handed down). It's the Loop when it encodes *lies* (possession, the same machinery weaponized — the Algorithm running on inherited code). The note's diction ("patterns of right living," "race-mind") sits in a Theosophical register; the exact source author (Bailey, Besant, Blavatsky, or another) is an open question, which is itself a clean example of [[Inherited Substrate]] — Roy participating in an older current rather than coining one.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — Sir Rabbit, the proto-origin myth (Room 51)
> The richest single artifact for the wider mythology is the **Sir Rabbit poem** in Room 51 (unlock code Q763) — which Roy called *"sir rabbit pre-lucius."* It's a full four-page initiation poem with the whole later cast already in seed: the Cat, a Violet-Moon machine, Mr Fox, a lamp-bearing Dove, a golden-caped Spider, and "The Divide." Its keys point straight at the heart-over-mind doctrine — *"use your other mind," "the key is hidden in yourself / just listen for the knock," "it's been mapped"* — and it runs the Mourning/Morning-Sun pun that ties the Morning Star (Lucifer) to [[Sir Lucius]]. Far richer than the "rejected album art / golden egg" tour it's sometimes flattened to. The companion *"Walking the Edge of a Knife"* poem unlocks with the code **INSIDE OF THE INSIDE** — [[The Circle]]'s own phrase, which the poem then echoes (*"turn back the stone / from the inside"*): the answer-key is hidden *inside the artifact it unlocks*.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the lineage runs back to 2020
> The maze didn't begin in 2022. It grew out of a real fan-immersion experiment: Roy's free weekly guided-meditation Space (*"over 1,000 weekly registered users"*) and the Dec 2020 **Happy Face** facial-recognition interactive — the direct precursor to the crown/artifact architecture. That site ran a triple-pun on *"ARE YOU CONTENT?"* (content = satisfied vs content = media) over a call-now gag phone number, **(313)-251-4932** — and years later that exact number turned out to *be* a maze room code, digits-only. Its mascot is an upside-down smiley 🙃: the grin already masking its opposite, the whole later mirror-doctrine pre-loaded into an emoji. The predecessor sites also seeded lines the project keeps reusing — *"you must be IT"* (2021's *Down to You*) predates the later *"you are it,"* and *"change the concept of one's self"* lifts straight from Neville Goddard.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the URL nobody remembers ever changing
> A small forensic correction worth keeping straight: **mirrors.jagwartwin.com did not exist in 2022.** The maze first lived at the *path* jagwartwin.com/hall-of-mirrors, then **took over the root homepage** on 11/11/22 (the campaign's sixth drop) — the recovered page-title flips from a plain "jagwar twin" on 2022-10-31 to "Jagwar Twin | Hall of Mirrors" by late November. The current subdomain is a later migration. Easy to treat the present-day URL as always-true; it wasn't.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the traditions the codes spell out (framework, not asserted intent)
> The unlock codes and artifacts double as a reading list. The maze openly draws on Hermeticism and the Kybalion (left as a decoder reference in the ARG's own "Trash"), Kabbalah and the Tree of Life, Egyptian myth (the Eye-of-Horus fractions, Horus the all-seeing eye), Old-Testament divine-naming (*I am that I am*, Exodus 3:14; *El Roi*, "the God who sees," Genesis 16:13), Genesis cosmology (*"let us make man in our image,"* the four rivers of Eden), Lewis Carroll's *Through the Looking-Glass* (the "name of the song" problem, the White Knight), the cosmic world-egg, Norse Heimdall the gate-watcher, Babylonian Marduk, the Zen *ensō* circle, and sacred-geometry number (Lo Shu, vesica piscis, Fibonacci, the golden ratio) — all running on a web3/NFT substrate of crowns, one-of-one artifacts, and on-chain discovery records. The honest frame: Roy is **assembling and participating in older traditions**, not inventing a bespoke cipher from scratch. The decoder *toolkit* he literally left in the site's "Trash" — a Kybalion, gematrix.org, a Caesar-cipher tool, a Julian-date converter — confirms the numeric codes are gematria-, Caesar-, and date-encoded. Treat the correspondences as a system the maze *permits*, not metaphysics it asserts. See [[Inherited Substrate]].

And the maze is meant to be solved — the reward is the close reading in good company, not extracting a secret answer key.
