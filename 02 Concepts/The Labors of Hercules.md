# The Labors of Hercules

> **One-line reading.** A trial-cycle the project genuinely runs on — Roy reads the Twelve Labors not as adventure stories but, following Alice Bailey, as a map of the soul's own journey: twelve faculties each tested and returned to the heart's rule, with the Lion of Leo (the ego grown tyrannical) the one that matters most here.

Most of the esoteric frameworks behind the Jagwar Twin world have to be reconstructed from hints. This one Roy hands you in plain sight. He keeps three Alice Bailey quotes in his public influences. He photographed a hand-underlined page of Bailey's *The Labours of Hercules* to his Instagram Story. He transcribed Bailey into his own conceptual spreadsheet. And in a livestream — to about fifty people, over morning cacao — he named the book outright and taught its core idea as his own. So this isn't a frame imported from outside the work; it's furniture the artist installed himself, and told you where he bought it. What follows leads with that grounded engagement, then offers a reading of how the cycle maps onto the catalogue — clearly marked as a reading, because the closer mapping is interpretive.

## At a glance

- **In one line** — the soul's twelve trials read as a single inner journey; each labor is a distorted faculty returned to the heart's rule, and the central one is taming (not killing) the ego-lion.
- **Where it shows up** — [[Not Your Homie]] (the strongest anchor: *tame The Beast… a champion*) · [[Welcome to the Circus]] (the lion ravaging in the open) · [[#1 Champion (la Conquistadora)]] (the conqueror conquered).
- **Connected ideas** — [[Sir Lucius]] · [[The Dweller on the Threshold]] · [[The Loop and the Circle]] · [[Mary]] · [[Superhuman]] · [[Inversion]].
- **Confidence** — grounded that Roy works the Bailey labors as a personal framework (he names the book, annotates it, teaches it). The mapping of specific songs to specific labors is a strong reading, not a stated fact — and is labeled as such throughout.

## What it is

The Twelve Labors of Hercules are the canonical trial-cycle of Greek myth: twelve ordeals the hero completes in sequence, each harder and more meaningful than the last, the whole set traversing the zodiac one sign at a time. That's the version everyone half-remembers from school — the lion, the hydra, the stables.

The version Roy reads is stranger and more useful. In the **esoteric tradition** — Theosophy broadly, and Alice A. Bailey's *The Labors of Hercules: An Astrological Interpretation* (1974, Lucis Publishing) specifically — the labors aren't adventures at all. They're a **map of the soul's path**, the stages a person crosses to move from sleeping, mechanical existence into integrated, awakened being. Hercules isn't a mythic strongman; he's the *prototype* — "an earnest, erring, deeply flawed human being" who learns through devastating trial and error. He is every aspirant who consciously begins the work.

Two practical notes before we go on. Bailey reorders the labors **zodiacally** (Aries = I through Pisces = XII), which is *not* the order of the classical Hercules myth-books — so when this page numbers a labor, it numbers it Bailey's way, because that's the way Roy does (the Lion is Labor V / Leo, not "Labor 1"). And Bailey reads the whole cycle as moving *against* the current of material instinct: the soul has to **choose** the trial. Election is the first requirement. Keep that word — *choose* — it comes back load-bearing in the lyrics.

This is exactly how Roy frames it. In his livestream he described *"a book by Alice Bailey called The Twelve Labours of Hercules, and Hercules, which is the soul, which is us, the initiate, goes through all twelve signs of the zodiac… but we all have these inside of us… in different aspects of our life."* The labors as an *internal* initiation — twelve faculties inside one person — is his stated reading, not an external imposition.

## How it works

What the framework *does* across the project is supply the deep grammar for its central drama: how do you deal with a powerful part of yourself that has stopped serving you? Bailey's answer — and the project's — is never *destroy it*. It's **integrate it**. That single move, worked out most fully in the Leo labor, is what ties the Twelve Labors to [[Sir Lucius]], to the [[The Protector|Protector]] arc, to [[Mary]], and to the album's whole shape.

Two claims are easy to blur here, and it's worth keeping them apart:

- **Roy works the Bailey labors as a framework for himself — this is established.** He names the book, annotates it, applies specific signs to his own birth chart, and teaches the entire cycle as an inner journey. Bailey sits as one named pillar of his larger system (the "I'm 33" board), alongside Walter Russell, the Kybalion, Kabbalah, Jung, and Plato.
- **Specific songs were composed *as* specific labors — this is a reading, not a stated fact.** Roy has never said "song X is labor Y." The mapping below is built on lyric and image, offered because it illuminates the work — held as a strong reading, not the author's blueprint.

A clean fit between a song and a labor doesn't settle which of those it is: a tidy match is equally consistent with deliberate design *and* with an attentive listener's pattern-finding. So the mapping earns its place by the anchors in each song, not by how neatly the whole thing lines up. Since Roy named the book, the question is no longer *"is Bailey here?"* (he is) but *"does this song actually map to a labor, or am I back-fitting his stated interest onto unrelated material?"* The lens is licensed; mapping every track is not.

**Roy reaches the labors *through his own chart.*** This is the method, and it's the cleanest tell of how he uses the framework: natal chart → sign → Bailey labor → soul-lesson. He posted the Libra labor (and is Libra Rising); he taught the Cancer labor (and has Venus in Cancer). His unit isn't "song equals labor" — it's *the soul's lesson per sign, lived personally.*

## The cycle as the project reads it

Bailey runs the labors in zodiacal order, Aries through Pisces. Read as faculties of the soul, each labor tests a power that has gone distorted, and the work is to return it to the heart's rule — the project's recurring *"my mind must serve my heart."* The arc, in one line: **Mind → Desire → Knowledge → Soul → Ego → the Feminine → Balance → Shadow → Speech → Death → the Collective → Wholeness** — the soul learning, stage by stage, not to misuse power.

## Leo — the Nemean Lion (the central labor)

The fifth labor is the one most clearly woven through the project's public work, so it gets the most depth.

**The myth.** An invincible lion terrorizes Nemea; its hide can't be pierced by any weapon. Hercules tracks it to a cave with **two openings**, chases it through, and the lion just runs out the other side. Sword and arrows bounce off. So he changes his approach entirely: he **blocks one opening**, *leaves his weapons outside*, enters through the other, and **strangles the lion bare-handed** in the dark. He cannot kill it from a distance with his usual tools. He has to enter the cave, drop the weapons, and meet the beast directly.

**Bailey's reading.** The lion is the **coordinated dominant personality** — the ego built up by all the earlier labors into something genuinely powerful, capable, self-sustaining. By Leo the aspirant is no longer a floundering mess; they've become highly successful by every worldly measure. *And that is precisely the danger.* The same ego-architecture that secured survival becomes a self-centered force devastating to everyone around it. The lion is *successful Hercules, turned against the world.* Blocking one cave-mouth seals the *emotional escape route* — no fleeing into excuses, victim-narratives, or defensive discharge. And the strangling isn't killing in the ordinary sense: what dies is the ego's *authority*, its dominance over awareness. The lion's *energy* is preserved. What changes is the hierarchy.

**The Strength card.** The tarot's Strength card (Major Arcana VIII; XI in some decks) shows a serene figure gently closing the jaws of a lion — not slaying it, not caging it from a distance, but in calm, direct contact. Its meaning is mastery through gentleness rather than force, instinct *integrated* rather than suppressed. **The lion is not the enemy.** The card is the steady state *after* the Leo labor is done right — Hercules's strangling is the active integration; the Strength card is the peace that follows.

**Beast integration.** Across all of it — Bailey's Leo, the Strength card, the broader Western mystery tradition — the move is identical: the Beast is not destroyed, it is **integrated**; integration is not amputation (the energy is kept, the authority changes); the means is *direct contact up close*, not combat at distance; and the aspirant must *put down the habitual weapons* — the defenses and deflections that worked before — to meet it. The Beast is, structurally, *the aspirant's own prior solution* — the ego built to survive, now grown too strong to share power. This is the operation Carl Jung calls **shadow work**: the disowned material holds real value, and the task is *relationship*, not elimination.

This is why [[Sir Lucius]] reads as the Nemean Lion — the dominant personality grown tyrannical — and why the project never frames the answer as "kill Lucius." The lion's skin, integrated and worn, *is* the Protector: the wound-born armor of [[Superhuman]], [[Loser]], [[Good Day]], subordinated to the soul rather than amputated.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the cave, the pituitary, and the lion-skin mantle
> Bailey reads the cave's **two openings** as the two lobes of the **pituitary gland** in esoteric anatomy — the dual modalities of consciousness, emotional/instinctual against higher reasoning. Blocking one opening is sealing the emotional escape route at the level of the body's own switchboard. The strangling is then not a death but a *demotion*: the personality keeps its power and loses its independence.
>
> The continuation sharpens "integration not destruction" into a concrete image. In the text Hercules *"killed the lion and stripped its skin, shewing it to the people,"* and then — per the Lucis Trust reading (*In the Tracks of Hercules*, Leo) — he **wears that skin as his mantle**, carrying it through every later labor and shedding it only at Capricorn (transfiguration). He doesn't destroy the personality's power; he slays its *independence* and wears it. The conquered lion becomes the disciple's vesture. So the Protector isn't killed and isn't kept on the throne — it becomes the coat you walk the rest of the road in.
>
> One Kabbalistic rhyme, held as resonance: this is **Geburah (Severity / Mars) tempered by Chesed (Mercy / Expansion)** — the same correction the Strength card draws. On that map, [[Superhuman]] is the *untempered* Geburah pole: severity with no mercy to soften it, the lion as brute force.

## The strongest lyric anchor

The clearest single piece of public evidence tying the framework to the catalogue is in [[Not Your Homie]]:

> *"But if you choose to tame The Beast / you've got to prove yourself to be / a champion."*

Three things land at once. ***The Beast*** — capitalized and definite-articled, a specific named entity, not just any beast. ***Tame*** — not *fight*, not *slay*; *tame* is the operative verb of the Strength card and Bailey's Leo, integration through contact rather than destruction at distance. (Swap in *fight* and the reading collapses — *tame* is the load-bearing word.) And ***champion*** — which, in a body of work built on ordeal, descent, and return, reads as the technical term for *one who has completed the trials*, not gym-floor motivation. The cluster *tame + Beast + champion* across three consecutive lines is tight enough to read as a deliberate touch on the Leo labor's field. The *"choose to"* is Bailey's election requirement, hiding in plain sight: the trial is voluntarily taken up.

The grounding goes past the lyric. Roy states the champion ethic in his own biographical voice — *"be willing to fight for it. Even if that means losing in the short term"* — said about being dropped by his major label. *Prove yourself to be a champion* is lived, not just sung. (Track 11 on [[Lucius Lullaby (album)|Lucius Lullaby]]; released as a pre-album single on October 31, 2025; credited to Jagwar Twin, MERCY, and Sir Lucius.)

## Cancer and Libra — the labors Roy taught

**Cancer (the Doe).** In his livestream Roy told this labor in full and applied it to himself — and confirmed Bailey's numbering on the way (*"cancer specifically is the fourth labor"*). Hercules must capture the thing he loves most, a wild doe. Two goddesses counsel him: one urges *instinct* ("like an animal, lower"), the other *intellect* ("higher") — and acting from either alone fails. A *third* voice succeeds: *"the synthesis between the instinct and the intellect… the intuition… super consciousness, the god voice thing in here."* He captures the doe, holds it, loves it. Cancer's gift is to love and nurture deeply, to be psychic; its shadow is to *"hold too tight and won't let go."* Then the turn: he is commanded to *let the doe go*, sacrificing it on the altar of Apollo the sun-god, where it is *"born again over and over again forever"* — *"letting go and not attaching to the outcome. Knowing that everything that comes to me is God's."* Roy threads it through his own chart (Venus in Cancer) and applies it to record labels, career, and love.

The triad and the imagery are faithful to Bailey's own text — the instinct/intellect/intuition ladder, the two advising goddesses (Diana/Artemis), the doe laid on the altar of the Sun-God, the eternal repetition (*"Again and yet again must all the sons of men… seek for the golden antlered fawn"*). One honest overlay to flag: in Bailey the doe *is* the intuition itself; Roy personalizes it as "the thing I attract, love, and create" — his Venus-in-Cancer reading. Faithful application, slightly shifted; not a misreading. It resonates closely with the project's heart-mind structure and its synthesis-move — and the *"altar of the sun-god… born again forever"* with the sun imagery of [[SOL]], where the recursion reads as *practice*, not entrapment.

**Libra (the Boar).** The page Roy annotated and posted to his Story (captioned *"Alice Bailey ⚔"*) is the Libra labor — confirmed by its own text (*"The Libran is not inclined to be either a zealot or a tyrant"*). His underlines cluster on *the cost of serving a humanity that misreads you*: *"it is very difficult to serve the incredible species called man"*; *"reason with him, and he will stubbornly insist on the primacy of his instincts."* And the densest mark of all, fully underlined: *"the perversity that upholds the opposite of what is said"* — which reads almost as a definition of [[Inversion]] (same form, opposite content; "call evil good, light darkness"). That these are *his* underlines is grounded; the thematic link to inversion, and the rhyme with the Circus's misread-truth-teller, is the reading. Stories are ephemeral — the screenshot is the archive.

>
> The triad resonates outward (resonance, not identity): instinct/intellect/intuition with the project's heart-mind structure and its synthesis movement; *"the god voice… in here"* with the throat/[[El Roi]] cluster; *"holds too tight, won't let go"* with the [[Sir Lucius\|Lucius]] possession-vs-release axis and protective overreach; *"sacrifice on the altar… born again forever"* with [[SOL]] and recursion framed as discipline rather than the loop's trap.

## The cycle's far end — Capricorn, Aquarius, Pisces

Three completion-labors close the wheel, and the catalogue touches them unevenly.

**Capricorn (Cerberus)** is the descent. Its labor is a journey into hell — Hercules brings up the three-headed dog — and yet its sign is the mountain-top, the goat that becomes "the unicorn of God." The summit is reached *through* the underworld; the descent *is* the ascent. This grounds the reading of the song [[Lucius Lullaby]] as a Capricorn labor: the childhood bedroom, the bottoming-out floor, *"life is but a dream"* — the lowest point read as the threshold-gate. **Aquarius (the Augean Stables)** is world-service: the land lies under "the accumulated filth of ages," and Hercules cleanses it not by shoveling but by **redirecting two rivers** through the stables. The cleansing is of the collective, not the self; its failure-pole is separateness and working for reward. **Pisces (the Cattle of Geryon)** is the return — gathering the dispersed self and bringing the whole herd home to the Sacred City, "the Server in Aquarius becoming the Saviour in Pisces."

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the gates, the descent, and the two unperformed labors
> Bailey's **gates doctrine**: *"Sagittarius is the little gate to Capricorn. There are two cosmic gates: Cancer, the gate into incarnation; Capricorn, the gate into the spiritual kingdom."* Sagittarius's own labor (the Stymphalian Birds) is about *right speech* — the "birds" are gossip, critical and self-pitying thought, "talking about things people aren't ready for"; its faculty is *"restraint of speech, and consequent harmlessness."* (One easy slip worth correcting: *silence* is the motto of **Scorpio**, not Sagittarius.)
>
> **Capricorn's keynote** is the summit-through-descent paradox stated outright — *"Lost am I in light supernal, yet on that light I turn my back"* — and its failure is *staying* lost in the light and refusing to turn back to serve. **Aquarius's** instruction is the same turn made collective: *"Turn now your back upon the brightness; reverse your steps; go back to those for whom the light is but a transient point,"* keynote *"Water of life am I, poured forth for thirsty men."* Read against the catalogue, the two completion-labors are largely *unperformed*: the algorithm/media songs ([[Online]], [[Happy Face]], [[Pay Attention]]) mostly *depict* the stables rather than cleanse them — [[Shine]] is the rare river-redirection.
>
> **Pisces / Geryon** as the Roy–Lucius image (held as a lead, keynote to verify): three-bodied Geryon = the divided being made one, slain by a single arrow; the cattle herded into a golden cup (the Grail) and returned to the Sacred City. This is the structural source for the "turn back to save" arc that Roy's stated **North Node in Pisces** would predict.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — Bailey's deep structure (the wheel, the crosses, the Dweller)
> Three Bailey doctrines do real load-bearing work elsewhere in the project, so they belong here in full.
>
> **The wheel-reversal.** *"The man who is immersed in form… follows necessarily the path of illusion and of appearances; but Hercules, the soul, follows the true Way, reverses the usual procedure and, figuratively speaking, goes against the tide."* One wheel, two directions: ordinary humanity travels it clockwise (mechanical — a *loop*); the disciple reverses and goes anti-clockwise, zodiacal Aries→Pisces ("against the tide") — a deliberate, transformative *return*. This is the source-anchor for the project's [[The Loop and the Circle|Loop-vs-Circle]] keystone: same structure, opposite direction. (Note the trap: the soul's path *is* sequential Aries→Pisces, but that's the developmental wheel — **not** the album's release order. Don't conflate them.)
>
> **The three Crosses.** The Mutable Cross (Gemini–Virgo–Sagittarius–Pisces) = ordinary humanity / the personality; the Fixed Cross (Taurus–Leo–Scorpio–Aquarius) = the disciple's cross, which Hercules *mounts* (its mottos: Taurus "power to do," Leo "to dare," Scorpio "silence," Aquarius "to know"); the Cardinal Cross (Aries–Cancer–Libra–Capricorn) = the Spirit / Initiate. Roy's stated placements — **Gemini Sun, Leo Moon, Libra Rising, Venus in Cancer, Mars and North Node in Pisces** — *span all three Crosses*, which is part of why the whole framework feels personally fitted to him.
>
> **The Dweller on the Threshold.** From Bailey's *Glamour: A World Problem* (1950): illusion is of the mental plane, glamour of the astral/emotional, maya of the etheric — and their combined sum in the integrated personality is **the Dweller on the Threshold**, the figure met at every gate of real change (Leo's cave, Capricorn's Hades-gate). This grounds the project's [[The Dweller on the Threshold|Dweller]] — and [[Sir Lucius|Lucius]]-as-Dweller — in Bailey's own system rather than in borrowed terminology.

## The comedy of it

Worth saying plainly, because the material is genuinely funny: this is a man teaching ancient soul-initiation over a cup of hot chocolate, and losing his own thread mid-lecture. On the Live he derails into a true-Gemini tangent about wanting *"to circle back or ask what was I saying… Sometimes I'll forget where I was going with the thing"* — and blames it on his sun sign. He treats his morning matcha as a metaphysical question (*"is it the information of this matcha leaf… or what being is talking to me now?"*), takes his caffeine choices as a matter of which entity is channeling through him, and signs off the whole esoteric broadcast like a radio DJ: *"I am at peace, in peace, in pieces. I'm in pieces, looking for pieces of one piece… alright, Elroy sounding off. Love you guys."* Cosmic fragmentation, delivered with a wink.

And the trial itself, in the visual lore, gets triggered by produce. In [[The Interview (series)|The Interview]], adjacent to the *tame-the-Beast* song, [[Sir Lucius|Lucius]] reaches into the fruit bowl, lifts a *browning banana*, and presses it like a button to play "not your homie" — the most important spiritual ordeal in the catalogue, cued off a piece of overripe fruit. He cheerfully concedes the hit means nothing (*"It's nonsense. It's all nonsense"*), said with satisfaction. And in [[#1 Champion (la Conquistadora)]] he wins the title only by drowning [[Mary]] out, then is left repeating *"number one champion"* alone, a cappella, after the music has stopped — boasting into a dead room.
