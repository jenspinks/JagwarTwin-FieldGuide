# The Loop and the Circle

> **One-line reading.** Two recursions with the same shape and opposite charge: the Loop is return without transformation, the Circle is return with the self remembered — and the whole project is the work of turning one into the other.

## At a glance

- **In one line** — the same circular shape, drawn twice: the Circle grows you outward from your own center; the Loop walks you forever without letting you become anyone new.
- **Where it shows up** — [[The Circle]] (the founding myth, the *33* closer) · [[LOOP]] (the trap named from the light side) · [[Welcome to the Circus]] (the Circle run outward as spectacle).
- **Connected ideas** — [[Sir Lucius]] · [[Inversion]] · [[Source vs Reflection]] · [[The Thread]] · [[The Circus]] · [[Mary]].
- **Confidence** — grounded fact (Roy's own words, released songs, public posts) braided with strong interpretation; the heavy esoteric framework is collapsed below and labeled as framework throughout.

## What it is

This is the single most load-bearing idea in the Jagwar Twin world. Nearly every other concept either serves the Circle or inverts it.

The Circle is Roy's name for a shape of return: no beginning, no end, the thing you're looking for already inside you — recovered by remembering it rather than going out and acquiring it. The Loop is that exact same circular shape gone wrong: a closed track you walk forever, ending up precisely where you started, with no one new having made the trip.

That's the whole tension in a sentence. Both are circles. Both spin. You cannot tell them apart by their shape — only by what happens to *you* on the way around. The Circle changes you. The Loop deletes the change. And the entire arc of the work is the labor of converting one into the other.

## How it works

**The Circle is felt; the Loop is measured.** Roy locates the Circle not in a chart position but in a live moment — feeling it when the crowd sings the song back to him. It is embodied, communal, present-tense. The Loop, by contrast, is counted: streams, reach, metrics, *"the algorithm, baby."* Same recursive geometry; opposite charge. You can hear the present-tense register in how Roy answers even a throwaway question — asked his favorite fruit, he won't generalize: *"raspberries. Just plain raspberries, today, at this moment, at 11:18 PST. But organic raspberries only."* That is the Circle's sensibility as a personality tic — truth is what's true *right now,* in this body, on this timestamp; nothing is allowed to abstract into a metric.

The sharpest line between them: **the Loop is return without transformation; the Circle is return with memory restored.** Both end where they began — but in the Circle the traveler is not the same person who set out, because remembrance traveled the circuit with them. The Loop's nightmare version is a return where *"everyone looks just like you"* (from [[City of Angels]]) — return with the self deleted. The Circle's version is return with the self transformed.

| | The Circle | The Loop |
|---|---|---|
| Mode | embodied, communal, present-tense | private, silent, repetition-compulsion |
| Measured by | the crowd singing it back; remembrance | streams, reach, metrics |
| Direction | grows outward from the inside | closed, self-referential, going nowhere |
| Voiced by | Roy | Lucius |

**The Circle's signature gesture is the crowd singing it back.** The song the maker gave returns to her through the crowd's own voices — and that single image is the whole idea at once. It's the **anti-metric** (you cannot stream-count communion). It **enacts the founding myth**: the maker hid the song *"inside their wings,"* the new kings told the Jagwars *they never could sing* — so the crowd singing it back *is* the Jagwars singing again, the suppressed song recovered audibly in real time. It's **reciprocity at scale**: *"It moves to me, I move to you"* (from [[Move To You]]) is two centers in mutual motion, and the crowd singing it back scales that into a closing loop of mutual movement, which is what makes it a circle and not a broadcast. And it runs on **remembrance, not reception**: the crowd doesn't get the song handed down top-down, it *remembers* a song that was always inside it. The open secret, sung in unison.

Its exact mirror-image is the [[The Circus]]: the same crowd energy pointed the other way. The Circle *receives* voices in communion; the Circus *extracts* them for the metric. Same singing crowd, opposite pole.

**The flip is deliberate, and it's Lucius's whole move.** [[Sir Lucius]] takes Roy's own recursive idea and turns the word against him — *"you're in a loop and you don't even realize it."* That's the operation called [[Inversion]]: keep the form intact, reverse the vector. You cannot tell the Circle from the Circus by shape — both are circular, both spin. The only place the difference is legible is *from inside*, by whether you feel more or less yourself.

## Where it appears

**[[The Circle]] — the founding myth.** The closing track of *33* (2022), and the doctrine's fullest statement. A feminine primordial Jaguar with *"a world tied on a string"* makes the Jagwars and teaches them to sing; *"new kings"* persuade them they can't; the maker *"hid the song inside their wings, on the inside of the inside,"* then left them to sing; empires *"built cages and sold everything"*; and at last *"a wise one … remembered she once held the string."* The refrain is the whole instruction: *"The circle will grow from the inside of the inside… But the inside of the inside must be where you go."* Roy has called it the front door: *"The Circle is Jagwar Twin's Genesis story."*

**First named, three years earlier, on the most vulnerable song.** The Circle is not late scaffolding. Roy named it as a worldview at his very first press cycle, talking about [[Move To You]] in 2019: *"Then I realized I was never lost, I was learning what I needed to learn on the exact timeline I needed to be on… There is no beginning and no end it's just a circle."* It is worth noticing *which* song the doctrine first attaches to — the album's most tender track, written when Roy *"felt like I was never going to make it."* The cosmology is born in exactly the register Lucius later mocks as the forgotten flop. (More on that buried irony below.)

**[[LOOP]] — the trap named from the light side.** A standalone 2025 single, written solo by Roy, and pointedly kept *off* the *Lucius Lullaby* album. The hook is the trap in plain speech: *"I feel like I'm stuck in a loop / I keep ending up back at you / On this carousel, I'm going round and around."* The coda answers it: *"close your eyes and just imagine you're going on a journey… this is called the journey."* It opened a publicly named three-single chain — LOOP → [[Not Your Homie]] → [[Welcome to the Circus]] — framed in press as one path seeking clarity *beyond* the system and another mastering it *from within*. LOOP is the clarity-side path. The middle link is itself an inversion of the Circle's own gesture: *Not Your Homie* is built on *"ring-around-the-rosie, my pocket full of posies"* — **the most communal circle-game there is, the children's ring turned into a plague-march** (*"ashes, ashes, we all fall down"*). The Circle's hand-in-hand ring, weaponized: same circle, walked as contagion instead of communion. Around its release Roy ran the break-the-LOOP campaign on X: *"If you're ready to break the LOOP leave a ∞,"* *"Break the LOOP and step into your destiny,"* *"Let your old self die,"* *"Root yourself in truth not trauma."* (The doctrine also surfaces as cheerful cryptic shitpost: *"Life is a loop,"* *"Time is a circle squared."*)

**[[Welcome to the Circus]] — the Circle inverted into spectacle.** Where the Circle says *"the inside of the inside must be where you go,"* the Circus says *"there's no exit sign in my maze."* In the theatrical material Roy asks, *"What happened to music that was created from the soul?"* — and Lucius answers, *"The algorithm, baby."* The Circus is the Circle's communion run backward into extraction. And the theatrics are funny on purpose: Lucius doesn't cue up his hits from a soundboard — he **plays each one by pressing a browning banana from the fruit bowl like a button**, as if the whole machine ran on spoiling produce. The algorithm operates via rotting fruit.

**The Loop, named by Lucius (theatrical canon).** The word *Loop* is Lucius's, not Roy's. In the theatrical "Interview" material he tells Roy *"you're in a loop and you don't even realize it,"* and defines that loop as Loser → Move To You → Happy Face — a tidy chart-shaped circuit. The fidelity rule holds: Lucius speaking is not Roy speaking. When Lucius names the Loop, that's the shadow's diagnosis, deployed as a sales pitch — and the pitch is played for laughs, because Lucius is written as a **failed hype-man running an MLM for spiritual stagnation.** His whole bit is a deadpan inversion of an inspirational speaker: *"when you're vulnerable you LOSE!"* (delivered while miming ballet, treating streaming analytics as a weapon — *Ballerina Boy* is "your lowest-performing song of all time"); *"happy face, bad feeling, hundreds of millions of streams — those are me, you need me."* The horror underneath the comedy is that he's not wrong about the numbers. **Every catastrophe he causes streams extremely well.** That's the joke: he is genuinely, hideously *competent* at the only game he plays.

**The Circle, re-sung as a lullaby.** *Lucius Lullaby* (2026) opens on a track titled *The Circle* — and here's the deadpan album-level joke: it contains **none of the original's words.** It's the keystone reduced to a wind-up music-box-and-violin loop carrying a single looped line — *"It's a beautiful life, it's a beautiful life, I'm dreaming"* — which isn't from *33* at all; it's the refrain of the *next* track. The song wears the **name** "The Circle" while carrying the **Lullaby's** words: a bait-and-switch, not a cover. The keystone doctrine, reduced to a nursery toy you have to crank. (The full anatomy of that substitution is below.)

## The buried irony at the center

Before the framework material, one thread worth pulling on its own, because it's the most quietly devastating fact on the page.

The Circle is first named, in 2019, while Roy is talking about [[Move To You]] — the most vulnerable song on *Subject to Flooding*, written from the floor of *"I felt like I was never going to make it."* That is the origin point of the entire cosmology. And in the theatrical material, *Move To You* is the exact song Lucius singles out to bury: *"does anyone remember Move To You?"* — delivered with a crow cawing for comic timing, the shadow adding his own sound effects to inter his maker's ballad. So the catalogue's deepest worldview is anchored to the one song the shadow has designated as dead weight. The cosmology's origin point is the song the mind keeps trying to throw away.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the parallel timelines: convergence, not construction
> Two of the project's principal forces have slow-emergence timelines that converge on 2026 — and tracing them settles whether the mythology was retrofitted. The Circle is *named* in 2019, becomes the keystone song on *33* in 2022, and is re-sung distorted in 2026. The Lucius/Loop line traces back to a 2008 mirror-self credit (the *"Rorrim Ehtni"* alias — *"In the Mirror"* spelled backwards — on an early band record), and is fully billed as antagonist by 2026. Two forces, two decades, arriving at the same year from opposite directions. The doctrine predates its keystone song by three years; *33*'s "The Circle" formalizes a frame Roy was already using. The right word is **convergence, not construction** — the mythology grew toward itself rather than being assembled to order.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — "you are it," the standing Circle slogan
> Claim #2 of the doctrine — that the thing you seek is already within — has a public-facing slogan running just as far back. The [[Down to You]] microsite (2021) states: *"No one else can be IT, you must be IT for your own unique journey."* It resurfaces as the 2025 MAGIK ARG drop-1 password *"you are it,"* which traces directly to **Alan Watts's** *"you're it"* doctrine (Watts is a Roy-named influence — Roy calls him *"a loyal servant of the Realm"*). So *"you are it"* is a standing Circle-doctrine slogan since at least 2021, not a 2025 coinage. Roy grounds the same idea repeatedly on X: *"Music is how the world remembers who it is,"* *"Every person has it inside of them. The trick is getting it inside out,"* *"Every maze has a center."*

> [!note]- Advanced reading — there's only one song
> Roy's Manifesto FAQ states it outright: *"The world needs music, but the world doesn't need more songs. There's only one song. I'm still learning how to write it."* Read through the Circle, that is the doctrine's spine. The one song is the primordial song the maker hid *"inside their wings"* and the kings suppressed; the entire catalogue reads as successive attempts to re-transmit it. The Wise One's *remembrance* is what recovers it — not writing a new song, but re-membering the only one. Recovery is anamnesis, not composition.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the feminine thread (origin, exile, return)
> The founding myth is bracketed by two feminine figures who hold the same object. It *opens* with a primordial Jaguar — *"a world tied on a string… she made other Jagwars, then taught them to sing"* — who possesses the string and gives the song. It *closes* with a Wise One who *"remembered she once held the string."* Beginning and end rhyme, and both are *she.* Read alongside [[The Thread]], the cosmogony resolves into one image: the feminine holds the string of feeling and song; the *"new kings"* (the masculine system of control — cages, commodification, *"you can't sing"*) cut it; recovery means re-membering that thread. That severance — heart cut from its animating line at civilizational scale — is the same cut [[Sir Lucius]] performs at personal scale. On this reading the divine feminine ([[Mary]], the Wise One, the primordial Jaguar) is one feminine *function* seen at three points of the cycle — origin, exile, return — **not** a single named character. The guardrail is archetypal continuity, not identification: the Wise One is not "Mary," and the question of who, if anyone, *is* the maker stays genuinely open.
>
> The Wise One holds two faces at once, joined by anamnesis. There is a **cosmic-source** face (she *was* the maker — a Sophia/Shekinah register, the divine wisdom in exile who remembers and returns) and an **immanent-collective** face: because the song was hidden inside *every* Jagwar, the Wise One is the template of remembrance any of us can instantiate — *"all of us, remembering."* Roy now grounds the collective face publicly (*"Jesus is my North Star"*; *"getting it inside out"*; *"Meet David Bowie"* — a Jesus, a Bowie, a Prince as exemplars). The Gnostic hinge is that personal awakening and reunion with the cosmic maker are the *same event*, which is why both faces can be true without contradiction.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — Source vs Reflection, the master key
> Circle-versus-Loop is one special case of a larger frame: *does a reflection return you to the source, or trap you in the image?* The Circle is return-to-source; the Loop is a reflection closed on itself. And [[Sir Lucius]] is the third step the binary doesn't capture — not just a distorted mirror but a reflection that **claims sourcehood** (usurpation). One grounded tell that the mirror is structural rather than decorative: across the sung catalogue, the pronoun counts run *I* ≈ *the* ≈ *you*, neck-and-neck — the mirror is the *grammar* of the writing, not only an image laid on top of it. See [[Source vs Reflection]].

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the four inversions of The Circle (Lucius Version)
> The *Lucius Lullaby* opener performs four precise inversions of the keystone. **Awakening → dreaming:** the song of remembrance is rendered as a literal lullaby, a sleep-song; the Circle's waking/remembering doctrine becomes *"I'm dreaming."* **Depth → platitude:** the cosmology flattens to *"it's a beautiful life."* **Circle → Loop:** the living shape becomes a mechanical music box — wound from outside, no will of its own, going nowhere ("beauty without agency" is the Lucius condition). **Many voices → one surface:** the original's communion becomes a single smooth surface hiding multiplicity. Per the credits, the track is *not* instrumental — "sir lucius" and "ROY" are credited as two distinct vocalists, with Roy on a literal Music Box plus bass/synth/keys and children's background vocals layered so the voices are *"aligned to sound like one voice unless you listen closely."* It flows seamlessly into track 2 (it reads live as a single overture). So the album opens *inside* the corrupted Circle, and the un-distorted doctrine is **absent from the record** — present only by its violation.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — communion vs spectacle: a unison is not a communion
> Both poles look identical from a distance: a crowd singing together. **Communion** is many *distinct* voices singing the truth back (the Circle's chorus — a crowd awakened). **Spectacle** is many voices forced into the *same* song (the Circus/Loop — a crowd synchronized). The in-lyric anchor is *"the crooks and believers all sing the same song"* (from [[Great Time to Be Human]]) — and a unison is not a communion. *The Circle (Lucius Version)* literalizes the dark pole: Roy and child voices *engineered* to sound like one voice — unison manufactured and passed off as communion. Across the corpus the sing-back device sorts cleanly by *voice*: Roy-voiced songs stage the communion-echo (crowd singing *with* — Long Time Coming, Loser's I→we bridge, Shine), Lucius-voiced songs stage commanded or harvested echo (the Pied-Piper *"sing along follow me"* of I Like to Party, Pay Attention's *"What's my name?"*, the Circus's *"scream and shout,"* the weaponized ring-around-the-rosie of Not Your Homie). A built-in crowd even lives at the mix level: the released lyric texts are saturated with parenthetical background echoes — *(In a loop)*, *(Watching me)*, *(It's been a long time coming)* — the record echoing its own claim back to itself.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the Babel hinge: nonsense, forced unison, and the anti-Babel
> There's a scriptural spine under the whole "unison is not communion" point, and Lucius hands it over himself. When Roy says his banana-button hit *"doesn't mean anything,"* Lucius agrees with relish: *"Exactly. It's nonsense. It's all nonsense."* That is the **Tower of Babel** in one line — Genesis 11's *"one language and the same words"* that God *confuses* (the Hebrew text's own pun, *balal*, "to confuse," folk-carried into English **babble**). Babel is the scriptural name for *one language collapsing into noise* — and a crowd taught to sing one song *back to Lucius* is not the Circle reclaiming its voice, it's the **Babel crowd**, whose biblical fate is to scatter. The Circle's communion is the exact inverse: **the anti-Babel, a Pentecost inversion** — many tongues, one understanding, distinct voices that nonetheless mean the same true thing. So the two poles aren't just "together vs. forced-together"; they're Babel (meaning evacuated, the tower-builder feeding the crowd noise) against Pentecost (meaning restored across difference). This reading stands on public material alone — the on-camera *"it's all nonsense,"* the Genesis pun, and *"all sing the same song"* — independent of any letter-cipher.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — The Wheel: vertigo instead of prayer
> *"Spinning you round and round"* (from [[Welcome to the Circus]]) is the Circle in its most mechanical form — the spin without consciousness, return without integration. The whirling dervish and the circus-spinee perform the *exact same motion*; everything turns on what's at the center. Laid out, the distinction is the whole Circle/Loop split in miniature:
>
> | The dervish's spin (Circle) | The circus's spin (Loop) |
> |---|---|
> | Chosen | Imposed |
> | Oriented toward the center | Disorienting, away from the center |
> | The self is the axis | The self is the rim |
> | Silence at the center | No center |
>
> If the Circle is the dervish's still center, the Circus is what happens when you remove the center: you spin without an axis — vertigo instead of prayer. The resonance web is deep — Wheel of Fortune (Tarot X), Samsara and its exit (moksha), Ezekiel's *"wheel in the middle of a wheel,"* Boethius' *Rota Fortunae*, King Lear's *"wheel of fire,"* the hamster wheel — but the operative distinction is always the same: which way is the center pulling you.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — Inversion on Roy's own body
> The Circle→Loop flip is one instance of a general law Roy wears literally. The tattoo *"FEAR Functions like Prayer in Reverse"* (his gloss: fear drives us *away* from our destiny; he acts out of love instead) names the mechanism — fear is prayer running backward, the same machine with the sign flipped. Inversion keeps the form intact and reverses only the vector; it is *not* negation. The same logic runs *ROSE ↔ ESOR* (palindrome) and the mirror-writing *"Rorrim Ehtni."* A pointed sub-case: the Pinocchio string-inversion. *"I've Got No Strings"* sells *stringlessness* as freedom — Lucius's pitch, severance sold as liberation — while the Circle runs it backward: *"the wise one remembered she once held the string."* Realness comes from re-membering the connection, not cutting it. And the theatrical arc itself is described as Lucius building *"his own Dark Circle within The Circle"* — the inversion staged as plot.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the Loop-era spine, and the resolution verb
> The theatrical Loop-era mythology runs (in its own unreliable-narrator voice): *"In the beginning there was the One. The One was divided"* → *"a shadow awoke. And the shadow was Sir Lucius"* → Lucius builds *"his own Dark Circle within The Circle and the loop continued"* → *"My mind must serve my heart, Roy repeated… as he stepped through the gate"* / *"The loop ends the moment you choose it"* / *"It's time to break the LOOP"* → *"Roy's prayer was answered through a crack in the sky"* / *"The tree would not fall into the flames"* → *"With infinity powering eternity, Roy fought to escape the LOOP as the rose bloomed."* The load-bearing detail: the resolution verb is **service** (*"my mind must serve my heart"*), not destruction. The Loop isn't broken by killing the mind; it's broken by re-subordinating the mind to the heart. Integration, not elimination — which is why the arc keeps refusing a clean victory.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the one song, three scriptural handlings
> John 8:32 (*"the truth will set you free"*) refracts across three eras and tracks the Loop-arc by how each handles the truth. *It's Your Time* (2022, heart-led): *"the truth is hard to tell."* *Bad Feeling* (2023, split): *"the truth is a lie that nobody can tell."* *Lucius Lullaby* (2026, mind-led): *"but nothing's what it seems."* The same verse, degrading from "hard but real" to "nothing is real" — the Loop measured in scripture-handling. A related light/dark pair: *"Life is a loop"* (name the trap) versus *"Life is but a dream"* (be sung *inside* it) — the two poles of the *"life is but a ___"* family. *"I'm dreaming"* may be the album-wide sedation motif, the lullaby that puts the Circle's waking doctrine back to sleep.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — framework correspondences (not authorial intent)
> The Circle's deepest resonances are framework rhymes, licensed by a shared theosophical/perennialist root, **not** claims the work makes outright. In the **Lurianic Kabbalah** Roy engages, *tzimtzum* is the maker's self-withdrawal leaving the divine concealed within creation, recovered by reintegration (*tikkun*) — exactly the founding myth's maker who withdraws and hides the song within. The same tradition's *"kingdom within"* (and Luke 17:21, *"the kingdom of God is within you"*) names the truth as *uncovered, not acquired* — the inside-of-the-inside. There is a clean **Plato's-cave inversion**: where Plato's cave holds shadows mistaken for the real, the Circle's cave holds the *truth* — the original relation forgotten then remembered. Tolkien's **Ainulindalë** supplies creation sustained as song. Across all of them the recurring shape is descent into forgetfulness and recovery by memory. Do not upgrade these rhymes to deliberate cross-encoding.

The films illustrate the Pattern; they don't encode it. > [!note]- Advanced reading — Joni Mitchell's opposite carousel

Her *"Circle Game"* (*"captive on the carousel of time… in the circle game"*) is the trapped-carousel image — the *same* image as the Jagwar Twin Circle, opposite charge: Joni's circle *traps*, JT's *grows from within*. Roy's account of getting her sign-off is on record: labels said she'd never clear it, he found *"a friend who knew a friend… who knew Joni,"* she loved it and signed off — *"two people have done it — me and Janet Jackson."*

> [!note]- Advanced reading — SOL, the song Lucius can suppress but cannot metabolize
> The Circle has a limit-case, and it has a name: **SOL** (the *"Song of Love"*). In the theatrical material, *Soul Is A Star* / SOL is the wordless song of love that Lucius can **bury but cannot digest** — and that gap is where the usurpation shows its seam. Twice he refuses it: in the bargain scene Roy tries to debut *"The Song of Love"* (*"my favorite song I've ever written"*) and Lucius cuts him off — *"STOP!! No one wants this love stuff"* — and substitutes a banana-button hit instead. Then, in the kidnapping finale, when SOL finally plays, the shadow visibly loses composure: *he gets agitated and surprised, has Roy taken away, then sits and listens, unsettled, until it finishes.* The reading: SOL is the **secret in the circle** — the open-secret song *"everyone's guessing, everyone knows,"* sung back. Lucius can keep it off the radio, override it, talk past it; he cannot make it not-true, and he cannot turn it into a metric. It is the one transmission the Loop can't close around. The mind can suppress the heart's song; it cannot author one of its own — and SOL is where that limit becomes audible.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the Circle as a literal fan community
> The doctrine isn't only sung — it's the operating name of the owned community. On the public @jagwartwin Discord, Roy uses *"the Circle"* as the name of the inner society: *"I'm so happy we got the circle going"* (2023); *"The circle is definitely a secret society"* (2023); *"only the circle knows about the circle"* (2024); *"welcome to the circle… This is where the real Jagwar fam plots"* (2024); *"the circle will grow"* (2024, echoing the song's refrain); *"circles never end ❤️"* and *"i might just start the set with the circle completely acapella in this arena"* (2024). The song's refrain becomes the community's slogan, enacted. (The comedy writes itself: *"only the circle knows about the circle"* is fan-club bouncer energy — gatekeeping a secret society whose secret is that it's a secret society.) Roy also says, in one breath, *"the realm will expand the circle will grow"* — leaving open whether **[[The Realm]]** is a distinct concept or just another name for the owned community (the blockchain/ren-faire end-run around the gatekeepers). Inner-Circle paid specifics are not public; this stays grounded on the public statements only.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the inside of the inside: trap or exit
> The Loop has no single home — it's distributed across this page's neighbors (The Circle, LOOP, Inversion, Source vs Reflection, Communion vs Spectacle, The Wheel, The Aquarium). The richest open knot is *"the inside of the inside,"* which the [[The Aquarium]] reads **both** ways: the Loop's deepest trap (the center of the closed system, most caged) *or* the Circle's exit (the place the glass is thinnest, the breach). Same phrase, opposite valence — and the work hasn't decided which, possibly on purpose.
