# The Loop and the Circle

> **One-line reading.** Two recursions with the same shape and opposite charge: the Loop is return without transformation, the Circle is return with the self remembered — and the whole project is the work of turning one into the other.

This is the single most load-bearing idea in the Jagwar Twin world. Almost every other concept either serves the Circle or inverts it. The Circle is Roy's name for a shape of return — no beginning, no end, the thing you're looking for already inside you, recovered by remembrance rather than acquisition. The Loop is that same circular shape gone wrong: a closed track you walk forever without ever becoming anyone new.

## The Circle, in Roy's own words

The Circle is not a late invention or marketing scaffold. Roy named it as a worldview at his very first press cycle. Talking about the song "Move To You" in 2019, he said: *"Then I realized I was never lost, I was learning what I needed to learn on the exact timeline I needed to be on… There is no beginning and no end it's just a circle."*

Three linked claims sit inside that frame:

1. **No beginning and no end.** Time and path are circular, not linear. "Lost" is an illusion, because every path leads to the same place.
2. **The thing you seek is already within.** In the founding myth (the song [[The Circle]], the *33* closer), the maker *"hid the song inside their wings, on the inside of the inside."* So the journey is inward, and recovery is remembrance, not acquisition. Roy puts the mechanism plainly elsewhere: *"music is how the world remembers who it is."*
3. **It grows from the center.** *"The circle will grow from the inside of the inside."* Restoration spreads organically, from the deepest interior outward.

Crucially, Roy locates the Circle not in a chart position but in a live moment — feeling it when the crowd sings the song back. It is embodied, communal, and present-tense. That is exactly what separates it from the Loop.

Roy has also named the Circle as the catalogue's front door. Asked in 2024 what a new listener should hear first, he answered: *"I would check out 'The Circle' first. The Circle is Jagwar Twin's Genesis story."* The doctrine is its anchor; the song [[The Circle]] is its fullest statement.

## The Loop, named by Lucius

The Loop arrives from the other side. It is the word [[Sir Lucius]] uses, in the theatrical material, when he tells Roy: *"you're in a loop and you don't even realize it."* Where the Circle is felt, the Loop is measured — streams, reach, metrics, "the algorithm." Where the Circle is communal, the Loop is private, silent, a repetition-compulsion going nowhere.

|  | The Circle | The Loop |
|---|---|---|
| Mode | embodied, communal, present-tense | private, silent, repetition-compulsion |
| Measured by | the crowd singing it back; remembrance | streams, reach, metrics |
| Direction | grows outward from the inside | closed, self-referential, going nowhere |
| Voiced by | Roy | Lucius |

The geometry is identical. Only the charge is flipped. And the move is deliberate: Lucius takes Roy's own recursive idea and weaponizes the word against him — an [[Inversion]] of Roy's Circle.

## The sharpest distinction

The cleanest line between them: **the Loop is return without transformation; the Circle is return with memory restored.** Both end where they began — but in the Circle the traveler is not the same person who set out. The journey loops back to its starting point only after a real passage; what differs is whether remembrance travels the circuit. The Loop's nightmare version is a return where *"everyone looks just like you"* — return-with-the-self-deleted. The Circle's version is return-with-the-self-transformed.

## The crowd sings it back

The Circle's defining gesture, and the cleanest image of the whole idea, is this: the song the maker gave returns to her through the crowd's own voices.

It works on several levels at once.

- **It is the anti-metric.** The Loop is counted. The Circle is felt — reciprocal, present-tense, and uncountable. You cannot stream-count communion.
- **It enacts the founding myth.** The maker *"hid the song inside their wings"*; the new kings told the Jagwars *"they never could sing."* The crowd singing it back is the Jagwars singing again — the suppressed song recovered, audibly, in real time.
- **It is reciprocity at scale.** *"It moves to me, I move to you"* (from "Move To You") is two centers in mutual motion; the crowd singing it back scales that into a closing loop of mutual movement — which is what makes it a circle and not a broadcast.
- **They already know it.** The crowd doesn't receive the song top-down; it *remembers* it, because the song was inside them all along. The open secret, sung in unison.

Its mirror-image is the [[The Circus]]: the same crowd energy pointed the opposite way. The Circle *receives* voices in communion; the Circus *extracts* them for the metric. Same singing crowd, opposite pole — communion versus mob.

## Where it came from

Two of the project's principal forces have parallel, slow-emergence timelines that converge on 2026 — and tracing them settles the question of whether the mythology was retrofitted.

| Year | The Circle | Sir Lucius / the Loop |
|---|---|---|
| 2008 | — | earliest Lucius trace (*Rorrim Ehtni*) |
| 2019 | earliest naming — on "Move To You" | — |
| 2022 | the Circle as keystone song (*33*, track 10) | — |
| 2026 | the Circle re-sung, distorted | Lucius billed; the Loop named as antagonist |

The doctrine predates the keystone song by three years. *33*'s "The Circle" does not invent the concept — it formalizes and mythologizes a frame Roy was already using in 2019. And it is worth noticing *which* song the doctrine first attaches to: "Move To You," the album's most vulnerable track, written when Roy *"felt like I was never going to make it."* The Circle is born not in his most assertive register but in the very mode Lucius later mocks as the forgotten flop.

There's a public-facing version of claim #2 — that the thing you seek is already within — running just as far back. The slogan *"you are it"* (the inside-of-the-inside turned into a directive) appears on a 2021 microsite: *"No one else can be IT, you must be IT for your own unique journey."* It resurfaces in the 2025 ARG, where it traces to Alan Watts's *"you're it."* A standing Circle-doctrine slogan, not a one-off.

## One reading: the feminine thread

The founding myth is bracketed by two feminine figures who hold the same object. It *opens* with a primordial Jaguar — *"there was a Jagwar with a world tied on a string, and she made other Jagwars, then taught them to sing"* — who possesses the string and gives the song. It *closes* with a Wise One — *"the wise one remembered she once held the string."* Beginning and end rhyme, and both are "she."

Read alongside [[The Thread]] (the thread as feeling, the animating principle), the cosmogony resolves into one image: the feminine holds the string of feeling and the song, the "new kings" cut it (*"the new kings told Jagwars they never could sing"*), and recovery means re-membering that thread. The kings are the masculine system of control — cages, commodification, "you can't sing." That is mind divorced from heart at civilizational scale: the same severance [[Sir Lucius]] is at personal scale. On this reading the divine feminine ([[Mary]], the Wise One, the primordial Jaguar) is one feminine *function* seen at three points of the cycle — origin, exile, and return — rather than a single named character.

## One reading: there's only one song

Roy's Manifesto FAQ states it outright: *"The world needs music, but the world doesn't need more songs. There's only one song. I'm still learning how to write it."* Through the Circle, that is the doctrine's spine. The one song is the primordial song the maker hid *"inside their wings"* and the kings suppressed, and the catalogue reads as successive attempts to re-transmit it. The Wise One's *remembrance* is what the song is recovered by — not writing a new song, but re-membering the only one.

## In framework terms

The Circle's deepest resonances are framework correspondences, not claims the work makes outright. In the **Lurianic Kabbalah** Roy studies, the *tzimtzum* is the maker's self-withdrawal, leaving the divine concealed within creation, to be recovered by reintegration (*tikkun*) — exactly the founding myth's maker who withdraws and hides the song within. The same tradition's *"kingdom within"* (and the Gospel's *"the kingdom of God is within you"*) names the essential truth as something uncovered rather than acquired. And there is a clean **Plato's-cave inversion**: where Plato's cave holds shadows mistaken for the real, the Circle's cave holds the *truth* — the original relation forgotten and then remembered. The recurring shape across all of them is descent into forgetfulness and recovery by memory.

A pointed contrast lives in Joni Mitchell's *"Circle Game"* (a confirmed *33* influence): her image is a *trapped carousel*, a circle you cannot get off; the Jagwar Twin Circle is one that *grows from within*. Same image, opposite charge.
