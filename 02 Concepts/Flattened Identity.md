# Flattened Identity

> **One-line reading.** Flattened Identity is what the machine does to a living self: it reduces a multidimensional person to a usable surface — brand, face, role, statistic, avatar — and then insists that surface is all there is.

## At a glance

- **In one line** — the reduction of a whole person to a usable surface, plus the system's insistence that the surface is the whole person.
- **Where it shows up** — [[Online]] (database identity, predictive profiling) · [[Happy Face]] (the minimum-viable mask, scaled to a generation) · [[Welcome to the Circus]] (the self as spectacle, authored by *"the algorithm, baby"*).
- **Connected ideas** — [[The Algorithm]] · [[Sir Lucius]] · [[The Mask]] · [[The Mirror]] · [[Witnessing vs Surveillance]] · [[Machine vs Human]].
- **Confidence** — a strong reading built on grounded vocabulary. Roy names *the Algorithm*, *programming*, and *masks* in his own voice; the song-by-song flattening readings are interpretation, labeled as such.

## What it is

Everybody has a public face. You curate one for a job interview, a different one for your group chat, another for your mother. That's not flattening — that's just being a person, and a healthy person can take the face off when the room empties out.

Flattening is what happens when the room never empties. It's the system reducing a whole self into something it can read and use: a profile, a content-type, a target demographic, a star. The backstage disappears. The role stops being a choice and becomes the rent. And the person comes to be valued only for how useful that role is to the machine.

The sharpest way to put it, and the line this whole page hangs on:

> **Flattened Identity is not the mask. It is the system insisting the mask is all there is.**

A mask you can put down. Flattening is when the world stops recognizing the face underneath — and eventually, so do you.

## How it works

The move repeats all across the machine side of the catalogue, and it always runs the same direction. Call it the flattening chain:

> person → profile · voice → content · witness → audience · artist → product · soul → star · self → utility.

Every arrow is a reduction. A *person* has depth; a *profile* is a database row. A *voice* is somebody trying to say something true; *content* is a unit of engagement. The right-hand side is always smaller, flatter, and — crucially — more *useful* than the left. That's the engine. The system doesn't flatten you out of malice. It flattens you because the flat version scales.

The reason this matters is that it gives the catalogue's villain his job description. [[Sir Lucius]] is the **glamour of flattening** — not simply a fake, but *the self optimized for the machine*: the persona that wins precisely by becoming legible to the spectacle. This is why "is Lucius real, or a bit?" is the wrong question. Flattening is exactly the state in which that distinction stops mattering to the system. What performs is what counts; the rest is dropped. Lucius is what's left when Roy's three survival-faces — Loser, Good Day, No Fake Friends — calcify into one theatrical identity that knows how to stream.

And it gives the work its hero-shaped resistance, too. Set against the flattening chain is Roy's own stubbornly uncategorizable stance: *"I'm no thing."* In the Mirror Sessions interview, when Briana Cuoco reaches for a box — *a hiker? sober?* — Roy keeps deflecting into *"I consider myself no thing,"* even finishing her apology (*"Pigeonhole me"*) as a punchline. Anti-flattening played as a running gag. But it's load-bearing: to be no thing is to be unflattenable. You can't reduce to a surface a self that refuses to be a type in the first place.

So the concept has a shape: a machine that turns selves into surfaces, a persona who's the surface optimized, and a person who keeps slipping the category. Hold those three and most of the catalogue's "machine" material clicks into place.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — where it sits in the machine chain
> The lab framing places flattening as the *closing term* of a three-step descent: **Inherited Substrate** (where the raw material of the self comes from) → **The Mirror** (how the self gets mediated into an image) → **Flattened Identity** (what the machine does to that image once it's been severed from the heart). It is the corrupt endpoint — the living self reduced to a useful reflection.
>
> In Kabbalah grammar this is **Yesod severed from Tiferet**: the image-faculty cut loose from the heart it was meant to serve. That severing is the whole diagnosis. (Hold this lightly — it's syncretic framework, not a claim the songs make.)

> [!note]- Advanced reading — keeping it distinct from its neighbors
> The concept only stays useful if you don't let it blur into the ideas next to it:
> - **vs [[The Mask]]** — a mask can be *chosen*: protective, ritual, theatrical, transformative. Flattening is *imposed* or *rewarded* until the person can no longer easily take it off.
> - **vs [[The Mirror]]** — the Mirror shows the self *as* image. Flattening is when the image is the *only* thing the system will recognize.
> - **vs [[The Digital Double]]** — the Double is the *copied* self. Flattening is what the system *does* to that copy: reduces it to a profile, a content-object, a predictive pattern. The Double is the artifact; flattening is the operation performed on it.

## Where it appears

The lyric-grounded anchors come first, then the distributed ones.

**[[Online]]** (*33*, 2022) names database identity almost clinically: *"there's an army up against me and they got my face / automatic in the static of the human race"*; *"we're stuck / just like a bug inside your database."* It even names predictive profiling — *"they know what you like in bed before you come in two"* — counterfeit intimacy where statistical prediction has become indistinguishable from being known. Roy's gloss: *"Internet is like a web and we're the spider's food."* Not users. Prey.

The trick of the song is that it *is* the sedation it describes — a horror about being metabolized by the feed, wrapped in an upbeat consumer jingle (*"'cause you can get it online / everyone loves the internet… you feel good!"*), with *"but it's okay"* shrugging off every fresh horror on loop. Roy called it *"so fun and so ridiculous,"* and that's the point: the comedy is load-bearing. The puns are merciless — *"Buy! Buy! Buy!"* turning into *"Bye! Bye! Bye!"* (consumption and farewell as the same sound), and *"maybe Jupiter is stupider than people say"* deflating the whole *we-come-from-space* guru pile-up in one rhyme.

**[[Happy Face]]** (*33*, 2022) names the algorithmic version directly: *"Hollywood on your timeline / telling you what to wear / and what to like and how to be"*, building to *"death of my generation."* The 2020 video renders the smiley as a **gridded globe** — the minimum-viable mask scaled to a planet. (Roy ran the joke himself in a 2021 Halloween short: clown mask, caption *"When they tell you to put on a happy face 🤡."*)

**[[Welcome to the Circus]]** (2026) is the self *as* spectacle — *"it's a spectacle, I'm the final boss."* It's also the one place in the public catalogue where the machine confesses authorship out loud: in [[The Interview]] EP3, after Roy admits the song is *"exactly what Lucius would make,"* Lucius replies, deadpan, *"the algorithm, baby."* The flattening machine names itself, with a wink.

**[[Ballerina Boy]]** (2025) carries the oldest version: the schoolyard chant *"you're not a real boy."* That's ontological violence — it attacks *being*, not behavior — reducing a living child to a role-violation, acceptable only if his body performs the correct category. The Algorithm before the Algorithm: social flattening, no machine required. And Lucius is *invested* in keeping that grave dug. In [[The Interview]] EP3 he mimes a ballet move and twists the knife: *"Do you know what your lowest performing song of all time is? Ballerina Boy. When you're vulnerable you LOSE!"* — the flattening logic (vulnerability doesn't stream) delivered as a punchline, with the stream count as proof.

The concept is *distributed*, though — it shows up across the catalogue long before it has a name:

- **[[Long Time Coming]]** (*STF*, 2018) collapses every social rank into one shared condition: *"rich man, poor man… prophet and priest… all of us bleed… nobody's free."* Every role flattens into the same posture — bleeding, kneeling, trapped, performing. It already contains the diagnostic blueprint of the Circus. The mirror-recognition lands too: *"every night when I look in the mirror / I see the disguise on my face getting clearer."* And the allegiance-shift — *"this one's for the faceless / the lost, not the famous."*
- **[[No Fake Friends]]** (*STF*, 2018) runs flattening *in reverse*: *"they ain't even on my radar"* — excluded people aren't enemies, they're erased from attention itself. Not *you're out*; *you don't register.* Flattening others down to background noise. (Same song trods the Walk-of-Fame underfoot — *"Hollywood I had the night watch / looked down at my stars"* — the heavens flattened into pavement.)
- **[[Superhuman]]** (*STF*, 2018) enacts the flattening it protests. The collective march (*"left, right, left"*) erases the individual into interchangeable units — which makes *"ain't gonna be no face in the crowd"* the song's own protest against the erasure its *"we"* is performing.
- **[[City of Angels]]** + **[[Playing to the Gods]]** (2026) give flattening its reward: *"everyone looks just like you."* (See Advanced reading.)
- **[[Great Time to Be Human]]** (2026) is the toxic-positivity rendering — the ad-libs as algorithmic emotional flattening, a sing-along where the content knows it's horrifying and the form refuses to stop smiling.
- **[[Weirdness]]** is the antidote pole — the part of you that *can't* be flattened (see Advanced reading).
- **[[Loser]]** (*STF*, 2018) holds the earliest explicit diagnosis: *"television on your frame / everybody's all the same."* (The word *frame* runs deep here; held with a grain of salt — see Source notes.)
- **[[All My Friends]]** (2023) — the crowd flattens you into a clone; the friends restore the particularity.

Beyond the songs: Roy's **Manifesto** (mirrors.jagwartwin.com) hands you the apparatus in his own hand, a substitution table where the industry quietly swaps *Immersion → Volume of content*, *Purpose → Reach*, and — the killer — *Feeling → Algorithm*. And the **Sir Lucius "One Hit Wonder" Instagram rant** ([[Public Posts]]) is flattening weaponized as comedy: logged into his own band's verified account, Lucius ranks Jagwar a *"one hit wonder"* who *"can't sell out a tour,"* sorts the field by who's *"cool"* (*"Charli xcx is cool, the 1975 are cool, Chappell Roan… cool, Jagwar… well…"*), sighs *"everyone is an artist or a guru or a dj these days. I'm bored,"* tells the fans to *"find something more interesting to do with your little life,"* and signs off *"Your loyal servant, Sir Lucius 🗡🍏."* That's the flattened metric turned into a weapon — the shadow trashing the artist by the industry's own scorecard, on the artist's own account.

## Advanced reading

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the framework neighbors (Goffman, Gurdjieff, Gnosticism, Jung, the apophatic register, data colonialism, Lacan)
> Several strands of thought illuminate this, worth naming as *resonances* rather than as influences the work claims. None is stated in the songs; together they describe the same gravity the catalogue keeps dramatizing.
>
> - **Gurdjieff** is the closest of these to load-bearing — the one most often named as a touchstone around this work. His diagnosis is *mechanical sleep*: most of a life run on autopilot by a crowd of "many I's," no single self at the wheel. That is flattening from the inside — not a machine reducing you, but a person who has *become* the reduction and stopped noticing. The *"programmed / deprogram"* language sits squarely on this nerve.
> - **Gnosticism** supplies the cosmology flattening rhymes with: a soul-spark (pneuma) trapped in a counterfeit world built by a demiurge who *misnames* it. Hear it in *"you're not a real boy"* (the demiurgic verdict, identity assigned from outside) and *"a bug inside your database"* (the spark catalogued as a glitch). Flattening is demiurgic misnaming with a server farm. (See [[Gnosticism - The World as Trap]].)
> - **Jung's persona** is the social mask — necessary, until it isn't. The pathology is *identification*: when the persona swallows the person and the disowned remainder hardens into shadow. Lucius is exactly that arc — a survival-face that ate its wearer, with everything it refused to be now wearing knives.
> - **Apophatic theology / via negativa** is the deep grammar under *"I'm no thing."* You can only say what God is *not*; the unflattenable self answers the box the same way — by negation. It runs against the **I AM** register (*Ehyeh asher ehyeh*, "I am that I am," Exodus 3:14): the name that is pure unbounded being and refuses every predicate. *"I'm no thing"* is the human-scale echo — a self that won't be pinned to a noun.
> - **Erving Goffman's dramaturgy** (life as roles, fronts, and backstage) describes the *healthy* version flattening corrupts. In Goffman there is always a backstage; flattening is precisely what happens when the backstage is abolished and the front becomes the whole self.
> - **Data colonialism** (the Couldry & Mejias register — human life turned into extractable data and value) names the economic engine that rewards the flattened, quantifiable self over the full one.
> - **Lacan's mirror-alienation** describes the self that mistakes its own legible image for its whole being — the *"I"* constituted through an image that is also *other*. (A resonance, not a claim Roy sources Lacan.)
> - The lab adds **algorithmic identity compression**: systems dropping "unnecessary" cultural and contextual data to make a self legible. That's flattening stated in engineering terms.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the anti-totalization guardrail (it's not "technology bad")
> The antagonist here is the *severing*, not the machine. This is the single most important thing not to get wrong, or the whole cosmology collapses into generic anti-tech mysticism. A machine subordinated to the heart is *creation*; the same machine cut loose from the heart becomes *the Algorithm*. Flattening = optimization severed from the heart = Yesod severed from Tiferet. The line to hold: the problem was never the mirror. It was the mirror with nothing behind it.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — witnessing vs surveillance, and the El Roi irony
> Two gazes can land on the same person. **Surveillance** gathers data; **witnessing** transforms the witnessed. *"The algorithm does not know you — it knows how to shape you."* Surveillance is a *forgery* of the soul's deepest need — to be seen and loved — sold back as being watched and ranked. The witnessing antidote has a name in the work: **El Roi**, *"the God who sees me"* (Genesis 16:13), the one who sees Hagar in the wilderness. The savage irony: that tattoo sits on *Lucius's* throat. The machine that watches everything cannot witness anyone. (See [[Witnessing vs Surveillance]].)

> [!note]- Advanced reading — City of Angels: flattening's actual reward is *reproducibility*
> The deepest single elaboration of what flattening *delivers* lives in [[City of Angels]]. Hollywood offers immortality, but the small print reads *reproducibility*, not resurrection — *"everyone looks just like you."* A circle needs distinct points held in communion; sameness deletes the points and collapses the Circle into a **Loop** (one point repeating). *"The particular self the dream promised to fulfill has already been deleted to fulfill it."* It says *we are all one* and means *you are all replaceable.* Twinning gone metastatic. The clone-imagery threads through [[Playing to the Gods]], [[The Watchers]], and [[All My Friends]].

> [!note]- Advanced reading — Weirdness is the named antidote
> If flattening is the disease, **[[Weirdness]]** is the cure the catalogue actually names. It's *"the vowel principle in biographical form… the living breath that refuses to be flattened into dead structure."* The Algorithm wants repeatability, categorization, compression, and marketable identity; weirdness resists all four — not out of opposition, but because it's *alive* in a way dead structure can't contain. The blessing underneath it: *your uncategorizable part may be your divine signature.* This is the same un-flattenable self as Roy's *"no thing"* — and here the inverse of flattening turns out to be more than resistance. It's **creation.**
>
> Two of Roy's 2022 X aphorisms run as a matched pair. *"No thing ever creates another thing"* (verified live, Feb 2 2022) and *"What is the point? There is no point"* (Sep 20 2022). Read flat they sound nihilist; read apophatically they're the opposite. The *"no thing"* is not zero — it's the **generative void**, the davar capstone (see [[The Empty Mirror]]): the empty glass that transmits perfectly *because* it holds nothing of its own, the silence a word comes out of. The same emptiness that, severed from the heart, becomes the hollow stare with nobody home is — held the other way — the fertile dark that *makes*. So flattening's true inverse isn't just the self refusing the box. It's the self that, by being no thing, can still bring a thing into being. *"There is no point"* then reads as freedom *from* imposed meaning, the clean ground meaning gets *made* on — not the absence of it.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — Online as the infrastructure that produces Lucius
> [[Online]] maps the conditions that *make* Lucius before he exists as a named figure: **the database precedes the mask; the algorithm precedes the performer.** Run the chain — database → prediction → recommendation → habit → identity → self-narration → the voice of Lucius. The detail that bridges flattening back to [[The Mirror]]: the database *"got my face"* is the first artificial reflection — *a mirror that replaces rather than reflects*, not a surface returning your image but a copy standing in for you. Flattening as usurpation.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — early-era flattening (Dream/Dream, and the half-true diagnosis)
> [[Dream - Dream]] (*STF*, 2018) activates flattening among its early dynamics — *"keep us in line,"* *"don't stick to one road,"* *"doing everything our own way."* But its worldview assumes the oppression is *purely external*, which is exactly what the later Lucius material destabilizes: by 2026 the oppressor is partly *internal*. The early songs diagnose a cage and point outward; the late ones discover Roy helped build it. That shift is the spine of the whole arc.

> [!question]- Open questions — what this reading can't settle
> Honest unresolved items. None is a gap in the songs; they're places where the interpretation has a real fork and we don't get to pick for free.
>
> - **Is Lucius the Algorithm's agent, product, or reflection?** This is the load-bearing one, because it decides whether flattening is done *to* him or *by* him. Agent: he runs the machine. Product: the machine extruded him. Reflection: he's just what Roy looks like in flattened glass. The catalogue supports all three at different moments, and the *"the algorithm, baby"* line is deliberately ambiguous about which.
> - **Is there an authentic spectacle the Circus perverts, or is all spectacle suspect?** If communion has a true public form, the Circus is a counterfeit of something real. If not, every stage is already the trap — and even Roy's own performance is implicated. The work flirts with both.
> - **When was the Manifesto written?** Unknown, and it matters: if *Feeling → Algorithm* predates the songs, flattening is a 2018-coexistent diagnosis; if it's later, the apparatus may be grounding applied after the fact. Either reading is defensible; the date would decide it.
> - **Is the "33 = recursive machine-awareness" pivot real, or smoothing?** The album-as-self-aware-system reading is elegant — possibly too elegant. It's worth holding the chance that the archive is tidying a loose pop artifact into more coherence than it was built with.
> - **Does [[Mary]] model the antidote, or only gesture at it?** If true witnessing is the cure for flattening, Mary (*"born to shine, face out of sight"*) is the one who sees without ranking. But whether the work actually *enacts* that gaze or just names the longing for it is unsettled.

## Source notes

- **Grounded (Roy's own words, released music, public posts, official videos).** That *the Algorithm* is Roy's own term — the Manifesto substitution table (*Feeling → Algorithm*) is in his hand. The *"programmed / deprogram"* language (*"we are all programmed by the world around us… if we all deprogram ourselves,"* Billboard, 2019) and the *masks* doctrine (*"we all walk around with these masks on… remove those masks we find that we're all human underneath,"* Prelude Press, 2019), stable across years of interviews. The *"I'm no thing"* / *"I consider myself no thing"* exchange (Mirror Sessions), and the matched 2022 X aphorisms behind it — *"no thing ever creates another thing"* (verified live, Feb 2 2022) and *"what is the point? There is no point"* (Sep 20 2022). The first-person fame-mask description — fame as an *"isolated kingdom where there's walls and they're stuck… a scary place to be"* (Honey Podcast). The lyric anchors of [[Online]], [[Happy Face]], [[Long Time Coming]], [[No Fake Friends]], [[Superhuman]], [[Loser]], [[Ballerina Boy]]. The *"the algorithm, baby"* confession and the Ballerina Boy mockery (*The Interview* EP3). The gridded-globe *Happy Face* video; the Sir Lucius "One Hit Wonder" rant.
- **Strong readings.** The flattening chain itself; the *"not the mask — the system insisting the mask is all there is"* formulation; the Lucius-as-optimized-self anchor; the per-song flattening readings (City of Angels reproducibility, Superhuman's self-protest, No Fake Friends' inverse flattening, Online as Lucius's infrastructure, Weirdness as antidote). Built on grounded anchors, but interpretive — labeled as such.
- **Framework (esoteric / syncretic — never authorial intent).** The Yesod-severed-from-Tiferet placement; the Inherited Substrate → Mirror → Flattened Identity chain; the Goffman / data-colonialism / Lacan resonances. The system *permits* these; it does not assert Roy intends them.
- **How we'd be wrong.** Some of the per-song flattening reads risk being *depth-by-enumeration* or genre-staple over-reading — the work's own counterargument flags Loser's five-meaning *"frame"* as possibly that, and No Fake Friends' real-vs-fake-friends as a pop staple. We keep the grounded / strong-reading line honest rather than treating every quotable line as proof. A few [[Online]] lyric lines are still cross-checked against multiple sources; the core ones quoted here are stable.
