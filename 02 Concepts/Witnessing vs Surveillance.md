# Witnessing vs Surveillance

> **One-line reading.** There are two ways of being seen — one that recognizes and restores you, and one that watches, ranks, and shapes you — and the project treats the difference between them as a spiritual fault line.

Both witnessing and surveillance fix a gaze on the self. They are opposites in what that gaze *does*. The distinction is the one the project's machine-versus-human thread turns on.

## The distinction

**Witnessing** is relational presence. It restores, recognizes, dignifies, integrates. To be witnessed is to be *transformed* by being seen — the gaze gives something back. This is the register of *El Roi*, "the God who sees me."

**Surveillance** is extraction. It predicts, categorizes, monetizes, and stabilizes behavior. To be surveilled is to be *shaped*, not known — the gaze takes something away. This is the register of the feed and the algorithm.

The functional test is simple: surveillance gathers data; witnessing transforms the witnessed. The algorithm does not know you — it knows how to shape you. That is why surveillance reads as spiritually wrong in this world: it's a forgery of the soul's deepest need, the need to be seen and loved, sold back as being watched and ranked.

## El Roi — the name on the throat

The anchor for the witnessing pole is *El Roi*, from Genesis 16:13, "the God who sees me" — the name Hagar gives God after being seen in the wilderness. Roy signs himself "El Roy," and the Hebrew is inked on [[Sir Lucius]]'s neck and throat.

The irony there is load-bearing. Lucius wears "the God who sees" on the very organ that keeps insisting *you don't know me*. The figure built entirely out of surveillance carries the name of true seeing on his throat. The machine that watches everything cannot witness anyone.

There's a biographical rhyme at the project's origin, too. When the name first became public (around 2015), Roy was credited everywhere and pictured nowhere — a press piece called him a "mystery man" whose site held only a video he didn't appear in, while a collaborator volunteered that he had actually *seen* Roy sing. The artist literally seen-but-unseen at the moment of being named: *El Roi* made biographical.

## The two clusters

**The surveillance side** gathers the project's machinery of being-watched: [[The Audience]], [[The Spectacle]], [[The Algorithm]], and [[The Aquarium]] (the self watched in a tank), along with parasociality and the feed. *"Did you like the show?"* from [[Welcome to the Circus]] is its signature line — the watched performing for the watchers, harm turned into product.

**The witnessing side** gathers the figures who see without consuming: *El Roi*, and the faceless presence of [[Mary]], who witnesses without taking ("born to shine," her face out of sight). Here the question is being *seen* versus being *consumed*.

## In the songs

- [[Online]] (2022) names surveillance and predictive profiling directly — being tracked, infiltrated, profiled before you've even acted. Prediction stands in as a counterfeit of intimacy.
- [[Not Your Homie]] (2026) puts *El Roi* on the throat and the line *"you don't know me"* in the mouth — the two poles fused in a single figure.
- [[Welcome to the Circus]] (2026) stages the audience and the harm-as-product show: *"did you like the show?"*

## The Online rite

The browser experience built around the *Online* release stages the doctrine as an interactive rite. A short poem ("the whole world is watching you behind your screen") gives way to a gate that asks you to *enable your camera* — to submit to being surveilled — and then to *cover the lens* to proceed. Both modes in one gesture: be watched, then blind the watcher to pass. The hand over the lens is the refusal that reclaims the self from the feed.
