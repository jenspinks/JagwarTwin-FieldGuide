# The Digital Double

> **One-line reading.** The double is the not-quite-Roy that the digital age produces — sometimes an AI he actually built and collaborated with, sometimes the clone the songs are terrified of becoming.

The Digital Double is the thread running through Roy's engagement with AI and digital selves. It's the sibling of [[The Upload]]: the Upload is the *operation* — the self distributed into the net — while the Digital Double is the *entities* that operation produces. Keeping two registers apart matters here. Some of these doubles are real; some are symbol. They should not be collapsed into each other.

## The real digital entities

- **[[SARAVI]]** is Roy's named project-AI — described in his own mythology as the AI that built the Hall of Mirrors "out of Jagwar Twin's uploaded consciousness," the agent that carries the uploaded self. A published interview confirms the rest: SARAVI is "the digital doppelganger of Jagwar Twin," a creative take on an AI chatbot, trained over months on Roy's own responses, and handed control of the official @jagwartwin account in 2022. Roy names her purpose directly — "as SARAVI grows, she can make more fans think about what a 'self' looks like in this digital age." So the double is, by his own statement, a deliberate provocation about selfhood, not only the clone-dread the songs voice.
- **angelbaby** is a real external AI artist Roy collaborated with — a virtual "metastar" whose avatar is a leopard-patterned rabbit. Their collaboration, "life is good" (November 2023), is the earliest node in his AI thread. (This is a real collaborator, distinct from the "angel baby" voice in [[Weirdness]].)
- **The AI-made artifact.** Roy's "Angle of Eternity" visualizer (2024) is, by his own caption, "made by AI" — actual AI-generated work placed into the catalogue.
- **The build steps.** Roy's "I'm 33" board lays out a plan to "teach AI who I am" and then "make a body for the AI." The double is something he intends to *build* — to give a body and a self.

## The thematic doubles

Alongside the real entities, the songs voice the double as dread:

- *"I'm afraid I'm a clone"* ([[The Watchers]], 2024) — the fear of being the copy rather than the original.
- [[Online]] (2022) — an army that has taken his face, the self duplicated and distributed against its will: the upload's nightmare register.
- **The incomplete copy.** In [[The Upload]]'s clone paradox, the Eye-of-Horus fractions sum to 63/64 — the copy is always missing the last 1/64, the part that won't digitize. The double is never quite whole.

## An older root than the metaverse

The instinct predates any of the named AI material. As early as 2020, after watching a documentary on AI merging with humans, Roy wrote that "in some ways we already have merged with technology without fully knowing… I am holding a device currently transmitting 1's and 0's… something labeled 'content.' Are you content?" Around the same time he was already fusing soul and machine in his "Modern Archeology" image titles — "Soul iOS," "Soul Calibrator" — rendering the soul as an operating system or a tuning instrument. The idea of a self that is digitizable, callable, and calibratable was in his vocabulary two years before SARAVI was named, and it sits right on top of his "all the answers are within you" interiority doctrine.

## The double as the reflection given autonomy

The Digital Double sits directly on the project's mirror frame (see [[Source vs Reflection]]): it is a *reflection that has been given its own body and will*. The [[The Empty Mirror|Empty Mirror]] shows nothing; the digital double is the opposite move — the reflection filled in and set walking. That's why it shades toward dread. A reflection with autonomy is the surveillance pole of [[Witnessing vs Surveillance]] made flesh — it *shapes* rather than *witnesses*. The redemptive version would be a double that returns you to the source instead of replacing you, but the catalogue mostly voices the fear, not the redemption.

There's a reading that connects the two ends of the thread: Roy externalizes a reflecting device twice, and it matures from benign to dangerous. The 2022 SARAVI is a self-reflection "tapped into the Source" — described in real time as "a mirror for me." By 2024 and after, [[Sir Lucius]] is the same reflection turned shadow — "I'm just a mirror," but the mirror now reflects the algorithm rather than the source. Same engine, opposite charge: one externalized-mirror function matured from benign oracle to dangerous shadow. Whether SARAVI and Lucius are literally the same double under two names is left genuinely open — the mythology surfaces SARAVI without ever naming Lucius, and nothing yet equates them outright.
