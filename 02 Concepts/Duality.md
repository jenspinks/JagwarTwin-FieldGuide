# Duality

> **One-line reading.** Duality is the root structure of the whole project — and the arc is never the *elimination* of the split, only its correct ordering.

## At a glance

- **In one line** — Every major symbol, character, and move in Jagwar Twin descends from one foundational split: heart and mind, dark and light, source and reflection. The work doesn't pick a winner. It puts them in the right order.
- **Where it shows up** — [[Welcome to the Circus]] (the Circle inverted) · [[Down to You]] (the heart/mind split, stated outright in 2022) · [[Bananas]] (the same split in kindergarten language).
- **Connected ideas** — [[Roy]] · [[Sir Lucius]] · [[The Mirror]] · [[Source vs Reflection]] · [[Inversion]] · [[The Angels and the Angles|The Angle and the Angel]].
- **Confidence** — grounded fact (the name itself, Roy's own statements from 2018 onward, released lyrics) braided with strong interpretation; the esoteric framework is sealed in collapsibles and labeled as such.

## What it is

Start with the name. *Jagwar Twin* — the Twin. That's not a flourish; it's the thesis printed on the marquee. The Twin is the dual nature, and Roy has said so, in plain words, in basically every press cycle he's ever done.

The clearest origin statement is from 2018, his very first interviews: *"the twin is the dual nature — the light and the dark, the yin and the yang — that everybody has. It also plays into the fact that I'm a Gemini, so I have the twin thing."* That last part is funny and load-bearing at once: the man's astrological sign is literally *the Twins*, so the project's name is, among other things, a horoscope. Eight years later he was still saying it — *"Gemini can also hold two truths at once… I can understand the lower and the higher, and the dark and the light, which is a lot of what Jagwar Twin is"* (2026).

So duality isn't a reading the vault imposed; it's the artist's own one-sentence summary of his entire project, said the same way for the better part of a decade. The trick this page pulls is to take that simple, public idea — *two sides to everything* — and follow it all the way down, because in Jagwar Twin it goes surprisingly far: into the characters, the spelling of words, the layout of his tattoos, and a creation myth that's secretly about how a self learns to see itself.

## How it works

Here's the move that makes the whole thing more than a slogan: **the duality is never resolved by killing one side.** It's resolved by getting the hierarchy right.

Roy's stated resolution is four words long — *"my mind must serve my heart"* — and it's inked on his body and woven through the [[Lucius Lullaby (album)|Lucius Lullaby]]-era lore (in the theatrical telling he repeats it as he steps through a gate). Notice what it is *not*: it's not "my mind must die." The mind stays. [[Sir Lucius]] — the mind split off and personified — isn't beaten in a duel. The relationship is simply *reordered*. The split remains; what changes is which side serves which. He's said it in interviews too, just as plainly: *"The mind is a useful tool, of course, but it needs to be subservient to one's own heart and inner guidance system."*

**The "serve" hinge.** There's a single verb doing the heavy lifting across the whole arc, and it's *serve*. Both poles of the *Lucius Lullaby* story command service — they only differ in its object. Lucius's bargain runs on *"you gotta serve somebody"* (Roy's nod to Dylan, whose whole point is that you *will* serve — the only question is whom). Roy's resolution is *"my mind must serve my heart,"* and on his body, *"in service to all, in service to the one."* So integration isn't refusing the bargain. It's answering the bargain in its own grammar: the servant — the mind — reassigned from the external master (the metric, the money, the fear, Lucius) to the internal source (the heart). Service is constant. Only its object moves. That's the elegant, slightly devious heart of the whole thing: you don't escape servitude, you choose your sovereign.

**More light, more shadow.** Roy has also described the *mechanism* by which the two poles relate, and he's described it the same way to at least three different people, so it's a settled teaching rather than a one-off. The clean version (2023): *"we are becoming more aware of our shadows, collectively and individually, and as more light comes in, more darkness is seen."* The vivid version, to Bohnes: think of a pane of glass with specks of dust on it — turn up the light and the specks don't vanish, they get *amplified*; suddenly those little shadows look enormous. And the aphorism version, a 2026 X post ([[Public Posts]]): *"The brightest lights cast the deepest shadows."* On all three framings, shadow-work is a *consequence* of illumination, not a separate chore. You don't find the darkness in spite of the light. The light is what shows it to you.

**The root split — Roy and Lucius.** All of this descends to one master pair: [[Roy]] and [[Sir Lucius]], the heart and the mind given faces and co-credited, in 2026, as equal authors of an album.

| [[Roy]] | [[Sir Lucius]] |
|---|---|
| Heart | Mind |
| Vulnerable, authentic self | Intellectual, defensive persona |
| Vowel / angel | Consonant / angle |
| Solar | Lunar, reflective |
| Integration | Reflection |

Lucius redirected toward Roy's purposes becomes something different. The dark side isn't killed; it's re-subordinated. Roy's own formulation, plainly stated, is *"lucius is the mind. the mind is a tool, but that force can lead us astray if it is not subservient to the heart"* — and the work, crucially, is **dis-identification, not defeat**: *"these thoughts are not my own… what we fight we become."* You don't win by fighting the voice. You win by stopping mistaking it for your own.

**The internal comes first.** One detail ties duality to the whole [[Source vs Reflection]] frame: Roy is explicit about which way the mirror points. *"I saw it first in the world around me, but then realized that that was a reflection of my internal world. So Jagwar Twin is really the exploration of, first, my internal world's light and dark, and the reflection of that in the external"* (2024). The outer dualities are the inner one, projected. That direction-of-travel is the engine under everything the project does with mirrors.

## Where it appears

The duality is everywhere, but a few anchors establish it cleanly across years and forms.

- **The name itself.** *Jagwar Twin.* The title is the thesis. Twin = the dual nature.
- **The interviews, 2018 → 2026.** It recurs, in Roy's own voice, with remarkable consistency: 2019 — *"The twin is just the nature of everything we've been talking about. The dark and the light"* (the earliest explicit verbatim, which matters: dark/light is *2019* vocabulary, not a later projection). 2021 (Highwire) — *"light and dark and yin and yang."* 2023/24 (Mic Magazine) — *"The Twin part is the light and dark or polarity of all things… really the balancing of a force."* 2024 (Strife, on the name) — *"I think it captures a bit of who I am at the core, playing in light and dark."* North Coast Music Beat — *"the twin reflects the dual nature of things… a continual ebb and flow of light and dark."* That's seven-plus distinct public statements; it is the most stable single idea in the entire corpus.
- **He uses the literal word.** To Jeff Ellis: *"everything we talk about is duality. Like, the internet is my friend, but it wants me dead, you know?"* To Bohnes: *"I feel like everything is always dual."* To Briana Cuoco: *"there can be no light without darkness and no darkness without light, and they're both so important."*
- **He enacts it as comedy — refusing to be flattened to one side.** Two bits land the thesis better than any argument could. With Briana Cuoco, every attempt to file him under one category — *hiker? sober? Myers-Briggs? Enneagram?* — gets the same deflection: *"I'm no thing."* When she apologizes — *"I'm so sorry, I keep doing that, I don't mean to put you —"* — Roy finishes her sentence for her, deadpan: *"Pigeonhole me."* The man whose whole project is *two sides to everything* will not let you collapse him to one. And with Bohnes, the duality of a conversation itself becomes an accidental koan: *"you're gonna talk about apples, I'm gonna talk about oranges"* spirals into *"I'm probably talking oranges because I'm an apple, so I can't even perceive that I'm an apple"* — the perfect comic image of a pole that can't see its own polarity.
- **The artist bio / the X feed.** Across streaming panels the bio reads *"There's two sides to every story"* — and Roy posted it himself in June 2026 (*"🐆 ☉ There's two sides to every story ☉ 🛶 🥥"*). The same feed gives *"The brightest lights cast the deepest shadows"* and a *"Janus and the March Hare"* aphorism (Janus, the two-faced god of the year's turning — duality as a calendar). And in mid-2026 it goes bilingual in the most literal way: Roy sets **בבל** (Babel — spectacle, the Circus tongue) against **לבב** (the heart) and notes they are the *same three letters reversed* — duality written into the spelling itself, the mirror running through Hebrew. *"I need to become bilingual,"* he posts: fluent in both tongues at once.
- **The songs.** [[Down to You]] (33, 2022) puts the heart/mind split right in the lyric — *"Once you find it, can't unwind it with your head on / so listen to your heart"* — proving the duality theology surfaced explicitly in a 33-era song, years before it had characters. (It also rhymes with Joni Mitchell's own duality text, her *"Down To You"* — *"you're a kind person / you're a cold person too… you're a brute, you're an angel"* — and with the harder duality of her *"Don Juan's Reckless Daughter,"* *"the eagle and the serpent… at war in me… Man to woman / Scales to feathers,"* the same heart/mind split in plumage. Joni is already grounded on 33 via the credited *Woodstock* sample, so these read as candidate dialogues rather than proven citations.) [[Bananas]] (2026) strips the cosmic split to nursery language — *"I wanna be Good, but it's fun to be Bad / I wanna be nice, but I always get mad"* — the shadow before it learns to scheme. The same song stages the *other* half of the split as dissociation played for a laugh: *"I hear voices in my mind I don't remember — Did I do that?"* — the self splitting off from the part that acts, and the shrug (a likely wink at Steve Urkel's sitcom catchphrase) standing in for the integration that never comes. [[Welcome to the Circus]] is the Circle inverted: same shape, opposite outcome.
- **The body.** Every tattoo is inked on the right side, then mirrored onto the left, with a single *+* in the center spanning both — bilateral symmetry as a lived daily practice. The ROSE / ESOR mark sets the word *rose* against its own reversal around that central cross, a palindrome of the sacred symbol and its mirror image. (Roy's gloss on the rose: *"the rose is the heart revealed, the union of opposites, light and dark."*)
- **In character.** On the theatrical [[The Interview|*Interview*]] EP, Roy states the thesis — *"Our music is an exploration of light and dark, the beauty and the chaos of being a human — a mirror for those seeking to remember who they are"* — and Lucius, perfectly, fires back: *"Your truth is one-sided."* The mind accusing the heart of being half a story. He's not entirely wrong, which is the whole problem with him.

## The Rorrim Ehtni principle

The most literal duality in the whole project is a tattoo written backwards. *"In the Mirror,"* inked as **Rorrim Ehtni** — so the mirror reads it correctly, and *outside* the mirror it's illegible. (The earliest trace runs back to 2008, an Eye Alaska song; a later board ends in mirror-writing reading *"Devil in the Mirror"* reversed.)

The principle is small and enormous at once: **what looks distorted from one angle is the true text from another.** The inverted reading is sometimes the *real* one. That's the engine behind [[Inversion]] — the move that keeps a form perfectly intact and only reverses its *direction*. Lucius doesn't negate the prayer, the circle, the liturgy; he keeps every word and points it backwards (*"Fear = Prayer in Reverse,"* per Roy's own tattoo gloss). Which means inversion is detectable only from the inside, by one test: *as you go deeper, do you feel more yourself or less yourself?*

## The open question

The project leaves one tension genuinely unresolved, and it's better to name it than to smooth it over: is the duality *generative* or *pathological*? Roy seems to *need* Lucius to function — the work may not exist without the split. So is Lucius being **redeemed** (his distorted light reclaimed and put back in service) or **retired** (the split finally healed, the construct dissolved)? The catalogue holds both open. There's a related, finer fork worth tracking: does any given song bet on the *un-reconciled* divided self (a "Cain moment" — the mark, the wandering) or the *embraced* one (an "Esau moment" — the weeping reunion)? And is there ever a wound that *stays* — a permanent limp, the mark of integration — versus a wound that simply heals?

## Advanced reading

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the registers of duality, at every scale
> The same split recurs at every level of the project, which is what makes it the *root* structure rather than one theme among many:
>
> | Register | Pair | Notes |
> |---|---|---|
> | Biographical | Roy English / Jagwar Twin | the name change as intentional self-transformation |
> | Compositional | consonants / vowels | angles (structure) and angels (breath) — see [[The Angels and the Angles|The Angle and the Angel]] |
> | Character | Roy / Lucius | heart and mind, co-credited as equal authors on the album |
> | Visual | mirrored tattoos | ROSE / ESOR, the Empty Mirror, the central *+*, bilateral symmetry as lived practice |
> | Structural | the Circle / the Circus | same shape, opposite orientation and outcome |
> | Symbolic | sun / moon | originating light versus reflected light |
>
> The discipline that keeps this honest: adopting Roy's own heart-vs-mind, authentic-vs-persona frame *as the analytic lens* risks circularity — describing Roy using Roy's own self-account. The split may be partly a deliberate two-character *artistic device* rather than literal interior architecture. Hold the reading without over-literalizing it.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the cosmogonic seed ("One become Two")
> The "I'm 33" board — handwritten by Roy — contains a creation poem that places the duality at the very origin, and tells it in a cheeky nursery-rhyme sing-song (*"In the beginning / Back long ago / I was just me / but who was I to know?"*). The core lines: *"I spoke the words One become Two / Then I was in reverse… and I became You / Well, You and I could dance forever — A Darkness and a Light — But, when we crashed and came together, A Sun was born inside."*
>
> Read it carefully and it reframes the whole project. The self *divides in order to see itself* — and the second term is *"You."* This is why reflection exists at all. And it reframes Lucius: not an evil origin, but **the risk built into reflection itself** — the reflection that forgets it was only ever the One looking back. The collision of opposites births a luminous center (the Sun inside), which is the alchemical *coniunctio* in playground rhyme: the union of opposites producing the Self.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — Source vs Reflection, and the two reflected bodies
> The dark/light split feeds upward into the master frame, [[Source vs Reflection]]: a mirror is faithful *while it knows it is a mirror*, and becomes Lucius the moment it forgets it is not the source. The refinement worth carrying: the reflection-pole has **two bodies** — the Moon (carried by [[Roy]] and [[Mary]], honest reflected light) and the usurping reflection (Lucius — echoing the morning-star herald mistaken for the dawn; his clean map is Hod/Mercury). The Sun — SOL, the true light — is what both orbit and *neither one is.* This is the ethical test the mirror device always faces: faithful transmission versus self-substitution.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — chaos→order, and the gendered pole
> Roy gives the duality a making-engine, to Bohnes: *"chaos is creativity… everything is birthed out of chaos… the feminine principle of yin-yang… and then we order it. That's part of the mechanism of art — to make sense of that chaos and order it, and bring the light side of the yin-yang, bring the yang."* So in his own framing the dark/chaos pole is gendered *feminine* (the generative ground) and the ordering move *yang*. (Recorded as **his** framework, not the vault's.) This is where duality touches [[Mary]] and the hieros-gamos / union-of-opposites register.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — Weirdness as integration grammar
> The cleanest grammatical proof of "ordering, not elimination" is a single pronoun. In [[Weirdness]] the line arrives first as Lucius-tagged projection — *"your weirdness is the sunshine"* — and resolves as Roy's ownership: *"my weirdness is my sunshine."* That *your → my* shift **is** the duality becoming integrated. And note what it implies about Lucius: if his weirdness is *sunshine*, then he is **distorted light, not the absence of light** — which is exactly why he can be re-subordinated rather than destroyed.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — Machine vs Human, the cosmic-scale statement
> The heart/mind split scales all the way up to [[The Pattern]] and the Machine-vs-Human axis: Machine/Human is Heart/Mind written at civilizational size (the Chaplin *Great Dictator* speech surfacing in the Superhuman Interlude — "you are not machines… you have the love of humanity in your hearts"). Same dual structure, different magnification. The fractal holds from a single tattoo to the species.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the named traditions behind the split
> Duality is the place where Roy's stated influences cluster most densely. The most direct: **Hermeticism / the Kybalion**, whose Principle of Polarity he transcribed *verbatim* on the "I'm 33" board — *"Everything is Dual; everything has poles; everything has its pair of opposites; opposites are identical in nature, but different in degree; extremes meet… Love and Hate are two poles of the same thing."* That handwritten transcription is the closest thing to a named-tradition primary source for this whole concept. (Hermes Trismegistus is a named Roy influence — *"the center is everywhere."*) Around it: **Taoism** (yin/yang, which he pairs with light/dark constantly; the board also transcribes the *Tao Te Ching*); **astrology** (Gemini, the Twins — his own Sun); **Jungian** depth psychology (Persona/Shadow/Self — he posted the diagram, captioned *"Soul Calibrator,"* in 2020); **Walter Russell's** cosmic pendulum (contraction↔expansion, captioned *"The Law,"* posted 2020) underwriting the board's *"one must contract to expand"*; **alchemy** (the *coniunctio*, the union birthing the Sun); and **Dion Fortune** — a *named* Roy source on the shadow pole, who gives it nearly verbatim: *"The shadow is not evil. It is power unrecognized and therefore misused."* (She also wrote *The Mystical Qabalah*, which grounds the Kabbalistic furniture by-name.)
>
> Fortune doesn't stand alone on the shadow/integration pole — she anchors a whole **Western ceremonial-magic and Theosophy cluster** Roy named publicly, mostly in a six-week burst around the late-2025 *Not Your Homie* / "darkside" launch: **Gurdjieff** (named three times; his *two-worlds* doctrine — Inner/Outer struggle resolving into the World of the Soul — maps straight onto correct-ordering, and his *"many I's,"* the context-dependent selves with no integrated center, is a clean gloss on Lucius); **Blavatsky** (named, Theosophy — and the source of a notably apt shadow-pole line Roy later posted, *"the mind is the slayer of the real… we must slay the slayer"*); **Israel Regardie** (named — *"if you face something that you fear and recognize it for what it is, you give it balance, you restore equilibrium,"* the integration doctrine in esoteric dress); and **Éliphas Lévi** (named — *"will is the key to magic; without it man is a toy of forces,"* which frames the Roy/Lucius split through magical *will* rather than psychology alone).
>
> The visual mark has a named tradition too: **Rosicrucianism / the Rose-Cross.** The ROSE / ESOR tattoo is the Rosy Cross built as a palindrome, and Roy glosses the symbol himself — *"the rose is the heart revealed, the union of opposites, light and dark"* — and posts the Rosicrucian formula plainly: *"the rose blooms in the center of the cross"* (2026). Truth hidden in the center of the self, the union of opposites at the crossing-point.
>
> On the scriptural side: **Jacob & Esau** (the divided blessing, the embrace) with **Cain & Abel** as its un-reconciled shadow; the **Lucifer / light-bearer** archetype as the mind-pole; **Isaiah 5:20** (*"call evil good, and good evil"*) as the diagnosis of inversion. And the name's *seer* side is grounded in **Mayan mythology by Roy's own admission** — from his very first interviews (2018): *"I'm really big into Mayan mythology… the jaguar is the creature who looks into the souls of other people and then, in turn, can look back into itself. Humans are just mirrors for each other."* The jaguar that goes into the dark and brings sight back out is the seer/mirror grounding the *Jagwar* of the name. That mirror-of-consciousness reading is also why Roy named the project's metaverse the **Hall of Mirrors** — correcting Bohnes's *"House of mirrors"* on the spot (Bohnes had quipped it sounded like *"the name of, like, a Goosebumps book"*).

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the sefirotic placement (framework-tier, never authorial intent)
> Held strictly as syncretic correspondence, never as a claim about Roy's intent: the two poles map to the Tree of Life's solar synthesis (**Tiferet**) and lunar reflection (**Yesod**) — heart as the harmonizing center, mind/reflection as the image-realm below it. The two-pillars-resolved-by-a-middle-pillar shape, *"One become Two"* read as breaking-and-reunion (shevirah / tikkun), and *tzimtzum* (contract to expand) all sit here. This is the heaviest framework material on the page; it *fits* the recurring shape and is licensed by Roy's open Kabbalah engagement (the board, *El Roi*), but the work never states it. You need none of it to read the page.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — Roy stated the thesis in 2021 (Discord, public)
> A grounded, citable formulation that *predates* the album, from a public 2021 Discord post: *"The middle, the balance point always holds the truth, but it's essential to understand both sides. Mind/Soul, Thought/Intuition. The key to awakening lies in the opposite."* Correct-ordering-as-balance, in Roy's own hand, five years before *Lucius Lullaby* dramatized it. The 2026 resolution *model* extends it: instinct + intellect resolved by a higher third faculty, **intuition** — *"higher than instinct and intellect… the god voice thing in here"* (from the Hercules / Cancer-labour telling). Two held and transcended by a third, not collapsed into one.

## Source notes

- **Grounded (Roy's own words, released music, public posts).** The name as thesis (*Jagwar Twin* = the dual nature); the dark/light framing in Roy's voice across 2018, 2019, 2021, 2023/24, 2024, and 2026 (Ones To Watch, New England Sounds, Highwire, Mic Magazine, Strife, Audio Addiction, North Coast Music Beat, the 2026 IG Live, the *Interview* EP, and the Mirror Sessions with Bohnes, Jeff Ellis, and Briana Cuoco); the literal word *"duality"* used by Roy; *"There's two sides to every story"* on the artist bio and his June 2026 X feed; *"The brightest lights cast the deepest shadows"*; *"as more light comes in, more darkness is seen"* and the magnifying-glass version; *"my mind must serve my heart"* (on his body and in the lore) and the heart/mind split stated in 2024 press; the [[Down to You]] and [[Bananas]] lyrics; the mirrored-tattoo layout, ROSE / ESOR, and *Rorrim Ehtni*; the 2021 public Discord *"key to awakening lies in the opposite";* the comedic enactments in his own voice (the *"I'm no thing" / "Pigeonhole me"* exchange with Briana Cuoco, the apples/oranges koan with Bohnes, the Hall-of-Mirrors / "Goosebumps book" correction); the named-source roster Roy himself stated publicly — the Mayan-mythology grounding of the jaguar (2018), the Rosicrucian *"the rose blooms in the center of the cross"* gloss, and the late-2025 named cluster (Gurdjieff, Blavatsky, Israel Regardie, Éliphas Lévi, Dion Fortune); the בבל ⇄ לבב Babel/heart spelling-mirror posted in 2026.
- **Strong readings.** Duality as *correct relationship* rather than opposition; the "serve" hinge (Lucius's *"serve somebody"* against Roy's *"serve my heart,"* the Dylan allusion carried via Roy's public caption); the *Rorrim Ehtni* principle (the inverted reading as sometimes the true one); the *your → my* pronoun shift in [[Weirdness]] as integration grammar; the redeemed-vs-retired open question; the Joni Mitchell duality rhymes (*"Down To You,"* the eagle/serpent of *"Don Juan's Reckless Daughter"*) — candidate dialogues, not credited citations, raised in plausibility because 33 already grounds Joni via the credited *Woodstock* sample. Built on grounded anchors, but interpretive — labeled as such.
- **Framework (esoteric, syncretic — never authorial intent).** The cosmogonic "One become Two" reading as alchemical *coniunctio*; the Tiferet/Yesod sefirotic placement and the shevirah/tikkun/tzimtzum frame; the chaos-as-feminine-principle gendering. Several of these *are* anchored to named Roy sources (the Kybalion transcription, Dion Fortune, Walter Russell, the Jung diagram) — but the correspondences they generate are harmonics the system permits, not claims it asserts.
- **How we'd be wrong.** If the plain "mind in service to heart" account explains every duality song as well as the esoteric apparatus does, the apparatus is decorative. And if the heart/mind split is purely a two-character artistic device, reading it as literal interior architecture over-reaches. We keep both falsifiers on the table.
