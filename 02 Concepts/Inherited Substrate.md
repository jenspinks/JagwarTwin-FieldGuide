# Inherited Substrate

> **One-line reading.** Not everything that resonates was put there on purpose — much of the work's symbolic richness is *received* from older traditions it participates in, and saying so out loud is the discipline that keeps every other reading honest.

## At a glance

- **In one line** — the provenance rule: before you call any echo a hidden code, ask whether the work *inherited* it from the wider culture rather than *invented* it.
- **Where it shows up** — [[Dream - Dream]] (a 1944 Johnny Mercer standard, credited) · [[Superhuman (Interlude)]] (a verbatim Chaplin sample) · [[The Watchers]] (Rockwell's *"Somebody's Watching Me,"* credited).
- **Connected ideas** — [[The Pattern]] · [[Source vs Reflection]] · [[Sir Lucius]] · [[Machine vs Human]] · [[Hall of Mirrors]].
- **Confidence** — grounded. This is a method, not a theory about Roy's psychology; the strongest cases are anchored to actual songwriting credits and Roy's own words.

## What it is

A project this saturated with biblical, mythic, and occult imagery invites one very tempting mistake: treating every echo as a hidden, deliberate code, planted just for you to find. *Inherited substrate* is the honest counterweight. It names the plain fact that a great deal of the work's symbolic density is **received** — quoted, sampled, interpolated, transformed, or simply *shared* with traditions far older than the music. A song that tells you to *dream* is built on a 1944 song about dreaming. The flood behind an album called *Subject to Flooding* is the same flood behind Genesis and Gilgamesh. Recognizing a resemblance is not the same thing as proving someone authored it on purpose.

So the whole idea fits in one sentence the vault keeps coming back to: **not all resemblance is reference.** You're allowed to notice that two things rhyme without claiming one caused the other. This page is the brake on the kind of reading that turns every coincidence into a conspiracy — affectionately known, internally, as *the page that stops tier-inflation.*

The point isn't to shrink the work. Tracking what's inherited makes the borrowing *more* interesting, not less — because once you can see the seam where an old song was handed forward, you can see what Roy *did* with it. Which is usually the whole story.

## How it works

The method runs on a single split and a single test.

**The split: inherited vs internal.** There are two kinds of symbolic material in the work, and telling them apart is what keeps interpretation grounded.

- **Inherited substrate** — drawn from the wider culture: jazz standards and nursery rhymes, biblical language, occult systems, family memory, older artists, public-domain myth. The work is *built from* these.
- **Internal architecture** — invented inside the project's own world: [[Sir Lucius]], the loop and the [[The Loop and the Circle|Circle]], [[The Algorithm]], the mirror recursion, the *El Roi* throat tattoo, the face-paint systems, the theatrical EPs. These are the work's own.

A symbol that *predates* the work is, by default, the project **participating in an older form** — not the artist secretly encoding something new. That default is load-bearing. As the vault's standing guard puts it: prefer *participating in an older form* over *the artist deliberately encoded this*, whenever a symbol predates the work.

**The test: did he receive it, cite it, transform it — or are we just recognizing a shared ancestor?** That one question sorts every cross-reference, and it produces a ladder of confidence. The plainest version:

- **Credited or stated** — there's an actual songwriting credit, a named source, a public board, or a direct statement from Roy. Solid floor.
- **Strongly implied** — no credit on that exact line, but the surrounding breadcrumbs make the borrowing clear.
- **Shared tradition** — Roy *and* the comparison both draw on the same ancient reservoir, with no claim that one took it from the other.
- **Just a resemblance** — interesting, not yet anchored. Held loosely, never asserted.

(There's a fifth rung below all of these — the *ruled-out* tier, where a tempting attribution fails the primary-source test and gets logged as failed so it can't sneak back in. It earns its own note in Advanced reading, because the worked example is genuinely funny.)

What the method *does*, across the whole body of work, is license **comparison without laundering it into proof.** You can sit *Dream/Dream* next to a Wu-Tang hook and notice they share a gesture, without claiming one is the source of the other. You can read flood mythology *through* the album title without claiming the album cites Gilgamesh. The discipline is what lets the vault be generous with connections and strict about claims at the same time.

And crucially: **this is a claim about culture, never about Roy.** *"This is inherited"* points at the reservoir the material came from — it is not a sneaky way of saying *"here's what Roy secretly meant."* Keep it pointed at provenance, not psychology, and it does its job.

The cleanest proof that this is a *practice* and not a one-off comes from a single album. *Subject to Flooding* (2018) carries credited external material from Johnny Mercer, Sarah Vaughan, **and** Charlie Chaplin — three documented transmissions on one record. That's not coincidence; that's a method. The catalogue, in part, *builds by relaying* — the song handed down, and then handed forward again.

## Where it appears

The grounded backbone of this page is a registry of credited interpolations and samples — borrowings with a paper trail, all framed as *participation in an older song,* never as invented encoding.

- **[[Dream - Dream]]** (*Subject to Flooding,* 2018) interpolates **Johnny Mercer's** 1944 standard *"Dream (When You're Feeling Blue)"* — Mercer and Sarah Vaughan sit right there in the composer credit. The doubled title is the tell: the old *Dream* and the new *Dream*, stacked. (A lyric site had quietly dropped Mercer from the credits; the primary source — Apple Music's composer field — restored him. Trust the credit, not the aggregator.) One open question rides under the credit: whether the track *samples Vaughan's exact 1957 Mister Kelly's take* or simply *interpolates the Mercer composition* is not yet pinned — the credit proves the lineage, not which artifact was lifted.
- **[[Precious Time]]** (*Subject to Flooding,* 2018) samples the spoken **pre-vocal banter** from Sarah Vaughan's 1957 *Live at Mister Kelly's* — the talk *before* she sings *Dream,* not the song itself. So one 1957 Vaughan recording feeds *two* tracks on the same album: the song (*Dream/Dream*) and the chatter before it (*Precious Time*). Vaughan is a credited writer. (One honest residue: the banter's exact words and timestamp still want a final by-ear pin against the *Mister Kelly's* recording — the attribution is confident, the transcription is the part still to confirm.)
- **[[Superhuman (Interlude)]]** (*Subject to Flooding,* 2018) is a verbatim sample of **Charlie Chaplin's** closing speech from *The Great Dictator* (1940): *"the kingdom of God is within man… you, the people, have the power… let us all unite."* Confirmed three ways — the Apple Music lyrics, a Charles Chaplin composer credit, and a pre-existing vault note. The voice here is Chaplin, sampled — neither Roy nor Lucius — an inherited voice placed into the sequence.
- **[[Soul Is A Star]]** (*33,* 2022) credits **Joni Mitchell** as a cowriter — *with her blessing,* in Roy's own delighted words: *"the fact that JONI MITCHELL gave me her blessing and is a cowriter is still mind blowing."* The sampled source is Joni's *"Woodstock."* (Worth flagging: the borrowed element is most likely *musical,* not the famous lyric — the song doesn't reproduce Woodstock's signature lines.)
- **[[Bad Feeling (Oompa Loompa)]]** (*Lucius Lullaby,* 2026; originally a 2023 single) interpolates *"Oompa-Loompa"* from *Willy Wonka & the Chocolate Factory* (1971); **Leslie Bricusse and Anthony Newley** are credited co-writers. Roy keeps the cautionary-jingle *function* and swaps the original *"I've got a perfect puzzle for you"* into the ominous *"I got a bad feeling 'bout you"* — which he glossed, deadpan, as *"a chocolate covered reminder to trust your gut."*
- **[[The Watchers]]** (2024 single) interpolates **Rockwell's** *"Somebody's Watching Me"* (1984) — Rockwell is a credited writer, and the hook surfaces verbatim: *"(I always feel like somebody's watching me)."*

Then there's the substrate *Roy describes in his own voice* — the part where this stops being a filing system and becomes a worldview. He's talked about the self as an inherited stack: *"every single human being who has come before you is within you, like encoded… you're like this mountain, stacked — and someone who learned to sing down the line is just embedded into you."* And about inherited *darkness,* too — answering a question about ancestors, he describes *"black snakes"* in a vision and the voices that come with them: *"I know that they're not mine… they're from mistakes that my ancestors made — lies that they believed,"* with a stated wish to *"heal that for you guys."* That literalizes *the song handed down* as DNA: craft and pain alike, received and passed forward.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the full tier ladder (and the C.R.E.A.M. that got ruled out)
> The four public rungs are the everyday version. The lab ladder has five, and the fifth is the one that does the cleanup:
>
> | Tier | What it means |
> |---|---|
> | **Credited / stated** | A composer credit, named source, public board text, or Roy statement. The grounded floor. |
> | **Adopted** | No credit on that exact line, but breadcrumbs and context make the reading strong. |
> | **Shared tradition** | Roy *and* the comparison draw on the same reservoir; no transmission claimed. |
> | **Tempting resemblance** | Interesting, not yet anchored. Hold, don't assert. |
> | **Ruled out / contamination guard** | A plausible attribution that *failed* primary-source testing — logged as failed so it can't be re-laundered later. |
>
> The worked example for that bottom rung: Wu-Tang's *"C.R.E.A.M."* (1993) and its spelled hook are **ruled out** as a *source* for the spelled *"D-R-E-A-M"* in *Dream/Dream* — no credit, no Roy statement, no cadence match. It's kept *out* precisely so it can't launder its way in and dilute the solid Mercer reading. **But** it's logged one rung up, as a *co-participant* in the same tradition: spelling-a-word-out as the shared gesture of passing the ancestral song down. The maxim that falls out of this is reusable and worth tattooing somewhere: **co-participant is not a source.**

> [!note]- Advanced reading — shared-tradition reservoirs (the same-well cases)
> These are the cases where Roy and the comparison both drink from the same old well, with no transmission claimed in either direction — the honest *"shared tradition"* tier:
> - **Flood mythology** — Genesis, Gilgamesh, the universal deluge archetype, behind the title *Subject to Flooding.* The canonical shared-tradition example.
> - **The anti-civilization lineage** — Rousseau's noble savage, Freud's *Civilization and Its Discontents,* deep ecology. Lucius *adopts and nihilizes* it in [[Moths to the Flame]]'s *"Civilization has been a mistake."*
> - **Fleetwood Mac's *"Go Your Own Way"*** — a resonance, not a source.
>
> The traditions the work participates in, gathered: biblical and scriptural language (the Genesis Flood; Chaplin quoting Luke; *El Roi* / Genesis 16); the American songbook and jazz standards (Mercer, Vaughan); mid-century pop and Motown (Rockwell, with Michael Jackson's *uncredited* hook on the original); the film-musical songbook (Bricusse & Newley); the singer-songwriter canon (Joni Mitchell, and her *"multiphrenic self"*); the anti-civilization intellectual line above; nursery rhymes and public-domain children's forms (named in the law as a substrate category — cf. *Welcome to the Circus'* *"ashes ashes"*); family and ancestral transmission (DNA, *the song handed down,* inherited craft *and* inherited trauma); and a Western esoteric / Theosophical-perennialist braid (the *Kybalion,* Kabbalah, Neoplatonism, Jung, Walter Russell — the materials Roy assembles on the *I'm 33* board).

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the scholarly neighbor (Kristeva) and why the vault is stricter
> The cleanest academic cousin to this idea is **Julia Kristeva's intertextuality** — the principle that texts mean *through* other texts and codes, which explains *why* echoes are natural and pervasive in the first place. The vault borrows the insight and then tightens the screw. Kristeva says echoes are *expected;* Inherited Substrate says how to *tier* them. The difference matters because the vault's authority here is internal — this is a scholarly frame elevated to an operational law. Which means *"inherited substrate"* is always a finding about **culture** and about **our own tiering** — never a verdict about Roy's intent. Misapplied, *"this is inherited"* can masquerade as a claim about *him* when it's only a claim about the *reservoir.*

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the method's own blind spot (it under-credits, by design)
> The discipline is honest enough to flag where it's broken. It's **asymmetric**: every tier *downgrades* — credit earns the top rung, everything weaker slides down — but there's no equal-and-opposite brake on wrongly demoting genuine *internal architecture* into mere *inheritance.* A real false-negative (an internal invention mistaken for a borrowing) costs Roy credit he's owed. The fix is a standing rule: *"inherited"* must clear the same primary-source bar as *"ruled out,"* or the method quietly under-credits the artist. Two more open self-audits ride alongside it: **(1)** *"inherited vs internal" may be a false binary* — Roy treats myth and scripture as *tools he reworks,* so inheritance and invention are often the *same act* (the Oompa-Loompa interpolation that is also a Lucius-coded gesture), which suggests the tiers might be **two axes** rather than one ladder, with a symbol scoring high on both at once. **(2)** The **falsifier**: a case that is simultaneously a *credited interpolation* AND a *demonstrably deliberate internal encoding* would prove the two-axes point and force the method to report both scores. These are open by design — the discipline is stronger for naming where it bends.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — Roy runs the method on himself (and disclaims it in the same breath)
> The discipline isn't imposed from outside; Roy enacts it. The sharpest instance is an aphorism he posted: *"I would map a crow onto Mercury but that's just personal. I don't recommend you do it."* He runs the esoteric correspondence-mapping *and* pre-emptively disowns it as idiosyncratic and non-prescriptive — the exact move Inherited Substrate enforces, delivered as a shrug. There's a deeper version on the *I'm 33* board: *"every generation carries within it the seed of all previous generations"* — the inherited-substrate idea written out as a theory of consciousness, in Roy's own hand.
>
> The *biographical* floor under the whole relay method is his **mother.** Two public anchors: a 2018 interview where Roy says his mom *"listened to pretty much only weird world music… Celtic chants and amazing African drumming, Native American flute music,"* and a venue bio noting he *"grew up serenaded by… Joni Mitchell and Bob Dylan, who the singer's mom played around the house."* The method of handing songs forward (Mercer, Chaplin, Vaughan, Joni) has its origin in *her records.*
>
> A production-lineage footnote in the same spirit: Roy has told of playing **Dr. Dre** the drumless, orchestral *"Worldstop"* — Dre asked where the drums were, and Roy declined to add them. The shrug at the most decorated drum-programmer in pop is the same instinct as the *"crow onto Mercury"* disclaimer: take what you inherit from a tradition, then refuse the part that isn't yours.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the Rockwell parable (why *that* song)
> On [[The Watchers]], Roy didn't just borrow a hook — he imported Rockwell's *whole myth,* and the rhyme is nearly point-for-point. Rockwell (Kennedy William Gordy, Berry Gordy's son) wrote a paranoid prayer-song under his father's shadow; he took a *chosen name to escape the lineage* (Kennedy Gordy → Rockwell, the way Roy English → Jagwar Twin); the original's most famous voice — Michael Jackson singing the hook — went *uncredited,* the ghost in the record. And the deepest reason of all: **contested authorship,** *who gets to claim a song,* is the exact engine of the entire Sir Lucius system. The comedy is built in. Berry Gordy first dismissed his own son's demo — *"don't quit your day job"* — then paraded the hit as his son's once it landed. Tier honesty: every fact about Rockwell is grounded; that Roy chose the song *for these reasons* is a strong reading, not a stated one.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — Coding Consciousness, where the sentence is borrowed but the title is Roy's
> A hand-written note of Roy's pairs an inherited esoteric sentence — early-20th-century Theosophical *"race-mind / patterns of right living"* diction (the source author unpinned) — with Roy's *own* title, *Coding Consciousness.* The clean read: what's *Roy's* is the **title plus the act of selection,** not the doctrine's wording. The note's grounded twin is the pair of Mirror Sessions family-transmission quotes above. The idea cuts both ways — patterns encoded into the collective mind can carry the **Circle** when they encode truth and craft (*"someone who learned to sing… embedded into you"*) or the **Loop** when they encode lies (*"voices that aren't mine… lies that they believed"*). Same mechanism, opposite content.
>
> One tiering note on the family-transmission material so it doesn't over-merge: the two Mirror Sessions quotes are grounded and biographically citable. Reading *"voices… I know they're not mine"* as a neighbor to the [[Sir Lucius]] / [[The Protector]] inner-voice-as-not-self is a *resonance,* not an identity — a **different mechanism** (inherited ancestral voice vs armor over a personal wound), kept distinct rather than fused. The *"mountain, stacked"* image likewise *rhymes* with the Capricorn/Good Time mountain without becoming it.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the retrospective-coherence guard (the genericness is the point)
> The substrate frame doubles as a brake on reading *later* meaning back into *earlier* work. Applied to [[Dream - Dream]]: the flatter, truer read is *an earnest 2018 indie-pop awakening anthem with a Mercer interpolation* — no proto-anything. Its *"rise up,"* its *"stairway to Heaven"* are maximally generic inherited aspiration-language, not encoded geometry. The genericness *is* the point: borrowed liberation-language is borrowed precisely because it's universal. When the retrospective-coherence guard and the substrate guard agree, they override fancier readings.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — open threads (the leads still hanging)
> A method built on provenance is only as honest as its list of *unfinished* provenance. Four threads are open, held as leads, not claims:
> - **The "Essex" credit.** *"Good Day"* lists a co-writer credited only as *Essex,* so far unidentified. Possibly a real collaborator, possibly an untraced interpolation source surfacing in the credit field — exactly the kind of loose thread this method exists to chase. Logged, not resolved.
> - **The Joni "prophet" lineage — handle with tongs.** An attractive thread reads Roy's family-transmission language as a *prophet* lineage, but a *"prophets"* quote got mis-sourced in an earlier pass, so the provenance is *not* clean. This is logged as a **lead, not a claim,** and as a contamination guard: don't build any *"prophet"* framing on top of it until the quote is pinned to a real public source. (The page deliberately uses none.)
> - **Does the relay continue?** *Subject to Flooding* proves a curation/relay *practice;* whether any *later* work samples or interpolates again — extending the Chaplin/Mercer/Rockwell pattern forward into the machine theme — is open. The [[Machine vs Human]] material gestures at it, but it isn't yet a documented fourth instance.
> - **The Joni borrow on [[Soul Is A Star]].** Credited cowrite, stated blessing — but whether the borrowed element is *musical* or *lyrical* is unconfirmed (the song doesn't reproduce *Woodstock's* signature lines, which tilts it musical). The credit is grounded; the *what* is the open part.

## Source notes

- **Grounded (credits, samples, Roy's own words).** The credited interpolations and samples are anchored to primary sources — composer credits and verified lyrics: Mercer and Vaughan on [[Dream - Dream]]; the Vaughan banter sample on [[Precious Time]]; the Chaplin *Great Dictator* sample on [[Superhuman (Interlude)]]; Joni Mitchell's credited cowrite (with her stated blessing) on [[Soul Is A Star]]; Bricusse & Newley on [[Bad Feeling (Oompa Loompa)]]; Rockwell on [[The Watchers]]. Roy's family-transmission and inherited-darkness statements are from public interview/session material; the *"map a crow onto Mercury"* and *"seed of all previous generations"* lines are public posts.
- **Strong readings.** That *Subject to Flooding* establishes a deliberate *relay/curation practice* (rather than a string of coincidences); that the Rockwell choice was *for* its parable; that the inherited voices *transmit rather than substitute.* Built on grounded anchors, but interpretive.
- **Shared tradition.** Flood mythology, the anti-civilization lineage, and the spelling-out gesture (the *C.R.E.A.M.* co-participation) are cases where Roy and the comparison draw on the same reservoir with no transmission claimed in either direction.
- **Framework (ours, not Roy's).** The Kristeva intertextuality frame and the Theosophical reading of the *Coding Consciousness* diction are scholarly lenses we bring to the material — they explain *why* echoes recur and *how* we tier them; the work never states them.
- **How we'd be wrong.** The method is asymmetric and can *under-credit* genuine invention (the standing counterweight to all of the above). And if *"inherited vs internal"* is really two axes rather than one ladder, some symbols deserve a high score on *both* — a possibility kept open, not resolved.
- **Open threads (held, not asserted).** The unidentified *"Essex"* co-writer credit on *"Good Day";* whether *Dream/Dream* lifts Vaughan's exact 1957 take or interpolates the Mercer composition (and the still-to-pin *Precious Time* banter transcription); whether the borrow on [[Soul Is A Star]] is musical or lyrical; whether any *later* work extends the relay into the machine theme; and the **Joni "prophet" lineage**, kept strictly as a lead and contamination guard after a mis-sourced *"prophets"* quote in an earlier pass — no *"prophet"* language is built on it here. See *Advanced reading — open threads.*
