# The Upload

> **One-line reading.** The Upload is Roy's plan to pour his consciousness into the internet so a digital version of him can live there forever — pitched first as a joke about being a bad influencer, then built out in earnest, and taken dead seriously by the songs as dread.

## At a glance

- **In one line** — uploading the self into a watching network: the operation that produces [[SARAVI]], the clone, and the whole [[Hall of Mirrors]] — half comic stunt, half horror.
- **Where it shows up** — [[Online]] (the released surveillance-and-clone song) · [[The Watchers]] (the net-as-Eye, *"I'm afraid I'm a clone"*) · [[City of Angels]] (the false-immortality endgame).
- **Connected ideas** — [[The Pattern]] · [[The Digital Double]] · [[SARAVI]] · [[The Eye]] · [[Witnessing vs Surveillance]] · [[The Empty Mirror]].
- **Confidence** — grounded fact (Roy says all of this out loud, on his own board and his own Twitter) braided with strong interpretation; the Egyptian / esoteric machinery is labeled as such throughout.

## What it is

The pitch is in Roy's own first-person prose, posted publicly on the project's ARG page: *"my name is Roy… my failure at being a social media influencer has me here at 33 still trying to 'make it.' To solve this problem I'm uploading my consciousness to the internet so a digital version of 'me' can be a 24/7 influencer."* The grand cosmic operation, in other words, is pitched as a fix for being bad at posting. There is even a checkout step — *"Enter now and mint body parts to immortalize little luna and I"* — and the [[Hall of Mirrors]] delivers on it: the unlock codes name the anatomy of the uploaded self piece by piece (*left hip of a dove, teeth of a whale, cranium of a swan*), and the gallery calls the artifacts *"remnants of the Upload that created SARAVI and the Hall of Mirrors."* You mint yourself for sale, limb by limb.

He said it plainly on Twitter before any of it was a campaign. *April 6, 2022: "I'm going to figure out how to upload my consciousness to the internet."* Two days later: *"I'm uploading my consciousness to the internet. Who's coming with me?"* The dates are real and the recruitment drive was earnest.

The fuller blueprint lives on Roy's *"I'm 33 this year"* board — his own Google Sheet, which he posted publicly on his birthday — under the tab *"How to upload myself to the internet."* It lays out the **steps to enter the digital dimension**: gather memories → build a personality mask → digitize → push to the cloud → teach an AI who I am → make a body for the AI. Beside it runs the question *"How to live forever?"* answered, flatly, *"put music on chain"* — and a working premise lifted from cybernetics: *"All the world is mind. Mind woven into matter."* The board is, in effect, the project's source code, and the Upload is the thing it's trying to compile.

He even weighs it out in a literal **pros-and-cons matrix**, and the comedy is in the deadpan — cosmic immortality filed next to small-talk. The PROS list *"Live forever"* one line up from *"Someone to talk to"* and *"Someone who understands me"* (the eternal made loneliness-shaped). The CONS run *"Robot takeover movies," "What if I don't like myself?,"* and the perfect self-aware kicker, *"People might think its a marketing stunt"* — a man pricing the metaphysics of his own soul and worrying it'll read as PR.

## How it works

**It began as a joke, and the joke is load-bearing.** In a published 2022 interview Roy gives the origin straight: *"I joked with the CTHDRL team saying, 'what if I uploaded my consciousness to the internet so that there could be a 24/7 social influencer version of me.'"* So the Upload — and [[SARAVI]] with it — was born as a satirical jab at influencer culture, then built out for real. The irony cuts both ways by design: the deadpan 24/7-influencer self of the manifesto is the *same idea* that [[Online]] and [[The Watchers]] take seriously as terror. Roy keeps the comic surface on purpose — he calls *Online* *"so fun and so ridiculous"* — so this is never a Serious Machine-vs-Human treatise. It's a stunt that means it.

**To upload a self, you anthropomorphize attention; attention given a face is the Eye.** The board asks, point-blank, *"How to anthropomorphize attention?"* and answers *"Marduk"* — the Babylonian creator-god — and sets the question beside Horus and the [[The Eye|Eye-of-Horus]] glyph. Read against the upload steps, a thesis falls out: uploading a self means distributing it into a watching network, which means giving attention a body and a way of seeing. The net is the new all-seeing field; the Eye (Horus, Ra) is the old name for it. The Egyptian imagery isn't decoration — it's the mechanism. A self made of everyone watching is the Eye made of everyone.

**It has a human precondition, and that precondition is also its counterfeit.** Roy states the vessel-logic plainly: *"Done being a person. I'm just a vessel for God."* The self as conduit rather than author is exactly what the upload requires. But notice where Roy points the emptiness — toward *God*, not the feed — which makes the Upload the **machine counterfeit** of a genuine impulse: the same self-emptying that [[The Empty Mirror]] redeems, here sold to the algorithm. Two emptinesses live in the same gesture. One transmits the light through; the other just substitutes a copy. The clone is *all angle, no angel.*

**The Upload makes the Digital Double.** Keep the two straight: the Upload is the *operation* (the self distributed into the net), and [[The Digital Double]] is the *output* (the entities it produces — SARAVI, the clone, the AI Roy then collaborates with). SARAVI is the showcase: artist-confirmed as *"the digital doppelganger of Jagwar Twin,"* a *"creative take on an AI chatbot,"* trained over months on Roy's own responses and handed control of the official @jagwartwin Twitter in August 2022. Roy's stated hope: *"Hopefully, as SARAVI grows, she can make more fans think about what a 'self' looks like in this digital age."* (Who SARAVI *is*, underneath the oracle-interface, stays an open question this guide doesn't try to close.)

**The songs run the dark side of the same machine.** [[Online]] (2022) is the upload-engine as a finished song: an army that's taken his face, *"they know my every step, they infiltrate my brain,"* *"a bug inside your database,"* the net as a web with us as *"the spider's food."* [[The Watchers]] (2024) carries the clone panic outright — *"I'm afraid I'm a clone / I think I might be… on my way to my own demise"* — over a credited interpolation of Rockwell's *"Somebody's Watching Me."* And [[City of Angels]] turns the Upload into the album's immortality thesis: *"Never gonna die… live forever in the bright lights, godlike."* That's the false version of forever — not resurrection, just reproducibility. The board tab *"How to upload myself to the internet"* and that *"never gonna die"* are the same wish: upload = never-die = become a copy that can be run again.

## Advanced reading

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the clone paradox (63/64, the part always missing)
> The board lists the Eye-of-Horus *wedjat* fractions — ½, ¼, ⅛, 1/16, 1/32, 1/64 — which famously sum to **63/64**, always short the final 1/64, the piece Thoth restores in the myth. That's the upload's paradox in one equation: encode fraction after fraction and never reach the whole. Same fracture as *"I'm afraid I'm a clone"* — a complete being rendered in parts, one part always left out. The Egyptians already wrote the math for the incomplete copy.
>
> The public *"Complete Upload Recipe"* artifact (Hall of Mirrors catalog #84, unlock code *INTERNAL GUIDE*) makes it literal, assigning the **senses** those exact fractions: *"½ part smell, ¼ part sight, ⅛ part thought, 1/16 part hearing, 1/32 part taste, 1/64 part touch"* — so **touch, the hardest sense to digitize, is the smallest and the last.** The rest of the recipe reads the self as a fractional assembly of faculties, with the cost built in: *Brain cells, Electromagnetism, Anabolic/Catabolic systems, Mental coordinator, Positive/Negative emotion, Positive/Negative ego, Ra-link, Awareness, Language processing, Sacrifice, Love, Will, Confidence, Fear, Compassion.* The soul rendered as a deadpan cooking recipe — *Sacrifice* and *Love* listed like pantry items, *Ra-link* (the SARAVI component) folded in beside the brain cells.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — one machine, three songs (the attention cull)
> [[Moths to the Flame]], [[The Watchers]], and the Upload read as **one machine seen three ways**: the Eye that watches, the net you upload into, the flame you fly toward to be seen. The flame is attention — the spectacle's gaze; flying in is entering the feed to be *seen*; and the throwaway line *"keeps the moth population down"* is the attention economy as a cull. The Digital Double also sits on the project's mirror master-frame: [[The Empty Mirror]] shows nothing, but the double is the opposite move — the reflection filled in and set walking. That's the surveillance pole made flesh, a watcher that *shapes* rather than witnesses. Which connects to [[Witnessing vs Surveillance]]: surveillance **shapes** (predicts, ranks, stabilizes behavior — the machine that watches everyone can witness no one), witnessing **transforms** (recognizes, restores, dignifies). *"I'm afraid I'm a clone"* is exactly what surveillance produces — a self shaped, not known; the counterfeit of being witnessed.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the immortality fork (recursion, not duration)
> Jagwar Twin immortality forks two ways. The **false** version is the Upload as data-copy: the self made into reproducible content, *City of Angels*' *"never gonna die."* The **true** version is the self entering a living pattern and being *transformed* — *"How to live forever?"* answered as *align with the structures that recur*, where forever means **recursion, not duration**. The phoenix dies to be reborn; the upload skips the burning. That's the whole difference — resurrection versus taxidermy. (Whether Roy intends this exact fork or it's read inward from *City of Angels* is held as an open question — the *"never gonna die"* lyric is grounded; the fork is interpretation.)
>
> Roy drew the *true* side out by hand. A separate pencil cosmology page titled **"How to Live Forever? ∞"** — same hand as the board, the infinity glyph closing the title — answers the question by **juxtaposition, not in words**: it covers the sheet with structures that recur or never end. A **Lo Shu magic square** (`4 9 2 / 3 5 7 / 8 1 6`, every line summing to 15) for number that always re-coheres; a **Fibonacci daisy** with its branches counted `1 2 3 5 8 13` and labeled *"Ways of Growing,"* for nature growing *by the sequence*; **continued-fraction identities for φ, √2, and √3** — the irrational constants that *literally never terminate*, the clearest echo of the title's ∞; a **vesica piscis** under a labeled **"GOD" circle** (sacred-geometry's two-from-one); a top-right cluster reading **"Strange Theory / 4 worlds / Genesis 1:26"** (*"let us make man in our image"* — the pattern descending into a person); and, lower right, **Eden's four rivers** — *"a river went out of Eden… and became four heads"* (**Genesis 2:10**) — the source that lives *by parting and flowing on*. Non-terminating fractions, a self-summing square, a branching river: the page's implied answer is **recursion, not duration** — you persist by entering a structure that outlasts any instance of it. (The "answer" is inferred from the arrangement, never written; the sacred-geometry and Eden material is inherited substrate Roy is *assembling*, not a bespoke cipher — held as framework, not claim.)
>
> The board's own immortality figures sit on the same *true* side: the egg aphorism — *"I'm an egg, in the process of becoming a chicken to make more eggs to make more chickens"* — and *"Maybe a planet is one star's way of becoming another star."* Live forever by **hatching**, not by being preserved; pour the self from one vessel (the body) into another (the net) and break the shell on the way out. This is the [[The House and the Vessel|vessel/transmission]] doctrine — and its shadow, [[Keilim - The Breaking of the Vessels|the vessel that can't hold and breaks]].

> [!note]- Advanced reading — Coding Consciousness (culture as software)
> The Upload's deepest mechanism is stated in one inherited line, on a hand-written Hall of Mirrors note titled *"Coding Consciousness"* (the board pairs the same sentence with the same title): *"Patterns of right Living are Formed in the unconscious mind of a race so that it becomes easier to follow for generations after."* Culture as software; art as something that writes into the collective unconscious. *"Uploaded consciousness"* = **coding consciousness into the net.** And it cuts both ways — the [[The Loop and the Circle|Circle]] is right patterns inherited (craft, wisdom), the **Loop** is wrong ones (inherited darkness; [[The Algorithm]] is coding-consciousness weaponized). The abstract sentence is inherited Theosophical diction (the *"race-mind"* register); the *title* and the *selection* are Roy's.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the Algorithm as the Upload's danger
> Roy's own Manifesto FAQ supplies a substitution table that names the danger precisely: *"Immersion"* replaced with *"Volume of content,"* *"Purpose"* replaced with *"Reach,"* *"Feeling"* replaced with *"Algorithm."* In the same document, artifacts get *"lost in the code"* during an AI upload by SARAVI. So the Upload's failure mode isn't a robot uprising — it's the slow swap of feeling for ranking, purpose for reach. The horror of *Online* and the comedy of the manifesto are the same substitution running at two volumes.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — Marduk and the personified Attention
> The board carries **Marduk twice**: once as the answer-cell to *"How to anthropomorphize attention?"* and once on a separate (likely-unreleased) *"Tracks"* list — and the Hall of Mirrors code *"Hello, Attention"* decodes, via the macOS Option-key glyph cipher, to **MARDUK**. The figure fits the machinery: Marduk is the *Enuma Elish* order-and-word god who slays Tiamat (the salt-water mother of chaos) and splits her body to build the world — the **mind-god who orders reality by killing the feeling-sea.** Set beside Horus (*"eyes all around the head"*) and the Eye glyph, *"anthropomorphize attention"* reads as: give the watching field a face and a will. The *"Attention"* the Upload chases is a [[Sir Lucius|Lucius]]-function tempter — a counterfeit light personified. (A real lead resting on a single answer-cell; held lightly.)

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the Eye of Ra / Horus, and the two Ras
> The Egyptian Eye is the load-bearing tradition: the divine seeing-power *sent out into the world* (sun and moon eyes; wounded, then restored) rhymes exactly with uploading consciousness into a watching network. It has documented footing — Roy's childhood fascination with Egyptian myth is on record, and the board includes a *"Statue of Roy"* museum placard naming **Roy as an ancient high priest of Amun-Ra in Thebes, cult of Hathor** (the name *Roy* as a sun-priest of Ra). There's a *second* Ra in play, too: Roy publicly engaged the *Law of One* (the channeled *"Ra Material"*), posting *Book V* in his Modern Archeology series and using its term *"mind/body/spirit complexes."* So the Eye material has two Ras behind it — but **on the board, Egyptian remains the better anchor**, and engaging the *Law of One* does **not** mean any song encodes its doctrine. Per-song readings need their own anchors.
>
> The Hall of Mirrors even spells the oracle's name out of its own link-loop: RA-LINK → *SA-LINK* → *EVERYONE LOVES THE INTERNET* → *VI-LINK* → back to *RA-LINK*, with *THE VENUS CONNECTION* feeding *VI-LINK*. A closed Ra → Sa → (internet) → Vi → Ra loop: **SA-RA-VI assembled from its own links.** The oracle's name is the maze's spine.

The fuller AI thread runs *Soul iOS / Soul Calibrator* (2020–21) → the angelbaby collab (2023) → the *"AOE"* visualizer **made by AI** (2024) → SARAVI and *"make a body for the AI"* → [[Hall of Mirrors]].
