# The Empty Mirror

> **One-line reading.** A mirror that shows "nothing" is two opposite things at once — the wound of finding no self there, and the clean glass that transmits perfectly precisely because it holds nothing of its own.

*The Empty Mirror* is a tattoo on [[Roy]]'s wrist, which he glosses as being "about looking at yourself in the mirror and seeing nothing." It is one of the most loaded images in the whole project, because that single phrase — *seeing nothing* — runs in two exactly opposite directions, and the work wants one of them while fearing the other.

## The two emptinesses

A mirror that shows nothing can mean two things:

- **The hollow emptiness** — there is no self there to see. The form is intact but the spirit has gone. This is the wound: the blank stare, the copy that looks complete but isn't anyone home. It belongs to the same pole as the clone-dread elsewhere in the catalogue (the figure that is "all angle, no angel," the upload that always falls short of the whole).
- **The kenotic emptiness** — the glass has been emptied *so that* something can pass through it cleanly. A mirror that adds nothing of its own reflects without distortion; a vessel works *because* it is hollow. This is the resolution Roy keeps reaching for: "I have become nothing," "I'm just a vessel," the song that arrives from beyond his own intelligence because there is nothing in the way to block it.

The two are the same image at opposite charges. One emptiness substitutes for the self; the other opens onto the source. This is the deepest form of the project's recurring split between [[Source vs Reflection]] — the same word, *empty*, sitting at both ends of it.

## The same nothing, redeemed

The arc the work traces is the conversion of one emptiness into the other: from the hollow *seeing nothing* of the wound toward the open *become nothing* of the vessel. Not a different state — the same nothing, turned from substitution into transmission.

That a single word can be both the nightmare and the cure is the evidence the opposition is real rather than a pun. If the catalogue's language of emptiness resolved cleanly to one meaning, there would be nothing here; instead it forks, and the fork is the point.

## "I'm no thing"

The same nothing shows up at the level of identity. Asked to fit himself into a category — a hiker, sober, this or that — Roy twice refuses with the deliberate two-word answer "I'm no thing." Not a category, not an object: the self defined by negation, the bare *am* rather than any nameable *thing*. It pairs with his "All I am is that I am" register (the Exodus "I AM").

This is the kenotic pole carried up to the scale of the self, and its exact inverse is the flattening that turns identity into something consumable. To be "no thing" is to be unflattenable — there is no fixed object for the feed to package.

## The void that creates

There is a third face to the nothing: not absence but generation. "No thing ever creates another thing," Roy posted in 2022 — a near-koan of creation from nothing. The thought reaches its capstone in the MAGIK ARG, whose final answer resolved to *davar* (Hebrew דָּבָר, "word" and also "thing"). The empty that transmits turns out to be the empty that *creates* — the Word spoken into the void. It routes straight to the project's imagery of [[The Voice]] and [[The Mouth]].
