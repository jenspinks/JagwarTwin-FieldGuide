# The Voice

> **One-line reading.** The Voice is the authentic self trying to speak against everything that silences, suppresses, or co-opts it — and the arc of the project is that voice finding its way back, until it can finally choose its own silence.

## At a glance

- **In one line** — the real self straining to be heard, against a system built to scream over it, stuff its mouth, or steal its tune.
- **Where it shows up** — [[Welcome to the Circus]] (the voiceless scream) · [[Ballerina Boy]] (the buried voice) · [[SOL]] (the voice recovered, wordless)
- **Connected ideas** — [[The Mouth]] · [[The Mask]] · [[The Algorithm]] · [[Sir Lucius]] · [[Mary]] · [[The Circus]] · [[El Roi]]
- **Confidence** — grounded fact (released lyrics, Roy's own posts and interviews) braided with strong interpretation; the esoteric framework is fenced off and labeled throughout.

## What it is

Most of the big ideas in this world are about *seeing*. This one is about *being heard* — which turns out to be the harder problem.

The Voice is the authentic self at the moment it tries to speak: the thing you'd say if nothing were editing you. Its shadow is Silence, and the project runs them as one of its central tensions — the true voice straining to sound while a whole apparatus works to shut it down. The cruelty is that it fails two ways at once. From the **inside**, the voice collapses — you open your mouth and nothing comes out. From the **outside**, the voice is commanded shut — *say less, smile more, perform.* The genius of the work is that it stages both at the same time, then spends years climbing back out.

And here's the twist that keeps the idea from being merely sad: not all silence is defeat. The project is quietly sorting two kinds, and it's moving *toward* one of them.

## How it works

**Two failure modes, sometimes in one song.** [[Welcome to the Circus]] is the master class. It stacks three mouth-failures in quick succession — the voice eaten from within, the voice commanded shut, the mouth turned into a weapon — so the listener feels the whole machine operating at once. The voiceless scream (the apparatus collapsing) and *"shut your mouth"* (the silencing order) are the two poles of the concept, and the song fires them in the same breath. That's the Circus's particular cruelty: it doesn't pick a method.

**Two kinds of silence.** The distinction is the spine of the whole arc:

- **Imposed silence** — the voice *prevented.* The scream with no words; *"shut your mouth"; the mouth stuffed from outside. Passive, inflicted, the Circus's instruction.
- **Chosen silence** — the voice *withheld.* The contemplative who waits; restraint over suppression; the voice that speaks when it's ready, not when it's told. Active, sovereign, the integrated state.

The project travels from the first toward the second. Its imposed exemplar is *"shut your mouth"*; its chosen exemplar — read below — is a figure who *"opened not his mouth"* by choice, and is stronger for it.

**The arc.** Plotted across the catalogue, the Voice has a shape: the **voiceless scream** of [[Welcome to the Circus]] → **commanded silence** (the instructed voice, the stuffed mouth) → the **raw desperation-voice** of [[Lucius Lullaby]], reportedly sung into a laptop microphone at the lowest point, *"at thirty-five,"* before the Circus optimized it → **integration** in [[SOL]]. A voice finding itself across registers and arriving, finally, at a silence it gets to choose.

**The system decides whose mouth is amplified.** [[The Algorithm]] is the throttle on all of this. It rewards the spectacle-voice ([[Welcome to the Circus]] breaking out) and starves the vulnerable one ([[Ballerina Boy]], reportedly the lowest-performing song, therefore the one buried). The suppression isn't only narrative — it's a metric. The truest voice in the catalogue is also the least-streamed, and that's the point being made, in receipts.

**Voice as something stolen.** The sharpest move the project makes with the idea is theft. In [[#1 Champion (la Conquistadora)]], the *entire chorus belongs to [[Mary]]* — the count, the verdict, *"know 'bout me,"* even the title line. [[Sir Lucius]] has no anthem of his own; he can only climb onto *hers* for *"number one champion,"* drown her out, and end up finishing the title alone, a cappella, after the music has stopped — a braggart boasting to an empty room. The song *performs* the voice-appropriation it describes. It's the catalogue's cleanest staging of who-controls-the-voice, enacted in the mix rather than argued in the lyric.

**The blessing-voice the machine can't reach.** Against all that suppression sits the divine-feminine voice — the one that *transmits* rather than performs. The motherly spoken outro of [[Ballerina Boy]] (*"my son, let your light shine… there's no one else like you out there"*) and [[Mary]]'s creation-grammar in [[#1 Champion (la Conquistadora)]] (*"A-B-C, count 'em"*) are the *true* voice. Notice that [[Ballerina Boy]] holds two distinct blessing-voices: the earthly father's in-verse *"there's nothing wrong with you, my son"* (a pardon, stated in the negative) and the motherly outro's *"let your light shine"* (a benediction, generative). One clears a charge; the other claims a nature. The Lucius-system can suppress this voice, bury it, even counterfeit it — but it can't absorb it, because it isn't a reflection to catch.

**Roy as relay, not source.** Crucially, the Voice doesn't always belong to Roy. He's frequently a *conduit* for inherited voices. [[Superhuman]] is a verbatim sample of Charlie Chaplin's *Great Dictator* speech (the composer field credits Charles Chaplin) — a borrowed voice that's neither Roy's nor Lucius's. *Dream a Little Dream* gets transformed from private consolation into the collective *"dream out loud."* This places the Voice inside a larger transmission cluster: the song as channel, not as broadcast. (The claimed interpolation of Joni Mitchell's actual voice in [[Soul Is A Star]] is unverified — see Source notes.)

## Where it appears

Short of an exhaustive list, these are the songs, videos, and posts that establish the concept.

- **[[Welcome to the Circus]]** — the triple voice-failure: *"My teeth are falling out my mouth / I'm screaming, but no words come out"* (collapse from within), *"Step right up, shut your mouth"* (the silencing order), *"I'll chew you up, spit you out"* (the consuming mouth).
- **[[Lucius Lullaby]]** — the raw desperation-voice: Roy reportedly singing into a laptop microphone at the lowest point, *"at thirty-five,"* before the Circus optimized the take — the origin-voice. In the same song, the false rescue-voice that counterfeits [[Mary]]: a soothing rescue offered in the mother's register but coming from the shadow.
- **[[Ballerina Boy]]** — the suppressed voice at its purest; the motherly/[[Mary]]-class spoken outro laid over the childhood wound; the lowest-performing song, therefore the buried one.
- **[[Moths to the Flame]]** — the descending dark-feminine vocalization: the track closes on a female voice (background vocal MERCY) that *descends* — candle blown out, voice sinks — inverting [[Mary]]'s rising *"born to shine."* The blessing-voice played in reverse.
- **[[SOL]]** — the integrated voice: *voiced but wordless,* primal non-lexical vocalization (Apple Music lists *No Lyrics Available* — not instrumental, but voice without words).
- **[[#1 Champion (la Conquistadora)]]** — the appropriation: the whole chorus is [[Mary]]'s; Lucius drowns her into a-cappella silence.
- **[[Happy Face]]** (2018) — the instructed voice. The hook is an order to perform, not feel: *"say you're good, say you're fine," "if you're sad, don't let it show."* Authentic speech papered over.
- **[[I Like to Party]]** (2021) — the mouth physically stuffed (force-feeding); the kingdom-within counter-broadcast cut off *mid-word*: *"you're the King—" / "we interrupt our program."* The program silences the awakening message in real time.
- **[[City of Angels]]** — the man so far inside the machine he has *"no voice left to get out of the way with."*
- **[[Hell of a Night Pt. 2]] (The Hallelujah Stickup)** (2018) — voice as weapon *and* survival mechanism; the explicit question *"who controls the voice?"*
- **[[Superhuman]]** (2018) — the borrowed Chaplin voice (*Great Dictator*, 1940).
- **[[Online]]** (2022) — the oscillating captured-human / machine voice (*"the voice inside that sounds like you"*).
- **Roy's @JagwarTwin posts (2025–26)** ([[Public Posts]]) — *"Silence is the language of God"; "Understanding is only understood in silence"; "The mind is the slayer of the real / We must slay the slayer."*
- **[[Honey Podcast]] S2E7** — Roy on the authentic self versus the edited one: making songs *"the record label executive might like,"* versus *"I feel this and I need to say this."* On masks: *"we wear these kind of masks to try to fit in."*
- **Roy IG Live** (12 Labours of Hercules, ~2026) — the *"third voice"* (see below).

- **[[Heart and Mind|The Heart/Mind Split]]** — the *"third voice"* / intuition synthesis; the mind as the *slayer*, *"slay the slayer"* as the heart overcoming it.

> [!note]- Resonances — the traditions the concept rhymes with
> The imposed-silence / voiceless-scream pole has deep correspondences. These are framed as resonance (the concept rhyming with older material the artist's work openly moves through), not as claimed authorial sources, except where Roy names them himself.
> - **The voiceless scream.** Edvard Munch's *The Scream* (1893) is the primal image: the open mouth that makes no sound the world will hear. The same shape recurs in scripture — **Job 30:20** (*"I cry to you, and you do not answer me"*) and **Lamentations 3:8** (*"even when I cry and shout, He shuts out my prayer"*) — the authentic voice that reaches no receiver. **Cassandra** is its mythic form: truth spoken plainly and condemned to be disbelieved, voice rendered socially inaudible. And the **dark night of the soul** (St. John of the Cross, 16th c.) names the interior version — prayer failing, the tongue silenced, the old consolations gone. Five faces of the same wound: the scream no one hears.
> - **Chosen silence as power.** Against all of that sits **Isaiah 53:7** — *"he was oppressed and afflicted, yet he opened not his mouth"* — voluntary silence as strength, the page's exemplar for the *chosen* pole.
> - **Silencing by distraction.** Marie-Antoinette's apocryphal *"let them eat cake"* and the Roman **bread and circuses** are the population-pacifier version — the voice not stuffed but *sedated*, quieted with spectacle and confection. This is the substrate under [[I Like to Party]] (*"bake a truth with a lie / then we sell 'em the cake"*; *Devil's Food*): the awakening message drowned not by an order but by a party.
> - **The sleeping crowd (Gurdjieff).** Roy's most-named public influence supplies the substrate behind the silenced *audience.* Gurdjieff's mechanical, sleeping man — *"the crowd neither wants nor seeks knowledge"*; *"you can't awaken using the same system that put you to sleep"* (both quoted by Roy on X, late 2025) — is why the Circus can demand silence and get it: a crowd already asleep doesn't need to be told to shut its mouth. The grounded part is that Roy names Gurdjieff repeatedly; reading the silenced audience through him is the vault's interpretation.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — Roy's "third voice" (the god voice in the throat)
> The Heart/Mind split isn't a flat binary. On an Instagram Live discussing the *12 Labours of Hercules* (~2026), Roy describes a **third voice** in his own words: *"there's a third voice… the synthesis between the instinct and the intellect… the intuition… super consciousness, the god voice thing in here"* — gesturing, as he says it, to the throat and chest. This completes the architecture: the binary of instinct (lower) and intellect (the mind / Lucifer pole) resolves *upward* into intuition — and it lands, physically, on the throat. That's the same spot as Lucius's [[El Roi]] tattoo and, in the esoteric mapping, the Da'at utterance-gate where *"reflection crosses into expression."* The grounded part is Roy's own words; placing it on the Tree of Life is the vault's reading, not his stated method.

> - *"Understanding is only understood in silence"* (2026)
> - *"Silence is the language of God"* (2025)
> - *"The mind is the slayer of the real / We must slay the slayer"* (2026)

Let the Disciple slay the Slayer."* Roy named Blavatsky explicitly in late 2025, so the lineage is grounded, not imposed. Blavatsky's title — *The Voice of the Silence* — is itself the whole paradox this concept turns on: the voice that only sounds once the noise of the mind is stilled. The same Theosophy supplies the *"Great Breath,"* the universal breath behind [[SOL]]'s wordless substrate.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the inherited hostile voice (the voice that isn't you)
> A different mechanism from the suppressing system, and one Roy names in his own voice. In a public Mirror Session (with Calum Hood), Roy describes a hostile inner voice he experiences as *not his own* and *ancestral*: *"those voices… I know that they're not mine… from mistakes that my ancestors made."* This isn't armor over a wound (the Protector's logic) — it's an inherited transmission, a voice that *sounds like you* but came from somewhere upstream. It pairs with [[Machine vs Human]]'s formulation — the real horror is *"not the dictator's loudspeaker but the voice inside that sounds like you"* — and with the relay cluster: Chaplin's borrowed voice is benign inheritance, the ancestral critic is hostile inheritance. Same machinery, opposite charge.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — vowels, breath, and the voice before structure (tradition-correspondence)
> One frame — drawn from the Kabbalah Roy openly studies, **not** a method he has stated — hears the authentic voice arriving *vowel-first.* In the Hebrew writing system the vowels are omitted from the consonantal text, and the reader must supply the animating breath; the dead letters need the living vowel to sound. On this correspondence, **consonants are structure (angles) and vowels are spirit (angels, breath, *ruach*)** — and the true voice arrives *before* structure can edit it. Which is why *"teeth falling out"* and *"screaming with no words"* (structure gone, pure breath left) sit so ambiguously between a failure state and the voice at its most raw. The lineage is documented public Kabbalah — Abulafia's letter-and-vowel permutation as ascent, the 13th-century vowels-as-spirit tradition, the 72-Name letters-as-angels. Roy's engagement with Hebrew and Kabbalah is itself grounded (his 2019 Hebrew post, the [[El Roi]] tattoo, the *I'm 33* board) — but **no public Jagwar Twin source states the angle/angel scheme as his framework.** It's correspondence only; see [[The Angels and the Angles]]. (A standing falsifier the vault keeps: do *not* present "vowels are spirit" as grounded for any era, and do not project it backward onto *Subject to Flooding* as a "protocol.")

> [!note]- Advanced reading — Da'at, the throat, and creation-by-word
> The deep-structure home of the Voice is the **throat as Da'at** — *"the abyss becoming speech,"* the gate where knowing crosses into utterance. [[Hell of a Night Pt. 2]]'s *"who controls the voice?"* sits exactly here, and so does Lucius's [[El Roi]] throat-tattoo: a figure made of reflection, wearing *"the God who sees me"* on the very organ of speech. Push further up the chain and you reach **creation-by-the-word** — *davar*, the Word that creates from No-Thing (the [[The Empty Mirror|Empty Mirror]] / MAGIK ARG capstone: *"No thing ever creates another thing"*). This routes the whole concept into Kabbalistic cosmogony via *Sefer Yetzirah*: the universe spoken into being, the throat as the human echo of that first utterance. Syncretic framework, never claimed as authorial intent.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the childlike voice (awaken or sedate)

(Caution: the 2026 *"children's voices"* claim is unconfirmed — the credited background includes adult vocalists — so read it as *childlike / lullaby texture,* not literal children.)

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the comedy around the wound (a guard against over-reading)
> The concept is sincere at its core, but the surface is frequently, genuinely funny — and reading it as solemn doctrine misses the texture. A few guardrails:
> - **[[Online]]** (2022) plays *playful,* not as a Serious Machine-vs-Human treatise. The captured-human/machine oscillation includes deadpan deflation — *"maybe Jupiter is stupider than people say."* Don't over-doctrine it.
> - **[[Bananas]]** has Lucius take a children's-entertainer sing-song voice (*"Hey, kids… well, alright"*) to license id-regression and arson — *"the Pied Piper, not the confessor."* The dissociation line *"I hear voices in my mind I don't remember / Did I do that?"* recodes a sitcom catchphrase; the banana doubles as a PA broadcasting his voice. The vault gloss: *"a banana peel under the conscience — funny until you realize the fall is the point."*
> - **[[Great Time to Be Human]]** (LL visualizer) is the cheerful-apocalypse beat. Keyed line-by-line to the lyric, Lucius brandishes a **spoon as a scepter** and strikes a **gong** on the sarcastic *"what a great time to be human"* refrain (the sacred ceremony rung with a kitchen utensil), and **waves goodbye to camera** on the *"Shanghai summertime"* chorus while shouldering a *"PROPERTY OF B.M.R.C."* **rocket launcher** — the production-house revealed as the war-machine brand, the show and the ordnance from one factory. The toxic-positivity anthem performed as a grin over a cull.
> - **[[Moths to the Flame]]** is the deadpan-doctrine beat — and the vault flags its own guard against over-reading. The spoken interlude (*"Civilization has been a mistake… the wild animals are wiser than we are… if a moth mistakes a flame for a mating call and flies into the flame — so what? It just keeps the moth population down"*) is an **edgelord-nihilist** shrug, cheerful horror stated as flat creed (a precise inversion of the Gurdjieff line — *wake up and labor to become human* turned into *regress to the animal*). The comedic register IS the disguise: read solemnly, it's a thesis; read straight, it's Lucius enjoying himself. The song is voiced *by* Lucius — so even the doctrine is a performance.
> - **Sir Lucius's voice in the wild** is comically vain: the @jagwartwin scorecard rant, signed *"Your loyal servant, Sir Lucius,"* ranking Charli XCX and Chappell Roan above himself and telling fans to *"find something more interesting to do with your little life."*
> - **Roy's own register around the literal voice is light** — the Discord aside *"get your voice back! lol."*
>
> The rule: be witty *around* the wound, never *through* it. The jokes are real; so is the depression underneath them.

> [!question]- Open questions
> The ones the concept hasn't settled — kept open on purpose:
> - **Is the authentic voice recoverable?** After years of the Lucius-overlay, is Roy's own voice still available — or has the Circus-voice become the only one the instrument can make? The arc *bets* on recovery ([[SOL]]); it doesn't prove it.
> - **Diagnosis or personal history?** Is the voiceless scream a general *diagnosis* of the silencing machine, or specific *autobiography* — and which lines are Lucius performing a condition versus Roy reporting his own substrate? The two voices share a throat, which is exactly what makes them hard to separate.
> - **Is [[Ballerina Boy]] the answer?** Is the buried, lowest-streamed song actually the most authentic track in the catalogue — the voice the system can't metabolize precisely because it can't sell it?
> - **Who is the machine-voice in [[Online]]?** Is *"the voice inside that sounds like you"* the machine ventriloquizing the human, or the sedated human ventriloquizing the machine? The horror works either way; the page doesn't decide which.
> - **The missing keystone.** Does Roy ever name [[Sir Lucius]] *directly* as the mind/intellect — the mental pole opposite the heart? The architecture leans on it (the "third voice" synthesizes instinct and intellect; the mind is the *slayer*), but a flat on-the-record statement *"Lucius = the mind"* would close the loop, and it isn't yet in hand.
> - **One entity or two?** Whether the faceless feminine *voice* ([[Ballerina Boy]], [[Weirdness]]) is the *same* figure as named [[Mary]] is the working assumption (Mary-class), not proven — a casting/credits source would settle it.
