# The Hall of Mirrors

> **The thread.** The project hides its pieces in a puzzle-world and asks the fans to bring them back into the light.

Jagwar Twin doesn't just release songs — it builds artifacts: a playable web3 archive, recurring title cards with manifestos buried in the fine print, a working notebook for its central fantasy. This path walks from the puzzle-world itself out through the objects that surround it, and lands on the emblem that watches over the whole thing. It's the best route in if what drew you was the sense that the work is a game with a hidden solution.

## The route

1. [[Hall of Mirrors]] — start in the playable archive: the web3 puzzle-world built from Jagwar Twin's symbolic vocabulary, where decoding the clues is how the hidden pieces come back to light.
2. [[The Mirror]] — the symbol the whole hall is made of: the device by which the self becomes visible only as something other, faithful only as long as it remembers it isn't the source.
3. [[The Empty Mirror]] — what the hall keeps showing: a mirror that reflects "nothing" is two things at once, the wound of finding no self and the clean glass that transmits perfectly.
4. [[The Manifesto Cards]] — the recurring title-card device that hides a tiny esoteric manifesto in its fine print, so the casual scroller sees an ad while the attentive viewer finds a thesis.
5. [[The I'm 33 Board]] — the working notebook behind the artifacts: the project's map of what a consciousness even is, a kind of source-code for the whole world.
6. [[The Jaguar]] — the emblem watching over all of it: the creature that looks into others and, in turn, looks back into itself.
