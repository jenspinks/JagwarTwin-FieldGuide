# Singles & EPs

> **One-line reading.** The standalone releases between the albums — where, more than once, the most important developments in the mythology happened off-album: the dream that births [[Sir Lucius]], and the singles that point straight at the Lucius era without ever joining it.

Not everything Jagwar Twin releases lives on an album. This page is the hub for the standalone singles and EPs — the connective tissue between [[Subject to Flooding|Subject to Flooding]] (2018), [[33|33]] (2022), and [[Lucius Lullaby (album)|Lucius Lullaby]] (2026). Some of these are throwaway one-offs; a few are load-bearing. The stretch worth paying attention to is 2023–2025, where the standalone releases quietly assemble the figure the 2026 album finally names.

## Overview

The singles fall into rough eras. Around the 2018 debut, standalone cuts like "Shine" sit at the edge of the *Subject to Flooding* record. The 2019–2021 gap between albums is still being mapped. The richest run is **2023–2025**, between *33* and *Lucius Lullaby* — a steady drip of singles and EPs in which the Lucius iconography and the Loop concept get built out in public, ahead of the album.

## Major releases

### Around the debut (~2018)

- [[Shine]] — a standalone single from the *Subject to Flooding* era, also the album's closer.

### Between *33* and *Lucius Lullaby* (2023–2025)

- [[Good Time (1-08)|Good Time (1:08)]] (2023) — the consequential one. Roy tells its origin publicly as a dream: a party, then Drake handing him *"the wheel"* and telling him it's his turn to drive, and Roy alone on a highway *as a rabbit*, *"looking for something, but I didn't know what I was looking for."* A second rabbit in clown makeup slides into the passenger seat — *"I'm Sir Lucius… I'm here to help you"* — and *"he had all the answers,"* factually right about everything, *"but I had a weird feeling in my heart… like he was telling the truth, but he was manipulating me in some way."* The dream ends in a crash (a white semi with a lightning bolt) and Roy wakes at 1:08, the title. This is the song where Lucius is, in effect, *born* — and notice both rabbits are the *same animal*: the shadow isn't an invader but an adaptation of Roy's own nature, which is why it can't simply be cast out. It's also the catalogue's cleanest early statement of its core rule, in Roy's own voice and dated to 2023: the heart knew what the mind couldn't argue against. The video carries the earliest fully-assembled Lucius look (face paint, knight's armor, *"I'm a mastermind"*) three years before the 2026 co-credit. See [[Sir Rabbit]].
- [[All My Friends]] (2023) — standalone single.
- [[The Circle - The Great Jagwar Myth|The Circle: The Great Jagwar Myth]] (2023) — a reimagined telling of [[The Circle]] from *33*.
- [[Life Is Good]] (2023) — standalone single.
- [[The Watchers]] (October 2024) — a short EP / release built on a conspiracy theme, interpolating Rockwell's 1984 *"Somebody's Watching Me"* and turning the net into a single watching Eye — the surveillance-and-clone fear that feeds straight into *Lucius Lullaby*'s "City of Angels."
- [[tomorrow (ANGLE OF ETERNITY)]] (2024) — single, featuring lovelytheband.
- [[dumbledore]] (2025) — EP.
- [[MAGIK]] (2025) — EP.
- [[SideQuest]] (2025) — EP.
- [[Ballerina Boy]] (2025) — a standalone single, and a key one: the buried-child wound at the heart of [[Roy]]'s story, given its own song. It did *not* become a *Lucius Lullaby* track.
- [[LOOP]] (2025) — a single where the [[The Loop and the Circle|Loop]] concept is made explicit; the first of a publicly-named chain (LOOP → "Not Your Homie" → "Welcome to the Circus") framed as one path seeking clarity beyond the system and another mastering it from within. LOOP is the light-side path — the trap named so it can be broken — and it carries Roy's resolution-line, *"my mind must serve my heart"* (published as part of the era's theatrical lore). That line is the exact counter-pole to the bargain Lucius drives on the album, *"you gotta serve somebody"*: the same verb, the fight only over who the servant answers to. LOOP stayed off the 2026 album.

## Who's present

The standalone era is where **[[Sir Lucius]]** visibly comes together. He isn't named as a co-artist until 2026, but "Good Time (1:08)" gives him his origin and his face, and the 2023–2025 singles keep circling his territory. **[[Roy]]** is the through-voice — and the 2025 "Ballerina Boy" single is where the catalogue's founding wound gets a song of its own. **[[Sir Rabbit]]**, the seeker-self, belongs to this era too: the rabbit of "Good Time (1:08)" is Roy's own image of himself before the split.

## What it's about

If the albums are the chapters, the singles are where you can watch the seams. Three threads matter most here. First, **the slow assembly of Lucius**: the iconography, the "mastermind" voice, and the dream-logic all arrive in 2023, well before the 2026 billing — proof that the figure accumulated rather than appeared. Second, **the concepts that stayed standalone**: "LOOP" names the Loop outright, and "Ballerina Boy" gives the buried child its own track, yet neither was folded into *Lucius Lullaby.* They orbit the album without landing on it — load-bearing pieces of the mythology that live just outside the record they prepare.

Third, **the drama was staged in public before the album dropped**. Across 2025 Roy released a theatrical YouTube series, *The Interview*, dramatizing the Roy-versus-Lucius struggle the album then crystallizes — including the central bargain, in which Roy brings a sincere love song and Lucius kills it (*"no one wants this love stuff"*) and pushes the commercial track instead. The era's loose pieces, in other words, weren't just leftover songs; they were how the story got told. Roy has even named the album's borrowed spine out loud — captioning the title track with *"You're gonna have to serve somebody. — Bob Dylan,"* the 1979 song whose thesis the whole record turns on.

The honest note: several of these releases are still lightly mapped, and a few are simply standalone songs without a deep place in the system. The page will grow as the between-album gaps fill in.

## Suggested listening order

To follow the mythology rather than the calendar:

1. [[Good Time (1-08)|Good Time (1:08)]] — the dream where Lucius is born
2. [[The Watchers]] — the conspiracy-theme turn
3. [[Ballerina Boy]] — the buried child, given a song
4. [[LOOP]] — the Loop named outright
