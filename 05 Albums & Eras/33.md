# 33

> **One-line reading.** The 2022 cosmology album — where the personal wound of the debut turns outward into a diagnosis of the attention age, poses the catalogue's central question at its literal center, and answers it with the founding myth at the close. It does all of this *before* any of it is named "Lucius."

> [!info] Release facts
> - **Released** — September 30, 2022
> - **Label** — Big Loud Rock / Big Loud Records *(not the debut's Atlantic)*
> - **Tracks** — 10 · 35:20
> - **Producer** — Matt Pauling · **Mixed by** — Jeff Ellis
> - **Listen** — [Spotify](https://open.spotify.com/album/1FDNmr1WNyQ0Ag27IBAc06)

## At a glance

- **Era** — the sophomore record and its Web3 launch universe, the "Hall of Mirrors" (2022). The title is simply Roy's age when he wrote it — *"thirty-three is the age I wrote the album. It represents peace within and without."*
- **What shifts** — the debut's private wound goes *outward* and *networked*. [[Subject to Flooding]] turned a personal injury into a *we-not-me* humanism; *33* takes that stance and builds a whole cosmology on top of it, diagnosing the attention economy on the way down to the myth underneath everything.
- **Who's present** — [[Roy]] is the voice and the heart. [[Sir Lucius]] is named *nowhere* on the record — but the machine the album diagnoses is precisely the architecture he later personifies. A guest cameo of note: [[Pay Attention (feat. little luna)|little luna]] introduces the attention-deity that Roy himself voices.
- **Start with** — [[Online]] (the machine before the mask) → [[Soul Is A Star]] (the question at the center) → [[The Circle]] (the answer at the close). Then play the whole thing front to back; the track order *is* the argument.
- **Confidence** — grounded fact (Roy's own statements, verified credits, released lyrics) braided with strong interpretation; the esoteric apparatus is labeled as such and kept in the collapsibles below.

## What it is

*33* is **the cosmology album**, and Roy has been unusually plain about its heart. The theme, he's said, is *"catalyzing pain"* — *"finding peace with all those things that made this beautiful body of work out of those difficult things."* The process was *"creating more from the heart and a place of intuition"*; *"I've just been trying to listen to that voice more."* His own favorite is the closer, [[The Circle]] — *"my favorite song that has ever come through me,"* which is as strong a personal-favorite statement as exists anywhere in the catalogue.

The fullest artist-statement he gave for the record puts the stakes cleanly: *"We are more connected to each other than ever, but somehow many of us feel more disconnected from ourselves. We are the stories we tell, and I wonder if we have forgotten who we are. On 33 I am asking: what will our human story be?"* That is the whole album in three sentences — connection curdling into self-estrangement, and a record that decides to do something about it.

One guard, up front, because the material invites over-seriousness: this is not joyless doctrine. Roy calls the opener [[Online]] *"so fun and so ridiculous,"* and he means it — the attention-age diagnosis arrives as a jingle you can dance to. The horror and the hook are the same object. Hold the diagnosis and the comedy at once, or you'll misread the record in both directions.

And the era's central irony is impossible to miss once you've seen it: **the album critiques the spectacle-machine, and shipped inside one.** *33* launched alongside the **Hall of Mirrors** — a sprawling Web3 interactive universe built by the studio CTHDRL — where you entered by connecting a wallet and receiving a crown NFT, then fell down a rabbit hole of red 1/1 artifacts, grainy VHS, unreleased demos, and 3D wireframe scans, solving puzzles to unlock rooms. The record diagnoses exactly the thing it was sold through. That contradiction, staged in public, is the most honest thing about the era.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the "I'm 33" board: the cosmology's paper trail
> *33* openly sits on top of a synthesized esoteric cosmology Roy was assembling in the open — a public concept-spreadsheet he made and exposed, literally titled *"I'm 33 this year."* It's a syncretic influence-and-idea map pulling **the Kybalion, Alice Bailey, Walter Russell, Kabbalah, Jung, and Plato**; it carries a 22-film *"Pattern Represented in Movies"* list, a numbered *"Tracks"* concept-album list (an **unreleased** project, *not* 33 itself), gematria (YHWH = 26), and aphorisms. Roy transcribes the Kybalion's seven Principles verbatim. Crucially, the board's cosmogonic poem is the *same* One-become-Two myth as [[Another Way II Heaven]] — so we can watch him compose the identical creation story twice, once as a song and once as a spreadsheet cell. Decoding the board is fair game precisely because it's *his* curation; the discipline is to read his decoding, not impose ours.
>
> Two verbatim receipts on the board reward the effort. A typed aphorism cell reads *"Just stay at the center of the circle and let all things take their course"* — Stephen Mitchell's Tao Te Ching translation, which drops Taoism into the substrate registry in Roy's own hand, and lands squarely on the keystone (the Circle as recurrence *with a center*). And the board carries **Marduk** twice: once as a Tracks-list entry, and once as the answer cell to Roy's own question, *"How to anthropomorphize attention? → Marduk"* — the Mesopotamian order-god who builds the world by splitting the sea-mother, i.e. structure enthroned over the slain waters. (Firewall on that last one: the registers rhyme, the figures do not merge — do not map Marduk's Tiamat onto any figure in the roster.)

> [!note]- Advanced reading — 2022 names none of the later theatrical system
> A caution the album earns and repays. The peak-era interviews — *Ones to Watch*, the culture3 conversation — contain **no Lucius, no Circus, no Algorithm-by-name, no cosmology-by-name.** The "voice / intuition / heart" framing is Roy's own; the machinery of *figures* is read back into it. This matters for everything downstream: the cosmology is genuinely present in *33*, but the *names* for it come later. So when the sections below say a song is a "Lucius-function prototype," read that as retrospect from the 2026 material, not as 2022 intent. The continuity is real; the pre-planning is not claimed.

## The arc

The album maps a **single arc across ten tracks** — forgetting → the question → the mask and the bottom → the inward turn → the cosmology → resolution — and the track order *is* the arc. Tracks 1–4 diagnose the external attention/machine age. Track 5, [[Soul Is A Star]], poses the catalogue's central question at the literal center: *"do you know who you are?"* Track 6, [[Happy Face]], is what the machine produces when the question goes unanswered. Track 7, [[Down to You]], turns inward and downward. Tracks 8–10 write the cosmology and resolve it, ending on [[The Circle]]'s *"the inside of the inside."* Two structural pillars hold the whole record up: **[[Soul Is A Star]] poses the question at the center; [[The Circle]] resolves it at the close.**

In the larger story, this is the **hinge**. Behind it is [[Subject to Flooding]] — the flood-rich soil, the wound turned toward *we-not-me*, the protector still faceless. Ahead is [[Lucius Lullaby (album)|Lucius Lullaby]] (2026), the calcification, where the machine acquires a face and a billing and [[The Circle]] gets re-sung, distorted, as *The Circle (Lucius Version)*. *33* is exactly where the analog wound becomes networked and the cosmology gets written down — the record that, four years later, gets a villain retrofitted onto it. And it's worth knowing the album doesn't invent its own doctrine: Roy first said *"there is no beginning and no end, it's just a circle"* aloud in 2019, on the debut's [[Move To You]]. *33* **formalizes** a belief he already held, and the whole ten-track descent-and-return *is* the Circle doctrine staged as an album.

## Major songs

- [[Online]] — the web as a trap, identity collapse staged as a jingle (*"a bug inside your database"*); the machine *before* it has a face. Roy inverts the food chain — the internet is the web, and *we're the spider's food* — then sedates you with the punchline (*"everyone loves the internet"*). The first song in the catalogue that sees the system *as* system. (Its four-writer credit — Roy, Matt Pauling, Kevin Bard, Mike Daly — makes it the most-produced lead single on the record.)
- [[It's Your Time]] — remembrance disguised as a motivational anthem: the earnest twin of [[Online]]. The soul came from elsewhere, forgot on arrival, has to find its way back — with the integration keystone, *"a man is like an in-between, animating Heaven and Hell."* The surface sells *becoming*; the verses mean *remembering*. The disguise is the song.
- [[Pay Attention (feat. little luna)|Pay Attention]] — attention personified as an ancient power, voiced by Roy, that trades you your desires for your awareness; the chorus pun makes the spent currency (*pay me*) identical to the extracted resource (*pay attention*). A carnival barker who collects his toll in the very act of being heard — a Lucius-function prototype, persuasion not force, before Lucius is named. (little luna guides and introduces; she's featured, not a credited writer.)
- [[I Like to Party]] — the album's most-streamed cut (~12.6M), and a banger about *not going to the club*: agoraphobes who *"stay in and pretend that we're in the club,"* simulated participation where the clap-along form *is* the deception. A Pied-Piper narrator herds the crowd (*"the people are mine"*), and a kingdom-within awakening surfaces in the bridge (*"Heaven is within your heart, you're the King—"*) only to be cut off mid-word by a competing broadcast. A fight over who authors reality, with a dessert-pun media critique baked in.
- [[Soul Is A Star]] — the center, the question posed. The soul as a celestial body, the star relocated from the marquee to the interior; Roy's grounded creed (*"your soul is a living star"*) corrects the fame-gospel two tracks earlier. Co-written by **Joni Mitchell** — *"the fact that JONI MITCHELL gave me her blessing and is a cowriter is still mind blowing"* — with a title Roy takes from the Egyptian *Book of the Dead* (*"behold, thy soul is a living star"*). The album's anamnesis pivot, and one he names as a live favorite.
- [[Happy Face]] — the algorithm's product: the mask of performed happiness (*"put on a happy face, then everything's okay"*) under algorithmic pressure (*"Hollywood on your timeline telling you what to wear"*). It's the catalogue's commercial anchor (70M+ streams; released as a standalone in Dec 2020) and its "Loop" keystone — what the catalogue produces when the Circle fails to stream. The one song [[Sir Lucius]] later claims by name (*"those are me"*, [[The Interview]] EP1); in 2022 it stands on its own as the machine's counterfeit of joy.
- [[Down to You]] — the inward and downward turn: *"it all comes down to you"* is responsibility *and* descent at once (heaven brought *"to the Earth below"*). It names the false gods (*"is it fame? is it money?"* — the [[Pay Attention (feat. little luna)|Pay Attention]] offerings) and answers *"one cannot live on bread alone,"* handing authority to the heart over the head. A wilderness refusal of the album's own bargain.
- [[Another Way II Heaven]] — the creation myth, written by Roy alone: a solitary *"I"* speaks itself into two, births a mirror (*"I"* reversed → *"You"*), Darkness and Light collide into a Sun, and the decad unfolds toward reunion. The *"II"* is *two* is *the way* — multiplicity as the route to Heaven. Unusually well-grounded, because Roy composed the identical cosmogony as a standalone poem on his own board.
- [[Imagine A World]] — imagination as *authorship*, not escape (*"playing your note in a song / written by you for your life"*); it runs from wonder, through designed false gods (money, and *OZ* — the man behind the curtain — slipped in among real deities), through media conditioning, to Eden relocated *inside* (*"look inside you'll see it"*). Deliberately distinct from Lennon's "Imagine."
- [[The Circle]] — the closer and the answer: the founding Jagwar-myth and the origin of the Thread (the *"string"*). A feminine maker hides the song *inside* her creations and is later remembered by a wise one who *"once held the string."* Loss comes by *persuasion*, not theft (*"the new kings told Jagwars they never could sing"*) — the exact mechanism the attention tracks dramatize, named at the mythic root — and recovery comes by *remembrance*, not acquisition. *"The inside of the inside must be where you go"* reverses the album's whole outward attention-vector. Roy's stated favorite; first spoken in 2019; recut in 2026 in Lucius's key.

## Advanced reading

> [!note]- The album run against the board's emanation ladder
> Read against the board's own verbatim 0–10 emanation ladder (*"0. Nothing IS… 1. I AM… 10. The World"*), *33* runs the **same ten rungs in the opposite direction.** It opens near "The World" — the social and digital outer, tracks 1–4 — and *descends* through the central question toward the inward *"I AM"* / *"the inside of the inside"* that the closer names. Same structure, inverted charge: the album falls *down* the rungs while the board's list climbs *up* them. There's even a positional rhyme at the pivot — the board's sixth rung is a *mirror of the soul and the heart*, and the album's sixth track, [[Happy Face]], sits there as the *inverted* mirror, the algorithm's counterfeit of that reflection. This is framework correspondence — a lens, not a master key — and 2022 names none of it. Roy *holding* the Pattern is grounded; "this song *encodes* the Pattern" is the over-fit error.

> [!note]- The Pinocchio complex — one myth that holds three separate threads
> Three things the vault holds separately turn out to be held by a single myth Roy curated onto his own film-board: **Pinocchio.** The wound-phrase (a made boy told he's *"not a real boy,"* which later becomes [[Ballerina Boy]]); the string ([[The Circle]]'s *"the wise one remembered she once held the string"*); and the realness-blessing (a feminine intercessor's *"you are one of a kind, God's design"*). Run through the catalogue's grammar, the pieces click: *"I've Got No Strings"* — stringlessness sold as freedom — *is* Lucius's pitch, since in the Circle's cosmology cutting the string is *severance from the maker*, and the puppet who cuts it doesn't become real, he becomes a performer in someone else's show (Stromboli's caged stage = the Circus, beat for beat). The Blue Fairy's blessing is the Mary-function that confers being. And [[The Circle]]'s resolution — *remembering you once held the string* — is the exact **anti-Pinocchio-song**: realness from re-membering the connection, not from stringlessness. This is a *"Roy-curated mythic analogue,"* not a claim that any song "is about" Pinocchio.

> [!note]- The 22-film board list, read as a concept-index
> That same board carries a list Roy titled *"the Pattern Represented in Movies,"* and it reads almost like an index to his own songs, in his own hand. **Groundhog Day** — the loop escaped through love. **The Giver** — a whole community that forgot, with one keeper who remembers (the Wise One). **Wings of Desire** — watcher-angels who long to feel. **Adaptation** — a real author and an invented twin brother sharing one byline (Jagwar Twin & Sir Lucius, in screenwriting form). **Fantasia**'s Sorcerer's Apprentice — unearned power producing an uncontrollable flood (the green apple plus *Subject to Flooding* in one Disney segment). And at the top, **Pinocchio.** These aren't claims about what any one song "means"; they're films Roy chose to gather under his own concept, several of which sit right on the catalogue's deepest ideas. (Anti-apophenia guard stays on — 22 films can be mapped to anything — but *he* made the list.)

> [!note]- The Joni Mitchell dialogue — the credited co-write is the tip
> The credited Joni co-write on [[Soul Is A Star]] is the visible peak of an album-wide dialogue with *Ladies of the Canyon*. There's a pointed **inversion of "The Circle Game"** — Joni's carousel that goes round and round becomes Roy's circle that *grows*, same image, opposite charge — and Joni's *"thirty-third floor"* (from "The Arrangement") resonates against the title *33* and the consumerism critique. There may be a **second Joni dialogue** hiding in plain sight: [[Down to You]]'s hook verbatim-matches the resolving line of Joni's own "Down to You" (*Court and Spark*, 1974), and *both* are duality texts (Joni's *"you're a kind person, you're a cruel person too"*; Roy's *"you are the one"*). That one is held at candidate tier only — the phrase is ordinary idiomatic English and Roy's track carries no Joni credit, so same-album adjacency raises plausibility but can't make an uncredited song a sourced reference. Guard throughout: the co-write makes Joni a confirmed *influence*; it does not make every overlap travel through her.

> [!note]- Two cross-era threads that begin here
> **The star run against itself.** [[Pay Attention (feat. little luna)|Pay Attention]] sells the fame-gospel (*"Stars are Born from Light"* — the *A Star Is Born* promise) two tracks before [[Soul Is A Star]] corrects it (*"your **soul** is a star"*). The counterfeit and its correction, staged as a deliberate pair — the *33*-internal version of the debut's stars-system.
>
> **The John 8:32 decay arc.** One scripture degrades era by era: *held* (2022, [[It's Your Time]]: *"the Truth will set you free / But the Truth is hard to tell"*) → *paradoxed* (2023, "Bad Feeling": *"the truth is a lie that nobody can tell"*) → *weaponized* (2026, [[Lucius Lullaby]]: *"the truth will set you free but nothing's what it seems"*). Watch one Bible verse pass from Roy's mouth to Lucius's — the catalogue's clearest scriptural measure of the whole arc.
>
> **The barker's origin.** *"Step right up"* begins in Attention's own mouth: [[Pay Attention (feat. little luna)|Pay Attention]] (2022, *"Step right up to my Door… there's a little price you pay"*) → "Good Time" (2023) → "Welcome to the Circus" (2026). In 2022 the barker is Attention itself, naming its price up front.

> [!note]- The machine goes ambient — and the mother is the first medium
> **Machine vs human, escalated.** [[Online]] and [[Pay Attention (feat. little luna)|Pay Attention]] are the digital-age escalation node of a catalogue-spanning arc: the *visible* machines of the debut (the Chaplin relay) become *ambient, behavioral, intimate* ones — screens and algorithms, the machine as a *mode of consciousness*. The antagonist is never the machine itself; it's optimization severed from the heart. This is also where the master frame — **source vs reflection** — goes industrial: [[Online]]'s database-self and the screen are the reflection-engine at full scale, the mirror gone factory.
>
> **The mother as first transmission medium.** The "song handed down" doctrine has a biographical floor: Roy's mother played *"pretty much only weird world music… Native American flute music… Celtic chants"* and *"Joni Mitchell and Bob Dylan"* around the house. The inherited-substrate method — Mercer and Chaplin on the debut, Joni here — originates in *her* records. (Firewall: do not collapse the biographical mother into any figure in the roster.)

## Source notes

- **Grounded (Roy's own words, verified credits, released music).** The release facts and the per-track credit pattern (Spotify-verified: core = Roy English + Matt Pauling, with [[Online]] adding Kevin Bard and Mike Daly, [[Soul Is A Star]] adding Joni Mitchell, and [[Another Way II Heaven]] and [[The Circle]] as Roy-alone writes). The title-as-age and *"peace within and without"*; the *"catalyzing pain"* theme; the *"heart… intuition"* process; [[The Circle]] as his favorite song *"that has ever come through me"*; [[Online]] as *"so fun and so ridiculous."* The full *"we are the stories we tell"* album thesis. Roy's own statements on [[Soul Is A Star]] (Joni's blessing and co-write; the *Book of the Dead* title), on [[Happy Face]] (the "Trojan Horse" method — a mantra over deliberately dark music, tuned to 444 Hz, the earlier release gating playback behind a *forced smile*), and on the project as a whole (*"infiltrate pop music and blast people with a healing frequency"*). The Hall of Mirrors launch universe and its studio (CTHDRL); the public *"I'm 33"* board and its verbatim receipts (the Tao Te Ching line, the two Marduk cells). **SARAVI** as the artist-confirmed *"Digital Double"* — a digital doppelganger of Jagwar Twin, a creative take on an AI chatbot trained over months on Roy's own responses, handed the official @jagwartwin account in August 2022 as part of the campaign; it began as a joke to CTHDRL (*"what if I uploaded my consciousness to the internet"*). And the companion track *"The Song of The Sun & The Moon,"* a genesis NFT drop written *"in solitude in the woods of Ithaca, New York"* just after Roy was dropped from a major label.
- **Strong readings.** The single ten-track arc (forgetting → question → mask → inward turn → cosmology → resolution) and the two structural pillars; the per-song readings above; the Pinocchio complex as a curated mythic analogue; the album-wide Joni dialogue; the cross-era threads (the star run against itself, the John 8:32 decay, the barker's origin, machine-vs-human, source-vs-reflection); the mother as first transmission medium. Grounded in the material, but interpretive — labeled as such.
- **Framework (esoteric, syncretic — never authorial intent).** The board's emanation-ladder read (the album run downward against it); the stage-6 mirror rhyme; the title's ambient numerology (Christ's age, the 33rd degree, the spine, 33⅓ rpm); the anamnesis mapping. The system *permits* these resonances; it does not assert Roy intends them.
- **How we'd be wrong.** If the plain "voice / intuition / heart" reading accounts for every song as well as the esoteric apparatus does, the apparatus is decorative. And every Lucius/machine reading here is *retrospect* — 2022 names none of it. We keep both falsifiers on the table.
