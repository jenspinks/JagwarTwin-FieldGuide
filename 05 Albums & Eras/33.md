# 33

> **One-line reading.** The 2022 cosmology album — where the personal wound of the debut turns outward into a diagnosis of the attention age, poses the catalogue's central question at its literal center, and answers it with the founding myth at the close.

*33* is the second Jagwar Twin album, released September 30, 2022. The title is simply Roy's age when he wrote it, which by his own framing *"represents peace within and without."* If [[Subject to Flooding|Subject to Flooding]] turned a personal wound into a *we-not-me* humanism, *33* takes that outward and builds a whole cosmology — and it does all of it *before* any of it is named "Lucius." The album moves from forgetting to remembrance: from an external critique of the machine, down through the question *"do you know who you are?,"* to the myth underneath everything.

## Overview

Roy has been plain about the album's heart. The theme, he's said, is *"catalyzing pain"* — *"finding peace with all those things that made this beautiful body of work out of those difficult things."* He describes the process as *"creating more from the heart and a place of intuition,"* trying to *"listen to that voice more."* His own favorite is the closer, "The Circle" — *"my favorite song that has ever come through me." *And he's careful that the tone isn't joyless: he calls the opener "Online" *"so fun and so ridiculous."* The diagnosis is sharp and playful at once.

One telling fact about how the album arrived in the world: it launched alongside a sprawling interactive web universe of its own — the spectacle operating at the promotional level *while the album critiques exactly that machine.* The record ships inside the thing it diagnoses.

## Major releases

Ten tracks, about 35 minutes, produced by Matt Pauling. The album maps a single arc — forgetting, then the question, then the mask and the bottom, then the inward turn, then the cosmology, then resolution.

- [[Online]] — the web as a trap; identity collapse staged as a jingle. The machine *before* it has a face.
- [[It's Your Time]] — remembrance disguised as a motivational anthem: the soul came from elsewhere, forgot on arrival, has to find its way back.
- [[Pay Attention (feat. little luna)|Pay Attention]] — attention personified as an ancient power that trades you your desires for your awareness.
- [[I Like to Party]] — that same force working a crowd, interrupted at the end by a competing broadcast: *the kingdom is within you*, cut off mid-word.
- [[Soul Is A Star]] — the center, and the question posed: *"do you know who you are?"* Co-written with Joni Mitchell.
- [[Happy Face]] — the album's commercial anchor and the catalogue's "Loop" keystone: the algorithm's product, the mask Lucius later claims as his own.
- [[Down to You]] — the inward and downward turn: *"it all comes down to you,"* responsibility and descending light at once.
- [[Another Way II Heaven]] — the creation myth, written by Roy alone: a solitary *"I"* speaks itself into two and births a mirror.
- [[Imagine A World]] — imagination as authorship rather than escape; Eden relocated inside.
- [[The Circle]] — the closer and the answer: a feminine maker hides the song inside her creations, a wise one *remembers*, and *"the circle will grow / from the inside of the inside."*

"Soul Is A Star" poses the question at the center; "The Circle" resolves it at the close.

## Who's present

This is still a faceless cast. **[[Roy]]** is the voice and the heart. **[[Sir Lucius]]** is not named anywhere on *33* — but the *machine* the album diagnoses is precisely the architecture he later personifies. "Online" and "Happy Face" are the two songs Lucius eventually claims as his (*"those are me"*), and their original videos already wore his visual iconography years before the 2026 billing. *33* is the hinge where the analog wound becomes networked, and where the cosmology finally gets written down.

## What it's about

The album's spine is **remembrance**: a soul that came from somewhere, forgot on arrival, and has to find its way home. That arc *is* the doctrine of [[The Circle]], staged across ten tracks — and it's worth knowing that Roy first spoke that doctrine aloud back in 2019, on the debut's "Move To You" (*"there is no beginning and no end, it's just a circle"*). So *33* doesn't invent the idea; it formalizes one Roy already held.

Two catalogue-spanning threads come to a head here. **Machine vs. human** escalates: the visible machines of the debut's Chaplin relay become the album's *ambient, behavioral, intimate* ones — screens and algorithms, the machine as a mode of consciousness. And the **source-versus-reflection** problem goes industrial: "Online"'s database-self and the screen are the reflection-engine at full scale. The antagonist, across all of it, is never the machine itself — it's optimization severed from the heart.

There's a real artistic dialogue running underneath, too. The Joni Mitchell co-write on "Soul Is A Star" is the visible tip of it: echoes of *Woodstock*, and a pointed inversion of *The Circle Game* — Joni's carousel that goes round and round becomes Roy's circle that *grows*. Same image, opposite charge. The conversation continues two tracks later: Joni's own "Down to You" (1974) resolves on the line *"it all comes down to you,"* which is exactly Roy's hook — and both songs are really about duality (Joni's *"you're a kind person, you're a cruel person too"*; Roy's *"you are the one"*). Roy rotates the idiom, though: the phrase usually means responsibility *lands* on you, and he aims it downward as descent — *"heaven in the sky to the earth below… it all comes down to you."*

The other thread the album runs against itself is the **star**. "Pay Attention" sells the fame-gospel version — *"stars are born from light,"* the *A Star Is Born* promise — two tracks before "Soul Is A Star" corrects it: the star you want isn't in the sky or on a marquee, *"your soul is a star."* The counterfeit and its correction, staged as a pair.

## A framework reading

*33* openly sits on top of a synthesized esoteric cosmology Roy was assembling — a personal board that pulls together Kabbalah, the Kybalion, Jung, Plato, and more, literally titled "I'm 33 this year." Read against it, the album runs like an emanation ladder in reverse: it *opens* out in the social and digital world (the attention machine of the first four tracks) and *descends* through the central question toward the inward *"I AM"* the closer names — the same structure the board climbs, run in the opposite direction. The title itself carries ambient esoteric freight (Christ's age, the 33rd degree, the spine's vertebrae) that the number *carries* whether or not Roy reaches for it. All of this is framework correspondence — a lens, not a master key. The grounded floor stays Roy's own: *the age I wrote the album; peace within and without.*

That same board carries a list Roy titled *"the Pattern Represented in Movies"* — and it reads almost like an index to his own songs, in his own hand. *Groundhog Day* is the loop escaped through love; *The Giver*, a whole community that forgot with one keeper who remembers; *Wings of Desire*, watcher-angels who long to feel; *Adaptation*, a real author and an invented twin brother sharing one byline. And at the top, *Pinocchio* — a made boy told he's *not a real boy* (the exact wound the later "Ballerina Boy" sings), a puppet defined by the strings that tie him to his maker, made real at last by a feminine blessing. These aren't claims about what any one song "means"; they're films Roy chose to gather under his own concept, and several of his catalogue's deepest ideas turn out to be sitting right there on the list.

A caution that the album earns: **2022 names none of the later theatrical system.** The peak-era interviews have no Lucius, no Circus, no Algorithm-by-name. The "voice / intuition / heart" framing is Roy's; the machinery of figures is read back into it. The cosmology is genuinely here — the *names* for it come later.

## Suggested listening order

The album order *is* the arc, so play it front to back. The shortest path through the throughline:

1. [[Online]] — the machine, before the mask
2. [[Soul Is A Star]] — the question at the center
3. [[Happy Face]] — what the machine produces
4. [[Down to You]] — the turn inward and down
5. [[The Circle]] — the answer; the founding myth
