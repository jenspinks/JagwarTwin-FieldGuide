# Family, Friends & Moments

Most of the Hall of Mirrors is trying to explain the universe. This corner is trying to remember a dog.

These are the artifacts where the mythology comes back down to earth: a grandmother's cow, a father's handmade clock, a birthday clip of a much younger man mugging in a studio, a fish named Oscar drawn with a little heart above it. Diagrams map the cosmos. These map a life.

## What this section collects

This page gathers the biographical bedrock: grandparents, parents, childhood objects, pets, friends, and live-show moments, scanned or photographed or sketched and then folded into the maze alongside the Tree of Life and the sigils.

The move is quietly radical. A project this dense with cosmology could have stayed in the clouds forever. Instead it keeps stopping to scan a wooden pull-toy, or a class-of-1941 graduation charm, or a birthday cake's worth of candles. The mythology only earns its weight because it keeps touching a real family, a real house, a real childhood, a real grief.

Reading these is simple, and that is the point. Ask *who, when, what era* before you ask *what does it mean*. The meaning is usually just love, remembered in an unusual medium.

## A note on the codes

Some of these rooms unlock with a phrase (`OSCAR`, `REBIRTH`, `HEIMDALL`). Others unlock with a letter-and-number key (`A749`, `R1063`, `G228`, `B1030`). Those are not unsolved riddles. They are **cell coordinates**: a column-plus-row address on Roy's *"I'm 33"* spreadsheet board, the same board so much of the cosmology is drawn on. So even the plainest family keepsake is quietly pinned back to the map. See [[The I'm 33 Board]].
