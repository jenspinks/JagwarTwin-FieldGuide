# Concept Images & Emblems

Some Hall of Mirrors artifacts are beautiful because they look finished.

These are not those.

These are the stranger, more useful objects: diagrams, emblems, board fragments, code-images, hand-drawn maps, symbolic props, screenshots, scans, and little conceptual grenades that seem to have fallen out of the machinery while the mythology was being built.

This is where the Hall stops whispering and starts drawing on the walls.

## What this section collects

This page gathers artifacts whose image, title, code, or content points directly at a major concept in the Jagwar Twin world. These are not just pictures of things. They are pictures that behave like arguments.

A crown with a snake is not only a crown with a snake. A rabbit at a gate is not only a rabbit at a gate. A handwritten note that says *"the value of Nothing"* is not just a motivational poster for extremely tired mystics.

These artifacts function like visual glossary entries. They help define the project's recurring ideas: attention, the pattern, the empty room, the gate, the world, the body, the senses, the sphere, the star, the flood, Eden, the rose, the sword, the dove, the rabbit, the mirror, and the question of how a self becomes legible without becoming trapped.

## Why these artifacts matter

The songs tell the story emotionally. The characters personify it. The interviews give us language for it. The concept images show the system *thinking*.

Many of these pieces feel like working notes from the mythological engine room: sketches from Roy's *"I'm 33"* board, fragments of symbolic math, handwritten cosmology, diagrams of the person, old personal objects turned into relics, and images where a code doesn't merely unlock the room but explains what the room is trying to say.

They matter because they show the bridge between private pattern and public art. You can see an idea before it becomes a song, before it becomes a character, before it becomes a room, before it becomes something the rest of us start arguing about at 1:00 a.m. like very normal people.

## How to read them

Start with the object itself. What is actually visible? Then the title. Then the concept links.

The order matters. If you begin with the wildest possible interpretation, everything becomes a prophecy and nothing becomes useful. The better question is not *"How mystical can I make this?"* but *"What does this artifact connect, and how strongly?"*

A strong reading usually has several anchors: the image itself, the title, a known Roy statement, a song tie, a repeated symbol, or a matching diagram elsewhere in the Hall. A weak reading has vibes and enthusiasm. Vibes are welcome here, but they are not allowed to drive without a licensed adult in the car.

## The main kinds of concept artifact

**Board fragments.** A large number of these appear to come from Roy's *"I'm 33"* board-world: handwritten math, music diagrams, Tree of Life structures, Happy Birthday contours, Adam/Eve calculations, Platonic ratios, Saturn squares, sensory hierarchies, and other attempts to map reality as pattern. They are not polished album art. They are closer to field notes from the inside of the inside.

**Emblems.** Some artifacts work like icons: the dove, the crown, the sword, the rose cross, the owl, the rabbit, the cube, the gate, the sphere. They condense a concept into one object. When an emblem repeats across songs, tattoos, drawings, videos, and rooms, it becomes part of the visual grammar.

**Personal objects turned symbolic.** Not everything here is a diagram. Some pieces are ordinary objects: a dog, an old CD, a desert house, a childhood image, a cow, a beach photo, a venue clip. The Hall treats these as relics, not because ordinary life is being inflated into mythology, but because the mythology keeps returning to ordinary life. The cosmic map only matters if it eventually touches a body, a family, a place, a memory, a wound, a joke, a dog named Potato Chip. That is not a side note. That is the point.

## Major threads on this page

**Attention becomes a god.** The *Marduk* artifact turns attention into an ancient figure: many-eyed, mythic, enormous, and slightly terrifying. The attention economy is not just a platform mechanic here. It becomes a deity demanding sacrifice, and the song world answers with [[Pay Attention (feat. little luna)]].

**Nothing becomes valuable.** Several artifacts circle emptiness: the empty room, the empty mirror, zero, *the value of Nothing*, absence, invisibility, the strange usefulness of becoming no one. This is not simple nihilism. Emptiness can mean loss, erasure, spiritual clearing, or the open space where something truer can finally appear. The hard part is telling the difference.

**The self becomes a map.** The *Enlightened Being*, *Sense Hierarchy*, body diagrams, and Tree-like sketches treat the human being as layered architecture: body, senses, feeling, thought, will, love, self, universal self. The self is not flat. It has levels, rooms, gates, thresholds, inner instruments, and possibly a very concerning amount of paperwork.

**The world has gates.** The rabbit at the Gate of the World, the *Gated* video, the *Eden Cube*, and related images all return to threshold imagery. A gate is a test of position. Are you outside looking in? Inside trying to leave? At the edge of the world? The Hall loves gates because the whole project is about crossing from performance into recognition, from image into self, from pattern into life.

**The truth has shape.** *"The truth is a sphere"* is one of the cleanest concept statements in the archive: truth is not a flat slogan but a whole object, seen differently depending on where you stand. That idea quietly governs the guide itself. A song, a post, a drawing, and a video can all be different angles on the same thing. None of them alone is the whole sphere. Together, they become harder to dismiss.

**The old self becomes an artifact.** The Eye Alaska CD, old footage, childhood images, and personal-history objects show the Hall preserving earlier versions of the person. These are not merely nostalgic. They are archaeological: the kid, the band, the abandoned path, the old name, the earlier sound, the thing that did not disappear just because a new mythology arrived.

## A note on the "I'm 33" board

Many of these concept images appear to be fragments of the larger thought-system visible in [[The I'm 33 Board]]. That board shows Roy working through music, number, geometry, theology, identity, and pattern as one connected field. Several artifacts here seem to be individual pieces of that board-world minted into the Hall: not separate ideas, but pieces of the same cosmology broken into collectible rooms. The board helps decode the artifacts; the artifacts show the board learning how to become mythology.

## What not to do

Do not treat every image as equally central. Do not treat every code as proof of a grand hidden plot. Do not collapse personal objects into symbols so aggressively that the human disappears. Do not make the map so clever that it stops being true.

The best artifact readings leave the object more visible than they found it. If an interpretation makes the artifact feel less human, less funny, less strange, or less specific, it is probably trying too hard.

## How this page is organized

Each entry below carries the artifact's number, title, unlock code, image or video, a short reading, and links to the strongest related songs or concepts. The links are intentionally selective. A concept image can touch twenty things in theory; the guide only links the connections that help you understand what you are looking at. The goal is not to win the maze. The goal is to notice how it was built.

A dense handwritten page of Roy's esoteric mathematics, effectively a leaf of the *"I'm 33"* board corpus. The code **26** is written on the page three times: the gematria of YHWH (10+5+6+5=26), *"ONE LOVE = YHWH = 26,"* and *"ADAM − KHAWAH = 26"* — which supplies the title. Around that core the page asks *"what is music?"* and answers with Happy Birthday melody contours (*"the greatest Song ever written," "When was I Born?"*), then stacks the tetractys (*"the essence of man"*), a magic square, Plato's *Timaeus* World-Soul lambda, a Fibonacci rabbit tree, and Penrose-flavored consciousness notes — Roy's recurring thesis that number, music, love, and God are one computation (*"Computations of GOD"*).
