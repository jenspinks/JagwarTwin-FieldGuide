### #48 · ROY STORY "SIR RABBIT · `Q763`
<a href="https://storage.googleapis.com/jagwar-twin-33.appspot.com/artifacts/0x2356561424a07a805749ee41a7ad1efe3b2cf7de7056cb2657eac17b04ab8d72..png"><img src="https://storage.googleapis.com/jagwar-twin-33.appspot.com/artifacts/0x2356561424a07a805749ee41a7ad1efe3b2cf7de7056cb2657eac17b04ab8d72..png" width="300"></a>

Four typed pages of the complete "Sir Rabbit" narrative poem arranged around a photograph of a verdigris-bronze leaping hare (a museum-style ancient amulet with an inlaid ring eye); the bottom-right stanza block still shows blue selection handles and an X-ed design frame, so the artifact is a captured layout-in-progress rather than a finished print. The poem is Roy's fullest self-authored initiation myth: Sir Rabbit rides a Cat to the machine Violet Moon, is boxed by the Fox, escapes by listening for "a heartbeat he forgot" ("the key is hidden in yourself"), meets the lamp-bearing Dove, is guided by a golden-caped Spider to the Great Divide, and leaps "straight through the Mourning Sun" back where he began, i.e. heart-over-mind, wake-from-the-dream, and the Circle's return, all pre-named. Roy himself captioned it "sir rabbit pre-lucius" (Discord 2023-10-12): Sir Rabbit is the seeker-self before the Lucius armor forms, and the poem seeds the whole later cast (Fox, Spider-guide, mind/heart split, "it's been mapped"). The code Q763 belongs to the alphanumeric grid-cell family (J763, B1034, H1066) that solver ibby said follows a cell-numbering pattern, and it shares row 763 with J763 (Gate of the World), reading like coordinates in Roy's spreadsheet-board universe rather than a phrase-code. The image also settles two transcription flags: "Mourning Sun" and "a little dot" are confirmed as written.
