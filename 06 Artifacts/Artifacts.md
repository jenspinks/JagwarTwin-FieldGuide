# Artifacts

The [[Hall of Mirrors]] isn't just a place where things were left behind. It's a way to descend deeper into the Jagwar Twin universe and see its foundations.

These are the **Artifacts**: hidden pieces of the Jagwar Twin world, unlocked through codes, rooms, puzzles, and collective fan excavation. Some are drawings. Some are poems. Some are songs, demos, old images, fragments of family history, screenshots, symbols, jokes, glitches, and things that look like jokes until they start behaving suspiciously like keys.

Think of this section as the evidence room, except the evidence sometimes sings.

## What counts as an artifact?

An artifact is a discovered Hall of Mirrors item with a code, title, media file, or gallery entry attached to it. These aren't ordinary screenshots or random reference images. They're pieces that were *placed* inside the Hall of Mirrors system and unlocked by people moving through it.

Some artifacts are obviously lore-heavy. Others are quieter: a childhood image, a sketch, a voice memo, a diagram, a phrase, a bit of visual debris. The trick is not to assume every item is secretly the Rosetta Stone. The trick is to ask a better question:

**What does this object connect?**

That's how this section is organized. Not as ~149 isolated curiosities, but as a set of grouped trails through the work.

## Why the artifacts matter

The artifacts are useful because they let the mythology touch the real world.

The songs give us the emotional arc. The videos give us the theatrical world. The interviews and posts give us language for the symbols. But the artifacts do something slightly different: they show the project leaving behind fragments of its own memory.

A sketch might clarify a symbol. A demo might show an idea before it became polished. A handwritten note might reveal how Roy thinks, in diagrams, numbers, animals, geometry, or jokes. A family image might ground a mythic theme back in ordinary life. A code might connect a room, a song, and a concept that otherwise looked unrelated.

The artifacts aren't the whole story. They're the objects on the table.

## How to read this section

The artifact pages are grouped by type and function, because that's the only sane way to handle a collection this strange without turning the site into a junk drawer with backlinks.

Each group page collects related artifacts, explains what they are, and connects them to songs, characters, symbols, and concepts wherever the connection is strong enough to matter.

The goal is not to force every artifact into canon. Some items are central. Some are supporting evidence. Some are probably just charming debris. All three categories are allowed to exist. Not every banana is a sacrament. Some bananas are just bananas. Others, unfortunately, are not.

## Artifact groups

**[[Artifacts - Drawings and Sketches]]**
Roy's hand-drawn diagrams, portraits, maps, symbolic sketches, cosmology fragments, and the wonderfully suspicious planetary bestiary. This is one of the richest sections, because drawings often show the project *thinking* before it becomes a lyric, video, or character. Here you'll find the visual grammar: eyes, trees, stars, animals, geometry, ladders, bodies, spirals, and diagrams that look like someone tried to explain the universe on a napkin and accidentally made it lore.

**[[Artifacts - Poems, Texts and Social Posts]]**
Poems, diary-like fragments, aphorisms, written notes, and the small number of social-media artifacts that were actually minted into the Hall of Mirrors system. This section is for language that escaped the ordinary release cycle. Some pieces read like side doors into the songs. Others feel like private weather reports from the same symbolic climate: shadow, light, identity, memory, fear, love, performance, and the weird burden of being alive in public.

**[[Artifacts - Songs, Demos and Mints]]**
Audio artifacts, demos, lost-song fragments, early-band material, voice memos, and MINT-room song releases. These are the artifacts that let you *hear* the work in motion. They can show an idea before it became official, a melody before it found its final home, or a theme that keeps returning in different costumes. Where possible, they're connected back to released songs and era pages.

**[[Artifacts - Family, Friends and Moments]]**
Family images, childhood traces, pets, live moments, personal-history fragments, and other artifacts that bring the myth back down to earth. This section matters because Jagwar Twin's world isn't only made from symbols. It's also made from a life. The mythology gets powerful when the cosmic material and the ordinary material sit close together: the father, the child, the room, the song, the bruise, the joke, the object someone kept.

**[[Artifacts - Ciphers and Code-Series]]**
Flood glyphs, numeric IDs, the SA-RA-VI upload-links, body-part codes, gate chains, and other artifacts that behave like *systems* rather than standalone images. This is the puzzle-brain section. It's also the section most likely to tempt overreach, so the rule here is simple: a code has to *connect* more than it *decorates*. Repeated numbers, symbols, or glyphs are interesting; a demonstrated relationship between code, concept, room, and song is much more interesting.

**[[Artifacts - Concept Images and Emblems]]**
Images, clips, and objects where the code, title, or content directly overlaps with a major concept in the Jagwar Twin world. These are the artifacts that function almost like illustrated glossary entries. Some point at [[The Circle]], [[The Mirror|the mirror]], [[The Pattern|the pattern]], the body, the star, the flood, the eye, or the tree. When an artifact isn't just "an image of a thing" but "the concept made visible," it belongs here.

## The "I'm 33" board

One of the most important related objects is Roy's *I'm 33* board, which is handled separately on **[[The I'm 33 Board]]**.

That board isn't just another artifact list. It's more like a working diagram of the symbolic engine: music, geometry, identity, birth, pattern, planets, numbers, and the question of where the self enters the song.

Many Hall of Mirrors drawing-artifacts appear to be individual pieces of that larger board-world: Eye of Horus fractions, Platonic diagrams, Tree-thinking, the Abyss, lightning-flash geometry, Adamic imagery, and other visual fragments that make far more sense read alongside the board. So the relationship works both ways:

- The **board** helps decode the artifacts.
- The **artifacts** show the board's cosmology broken into collectible pieces.

## A note on confidence

This section uses the same interpretive discipline as the rest of the guide.

If an artifact is directly tied to a code, title, room, song, or public source, that connection is stated plainly. If the connection is a strong reading, it's marked as a reading. If it's interesting but uncertain, it stays tentative.

The Hall of Mirrors rewards curiosity, but curiosity isn't the same thing as proof. A good artifact reading should make the map *clearer*, not just make the wall more crowded.

## The useful question

When looking at any artifact, start here:

> **What does this object help us see that the songs alone do not?**

Sometimes the answer is a symbol. Sometimes a character. Sometimes a song connection. Sometimes it's simply *tone*: a reminder that this whole elaborate mythos is also funny, handmade, emotional, unfinished, and human.

The artifacts are where the Hall of Mirrors stops being only a metaphor and becomes a room full of things someone left for us to find.

*Please do not lick the artifacts.*
