# Songs

Readings of individual Jagwar Twin songs, grouped by the album or era each belongs to.

## Subject to Flooding (2018)

The debut — a personal wound turned into a *we-not-me* humanism, seeded with everything that comes later.

- [[Long Time Coming]] — the album opener.
- [[Dream - Dream]] — built on a borrowed 1944 standard; the dream as a recurring device.
- [[Loser]] — the breakthrough single, and the lens Roy named for viewing the whole world.
- [[Good Day]] — track 4.
- [[Superhuman (Interlude)]] — a ~22-second spoken intro lifted from Chaplin's *The Great Dictator*.
- [[Superhuman]] — the song the interlude opens into.
- [[Move To You]] — Roy's stated favorite on the album.
- [[No Fake Friends]] — styled *N.F.F.*; the loyalty theme the later work returns to.
- [[Roy Voice Memo - S1 Arrowhead]] — a spoken interlude, a captured conversation left on the record.
- [[Hell Of A Night]] — track 10, leading straight into its second half.
- [[Hell of a Night Pt. 2 (The Hallelujah Stickup)]] — the explosive escalation of the song before it.
- [[Precious Time]] — the penultimate track, before the closer.
- [[Shine]] — the album closer.

## 33 (2022)

The cosmology album — the personal wound turned outward into a diagnosis of the attention age.

- [[Online]] — the opener; the diagnosis at its most fun and ridiculous.
- [[Another Way II Heaven]] — an alternate route out.
- [[Down to You]] — track from the cosmology set.
- [[Happy Face]] — the performed smile, examined.
- [[I Like to Party]] — the spectacle from the inside.
- [[Imagine A World]] — the penultimate track; the world re-pictured.
- [[It's Your Time]] — the call to the listener.
- [[Pay Attention (feat. little luna)]] — attention named directly, with little luna.
- [[Soul Is A Star]] — the center track.
- [[The Circle]] — the album closer; Roy's favorite song that ever came through him, and the founding myth.

## Lucius Lullaby (2026)

The shadow album — a sustained descent where Sir Lucius gets the microphone, credited jointly to Jagwar Twin and Lucius.

- [[The Circle (Lucius Version)]] — the music-box overture that runs straight into the title track.
- [[Lucius Lullaby]] — track 2; the counterfeit rescue-voice (not to be confused with the album of the same name).
- [[Great Time to Be Human]] — track 3; the Chemist who *"made a god"* by error.
- [[Welcome to the Circus]] — Lucius at full external power; the song that breaks the world open.
- [[Bad Feeling (Oompa Loompa)]] — the *Wonka*-timed single, folded into the record.
- [[SOL]] — sun and soul; the light at the heart of the dark.
- [[Playing to the Gods]] — the refrain that "City of Angels" develops.
- [[City of Angels]] — the full track that "Playing to the Gods" opens.
- [[Moths to the Flame]] — the pull toward the light that burns, running seamlessly into "Bananas."
- [[Bananas]] — the dark-nursery turn; *go bananas.*
- [[Bounce]] — the spring back, inside the descent.
- [[No. 1 Champion (la Conquistadora)]] — track 8; Lucius appropriating Mary's voice to claim a championship.
- [[Not Your Homie]] — the pre-album single; the disavowal.
- [[Weirdness]] — track 13; the one late turn into first light, the pleading mode underneath.

## Singles & EPs

The standalone releases between the albums — where, more than once, the most important developments happened off-album.

- [[All My Friends]] — standalone single, 2023.
- [[Good Time (1-08)]] — the single whose video assembles the full Lucius visual vocabulary years before the name.
- [[Life Is Good]] — a collaboration with the Hume AI artist angelbaby.
- [[LOOP]] — the single that builds out the Loop concept in public.
- [[MAGIK]] — the *MAGIK* EP that preceded the *Lucius Lullaby* album.
- [[SideQuest]] — the *SideQuest* EP, 2025.
- [[The Watchers]] — a single / three-song EP, pointing at surveillance.
- [[Ballerina Boy]] — the buried-light figure given a song.
- [[dumbledore]] — a *MAGIK*-era release with a "Dark Arts Version."
- [[tomorrow (ANGLE OF ETERNITY)]] — a collaboration with lovelytheband.
