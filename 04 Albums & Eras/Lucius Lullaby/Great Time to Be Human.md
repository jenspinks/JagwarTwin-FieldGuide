# Great Time to Be Human

> **One-line reading.** The album's toxic-positivity anthem: [[Sir Lucius]] stages human history as a children's sing-along, the verses telling nursery-rhyme fables of grief and violence while the refrain papers every one of them over with rainbows and forced gratitude. The cruelty is in the cheer.

> **▶ Listen:** [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TpQGUpTZlJU)

This is one of the oldest songs the album owns and, arguably, its clearest thesis statement in miniature. It arrives early in *Lucius Lullaby* as the [[Happy Face]] mask scaled from a single face to an entire species — a whole civilization agreeing to grin. It sits between the song that *confesses* the lie and the song that *instructs* the mask, and shows the mask become a group activity.

## At a glance

- **Era / release** — First a **single** (April 14, 2023, out in the UK the same day), the centerpiece of Roy's spring-2023 US tour with Transviolet; then **reissued as track 3** of *[[Lucius Lullaby (album)|Lucius Lullaby]]* (2026). The 2023 framing was purely humanist; the album is where it grows fangs.
- **In one line** — A happy chorus that *is* the dark thing: grotesque bedtime fables answered, every time, by rainbows and *"what a great time to be human!"*
- **Who speaks** — Both [[Roy]] and [[Sir Lucius]] are on the credit. The best reading is Lucius staging Roy's lament as a children's spectacle: the content knows it's horrifying, the form refuses to stop smiling. [[The Crowd]] appears as the synchronized sing-along the whole song is built to trap.
- **Key symbols** — [[The Empty Mirror]] · [[Inversion]] (the dog/god flip) · [[The Mask]] · [[The Circus]] · [[The Algorithm]]

## The song, mapped

It would be easy to call this a dark song with a happy chorus. It is exactly the reverse. The engine runs the same four moves on repeat: *state a cheerful frame, narrate an atrocity in nursery-rhyme meter, fire a children's-show reaction-button at the wrong emotional beat, and answer all of it with the same rainbows-and-bliss refrain.* Read as a **diptych** — a boy's fable, then a girl's — each answered by the identical chorus. Here it is, move by move.

| Beat | In the song | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **Cold open** | *"What a great time to be human!"* | The title-hook stated flat, before any story — the caption arrives *before* the horror it will caption. |
| **Boy, part 1** | *"There once was a boy who lost his own Mother / loved him to death and smothered his brother (oops!)"* | Grief and fratricide in a bedtime cadence; the kids in the street *"danced to destroy (yay!)."* Ecstatic ruin, buttoned by cartoon cues. |
| **Boy, part 2** | *"the boy became Man then ran to his Lover / she cast a spell to bring back his Mother"* | Maturation *into repetition* — adult love conscripted to re-animate the dead parent. Then the preacher *"banged a gong,"* and the hinge line lands: *"the crooks and believers all sing the same song."* |
| **Refrain** | *"rainbows, butterflies / what is all this? / Shanghai Summertime / follow your bliss / everything on the earth keeps blooming / what a great time to be human!"* | The erasure. Not grief — anesthesia. Fired straight after fratricide and back again after every wound. (A short *"Hey hey hey / what a great time to be human!"* tag also recurs.) |
| **Girl, part 1** | *"here once was a girl who drank from The Fountain / she left her youth halfway up The Mountain"* | Youth lost, sought, and *punished*: she goes back for it, *"Someone was there,"* and *"they had her committed and shaved off her hair."* Institutional violence. |
| **Girl, part 2** | *"they threw her a party with diamonds and spoons / but They were bloodsuckers with crocodile shoes (boo hoo)"* | Predator-consumers, death worn as status — and then the album's sharpest image, the dog/god botched creation (below). |
| **Coda** | *"Shanghai Summertime / Goodbye / what a great time to be human!"* | The lone exit-word — *"Goodbye"* — appears once and is instantly swallowed back into the hook. Even leaving is reabsorbed. |

Two structural notes worth flagging. First, the fables are **parallel in form but asymmetric in fate**: the *boy matures into repetition* (the mother-wound repeated through the Lover), the *girl is dispossessed* (her youth stolen, then punished for wanting it back). Masculine becoming as repeated trauma; feminine becoming as theft and punishment. The diptych quietly catalogues a set of *failed systems* — kinship and religion in the boy's half (Mother, brother, Lover, preacher), then youth, medicine, and creation in the girl's (the Fountain, the Mountain, the institution, the Chemist, the god). Second, those proper nouns are not transcription noise — **Mother, Man, Lover, The Fountain, The Mountain, Someone, They, The Chemist** are capitalized as official text (verified in-hand against Apple Music). They're *stations*, not people. The song is a miniature anthropology.

## What it means

A few claims that cut across the whole pageant:

- **The refrain is the violence.** The cruelty isn't in the events — a brother smothered, a girl committed and shaved, bloodsuckers in crocodile shoes. It's in the smile forced over them. The ideology insists you stay grateful *while it shows you the wreckage.* Everything else in the song serves this one inversion.
- **The album's masking arc runs through it.** [[Lucius Lullaby]] *confesses* the lie (*"it's all just a lie"*); [[Happy Face]] *instructs* the mask (put it on); this song shows the mask become a **group activity** — the [[Happy Face]] scaled from one face to a whole species agreeing to grin.
- **"The crooks and believers all sing the same song."** Crooks and believers are supposed to be opposites; the whole moral order depends on the difference, and the spectacle dissolves it. Under the cheer, the liar and the faithful function identically, because what matters is not what you *mean* but that you're singing along. This one line is the seed of the vault's whole [[Communion vs Spectacle]] distinction (more below) — the single sharpest place that concept touches the ground anywhere in the catalogue.
- **Growth is not healing.** *"Everything keeps blooming"* whether you heal or not — life continues regardless of suffering, beautiful and brutally indifferent. And when the lone exit finally appears (*"Goodbye"*), the chorus swallows it back into the hook. You can read that swallowing two ways — as *"Goodbye"* being a real exit the pageant then reabsorbs, or as proof there was never an exit to begin with — and the song won't decide for you. Either way, the cheer gets the last word.
- **Nobody in particular is cheering — so everybody is.** A mechanical count of the lyric returns *I = 0, you = 2*: the song has no first-person "I"-engine at all, a collective/ironic voice with no one behind it (the sharp contrast being the album's "I"-driven tracks like [[#1 Champion (la Conquistadora)]]). The absent "I" is the point. The cheer belongs to no one and to everyone. (More in *Advanced reading*.)

## The god made by mistake

The album's whole mirror-logic condensed into a joke: *"she went to The Chemist to make her a dog / but he was dyslexic, so he made a god / god, god, god (woof!)."* She asks for a **dog** — a companion, creaturely love, something humble to be loved *by* — and the broken maker hands her a **god** instead: abstraction, authority, worship. And he does it *by error*, a typo, a dyslexic's slip. Divinity manufactured without understanding.

The genius is the *"(woof!)."* The made-god still **barks**. The dog is trapped inside the god, and the reversal collapses in both directions at once — dog becomes god, and god is exposed as dog. It's a perfect emblem of the album's counterfeit divinity, the maker without wisdom: kin to [[Sir Lucius|Lucius]] as false light, to [[City of Angels]]'s false immortality, to Lucius counterfeiting [[Mary]]. A people that misreads its own need for creaturely love and builds an idol instead. It is also, unmistakably, the funniest line on the record — which is exactly why it lands. A god-making cosmology delivered as a spelling mistake, and the whole theology folded into one syllable of barking.

## Characters

- **[[Sir Lucius]]** — the grinning emcee staging the pageant; co-credited vocalist (*"sir lucius"*). In the theatrical *[[The Interview]]* series he yells this song's dog/god line at a bound Roy, offering it as *"a little something for you — a gift"* that he *"took a few liberties with."* The relentless positivity is his register — but *don't flatten the song to pure Lucius* (it's too grief-conscious for that; see below). (Housekeeping: his yelled lines aren't synced to the playing track, and the *"another song about ME"* he says right after belongs to [[#1 Champion (la Conquistadora)]], not this one.)
- **[[Roy]]** — the lament *inside* the grin; the grief the form refuses to let stop the smile. Co-credited vocalist (*"ROY"*), and — at author level — credited separately again as writer, producer, and player. The song is the argument between the two of them, performed as a sing-along.
- **[[Mary]]** — present only as her *counterfeit*. The spell-casting Lover is the album's false feminine, the partner asked to resurrect the past rather than restore the man to wholeness — the counterfeit that re-animates the wound where Mary would heal it. (Mary is not literally in the text.)
- **[[The Crowd]]** — the many voices forced into the one song; not awakened, synchronized. The sing-along the whole song is built to trap.
- **[[The Chemist]]** — the maker-without-wisdom who makes a god by error; a capitalized station, readable as failed alchemist, scientist, [[Gnosticism - The World as Trap|Demiurge]], and producer all at once (which mask stays open).
- **The boy and the girl** — the two fable-protagonists, best read as Everyman *stations*. The boy carries a possible Cain/Abel shadow (fratricide in the opening breath); the girl a fountain-of-youth / institutionalized-woman shape (both held as framework; see below).

## Symbols

- **[[The Empty Mirror]]** — the operation of the whole song. Roy wears a tattoo by that name (*"looking at yourself and seeing nothing"*), and this is that operation set to music: the cheer reflects back only the *desire* — rainbows, bliss — while the *reality* of the fables goes entirely un-reflected. Look into the song's smile and see nothing of what just happened.
- **[[Inversion]]** — the dog/god reversal made literal; god becomes dog and dog becomes god in the same breath. The vault's Reversal operation put on stage (kin to *Rorrim Ehtni*, the mirror-writing).
- **[[The Mask]]** — [[Happy Face]] scaled to a civilization; the species-wide grin.
- **[[The Circus]]** — the machine the whole pageant runs inside; the 2026 visualizer stages the song there and puts a *"PROPERTY OF B.M.R.C."* rocket launcher on screen — [[The Circus|BMRC]] being the industry-Babylon that manufactures both the show *and* the ordnance.
- **[[The Algorithm]]** — the ad-libs as emotional-flattening buttons; the ironic pop surface as algorithmically optimized (more in *Advanced reading*).

## Advanced reading

> [!note]- Communion vs Spectacle — why the hinge line matters *(tap to open)*
> The catalogue otherwise treats collective singing as *sacred* — the [[The Circle|Circle]], the crowd singing it back. This song forces the counter-case, which the vault holds as [[Communion vs Spectacle]]: **communion is many distinct voices singing the truth together; spectacle is many voices forced into the same song.** The crowd here is not awakened. It is synchronized. *A unison is not a communion.* As a Lucius-voiced track it stages the **commanded/harvested echo** — the crowd made to sing one song — against the Roy-voiced version where the crowd sings *with*. Read in a Babel key it darkens further: a crowd taught to sing one song is the Babel crowd (*"one language and the same words"*), whose scriptural fate is collapse — *"the crooks and believers all sing the same song"* is that forced unison stated in a lyric. This stands on the public material alone. It is the single highest-value in-lyric anchor for the whole sing-back thread in the corpus.

> [!note]- The dog/god line as failed alchemy and the Demiurgic typo *(tap to open)*
> Read through the vault's [[Inversion]] operation, the dog/god flip is the same machinery as *Rorrim Ehtni* (mirror-writing) and the Empty Mirror — a reversal that runs both ways at once. As **failed alchemy**, it's elevation with no soul in it: the Chemist performs the great work of making and produces a botched result *because he doesn't understand what he's making.* In a Gnostic key (see [[Gnosticism - The World as Trap]]) it's the **Demiurge as a clerical error** — the ignorant lower maker who mistakes himself for the source, here reduced to a dyslexic's slip. Framing god-making as a *typo* mocks the whole idea of divine intention; this song is one of the vault's named instances of it. Held honestly, though: the dog/god *text* is grounded, the Demiurge reading is a framework correspondence. dog↔god is among the oldest reversals in English, and *"dyslexic, so he made a god"* is first of all a wordplay gag — building a botched-Creator cosmology on it is real, but the weight the reading carries outruns the line. Mark it and hold it lightly.

> [!note]- The ad-libs, "Shanghai Summertime," and the anesthetic surface *(tap to open)*
> The ad-libs enforce the whole design. *"Oops! / yay! / boo hoo / woof!"* are children's-show emotional cues fired at exactly the wrong moments — a brother is smothered and the song says *oops*; kids dance to destroy and it says *yay*; bloodsuckers turn up in crocodile shoes and it says *boo hoo*. They're reaction-buttons, the sound of horror being processed into content, and content needing applause. Call it the algorithm laughing at the boy who lost his mother — the ironic pop-affect surface as algorithmically optimized. As **framework correspondences** (not claims about intent): *"follow your bliss"* is Joseph Campbell soured into bumper-sticker anesthesia — the target is bliss-as-slogan, not Campbell — part of the album's method of borrowing familiar phrases and turning them (cf. *"put on a happy face,"* *"all your dreams are coming true"*); and *"Shanghai Summertime"* reads as *shanghaied*, a sunny image hiding coercion (kidnapped aboard a ship), rhyming with the album's captivity logic (*"you gotta serve somebody,"* the [[The Interview|EP6]] kidnapping). It could be only exotic scenery — the spectacle operation is identical either way.

> [!note]- The visualizers — a 2023 one, and the 2026 comic tour de force *(tap to open)*
> There are, it seems, **two** official visualizers. The first went up the day the single dropped — Roy's 4/14/2023 release post says *"Watch the Visualizer now on YouTube!"* — so the song had an official 2023 video three years before the album absorbed it. The one analyzed below is the **2026, LL-era** visualizer (credited *"Jagwar Twin, sir lucius,"* dated to Roy's birthday via a *"Happy birthday El Roy"* comment), choreographed to specific lines. Whether these are two distinct videos with distinct staging, or the same footage re-released, isn't settled here — worth flagging so a reader doesn't assume the barefoot/spoon-scepter staging goes all the way back to 2023.
>
> And it's a comic tour de force. Lucius goes **barefoot** on *"new shoes on their feet they danced to destroy"* — stripping off the exact consumer-uniform the lyric indicts, and staging a Moses / *"take off your shoes, holy ground"* inversion as false-sacred right before the gong. He shoulders the *"PROPERTY OF B.M.R.C."* rocket launcher and **waves goodbye to camera** on the sunny *"Shanghai Summertime"* chorus — the cheerful apocalypse, with BMRC revealed as the weapons-and-war brand. And on the sarcastic *"what a great time to be human"* refrain he brandishes a **spoon as a scepter** and strikes a **gong** — the sacred rung with a kitchen utensil. (Recurring LL-visualizer props are present too: the gong, ice-cream/cigarette, the Anonymous Club hat.) [[The Circus|BMRC]] now appears twice-over across the catalogue — as the Circus's production house (*"Produced In Cooperation With B.M.R.C,"* [[Welcome to the Circus]]) and as this rocket brand — the industry-Babylon that makes both the show and the ordnance. No confirmed expansion of the initials; held open.

> [!note]- Placement, phase, and the pronoun-evacuated voice *(tap to open)*
> Track 3 is the **first non-Lucius-titled slot** on the album — the opening third otherwise being Lucius's own territory ([[The Circle (Lucius Version)]], [[Lucius Lullaby]], [[Welcome to the Circus]]). The record opens *inside* Lucius's turf, then hands off here. It opens the album's long middle movement (tracks 3–12), the stretch where the [[The Circus|Circus]] operates at full power. It sits in the record's [[Nigredo|nigredo]] — the blackening, the loop with the exit closed — which is where most of the album lives (active systems here: Spectacle, Algorithm, Consumption; active symbols: Mirror, Mask). And it is **pronoun-evacuated**: a mechanical count returns *I = 0, you = 2*, no first-person "I"-engine at all, in sharp contrast to the "I"-driven tracks like [[#1 Champion (la Conquistadora)]]. The absent "I" is the point — nobody in particular is cheering, so *everybody* is. See [[Pronoun Audit (I-You)]].

> [!note]- Cain/Abel — the boy-fable as an un-reconciled twin *(tap to open)*
> A structural parallel, not a claim Roy has made: the boy who *"smothered his brother"* flags as a possible **Cain/Abel** figure — fratricide in the opening breath. It matters because the album's core question is whether the twin split (Roy and Lucius) reconciles. [[Jacob and Esau]] are the twins who *reintegrate* — they embrace and weep. Cain and Abel are the twins who *don't* — murder, exile, the mark. Lucius-as-Esau (reconcilable, the hopeful arc) versus Lucius-as-Cain (separation as a permanent condition) are two prognoses for the album's open integration question, and this fratricidal little fable quietly puts the darker one on the table. It survives elsewhere in the vault only as this aside; the falsifier is simple — no Roy statement invokes Cain, structural parallel only.

> [!note]- The collaborators — Josh Dun and a favorite band made flesh *(tap to open)*
> The five-writer credit lists *"Josh Dunn,"* which reads as **Josh Dun**, drummer of Twenty One Pilots — a band Roy has called, in a public interview, his *"favorite band ever"* (*"I love their new album… I think that they're genius"*). (Two housekeeping questions stay open on the credits: how the five-way split is actually apportioned in the ASCAP/BMI registries, and whether streaming platforms carry a *"feat. sir lucius"* tag or leave the character-billing to the credits alone.) Dun plays the drum kit here and again on [[Bad Feeling (Oompa Loompa)]], turning a named favorite into a two-track physical presence on the record — and the two of them **played the song live together in Columbus** in May 2023, after Dun had cut the studio drums. Background vocals include **little luna**, the same featured collaborator from *33*'s [[Pay Attention (feat. little luna)]] — a recurring real person, not a cipher — alongside Julian Bell, Charlotte Egner, Reagan Egner, Tom Peyton, and Sean Van Vleet. Roy English himself plays **melodica and keyboards** on it — the reedy toy-organ wheeze that gives the track its nursery-carousel air; Ryan Spraker rounds it out (bass, keys, programming, background vocals); Jeff Ellis adds recording; Dale Becker masters, as he does every track on the album. (Loosely held: the Roy/Lucius alter-ego structure rhymes with TØP's own "Blurryface," but that's a resonance, not a source.)

## Source notes

- **Grounded (released music, credits, public posts, public interviews).** The two-phase release history (2023 single → 2026 track 3, 3:23) and the spring-2023 Transviolet tour that the single anchored — it played live for the first time the night it dropped, opening the tour in Orange County; the five-writer credit (the album's most-collaborative track) and the dual *"sir lucius" / "ROY"* vocal billing; Josh Dun on drums (here and on *Bad Feeling*, and live in Columbus), little luna and the rest on background vocals, Spraker producing with Roy; the capitalized archetypal *stations* as official text; the dog/god line in the lyric *and* yelled by Lucius in *[[The Interview]]* (EP7); the album's own *"twisted takes on adolescent musical themes for hands that rock the cradle"* self-billing, of which this nursery fable is a named instance; a 2023 official visualizer linked at the single's 4/14/2023 release (*"Watch the Visualizer now on YouTube!"*) and the separate 2026 visualizer with its barefoot beat, spoon-scepter, gong, and *"PROPERTY OF B.M.R.C."* rocket launcher — whether the two are distinct videos is still open; a *Great Time To Be Human* live acoustic video released November 2023. And, from the business side, the song's real marketing life: on a public Discord, a team member grouped it with *Happy Face*, *Good Time*, *I Like to Party*, and *Life Is Good* as the *"similar feel"* cohort meant to steer *Bad Feeling*'s viral audience — grounding the song's commercial, big-team character from the inside.
- **Roy's one direct quote** about the song, from a 2023 feature, is telling for what it *lacks*: *"it's an observation of the human experience. It can be pretty strange, but something about it is so beautiful"* — pure humanism, zero Lucius cosmology, three years before the album reframed it. That same feature makes no mention of Lucius, the Circus, the Algorithm, mirrors, masks, or the mythos at all. He tied the title to the road (*"been seeing all sorts of cool weird stuff in America and that seems fitting"*), used *"what a great time to be human"* as a personal signoff all spring, and deflated the industry-speak about *"promising streams"* with a shrug: *"I don't know what that means… but I'm sure they will lead to the ocean. Or into a moose's mouth."*
- **Strong readings.** The refrain-as-violence; crooks-and-believers → [[Communion vs Spectacle]]; the failed-systems diptych with its masculine-repetition / feminine-dispossession asymmetry; the spell-casting Lover as false feminine; the preacher's gong as ceremony without transformation; *"bloodsuckers with crocodile shoes"* and *"diamonds and spoons"* as predator-and-consumer at once (read broadly — the industry, the *"new kings,"* but not only them); dog/god as counterfeit creation; the empty-mirror cheer; the ad-libs as algorithmic emotional flattening; *"goodbye"* swallowed by the refrain; Lucius staging Roy's lament rather than the song being pure Lucius. Grounded in the material, but interpretive.
- **Framework / resonance (never authorial intent).** The Demiurgic typo (Gnostic); *"follow your bliss"* as soured Campbell; *"Shanghai Summertime"* as *shanghaied*; the nursery-rhyme substrate the album runs across (`#1 Champion`'s *"Mary, Mary,"* *Not Your Homie*'s ring-around-the-rosie, *Bad Feeling*'s Oompa-Loompa, *Lucius Lullaby*'s *"Row, row, row your boat"*); the Cain/Abel twin-frame.
- **How we'd be wrong.** The plainer reading is live and worth keeping on the table: a blackly funny dark-comedy exercise in human awfulness — cheerful tune, horrific lyrics, the irony *is* the joke — in the Tom Lehrer / Randy Newman / *"Pumped Up Kicks"* lineage. This matters *especially* here because it's the album's most collaborative track (five writers), the least likely place for deliberate single-author cosmology; the gendered fable-anthropology and dog/god alchemy are at least as plausibly emergent from a writers' room as encoded. The explicit falsifier: a Roy statement framing the song as gallows-humor-for-the-effect would demote the Communion-vs-Spectacle thesis from the song's center to an overlay. Hold both.
