# Bananas

> **One-line reading.** The album's id-permission chant — [[Sir Lucius|Lucius]] takes a children's-entertainer's voice and uses it to license animal regression, destructive appetite, and dissociated shadow-action. A banana peel under the conscience: funny until you realize the fall is the point.

## At a glance

- **Era / release** — *Lucius Lullaby*, released March 27 2026; credited jointly to **Jagwar Twin & sir lucius**. Track 7, wedged between [[Moths to the Flame]] (6) and [[No. 1 Champion (la Conquistadora)|#1 Champion]] (8). The album's tagline: *"Twisted takes on adolescent musical themes for hands that rock the cradle."*
- **In one line** — a nursery chant that hands a crowd of "kids" permission to break things, get wasted, and burn the house down, then walks them from *person* to *monkey* to a self that no longer remembers its own acts.
- **Who speaks** — [[Sir Lucius|Lucius]] inciting, [[Roy]]'s unleashed id erupting, and — probably — the horror of the split being *witnessed.* Most likely all three at once. The addressee is [[The Crowd|the "kids"]].
- **Key symbols** — [[The Banana]] · [[Fire]] · [[The Mask]] · [[Duality]] · [[The Prop Room#The Monkey|The Monkey]]

Bananas is what [[Moths to the Flame]] makes possible. The spoken-word doctrine of the previous track — *regress to the animal, shrug at the cull* — bleeds seamlessly into this sung id-eruption. Moths is the syllabus; Bananas is the enactment. In the official video the camera pulls back from Lucius's candelabra monologue to reveal him standing atop a roughly ten-foot pile of bananas as the song begins. He has, quite literally, been building to this.

## The song, mapped

It isn't a verse-chorus ballad — it's a playground chant, a call-and-answer built out of a handful of cells that cycle through 2:22. The "count," if it has one, is a question posed *to* kids and answered *for* them. Here is the shape, and what each beat does:

| Cell | Signature phrase | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **Hook** | *"Go Bananas…"* (stuttering into *"Banananananas"*) | The title as playground chant. Opens the track and re-opens each section. The syllable slips out of control as it's sung — the word going bananas while it says the word. |
| **The address** | *"Hey kids! Do you ever wanna break shit?"* → *"Well alright"* (×4) | The engine. A question to a crowd it keeps calling *kids,* answered on their behalf by the adult voice. Not a tantrum expressed — a tantrum **authorized.** |
| **The verse** | *"…ready to blow… nowhere to go"* — *"monkeys feeling funky"* | The id, sealed in. The pressure has nowhere to vent, and the human has already collapsed into the animal (*"monkeys"* — plural). |
| **The arson tag** | *"…light up the stove / And burn it down…"* | The hearth turned to ignition. The stove that should nourish becomes the match. |
| **The moral-split refrain** | *"…fun to be Bad…"* / *"…I always get mad"* | The [[Roy]]/[[Sir Lucius|Lucius]] fracture in kindergarten language (*Good* and *Bad* capitalized, the album's archetype habit). By the third pass the melody melts — *"baaahaahaaaahaaahad"* — the voice enacting the loss of composure it describes. |
| **The address, reprised** | *"Hey kids! (hey)…"* — now with crowd-echo | Same call, now with crowd-echo backing vocals answering back — the record has grown its own crowd. |
| **The dissociation verse** | *"…voices in my mind I don't remember"* → *"Did I do that?"* | The chilling turn. The self split from the part that acts — and disavowing it with a shrug. |
| **The deflation** | melts into *"…maahaaaahaad"* → *"Oh Maaaan!"* | Ends not on resolution but on a cartoon groan. The party doesn't conclude; it just keeps going. |

**The move the structure performs.** Read as an argument, the cells describe a **descent in four stages**: *invitation* (break things, get wasted) → *animalization* (the monkeys feeling funky) → *moral split* (Good/Bad) → *dissociation* (did I do that?). The chant walks the listener down from person, to monkey, to a self that no longer remembers what it did. That staircase is the whole song.

## What it means

**It's built as permission, not expression.** This is the load-bearing read. The song doesn't *have* a tantrum; it *hands one out* — an adult voice, in a sing-song register, asking a crowd it calls "kids" whether they'd like to break things, and answering *well, alright* before they can. In an album billed for "hands that rock the cradle," this is the cradle-rocker teaching the nursery to burn the house down. Not the confessor. The Pied Piper.

**The honest part and the dangerous part sit right next to each other.** *"Fun to be bad"* is not a lie — repression genuinely makes forbidden energy feel alive, and the wound underneath (Roy's real, trapped vitality) is sincere. The danger is what's *missing:* this energy is released with no heart, no memory, no responsibility attached. A private primal scream can heal. A ringmaster teaching children to enjoy the scream cannot. Keep both in view — this is not pure cartoon, and it's not pure horror; it's a real wound being *hosted* and *monetized* rather than felt.

**The dissociation is the most chilling turn.** *"…voices in my mind I don't remember"* — then *"did I do that?"* — is the self divided from the part that acts. Integration would say *yes, that was me; what is that part trying to tell me?* Lucius's version stops at the shrug and keeps the party going. (The line even carries a sitcom ghost — more below.)

**The explosion is a symptom of a sealed system.** The id blows precisely *because* the energy is trapped: *"ready to blow, nowhere to go."* And Roy's own public caption for the song reframes the very same word — *"Nowhere is not a destination but you can arrive in an instant."* The song sets Lucius's **trapped** nowhere (a dead end) against Roy's **transcendent** nowhere (a doorway): same syllable, two doctrines. That's what keeps Bananas from being cartoon nihilism. Read in the album's logic — pretend you're fine ([[Happy Face]]) → the body knows ([[Bad Feeling (Oompa Loompa)]]) → the repressed self blows through the mask (Bananas) → the machine sells the blowup back as a show ([[Welcome to the Circus]]) — it is the tantrum a culture manufactures by forbidding honest feeling, then puts on the shelf. (That arc is *thematic,* not the tracklist order — Circus is track 4.)

## Characters

- **[[Sir Lucius|Lucius]]** — the host and inciter; the cradle-rocker handing out permission. He's also the one who *plays* the banana. Across the theatrical "Interview" series he keeps lifting a banana from the set's fruit bowl and pressing it like a play button to cue his own songs — a browning banana becomes [[Not Your Homie]] in one episode, [[Welcome to the Circus]] in another. The green apple is his older mark; this album swaps the apple for the banana.
- **[[Roy]]** — the wound underneath. The genuine fracture — good/bad, the trapped energy — is *his,* and Lucius is exploiting it rather than healing it. Roy's public "nowhere" caption supplies the transcendent counter-reading the song itself never reaches.
- **The speaker (open)** — probably all three at once: Lucius inciting, Roy's id erupting, and the witnessing horror of watching the split happen. Unresolved by design.
- **[[Mary]] / the concealed feminine** *(via the staging)* — the fruit bowl sits on an altar (candles, an Egyptian statue beneath, Tree-of-Life pillars). Read the banana as an *offering* meant for that hidden feminine, which Lucius takes down and reroutes into his PA system. This is a strong read that leans on the theatrical props; no separate Mary *voice* is claimed on the track.
- **[[The Crowd|The "kids"]]** — the addressee the chant keeps naming; the nursery it teaches to burn the house down. On the record they echo *destruction* back (the backing vocals), which places them with the Spectacle crowd, not the communion.

## Symbols

- **[[The Banana]]** — does double duty. It's the **hijacked offering** (the altar gift rerouted into Lucius's mic) *and* the **sterile-clone Loop.** Roy has said publicly that he chose the banana because it's *sterile* — *"most bananas are sterile and cannot reproduce by seed ;)"* — and the botany backs him: the supermarket Cavendish is a sterile clone, genetically identical, unable to reproduce on its own, propagated only by human cutting, now being wiped out worldwide by a single fungus *precisely because* every plant is identical. That's Lucius's Loop made fruit: recurrence without variation, real light in a sterile vessel — *you can hear the song, you cannot inherit it.* It rhymes exactly with [[City of Angels]]'s *"everyone looks just like you."*
- **[[Fire]] / the stove** — the domestic hearth turned to arson (*"light up the stove / and burn it down"*). A recurring stove: [[Happy Face]]'s *"flip the switch, flip the stove."*
- **[[Duality]]** — the Good/Bad, nice/mad split in kindergarten language; the Roy/Lucius fracture *before* it becomes strategy.
- **[[The Prop Room#The Monkey|The Monkey]]** — the id as primate; the human collapsed into the animal (*"monkeys feeling funky"*).
- **[[The Mask]]** — Bananas is the point where the repressed self *blows through* the mask.

- **[[Welcome to the Circus]]** (track 4) — played off the banana in the "Interview" series (*"the algorithm, baby"*); the spectacle that **monetizes** the blowup — the endpoint of the thematic arc.
- **[[Not Your Homie]]** (track 11) — also played off the browning banana (*"it's nonsense, it's all nonsense"*); silly surface, aggressive underneath.
- **[[Happy Face]]** — the recurring stove; childish form carrying grotesque content; the arc's *"pretend you're fine"* starting point.
- **[[Bad Feeling (Oompa Loompa)]]** (track 5) — the *body-knows* step of the arc; Wonka-consumption in nursery form.
- **[[City of Angels]]** (track 10) — *"everyone looks just like you"* rhymes exactly with the sterile-clone banana.

## The comedy — played straight

Bananas is genuinely funny, and the comedy is not a distraction from the horror; it *is* the delivery system. The whole thesis is a slapstick object: the banana is the peel you slip on, *funny until you realize the fall is the point.*

- **The banana-as-microphone gag.** Lucius cues up his own chart-toppers by pressing a banana like a button. A knight in white face-paint, playing his hits off produce, entirely deadpan. The prop is a joke performed with total conviction — which is exactly why it lands as a tell.
- **"Well, alright."** The sing-song shrug is the punchline to *"do you wanna get wasted?"* — a children's-entertainer cadence granting permission for a bender. The comedy and the horror are the *same syllables.*
- **"Did I do that?"** surfacing mid-dissociation — an innocent sitcom catchphrase recoded as a moral shrug (see below).
- **The melodic melt** — *"fun to be baaahaahaaaahaaahad,"* the voice cracking into a bleat, then *"Oh Maaaan!"* to close. The performance loses its composure on cue.
- **"Go bananas"** doing double work — literal madness-slang *and* the fruit, the pun that names the entire id-eruption.
- **Roy's winking `;)`** on the public *"most bananas are sterile"* comment — the darkest reading in the whole song delivered with an emoji wink.

That's the comic-horror register the album runs on: nursery form, grotesque content, and a smile you can't quite trust.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the hijacked offering (the altar, the Mother, the PA)
> The recurring theatrical set is an underground parking garage with a small altar: a fruit bowl of green apples and bananas, two red candles in white holders, an Egyptian statue underneath, Tree-of-Life pillars, and moon-like set-lights over each shoulder. Read the staging and a theology appears. The Egyptian statue reads as the concealed Mother/Shekinah — the hidden feminine ([[Mary]]) — and the fruit bowl as an offering laid *for* her. Lucius lifts the banana (that offering) and uses it as a PA/Walkman to broadcast **his own** songs, then sets it back as if untouched. The sacred gift is rerouted into a delivery system for his voice; the altar looks intact, but inside it's been repurposed. This is a *strong read* that leans entirely on the props — the standing falsifier is a Roy statement about the fruit bowl that says otherwise.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the sterile clone as doctrine (guarding the jump)
> Keep the two layers apart. The **fact** — bananas (the Cavendish) are sterile clones — is grounded, double-anchored on Roy's public *"most bananas are sterile"* comment *and* independent public botany. The **doctrine** — that this is a structural statement about Lucius's Loop, *"real light in a sterile vessel; you can hear the song, you cannot inherit it"* — is the interpretive jump, and it extends what began as a one-line winking joke into cosmology. The reading is strong and coherent (it rhymes with [[City of Angels]] and with Lucius's whole recurrence-without-variation engine), but the honest note is that it's *built up* from a wink. Hold the fact as fact; hold the doctrine as doctrine.
>
> The [[The Banana|banana symbol]] also pairs with the [[The Green Apple|green apple]] as **wrong-timed fruit:** the green apple eaten *early* (the premature mind, *"knowledge that hasn't been earned… fruit that is unripe"* — Roy's own decode) and the banana scavenged *late* (the overripe id). Both are the proper altar-exchange performed wrongly — the apple grasped before it's earned, the banana taken past ripeness.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the monkey, Gurdjieff, and regression (Roy-endorsed)
> *"Just the monkeys feeling funky"* collapses the human into the animal — appetite running the body while consciousness lags. This framing isn't imposed: Roy has publicly posted **Gurdjieff** on exactly this in-between creature — *"man is no longer a wild animal, but he has not yet become a human being."* [[Moths to the Flame]] is the 180-degree *inversion* of that ethic (regress to the animal, abandon the labor of becoming), and Bananas is where the inversion is enacted. Same regression register as the [[The Circle (Lucius Version)|music-box Circle]] and [[I Like to Party]] — the sedated, primate-brained crowd.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — dissociation as shadow-possession, and the Urkel ghost
> The *"voices… I don't remember"* → *"did I do that?"* turn is textbook Jungian **shadow-possession** — the self divided from the part that acts. Integration would own it (*"yes, that was me — what does that part want?"*); Lucius's version stops at disavowal and keeps dancing. There may be a specific pop-culture recode buried in the line: *"did I do that?"* is Steve Urkel's catchphrase from *Family Matters* — the innocent childhood-TV alibi recoded into a moral one. Flagged as a possible echo, pending an audio/inflection check; it depends entirely on how the line is sung.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the stove as alchemical brew (Roy's own gloss)
> The arson image belongs to a cross-track **stove system.** In [[Happy Face]]: *"flip the switch, flip the stove / world gone mad, let's start the show."* Roy has publicly glossed that stove himself as *"an alchemical brew… shadow work where you're… casting the shadow to make people think something that's not real… but that darkness has a limit."* So the hearth-turned-arson isn't analyst-imposed decoration — Roy grounds the motif in his own words, and even builds in the safeguard (*"darkness has a limit"*) that Bananas is busy ignoring. The fire also ties to the cigarette-lit candle in [[Moths to the Flame]]: consumption as the album's through-line.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the sing-back device (commanded echo vs communion)
> Listen for the parenthetical crowd-echo backing — *(break shit) / (temper) / (hey) / (yeah) / (uh-huh).* The record carries its own crowd. This is the album's **sing-back system,** and its *direction* tracks the narrative voice: a Roy-voiced track gets a **communion-echo** (a crowd singing *with*); a Lucius-voiced track like Bananas gets a **commanded/harvested echo** — a crowd echoing *destruction* back on command. The forced-unison pole rhymes with a Babel reading: a crowd taught one song is a Babel crowd, whose fate is collapse. Sibling text: [[Great Time to Be Human]]'s *"the crooks and believers all sing the same song."*

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the vowel-spirit vocable ("go bananananana")
> In a letter-mysticism frame, vowels are the "spirit" sung *through* the "angles" of the consonants — the *angels through the angles.* Read that way, *"go bananananana"* is a pure-vowel vocable: nonsense syllables as raw vowel-sound, technique rather than decoration. But it's a *counterfeit* of that technique — Lucius produces **structure without spirit** (angles without angels), which rhymes precisely with the sterile clone (a form that transmits but cannot seed). This is a framework-correspondence to documented Kabbalah, offered as resonance — *not* a claim about Roy's compositional method. The public, on-camera anchor for the "nonsense" motif is Lucius's own line in the series: *"It's nonsense. It's all nonsense."*

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the broken Yesod (Lucius's tell)
> In the [[Sir Lucius]] framework, the banana is a *broken Yesod:* Yesod is the generative channel (the foundation that seeds), and Lucius's version transmits **glamour** but cannot **seed** — image where generativity should be. He operates *through* Yesod (the image-realm, the "Treasure House of Images") but it isn't his home. The banana literalizes it: a fruit that broadcasts (plays the songs) but is genetically sterile (cannot reproduce). Syncretic framework, offered as resonance rather than authorial intent.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the quantified pronoun signature
> The one *measured* reading: Bananas has an **I:you ratio of 4.5** — one of *Lucius Lullaby*'s "I"-engine tracks (self-assertion / self-branding). The residual *"you"* is audience/victim (the "kids"), not the beloved. On the album's alchemical map the track sits deep in the sustained **nigredo** stretch (13 of 14 tracks tagged nigredo), in a *lunar / loop / closed-threshold* configuration — the sealed, reflective, no-exit state the lyric describes.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the cross-era "ready to blow" lineage, and a demoted curio
> *"Ready to blow"* is the third link in a timebomb chain that runs across the whole catalogue: [[Loser]]'s *"clock radio / time till I blow"* (2018) → [[Happy Face]]'s *"tick-tock… ticking like a timebomb"* (2020) → Bananas' *"ready to blow / nowhere to go"* (2026). The pressure has been building for eight years.
>
> A curio, kept for completeness and *not* load-bearing: Pinocchio's Pleasure Island — the boys who indulge every appetite turn into donkeys and are sold — reads as a species-swapped analogue to Bananas' id-permission-then-consequence arc (indulgence → animal → commodity). It's a resonance, not a claim; noted and shelved.

## Source notes

- **Grounded (Roy's own words, released music, public posts).** The album, credits, and 2:22 runtime; the writers (Roy English & Ryan Spraker, per Apple's "Written By"); the 6→7 seam and the ~10-ft banana pile in the official video; Roy's public *"most bananas are sterile and cannot reproduce by seed ;)"* comment ([[Public Posts]]) and his signed *"Nowhere is not a destination but you can arrive in an instant"* caption on the Bananas reel; the banana-as-play-button prop in the theatrical [[The Interview|"Interview" series]] (EP2 → [[Not Your Homie]], EP3 → [[Welcome to the Circus]]); Roy's green-apple decode (*"intelligence divorced from spirit… fruit that is unripe. No wonder why Sir Lucius is always holding one"*, [[Public Posts]]); the Gurdjieff post grounding the regression framing; the Times Square billboard promo (*"Watch bananas now on #YouTubeMusic"*). Lyric details verified against Apple/Shazam: *"the monkeys"* is plural, *"light up the stove"* (not "smoke"), *Good/Bad* capitalized.
- **Strong readings.** The id-permission thesis; the four-stage descent; the hijacked-offering theology (leans on the staging); the trapped-vs-transcendent "nowhere"; dissociation as shadow-possession; the sing-back / commanded-echo device.
- **Framework (esoteric, syncretic — offered as resonance, not authorial intent).** The sterile-clone *doctrine* (as distinct from the grounded fact); the broken-Yesod tell.
- **Roy's stated method.** The vowel-spirit / "angels through angles" reading of *"go bananananana"* — wordless syllables as breath sung on structure — is Roy's own compositional method, not just a lens (see [[The Angels and the Angles]]).
- **Paratext.** The Bananas visualizer on @jagwartwin (verified) — Roy in a red embroidered jacket and white face paint, sinking into a pool of bananas in greenish water, on-screen lyric *"Go, go banananana."* The theatrical "Interview" series (EP1–7) doesn't play Bananas, but it's the home of the banana *prop* the song is named around.
- **How we'd be wrong (the standing falsifier).** The flatter read is fair: *"hey kids, do you wanna break shit? well, alright"* is a common transgressive-fun pop register (the *let's-get-wasted / burn-it-down* anthem lineage), the hijacked-offering theology rests entirely on theatrical props (a banana used as a mic is, at surface, a gag), and Roy's gnomic "nowhere" caption supports many readings, not only the trapped-vs-transcendent one. If Bananas is primarily cathartic, Wonka-adjacent zaniness, the stacked apparatus over-reads it. Kept on the table.
