# MAGIK

> **One-line reading.** A pure witnessing song — a voice telling someone at risk of disappearing that their ordinary presence is already miraculous, and that a plastic world's failure to see them doesn't make their light any less real.

> [!info] Album & credits
> **Album:** *MAGIK* EP (2025, Big Loud Rock) — a 4-track EP, released **May 16, 2025**
> **Written by** Nick Bailey · John Byron · Roy English · **Produced by** Jagwar Twin & Ryan Daly (both also recording engineers) · mixed by Jeff Ellis · mastered by Dale Becker
> **Full lyrics:** [Genius](https://genius.com) · streaming
> **▶ Listen:** [Visualizer (YouTube)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=E8_rynO3E3c)

The tender one. In a catalogue full of masks, threats, spectacle and figures arguing with themselves across tables, MAGIK is the song that puts all of that down and just *tells someone they're already enough.* It opens the era that becomes the [[Lucius Lullaby]] descent — and it reads, in hindsight, like the light the album then spends its whole runtime watching [[Sir Lucius|Lucius]] try to put out.

## At a glance

- **Era / release** — the *MAGIK* EP, May 16, 2025 (Big Loud Rock); the title track. It ran ahead of the *Lucius Lullaby* album and its whole shadow-arc.
- **In one line** — *"Everything you do is Magik / living in a world so plastic"* — value located inside the person, before the world can package it.
- **Who speaks** — [[Roy|Jagwar Twin]] at his most undefended: the witness, the blesser. Not Lucius. The "you" he sings *to* is left deliberately open (see below).
- **Key symbols** — [[The Labyrinth]] (*"the middle of a maze"*) · [[The Moon]] (*"a lasso round the Moon"*) · the eyes / seeing (*"I see it in your eyes"*) · [[The Spectacle|the plastic world]].

## The song, mapped

MAGIK isn't built like an argument — it's built like a spell. It opens wordless, states its claim as a question, answers it, then turns the whole thing outward onto *you.* A closed, circular, lullaby shape: two near-identical chorus cycles bracketing a single new verse, the whole thing bookended by the same nonsense syllables it began with.

| Beat | In the song | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **Invocation** | *"(Boo doo dodo dodo doo doo) / Baba bada baba ba"* | Wordless vocalise — no words yet, just the incantation texture. These non-lexical runs are *by design*, not a transcription gap (Spotify even formats them, *"Ba-ba, ba-da"*). |
| **Thesis (call & response)** | *"Are you Magik? / Yes I am Magik"* | The claim stated as a Q&A before any case is made. Then a pivot to direct address: *"Tell me, do you know?"* |
| **Verse 1 — the reassurance stack** | *"You don't gotta disappear / everybody needs you here"* → *"you don't have to try so hard / you're more than who you think you are"* | Four reassurances in a row, aimed at someone at risk of erasing themselves. It doesn't diagnose the disappearance — it interrupts it. |
| **The empathy hinge** *(recurs both cycles)* | *"And I know it's so upsetting / you're so upset and you shouldn't be / if only you could see…"* | The conditional *"if only you could see"* launches every chorus. The whole song turns on this: the problem is never worth, only sight. |
| **Chorus — the core** | *"Everything you do is Magik / living in a world so plastic / you don't even see it / but you have it / that's it… / Life without a You would be tragic"* | The seeing, delivered. *Magik* set against *plastic*; the blessing landed as fact. Note the one-word variant across cycles — cap-**Y** *"a You"* first time, lower-case the second — the addressee briefly elevated to a proper noun. |
| **Post-chorus tag** | *"Magik / Magik"* + the *(Boo doo…)* return | The hook word isolated, then the wordless motif comes back. |
| **Verse 2 — the maze & the moon** | *"A lasso round the Moon… everywhere you are is so bright / but you don't even know that, right? / I see it in your eyes / you just gotta find your way / you're the middle of a maze / tell me what it takes"* | The one new movement. The seer names what the seen can't see; the labyrinth image arrives (the addressee at the *center*, not lost outside it); a lasso is thrown at the moon. |
| **Chorus 2 + outro** | empathy hinge → chorus → *"Magik / Magik"* + doubled *(Boo doo… / Baba bada…)* | The song dissolves back into the wordless motif it began with, framing the whole thing as an incantation that never really "argued" anything. |

**The move, in one sentence:** MAGIK never says *here's why you matter.* It asserts by blessing. Wordless invocation → *"are you Magik?"* → *"yes I am"* → turned outward → reassurance → *"if only you could see"* → the seeing handed over in the chorus. Perception, not persuasion, is the entire axis.

## What it means

A few claims that cut across the whole song.

**It's witnessing, not surveillance — the catalogue's clearest case.** Surveillance says *I see you, therefore I can predict you, shape you, sell you.* MAGIK says *I see you, therefore you don't have to disappear.* The addressee isn't exposed; they're returned to themselves. This is the [[Witnessing vs Surveillance]] axis at its plainest — the seer who *restores* the seen rather than consuming them. (Roy's shadow wears *El Roi*, "the God who sees me," on his throat and uses sight to unmake people; MAGIK is the same faculty pointed the other way.)

**The magic is ordinary, not performative.** *"Everything you do is Magik"* — already present, right now, in whatever you're doing. The problem is *perception, not capability.* That's the exact inverse of [[Sir Lucius|Lucius]]-style spectacle, which manufactures impact and needs a crowd to exist. MAGIK recognizes radiance instead, with no mask, myth or stage-trick anywhere in it. This is why the song is unmistakably Roy-side: **it blesses without recruiting.** Never *follow me / perform for me / become useful to me* — only *you already have it.*

**"A world so plastic" makes it an anti-Circus song.** The plastic world is the artificial, surface-driven, manufactured one — the [[The Circus|Circus]] / [[The Algorithm|Algorithm]] / [[The Spectacle|Spectacle]] register compressed into a single adjective. It's a world that trains people to measure themselves by external forms. MAGIK answers by finding the value *inside* the person before the world can package it. It's the tender counter-pole to the whole spectacle machine.

**The "you" is unstable on purpose — and that's the strength, not a gap.** Lover, fan, child, friend, listener — or Roy addressing a wounded part of himself. Sung outward, it's consolation; sung inward, it's self-rescue; sung to *you*, it's a direct blessing. The song refuses to choose, and the refusal is load-bearing: it's unresolvable *by design*, not a loose end to tie off. Don't collapse it to one person.

**The directness is the radical gesture.** MAGIK risks sounding simple *because it's so direct* — and that's the point. Lucius songs run on irony, masks, threat, inversion, spectacle; MAGIK does almost none of it. Its radical move isn't complexity, it's the flat refusal to play by the plastic world's terms. It's the easiest song here to underestimate and one of the hardest to actually pull off.

**The maze deepens the rescue.** Not *"you're lost in a maze"* but *"you're the middle of a maze"* — the addressee at the **center** of the labyrinth, not stranded outside it. The way through isn't escape from the self; it's finding the center correctly. And the witnessing voice works like [[The Thread]]: it doesn't solve the maze *for* you, it helps you find your own way through it. (Project-wide, this image keeps escalating: *"middle of a maze"* here becomes *"you are the maze"* on the 2026 *Welcome to the Circus* live material, and lands on the rollout password *"you are it."* You aren't lost in the labyrinth — you *are* it, and the exit is not *out* but *in.*)

## Characters

- **[[Roy|Jagwar Twin]]** — emphatically the witness and the blesser; the undefended, Roy-side voice with no persona over it. The cleanest demonstration of what he is *without* Lucius: recognition instead of manufacture, blessing instead of recruitment.
- **The "you"** — not a fixed character. An open second person (lover / fan / child / friend / listener / a wounded part of Roy). Present it as open; it's meant to stay that way.
- **[[Sir Lucius]]** *(by contrast, not by presence)* — Lucius never appears *in* MAGIK; he's its structural opposite. Spectacle vs witnessing, manufacture vs recognition. MAGIK is the light; the album that follows is the shadow trying to metabolize it and failing.
- **[[Mary]]** *(a faint resonance only)* — she surfaces at the edges through the lunar register and the *"lasso round the Moon,"* but **MAGIK is not a Mary appearance.** It's the *blessing-voice* the Mary-class figure carries elsewhere, sung here in Roy's own mouth.

## Symbols

- **[[The Labyrinth]]** — *"you're the middle of a maze."* The addressee at the center, not lost outside it; the way through is inward.
- **[[The Moon]]** — *"a lasso round the Moon."* Reaching for the unreachable as a gesture of love; the lunar register that runs through the project's Mary material.
- **The eyes / seeing** — *"I see it in your eyes,"* *"if only you could see,"* *"you don't even see it / but you have it."* Sight *is* the song's mechanism: the seer restores the seen.
- **[[The Spectacle|The plastic world]]** — *"a world so plastic."* The manufactured, surface-driven world; the [[The Circus|Circus]]/[[The Algorithm|Algorithm]] register in one word, and MAGIK's tender counter-pole to it.
- **Magik (the word itself)** — held between two senses: the everyday wonder Roy talks about publicly (*"there is so much magik everywhere… we know it when we're young"*) and the possibility of the esoteric *"Magick"* (see Advanced reading). One word, two charges.

## Connected songs

- **The blessing-voice cluster** — [[Ballerina Boy]] (*"let your light shine"*), [[Weirdness]] (*"you were born to shine"*), [[#1 Champion (la Conquistadora)]]: all of them bless a shamed or hidden light into its own worth. MAGIK is the purest statement of the mode.
- **[[Shine]] (2018)** — the vocabulary *ancestor.* Shine's first-person, communal claim (*"everywhere I go, I'm gonna shine"*) is the seed that later gets redirected into MAGIK's second-person **blessing** (*you* were born to shine). Shine is the source of the word, not itself a Mary appearance.
- **[[SOL]]** — MAGIK's mirror at the album's *other* end. Both locate the saving force outside strategy and spectacle (blessing and voice, not control); both are the light the shadow can't absorb. MAGIK opens the arc as that light; SOL closes it — the one song Lucius can't get through.
- **[[dumbledore]] (2025)** — a sibling EP that shares the exact word *"Magik"* at the **opposite charge.** Here it's a tender, innate blessing; there it's the power that gets you burned (*"you would've burned that witch"*), stapled to a straight-faced flip of a nursery rhyme (*"if you're magic and you know it / clap your hands"*). Same word, inverted.
- **[[Welcome to the Circus]]** — the anti-pole environment: *"a world so plastic"* is the Circus condition, and WTTC's *"you are the maze"* escalates MAGIK's *"middle of a maze."*
- **[[I Like to Party]] (2021)** — the antecedent of the you-are-the-center axiom: *"you're the Kingdom, you're the King."* It rhymes straight forward into the MAGIK rollout's *"you are it."*
- **[[The Circle]]** — the doctrine underneath: *"you are it,"* the inside-of-the-inside / co-creator idea. MAGIK's maze-center is the same move rendered as a rescue.

## The rollout — the "middle of the maze" ARG

MAGIK didn't just release; it was *unlocked.* In the run-up, Roy posted a series of **four password-protected drops** in the official Jagwar Twin Discord — in a channel literally named **#middle-of-the-maze**, after the lyric — dated April 30, May 1, May 7 and May 12, 2025. Each solved password opened a piece or stem of the still-unreleased song as the prize, building to the full release on May 16. (On release day Roy credited the fans who cracked it: *"you guys got the logos riddle clap clap clap."*)

The four passwords, read in order, are a compact theology of the Self:

1. **you are it** — the Self. (Alan Watts's signature doctrine — *"You're It… you are the universe experiencing itself"* — and the public hint-post that names Watts is the very one that hands you the password. It's also a standing Circle-slogan since at least 2021: the 2021 *Down to You* microsite already said *"No one else can be IT, you must be IT for your own unique journey."*)
2. **heart** — the Self's organ.
3. **alcyone** — the fixed central star of the Pleiades: the still point, the center that doesn't move.
4. **davar** — Hebrew *דָּבָר*, "the Word" *and* "the thing": creation-by-speech. The final drop paired a darkened *Salvator Mundi*, John 1 (*"In the beginning was the Word"*) and a Hebrew *davar* photo.

So the maze you're in the middle of turns out to have a center made of *you → your heart → the fixed star → the creating Word.* The rescue and the cosmology are the same shape.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — davar, No-Thing, and creation-by-speech
> The fourth drop, *davar*, doesn't sit alone. It pairs with a Roy aphorism from 2022 — *"No thing ever creates another thing"* — where *lo davar* ("no-thing") is the wildcard: the Word that creates *from* No-Thing. This routes MAGIK's rollout straight into the project's [[The Empty Mirror|empty-mirror]] material and the whole [[The Voice|Voice]] / [[The Mouth|Mouth]] complex — creation as an act of speech out of emptiness. The rollout's endpoint isn't a marketing beat; it's the same origin-by-utterance the mythology keeps circling.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the paratext (Jung, Snow White, the star and the spider)
> The hint-posts around the drops are their own small text. A *building/shadow* post quoted Jung — *"the taller the building, the greater the shadow"* — captioned *"When's the last time you watched Snow White tho?"* (the shadow-arc of the whole *Lucius Lullaby* era, previewed). The SideQuest reel named Watts *"a loyal servant of the Realm."* And the MAGIK visualizer (May 16, 2025) carried the caption **"The star conjunct the spider at first breath"** — a natal-chart register that ties back to the *alcyone* (fixed-star) drop and the recurring spider motif elsewhere in the world. Whether the caption encodes an actual birth chart is raised, not resolved.
>
> One genuinely strange cross-domain fact: Roy recycles his *own* song lyrics — and even his own misspelling — as puzzle codes elsewhere. *"EVERYTHING YOU DO IS MAGIK,"* *"YOU'RE THE MIDDLE OF A MAZE"* and *"DAVAR"* all turn up as candidate codes in the separate [[Hall of Mirrors]] web3 ARG, where the guidance is explicitly to *"reproduce Roy's misspellings as written (MAGIK…) first."* The lyric is a lyric and a key at the same time.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the K-spelling, held open
> Is *"MAGIK"* an intentional nod to Aleister Crowley's *"Magick"* — the spelling coined to separate intentional will from stage illusion, which would tie into the broader esoteric framework and the Alice Bailey influence Roy has publicly cited? Possibly. But it's raised, not asserted, and deliberately so: the lyric's *content* leans hard toward innate, unrecognized **worth**, not occult **will**. The song earns the "wonder" sense of the word far more than the "will" sense. Held as a question the material doesn't quite settle.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the shine-lineage and the kabbalah lens
> **The shine-thread.** MAGIK is arguably the endpoint of a vocabulary that begins in [[Shine]] (2018): a first-person/communal *"I'm gonna shine"* that the divine-feminine, Mary-class voice later redirects into second-person **blessing** — *"you were born to shine"* ([[Weirdness]]), *"let your light shine"* ([[Ballerina Boy]]), and the pure witnessing-blessing of MAGIK. The claim gets handed from the singer to the listener over eight years.
>
> **A held kabbalah reading.** One esoteric sweep floated MAGIK as *birur* — the lifting or sorting of the sparks, seeing the hidden light in the ordinary and raising it. It's a suggestive fit for a song about seeing radiance where others see only the plastic. But it's a *candidate*, anchored in lyric and interview rather than in anything Roy has stated, so it's held rather than folded into canon. (For the frame itself, see [[Kabbalah - The Tree of Life]] and [[Keilim - The Breaking of the Vessels]].)

## Source notes

- **Grounded (released music, public posts, public interviews).** The *MAGIK* EP as a 4-track Big Loud Rock release, May 16, 2025, and the full song credits above (writers, producers, engineers, performers, background vocalists) are verified against the streaming release. Roy's release-day and run-up posts are public: *"MAGIK is out now!! What do you guys think?"* (May 16), *"im so excited for Magik to be released guys…"* (May 12), and a string of magik-philosophy lines in the weeks before — *"there is so much magik everywhere. and we know it when we're young. hopefully we rediscover it when we get older,"* *"you are magik."* His aphorisms in the same window: *"Magik happens when you align your personality with your soul's intention,"* *"Create space for magic to happen."* Roy explicitly files all of this under his own *"magik & reflection philosophy"* label. The **ARG** (four password-protected Discord drops in #middle-of-the-maze; passwords *you are it → heart → alcyone → davar*; the visualizer, Snow White / Jung, and Watts hint-posts) is grounded in his own public-channel posts. A fan reaction video by "Steven" was boosted by the project's Michael Morgan in the official Discord.
- **Strong readings.** Witnessing-not-surveillance as the song's core action; the magic-is-ordinary / blesses-without-recruiting formulation; the anti-Circus reading of *"a world so plastic"*; the maze-center as inward rescue and the witnessing voice as the Thread; the blessing-voice cluster and the Shine-to-MAGIK lineage; MAGIK as the light SOL later mirrors. Grounded in the material, but interpretive.
- **Softer / held.** The *"lasso round the Moon"* as a faint *It's a Wonderful Life* echo (George Bailey offers to lasso the moon — notably for a woman named Mary, which quietly rhymes with the project's [[Mary]] without carrying weight); the exact wording isn't confirmed as a deliberate reference. The K-as-Crowley reading; the *birur* reading; whether the visualizer caption encodes a real natal chart — all raised, not resolved.
- **Open question.** MAGIK's exact **position** on the *MAGIK* EP isn't confirmed here, so no sequence-meaning is asserted from tracklist order.
- **How we'd be wrong.** If the plain "witnessing / ordinary radiance" reading accounts for every line as well as the esoteric apparatus does, the apparatus is decorative — and the cross-links into the blessing cluster rest partly on those *other* songs' own verification. Both stay on the table.
