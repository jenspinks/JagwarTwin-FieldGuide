# Life Is Good

> **One-line reading.** Joy as a *decision*, not a description — the speaker sees the flames, the lies, the fighting and his own sadness, and chooses *"life is good"* anyway, because the world stays worth loving even when it isn't.

> [!info] Single & credits
> **Standalone single** — angelbaby & Jagwar Twin, 2023 · not on an album, no track number
> **Written by** Dallas Caton · Jay Stolar · Jordan Palmer · Brandyn Burnette · **Josh Dun** · Luke Villegas · Roy English
> **Produced by** angelbaby · Jay Stolar · Jordan Palmer — **Vocals** angelbaby (with Jagwar Twin) · **Mixed by** Jeff Ellis · **Mastered by** Dale Becker
> **Tuning** 444 Hz *(Roy: every Jagwar Twin song is tuned to it — "a healing frequency")*
> **Full lyrics:** [Genius](https://genius.com/artists/Jagwar-twin) · [Spotify](https://open.spotify.com/track/6Y0j8yUF3rPSTrTtVEsCNh) · streaming
> **▶ Listen:** [Visualizer (YouTube)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y9vm_C3tZYA)

A nursery rhyme about the end of the world. That's the whole trick of it: a bright, three-minute sing-along that cheerfully reports the house is on fire and shrugs *"but at least we're having fun"* — and means both halves. It's the catalogue's forced-positivity thread caught in the act of growing up.

## At a glance

- **Era / release** — standalone single, early November 2023; Jagwar Twin's collaboration with **angelbaby**, an AI/virtual artist. It arrived first as a limited Web3 drop on **Sound.xyz** (a 0.01-ETH edition), then everywhere on DSPs days later. Toured heavily through 2024 into 2025 (Slovakia, May 2024; Brussels, June 2024; Dubai, December 2024), often with angelbaby credited as the on-screen "sound."
- **In one line** — the smile you choose *against* the flames, held so deliberately you can't quite tell if it's wisdom or evasion — and the song won't tell you which.
- **Who speaks** — [[Roy|Jagwar Twin]] as the bright-surfaced bard *and* the one confessing underneath, co-voiced by an AI. Addressed to the crowd — "kids" gathered for a song.
- **Key symbols** — the [[The Voice|antenna]] (the mind as receiver) · the smile · the [[Fire|flames]] · the circle 'round the sun · the [[The Veil|illusion]] / plastic reality.

## The song, mapped

The form *is* the argument: a boast, repeatedly punctured by a confession, ending on a refusal to choose. It's short by design — *"and it won't be very long"* — built from only two real verse-ideas and a heavily repeated chorus, exactly the way a children's song is built. And that's not an accident of taste. Roy has a name for it: **"Trojan Horse music."** In a 2024 interview he traces the method to an early mentor, producer Jeff Bhasker, who *"made me study 'Twinkle Twinkle Little Star,' study nursery rhymes, and study why they're the most genius songs of all time"* — the idea being that the simplest songs are secretly the most complex, *"like Einstein's E=mc²."* The bright, singable chorus works *"like a mantra"*; the dark cargo rides in underneath it. Life Is Good is that technique caught in the open — a nursery rhyme deliberately loaded with the end of the world. Following its own moves:

| Section | In the song | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **Hook-seed** | *"Life is good / I'm great"* | The thesis stated bare, before the chorus fully lands. |
| **Chorus** *(the engine)* | *"…Another circle 'round the sun / The world's in flames / but at least we're having fun"* | The decision. Good *despite* the fire, not *because* of fortune. "Another circle 'round the sun" = a year turning — cyclical time, the [[The Loop and the Circle|LOOP]]'s recurrence. Recurs 3+ times, sometimes flipping to *"I'm good / I'm great."* |
| **Bard verse** | *"Hey kids I'll sing you a song / and it won't be very long… Some people tell me I'm strange / but I like living this way"* | Speaker-as-storyteller, plus a flash of self-acceptance. The song announces *itself* as a song sung to children while the world burns. |
| **The "ah ah ah"** | *(wordless)* | The mantra hook — the sing-along machine made audible. Try not to catch yourself humming it. |
| **Boast → break** | *"La viva es buena / my life is great / my head's an antenna / I'm wide awake"* → *"…but I'm mad / and I'm sad / and I'm misunderstood"* | The core juxtaposition in miniature: a grand Spanish flourish, then the same phrasing deflated into a sulky plain-English confession. |
| **Chorus ×2** | *"Life is good…"* then the *"I'm good…"* variant | The party reasserts itself, back to back. |
| **Dissociation verse** | *"Face down but I can feel my body moving / is this real or am I in an illusion / get up get up I'm only human / pupils dilated like I'm 'bout to lose it / so crazy so I turn off the news… every one fighting, tell me which side are you / keep a smile on my face, too happy to choose"* | Eight lines, sung straight through with **no chorus to break the fall** — overwhelm → media-withdrawal → the closing **refusal**. This is the song's real weather. |
| **Outro** | chorus ×2, then *"but at least we're having fun"* ×3 to fade | It ends on the *consolation*, not the resolution. The last word is *"at least"* — structurally refusing to settle the wisdom-vs-evasion question. |

A note on two lines people mishear. *"La viva es buena"* is the actual sung lyric (not a slip for *"la vida es buena"*), and it's **"Another** circle 'round the sun," apostrophe-round — both confirmed against the released recording. If you've seen this song written up with a *"Nether circle round the sun"* underworld cosmology, that was a transcription error; the real line points at the loop, not the underworld.

## What it means

The chorus is **joy as a decision, not a verdict on the evidence.** Everything hangs on that structure. It's good *despite* the flames — a choice re-made every time the line comes back around, which is why the song can hold *"the world's in flames"* and *"at least we're having fun"* in the same breath without flinching.

That makes it a genuine step forward from [[Happy Face]], not a repeat. Happy Face wears the smile as pure disguise — a mask over the ache. Life Is Good *names the ache* — *"I'm mad / I'm sad / I'm misunderstood"* — **and still chooses "life is good" anyway.** That's polarity-holding, both true at once, not anesthesia. You are not in denial if you can say the pain out loud. The forced-positivity thread has learned to keep its eyes open.

But it doesn't get to feel clean about it, and this is the load-bearing tension the song refuses to resolve. *"Keep a smile on my face, too happy to choose"* reads two ways at once:

- **Defiance.** Refusing to let the outrage-machine — *"which side are you"* — eat your humanity. You stay *"only human,"* you love the world even as it burns, precisely so the fire doesn't win.
- **Complicity.** Fun-over-truth dissociation — *"turn off the news"* — the dumbing-down that [[Welcome to the Circus]] runs on. Grinning your way out of every hard question.

The *"I'm mad and I'm sad"* admission tips it toward defiance — again, naming the pain is the tell that you're not asleep. But the dissociation imagery keeps complicity alive: face-down, *"pupils dilated like I'm 'bout to lose it,"* a body that moves on its own while the mind asks *"is this real."* **The smile being either wisdom or evasion is the song.** Don't resolve it; the ambiguity is the content.

Two more threads run underneath. The speaker is **overwhelmed by awareness, not asleep to it** — *"my head's an antenna / I'm wide awake,"* a mind tuned to receive everything at once (culture, fear, the collective anxiety), *too* awake, and the smile is partly a way to survive the reception. And the delivery is **the Trojan-Horse device made self-aware**: the same bright coating that lets [[Not Your Homie]] and [[dumbledore]] smuggle dark cargo, except here the song *knows* it's doing it and says so out loud — *"I'll sing you a song."*

The deepest kinship in the catalogue is with **[[tomorrow (ANGLE OF ETERNITY)]]**. Both songs refuse certainty and ideological possession and choose presence and humanity instead — from opposite tempers. *tomorrow* does it in a spiritual register: *"maybe I don't know, and that's fine with me."* Life Is Good does it playfully: *"living in a lie… too happy to choose."* Same underlying question, asked twice: **how do you stay human when the world is overwhelming?**

## Characters

- [[Roy|Jagwar Twin]] — the bard and ringmaster of the bright surface, and the one confessing underneath it; the storyteller who sings you a song while the world's in flames.
- **angelbaby** *(@angelbaby / angelbaby3045.eth)* — the credited AI/virtual collaborator, publicly billed as co-artist, producer and vocalist. A "metastar" from the Web3 entertainment company Hume (CAA-signed; known for AI-voice work, including a track using Grimes' AI-generated voice), presented as a leopard-patterned rabbit with a septum piercing, styled in lowercase. A real external collaborator — Roy refers to the persona as "he." Chronologically, angelbaby is the **earliest node in Roy's documented AI / digital-double thread**, predating the "made by AI" [[Bounce]]-era visualizer work and everything after. See [[The Digital Double]].
- **The "kids" / the crowd** — addressed directly (*"Hey kids I'll sing you a song"*): the audience being entertained while the world burns. See [[The Crowd]].
- **Josh Dun** *(Twenty One Pilots)* — a credited co-writer. Behind the glass rather than a character in the song, but a notable name given how often Jagwar Twin gets compared to Twenty One Pilots.

## Symbols

- **The [[The Voice|antenna]]** — *"my head's an antenna / I'm wide awake"*; the mind tuned to signal, receiving everything. The posture of being *too* connected to the collective feed.
- **The smile** — joy held as a *chosen* posture against the flames; the load-bearing ambiguity, defiance or evasion, deliberately unresolved.
- **The [[Fire|flames]]** — *"the world's in flames"*; the burning world the joy is chosen against, the apocalyptic backdrop it shares with [[All My Friends]]. The line became its own social shorthand — Jagwar Twin kept re-posting *"the world's in flames 🐇"* clips (the rabbit emoji tying the chorus into the wider rabbit iconography) long after release.
- **The circle 'round the sun** — a year turning, cyclical time; the corrected *"Another"* points it at [[The Loop and the Circle|LOOP]]'s mechanical recurrence — another lap, same track.
- **The [[The Veil|illusion]] / plastic reality** — *"is this real or am I in an illusion" / "living in a lie and we can't see the truth"*; the fake-reality and media cluster, quietly sharpened by *who* is co-singing it.
- **The body-that-moves-on-its-own** — *"face down but I can feel my body moving"*; kinetic dissociation, the mind detached from the moving body (the [[Bounce]] image).
- **The news, turned off** — *"so I turn off the news"*; media-withdrawal as a survival move against the overload.

## Connected songs

- [[Happy Face]] — the forced-positivity thread. Life Is Good is the **more integrated step**: Happy Face's smile is pure disguise; Life Is Good admits the sadness and *still* chooses joy. Roy pairs the two himself, and names Happy Face as *the* worked example of the Trojan-Horse method — even gating its 2019 release behind a facial-recognition site (deathofmygeneration.fun) that made you *hold a smile* through dystopian imagery for the song's whole length before it hit DSPs.
- [[tomorrow (ANGLE OF ETERNITY)]] — the **deepest kinship**: both refuse certainty and choose presence and humanity, from different tones. *"That's fine with me"* against *"too happy to choose."*
- [[All My Friends]] — the **2023 sibling** (roughly six months earlier): two songs that meet the world's end with *chosen togetherness*, both carrying the same beautiful-and-unsettling double-edge (chosen love, or acceptance of the going-down).
- [[Bounce]] — the body-moving-while-mind-elsewhere image. In Bounce the motion *avoids* knowing; here the body moves while the mind *questions* reality.
- [[Welcome to the Circus]] — the trap the song survives *inside*, or is complicit *with*: the fun-over-truth machine.
- [[Good Day]] — major-key brightness stretched over instability; the same surface-vs-substrate kinship.
- [[Bad Feeling (Oompa Loompa)]] — angelbaby's press framing, *"we can't pretend that everything is just perfect,"* rhymes almost exactly with Bad Feeling's *"everything's perfect here"* (and the two singles rolled out weeks apart).
- [[Duality]] — joy and sadness held as a polarity, both true at once; the song files cleanly as a Duality instance.
- [[The Digital Double]] — the AI-collaborator lineage that angelbaby opens.
- [[Not Your Homie]] · [[dumbledore]] — fellow sweet-surface-over-darkness devices.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the antenna, the smile, and the Hermetic frame *(tap to open)*
> Roy's public toolkit includes the Hermetic principles (they're transcribed on his "I'm 33" board — see [[The I'm 33 Board]] and [[Hermeticism - As Above So Below]]), and two of them map onto this song's core images with unusual precision.
>
> **The antenna as the Vibration principle.** *"My head's an antenna / I'm wide awake"* is the posture of the Kybalion's Vibration principle as Roy has framed it — *"tune to desired frequency,"* *"the mind lives within a sea of information"* — and as he put it publicly in 2020: *"everything is a vibrational frequency. Light, matter, sound, it's all the same thing at variable speeds."* Three public anchors (the board cell, the 2020 quote, the lyric) lift this past coincidence into engagement — though the specific line-to-doctrine match is still a reader's mapping, not something Roy stated as intent. It reframes the antenna: the mind isn't just anxious, it's *tuned* — receiving a frequency it can't switch off.
>
> **"Too happy to choose" as the Polarity principle.** In the board's frame the smile that won't pick a side enacts Polarity — *"all truths are but half-truths," "extremes meet," "change the vibrations of hate into the vibrations of love."* Read this way, the refusal isn't mere avoidance; it's holding the warring sides as **two degrees of one thing**. That *sharpens* the tension rather than dissolving it: it hands the defiance reading a doctrine to stand on, while the dissociation imagery keeps the complicity reading alive. The song gets to be principled *and* suspect at once.
>
> One lighter thread: *"strange but I like living this way"* has a small kinship with the strangeness-as-okay motif that runs through [[Weirdness]] — self-acceptance as its own quiet frequency.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the AI meta-layer, and one guardrail *(tap to open)*
> There's a coincidence here that the material almost stages on purpose: a song asking *"is this real or am I in an illusion"* — a song *about* fake, plastic reality — is co-performed by an actual synthetic entity. The universe supplying its own punchline. How far the resonance travels depends on an open question: did Hume/angelbaby *generate* parts of the record, or is it human-written and AI-*performed*? Roy's own account is disarmingly low-key — he heard angelbaby's work while getting into AI and Web3, and his manager at the time (Linus) knew the team and connected them; they *"got in the studio and just had fun,"* no pressure. That framing tilts toward incidental. But the collaboration still sits at the head of the [[The Digital Double]] lineage, and a song about illusion sung partly by an illusion is hard to fully un-hear.
>
> **The guardrail:** angelbaby's name echoes the "angel baby" / divine-feminine imagery elsewhere in the project (as in [[Weirdness]]). Resist the pull to fuse them. angelbaby is a real external collaborator — a name-echo, not the symbolic voice. And angelbaby's Hume avatar happening to be a **leopard-patterned rabbit** — fusing Jagwar Twin's own jaguar/leopard and rabbit-eared motifs a couple of years before the rabbit-eared [[Sir Lucius]] — is a genuine curiosity, but it's *Hume's* design, not Roy's. Note the rhyme; don't fold it into the iconography as intent.

- **Framework & resonance (engagement, not authorial intent).** The Hermetic mappings (antenna↔Vibration, *"too happy to choose"*↔Polarity), which have real public anchors but stay reader's-frame at the line level; and the AI meta-layer, whose reach depends on the unresolved authorship question.
- **Still open.** Whether angelbaby *generated* or only *performed* (and so how much thematic weight the AI collaboration carries); the exact vocal split between Jagwar Twin and angelbaby; and a housekeeping tangle worth naming — the single's *label/platform* credits are genuinely mixed across sources, though they resolve into distinct real roles once separated: **Sound.xyz** is the Web3 drop platform the limited edition minted on; **10K Projects** is the DSP label Spotify lists; and **Big Loud** is Roy's usual label/booking home across the rest of the catalogue (which makes 10K Projects the odd one out here). The *release date* likewise clusters across a few early-November 2023 days — the Sound.xyz mint (Nov 2), the everywhere/DSP date (Nov 3), and the run-up social announcements (late October). None of it changes the reading; all of it wants pinning before it's stated as flat fact.
