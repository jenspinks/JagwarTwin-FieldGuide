# Pay Attention (feat. little luna)

> **One-line reading.** The song personifies Attention as an ancient power that offers you everything you want in exchange for the one thing it actually consumes — your awareness — and stages the whole transaction as a carnival pitch that collects its toll in the very act of being heard.

## At a glance

- **Era / release** — *33*, September 30 2022 (the "cosmology album"); track 3, in the digital-age opening cluster (Online → It's Your Time → **Pay Attention** → I Like to Party).
- **In one line** — a personified Attention runs a carnival-barker con: it offers you your desires, names a "little price," and the price turns out to be you.
- **Who speaks** — a two-hander. **little luna** is the guide/MC who introduces you to the deity and voices the chorus-terms; **Roy voices Attention itself** — the pitch, the bridge, the naming ritual. (No Crowd, no Mary here; the "crowd" is a production echo built into the chorus.)
- **Key symbols** — [[The Light-Bearer]] · [[The Circus]] · the Door · the Eye · the Empire / television · the Star · the Name.

## The song, mapped

*Pay Attention* is a dialogue staged as a pitch: the guide delivers the mark, the deity closes the sale. It's first-person throughout — the deity's "I" — and the [[Stylized Capitalization|deliberately capitalized words]] (Attention, Door, Store, BIG, Stars, Born, Light, Mind, Empires, MaMa) carry the emphasis like stage lighting. Short excerpts only below; full lyrics live at [Genius](https://genius.com).

| Beat | In the song | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **Intro** *(little luna)* | *"Hello, Attention / I'd like to introduce you to someone"* | The guide hands you off — introduces the *listener* to the deity. |
| **The hook** *(Attention)* | *"Are you happy… or do you dream of more?"* → *"so much that I can help you find"* | Opens on your dissatisfaction, then positions itself as the fix. |
| **The barker call** | *"Step right up to my Door… there's a little price you pay / But I can show you what's in Store"* | Carnival patter + the price named *and* minimized in one breath. |
| **Desire enumeration** | *"You want the money? / the car? / the big BIG house?"* → *"Everybody want…"* | Names your wants, then universalizes them as everyone's. |
| **Chorus (the thesis)** | *"Everybody's gotta pay me / You gotta pay Attention"* | The pun *is* the argument: currency spent = resource harvested. |
| **Verse 2 (the flattery)** | *"They say Stars are Born from Light / And you're so much more than you Mind"* | Borrows sacred/cosmic idiom; flatters with a soul-beyond-mind promise. |
| **Incantation** | *"Attention!"* ×8 | The name chanted as pure incantation between sections. |
| **Bridge (the scale-up)** | *"May I have all your ears to the back of the room"* → *"a little bitty problem, but I know what to do"* → *"eyes all around my head… see anything when you see my view"* → *"a small price and a rendezvous"* → *"I've been behind the Empires that rise / What they televise is just my disguise"* | Five moves: MC-ask → problem-hook → claimed omnipresence → the deal → the reveal. The frame widens from one listener to all of history. |
| **Naming ritual (climax)** | *"What's my name? / Attention!"* ×3, escalating to all-caps | Answering *is* paying — the form collects its own toll. |
| **Outro** | chorus with ad-libs *(Who Who?) (Pay Attention)* → a final lone *"MaMa"* | Returns to terms, then trails off on one unparsed word. |

**The argument's shape.** Watch the two dials move in opposite directions. The pitch keeps shrinking the cost — *"a little price," "a small price," "a little bitty problem"* — while the chorus quietly reveals the cost is total (your attention itself). Meanwhile the *frame* keeps expanding: one dissatisfied listener → everybody → *"behind the Empires that rise."* And the closer converts your engagement into the exact payment it's been describing. Say the name and you've paid.

## What it means

A handful of claims that cut across the whole pitch.

**Attention is the hidden god of the modern world — a conversation with a *force*, not a person.** It tempts only by offering (money, status, security, recognition), never by threatening, and the cost stays understated precisely *because* the payment is awareness. That's the whole trick of the chorus pun: *"pay me / pay Attention."* The currency you spend and the resource being extracted are one substance. It weaponizes a scold every distracted person has heard — the schoolteacher's *pay attention* — and turns the nag into a deity's toll.

**It co-opts the language of light to make the bargain holy.** *"They say Stars are Born from Light"* puts sacred, cosmological vocabulary in the tempter's mouth; *"you're so much more than you Mind"* flatters with a soul-beyond-mind promise. This is the counterfeit-light move — a lower, acquisitive power borrowing a higher source's idiom, mistaking (or pretending) the channel is the source. It's the exact error the project elsewhere pins on [[Sir Lucius]]. *(That the flattery could be just flattery, with no metaphysics attached, is the honest counter-read — see the deadpan-con note below.)*

**The bridge reveals the true scale of the narrator.** The intimate pitch (*"a little bitty problem"*) suddenly widens to a room (*"all your ears to the back of the room"*), then to the whole span of history: *"behind the Empires that rise… televise is just my disguise."* Empires rise and fall; Attention remains. What read as a psychological pressure gets reframed as a trans-historical constant that kingdoms, institutions, and media all compete to capture and none of them owns.

**The song enacts its own bargain — temptation through consent, distilled.** Attention compels no one. The listener is invited and *assents*. The danger isn't external domination; it's the willing surrender of agency for comfort, validation, status — the bargain works because the mark already wants what's on offer. And the closing ritual seals it as form: *"What's my name? / (Attention)."* To answer is to pay. It's a closed loop — the demand manufacturing the surrender it asks for — awareness pulled *outward* rather than in. This is the catalogue's cleanest statement of the move the whole Jagwar Twin world keeps circling: you are never taken, you always say yes.

## Characters

- **Attention** *(personified)* — the tempter-deity: the pitch, the bridge, the naming ritual. **Voiced by Roy.** An early prototype of the [[Sir Lucius]] *function* — persuasion not force, leverage on existing desire, intimate knowledge of the want, concealment behind visible structures (*"behind the Empires," "televise is just my disguise"*), proprietary authority (*"my name"*). Lucius never appears; the song works the territory he'll later personify.
- **little luna** — the **guide / MC / recruiter** who introduces you to the deity and carries the chorus-terms. A *character* here, not a background vocalist. (She's also a real, recurring Jagwar Twin collaborator — see Source notes.)
- **[[Roy]]** — the human artist behind the deity's voice. Note the **inversion of the usual casting**: here Roy plays the tempter, the persuader-role Lucius normally carries, while a second voice guides the listener toward *him*.
- **[[Sir Lucius]]** — **absent by name**, present as function. His persuasion-and-concealment operation is exactly what Attention prototypes, two years before he's named.

## Symbols

- **[[The Light-Bearer]]** — *"Stars are Born from Light":* the counterfeit-light move, sacred idiom in an acquisitive mouth.
- **[[The Circus]]** — *"Step right up"* is the barker's seed; the spectacle Attention runs while hiding behind it. (This line is the origin of a cross-catalogue chain — see Advanced reading.)
- **The Door** — *"Step right up to my Door":* the threshold/opening; part of the House-and-Vessel container imagery, alongside the *"big BIG house."*
- **The Eye** — *"eyes all around my head… see anything when you see my view":* claimed omnipresent perception. (An eye emoji also fronts the 2022 cipher tweet — see Source notes.)
- **The Empire / television** — *"behind the Empires that rise… televise is just my disguise":* media and power as the deity's concealment. Recurs as the man-behind-the-curtain in [[Imagine A World]].
- **The Star** — the *fame-star* here (*"Stars are Born from Light"*), corrected two tracks later to the soul-star in [[Soul Is A Star]].
- **The Name** — *"What's my name?":* name-as-power, invocation as the toll.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the "Step right up" barker chain (grounded census)
> The barker call doesn't start with the Ringmaster. It starts here, in the deity's own 2022 mouth, and recurs across the catalogue as one lineage:
> - **Pay Attention (2022)** — *"Step right up to my Door… a little price you pay"*
> - **[[Good Time]] (2023)** — *"Step right up, get on the floor"*
> - **[[Welcome to the Circus]] (2026)** — *"Step right up, shut your mouth"*
>
> The three-word invitation is grounded in all three songs. What's *asked* changes across them — a price, then a party, then silence — and it's tempting to read that as a descent from "cheaper" to "more coercive." Log the shift; don't adjudicate the direction. The load-bearing find is just that the Ringmaster's signature call is already fully formed in the attention-deity years before Lucius takes the tent. *(A grace note: "May I have all your ears to the back of the room" is the oration-ask of a crowd-turner — a faint echo of Antony's "lend me your ears.")*

> [!note]- Advanced reading — Attention as an early Sir Lucius prototype (retrospective)
> Everything Attention does, Lucius later does with a face: persuasion not force (*"I can show you," "I'll get it for you"*), leverage on a desire you already hold, intimate knowledge of the want, concealment behind visible structures, proprietary authority. The counterfeit-light move — sacred idiom in an acquisitive mouth — is the specific Lucius error (channel mistaken for source) rendered as a sales tactic. **Guardrail:** 2022 names no Lucius, no Circus, no Algorithm anywhere in the press cycle. So this is a *function* read anchored on later work and on Roy's board, not a claim about stated 2022 intent. The song works the territory; it doesn't name the tenant.

It gives the catalogue's recurring **flood** a counter-figure who *wins* against the water by killing it. Roy placing Marduk beside his own creation-poem reads as holding *both* cosmogonies of the split at once: division-as-dance (his "One become Two") and division-as-conquest (Marduk bisecting the mother).
>
> **How far this goes.** The grounded floor is solid: two board cells + the Discord line + the coded artifact. Everything built on top — that the *speaker* of this song *is* Marduk — stays a reading, held open rather than asserted. **Firewall:** in this myth Tiamat is the slain sea-mother; she shares a feminine-source *register* with Mary but the *figures* stay separate. Do not fuse them, and do not read the song's final *"MaMa"* onto either — that word is flagged, unparsed, and load-bearing on nothing.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the Eye, and the harvested-energy economy
> Two threads that connect the song to Roy's own board.
>
> **The Eye.** *"I got eyes all around my head… see anything when you see my view"* maps onto the board's "eyes all around head" note and its Eye-of-Horus glyph — the deity's omnipresence as *the Eye made of everyone watching*. That's mechanism, not decor: the anthropomorphized Attention *is* the aggregate of every gaze. (The eye emoji fronting the 2022 cipher tweet is the same motif surfacing in the paratext.)
>
> **Life Force → money → attention.** The board transcribes an Alice Bailey doctrine — that *Money* is the *"Object representing externalized Life Force"* (*"on the physical plane it is energy"*). Roy demonstrably engages an externalized-life-force economy. *Pay Attention* runs the same ledger one step further: it makes *awareness* the currency, so the harvested resource sits right on the board's substrate — Life Force → money → attention, one transmuted energy, collected by a deity. The board engagement is grounded; the song-to-doctrine correspondence is the reading. The song never cites Bailey.

> [!note]- Advanced reading — the resonance register (framework, not intent)
> The figure gathers several archetypes without being reducible to any one — all framework-attributed, none asserted as Roy's intent:
> - **Mammon / idolatry** — money, status, and visibility as objects of devotion; *"what's my name?"* as a demand for invocation.
> - **The Demiurge** — a lower power holding consciousness captive by directing awareness *outward* (*"see anything when you see my view," "eyes all around"*).
> - **Hermes / the trickster** — messenger, advertiser, dealer, promising access while staying invisible behind the message.
> - **The Ringmaster** — *"Step right up,"* a textual anticipation of [[Welcome to the Circus]].
> - **The marketplace-of-souls bargain** — *"a small price and a rendezvous"* sharpens the deal into a tryst; the deal-as-liaison register.
> - **Name-as-power** — *"What's my name?"* resonates (a pointer only) with the vault's name-as-power material, the *El Roi / El Roy* throat motif.

## Source notes

- **Grounded — the release.** *33*, September 30 2022, Big Loud Rock; track 3 of 10, 3:04. Written and produced by **Roy English & Matt Pauling** (both credited on both). **little luna is a featured vocalist, not a credited writer or producer.** Officially styled with a lowercase *little luna*. Credits verified track-by-track from Spotify's Credits view.
- **Grounded — the vocal casting.** little luna = guide/introducer/chorus; Roy = Attention (the pitch, bridge, and *"What's my name?"*). A two-hander: the guide delivers the mark, the deity collects.
- **Grounded — the capitalization.** The stylized capitals (Attention, Door, Store, BIG, Stars, Born, Light, Mind, Empires, MaMa) are deliberate thematic emphasis carried from the official lyric — **not** an acrostic code. (Tested and confirmed: a cipher sweep found *"Pay Attention"* and *"Are you paying attention?"* do **not** validate as Hall-of-Mirrors unlock codes — a useful negative, given how much the song foregrounds the phrase. The real code for the *"Hello, Attention"* artifact is MARDUK, seeded elsewhere.)
- **Grounded paratext — the ARG Upload manifesto.** The song had its own mint page, `pay-attention.jagwartwin.com` (audio 3:04), header **ARE YOU PAYING ATTENTION**, with a deadpan first-person pitch in Roy's voice: *"my failure at being a social media influencer has me here at 33 still trying to 'make it'. To solve this problem I'm uploading my consciousness to the internet so a digital version of 'me' can be a 24/7 influencer. Enter now and mint body parts to immortalize little luna and I. There is also a music NFT locked inside but you'll have to pay Attention."* (See the joke-origin note below.)
- **Grounded paratext — the NFT drop.** The *"Pay Attention"* NFT was the 3rd Jagwar Twin music NFT, unlocked by the CTHDRL "digital body parts" auction on May 2 2022 (which sold out) — the origin of the whole [[Hall of Mirrors]] body-part unlock-code system (*"mint body parts to immortalize little luna and I"*). *Pay Attention* was one of the 6 song-mint subdomains of the 2022 ARG (with Online, It's Your Time, I Like to Party, Soul Is A Star, Happy Face).

No dedicated character file exists for her; she's handled inline, which is why her name isn't wikilinked here.
- **The deadpan con (why it's funny, and why that matters).** The whole song is a straight-faced huckster pitch — *"Step right up," "I can show you what's in Store," "Whatchu want? I'll get it for you"* — and the joke is that the used-car-salesman is a cosmic deity. The bathos is the tell: the same voice that claims *"I've been behind the Empires that rise"* also diagnoses *"a little bitty problem, but I know what to do."* The ARG manifesto plays the same key — the culture3 origin confirms the whole Upload "began as a joke" to the CTHDRL team — and Roy has called the sibling track *Online* "so fun and so ridiculous." The comedy isn't a distraction from the critique; it *is* the critique. Which is also why the deity framing is the deeper read and not the only one: the song works perfectly as a witty attention-economy satire — an advertising or influencer pitch — with no metaphysics required. The Lucius / Demiurge / counterfeit-light layer is interpretation; the lyric doesn't assert it.
- **Interviews.** The 2022 album-cycle press (Ones to Watch; culture3 / Clovis McEvoy; The Honey POP; Atwood; Alternative Press; Tokyo Journal) covers the album, the Hall of Mirrors, and the Upload — but **no** Roy quote in the corpus glosses the *song* "Pay Attention" by name. The song-specific grounding is the ARG page + the Marduk confirmation + the cipher tweet, not an interview gloss.
- **Open questions.** Was the attention-economy critique deliberate *here* in 2022, or read back from later work? (2022 names no Lucius/Circus/Algorithm.) Are the *"Empires"* media, political, or civilizational — or all three? Is the speaker literally Marduk, or is the song merely Marduk-shaped? And the final lone *"MaMa"* stays unparsed — flagged, built on nothing. *(The album-art bust and the exact ad-lib line-splits are noted but not load-bearing.)*
